## Executive Summary

- Strong emphasis today on manipulation and control foundations: new tactile, proprioceptive, and primitive-based policies significantly boost dexterous and long-horizon performance.
- Navigation and SLAM advances focus on human-aware social navigation, geometry-centric dense SLAM, event-based VIO in dynamics, and robust/global localization with strong cross-FOV guarantees.
- Multiple works tighten the bridge between learning and control theory: residual-adaptive sampling MPC, STL-constrained motion planning, safe RL via MPC-style safety filters, and symplectic dynamics learning.
- VLA and foundation-model-based control continue to mature, with onboard multi-agent architectures, native memory compression, latent memory tokens, and attention bottlenecks for better generalization.
- Institution-priority highlights include CMU’s subject-conditioned diffusion for personalized gait generation and MIT’s cryptographically secure loop-closure framework for multi-robot SLAM privacy.
- Quadruped and humanoid whole-body control progresses via large-scale quadruped motion foundations and immersive VR/LLM-assisted humanoid teleoperation for locomotion-plus-manipulation tasks.
