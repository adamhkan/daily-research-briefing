Executive Summary

  • Dexterous manipulation is a major focus, with multiple new systems (DexViTac, DexEXO, TeleDex) and control methods enabling scalable visuo-tactile data collection and accessible teleoperation.
  • Vision-language-action and generative control are being optimized for fine-grained, low-latency manipulation via new architectures (VolumeDP, KineVLA) and adaptive flow-matching heads (ProbeFlow, GeCO).
  • Several works tackle safety and formal guarantees in motion planning and control, including neural HJ reachability, STL-constrained RL, and specification-aware shaping of foundation model policies.
  • Learning-enhanced MPC and port-Hamiltonian modeling advance principled, high-performance control for vehicle-manipulator and mobile systems, with real-time GP and NN/GP-based MPC frameworks.
  • Navigation and path-planning receive attention in extraterrestrial and lunar settings as well as omnidirectional visual-language navigation, emphasizing robust mapping, DSG-based reasoning, and global replanning.
  • New datasets and perception tools for deformable objects and demonstration interfaces (TrackDeform3D, human-gripper studies) aim to improve data quality for complex manipulation tasks.

A token-efficient, hierarchical LLM interface improves referring accuracy and navigation success while reducing prompt length for aerial and ground robots in cluttered, multi-room environments.Link

DexViTac: Collecting Human Visuo-Tactile-Kinematic Demonstrations for Contact-Rich Dexterous ManipulationProposes DexViTac, a human-centric system that synchronously records first-person RGB, high-density tactile data, end-effector pose, and hand kinematics for contact-rich dexterous tasks. A kinematics-grounded tactile representation and a 2,400+ demo dataset yield >85% success across four real-world tasks, outperforming baselines under occlusions.Link
VolumeDP: Modeling Volumetric Representation for Manipulation Policy LearningTsinghua UniversityIntroduces a visual imitation policy that lifts image features into a 3D volumetric representation and selects task-relevant voxels as spatial tokens for action prediction. The approach achieves 88.8% success on LIBERO, significantly outperforming prior methods, and shows strong robustness in ManiSkill, LIBERO-Plus, and real robot tests.Link
KineVLA: Towards Kinematics-Aware Vision-Language-Action Models with Bi-Level Action DecompositionDefines a kinematics-rich VLA setting where language specifies fine-grained motion attributes and introduces KineVLA with a bi-level action representation decoupling goal and kinematic details. New datasets and experiments on LIBERO and a Realman-75 robot show improved precision, controllability, and generalization for kinematics-sensitive manipulation.Link
DexEXO: A Wearability-First Dexterous Exoskeleton for Operator-Agnostic Demonstration and LearningUniversity of California, Los AngelesPresents DexEXO, a hand exoskeleton emphasizing comfort, cross-user adaptability, and visual/kinematic alignment with the target robot hand. The design supports scalable cross-operator data collection and enables diffusion policies trained directly from wrist-mounted RGB to achieve competitive dexterous manipulation without complex visual post-processing.Link
TeleDex: Accessible Dexterous TeleoperationUniversity of California, Los AngelesIntroduces TeleDex, an open-source teleoperation system that uses commodity smartphones to stream 6-DoF wrist and 21-DoF hand poses for controlling robot arms and multi-fingered hands. It supports phone-only and hand-mounted modes, enabling fast, low-cost collection of dexterous demonstrations to adapt manipulation policies during deployment.Link
Contingency-Aware Planning via Certified Neural Hamilton-Jacobi ReachabilityNortheastern UniversityCombines Fourier Neural Operator-based approximations of Hamilton-Jacobi reachability with sampling-based planning to achieve certified safe navigation in unknown environments. The method guarantees under-approximated safe reach-avoid sets, integrates a recovery policy, and is demonstrated on a KUKA youBot achieving asymptotically optimal, contingency-aware motion.Link
Efficient and Reliable Teleoperation through Real-to-Sim-to-Real Shared AutonomyProposes a shared-autonomy pipeline where a kNN-based human surrogate is learned from minutes of real teleoperation data to train a residual copilot policy in simulation. The resulting copilot assists fine-grained manipulation (e.g., nut threading, gear meshing), improving novice success, expert efficiency, and demonstration quality upon real-world deployment.Link
GMT: Goal-Conditioned Multimodal Transformer for 6-DOF Object Trajectory Synthesis in 3D ScenesIntroduces GMT, a transformer that synthesizes continuous 6-DoF object trajectories conditioned on 3D geometry, point clouds, semantics, and goal poses. Experiments on synthetic and real benchmarks show superior spatial accuracy and orientation control over human-motion baselines, establishing a new reference for learning-based manipulation planning.Link
Consistency-Driven Dual LSTM Models for Kinematic Control of a Wearable Soft Robotic ArmProposes a dual-LSTM model that jointly learns forward and inverse kinematics of a pneumatically actuated soft wearable arm with a cycle-consistency loss to handle hysteresis and one-to-many mappings. Experiments show improved tracking accuracy and stability, enabling collaborative tasks like handover, obstacle-aware pick-and-place, and drawer operation.Link
Specification-Aware Distribution Shaping for Robotics Foundation ModelsPresents a post-training framework that modifies the action distribution of pretrained robotics foundation models at run time to satisfy Signal Temporal Logic constraints. By forward simulating candidate actions and enforcing STL feasibility, it achieves complex spatio-temporal objectives and safety without changing model parameters.Link
