Executive Summary

  • Mobile manipulation sees two notable advances: a unified framework for efficient yet reliable continuous operation, and ExpressMM for socially expressive, interruptible mobile-manipulator behaviors.
  • Dexterous and contact-rich manipulation progresses via BiCoord’s long-horizon bimanual benchmark, GraspSense’s force-map-aware grasp planning, and instrumentation-guided gentle button pressing.
  • New learning and control methods target robustness and efficiency in manipulation: closed-loop referring-aware visuomotor policies, hierarchical multi-frequency policies (HiPolicy), and VLA training with explicit language–action grounding.
  • Motion planning and control are strengthened by learning-guided force-feedback MPC for deburring, evolutionary motion parameterization for soft scooping, and multi-agent CoEnv for compositional sim–real collaboration.
  • Navigation and localization advances span few-step visual navigation, precise last-meter image-goal navigation, underwater AUV planning and control (plus a UUV survey), and 3DGS-based UAV visual localization.
  • Whole-body and adaptive robot capabilities are enhanced via differentiable invariant sets for legged limit cycles, probabilistic finite-step invariants for hybrid systems, and MIT’s ZipFold modular shape- and stiffness-changing actuators.

Synergizing Efficiency and Reliability for Continuous Mobile ManipulationHong Kong University of Science and Technology; University of Science and Technology of ChinaProposes a unified framework for continuous mobile manipulation that balances long-horizon efficiency with robustness under uncertainty. A reliability-aware trajectory planner embeds execution reliability into spatiotemporal optimization and is paired with a phase-dependent switching controller, yielding higher task success (26.67%–81.67% gains) and efficiency across diverse end-effector constraints.Link
ExpressMM: Expressive Mobile Manipulation Behaviors in Human-Robot InteractionsExpressMM integrates a high-level language-guided planner with a low-level vision-language-action policy to generate expressive, intent-communicating behaviors for mobile manipulators. It supports interruptible HRI, allowing users to redirect actions mid-task, and user studies show improved interpretability, perceived safety, and usefulness in collaborative assembly.Link
BiCoord: A Bimanual Manipulation Benchmark towards Long-Horizon Spatial-Temporal CoordinationBeihang UniversityIntroduces BiCoord, a benchmark of long-horizon, tightly coordinated bimanual tasks requiring continuous inter-arm dependency and dynamic role exchange. New temporal, spatial, and spatio-temporal metrics expose that strong baselines (DP, RDT, Pi0, OpenVLA-OFT) struggle on such coupled tasks, highlighting open challenges in cooperative dexterous manipulation.Link
GraspSense: Physically Grounded Grasp and Grip Planning for a Dexterous Robotic Hand via Language-Guided Perception and Force MapsIntelligent Space Robotics LaboratoryPresents a grasp-and-grip planning pipeline that computes per-surface admissible force maps from reconstructed object geometry, enabling selection of grasps that avoid mechanically weak regions. A finger-wise impedance controller scales stiffness based on local admissible forces, yielding safer grasps on delicate cups and reframing dexterous manipulation as a joint grasp–force planning problem.Link
Learning-Guided Force-Feedback Model Predictive Control with Obstacle Avoidance for Robotic DeburringINRIA; LAAS-CNRSCombines force-feedback MPC with diffusion-based motion priors to handle contact-rich industrial deburring with collision constraints. The diffusion model provides robust motion initialization across instances, while MPC enforces precise force tracking, torque feasibility, and obstacle avoidance, enabling stable insertion and circular deburring in challenging configurations.Link
Simulation-Driven Evolutionary Motion Parameterization for Contact-Rich Granular Scooping with a Soft Conical Robotic HandMRON SINIC X CorporationDevelops a physics-based simulation of a deformable soft conical hand that morphs from flat sheets to adaptive conical structures for granular scooping. An evolutionary strategy optimizes scooping trajectories within this model, producing motions that generalize across containers and tasks, validated in both simulation and real-robot trials.Link
You're Pushing My Buttons: Instrumented Learning of Gentle Button PressesInvestigates training-time instrumentation to learn gentler button-pressing policies using audio from a microphone fingertip and privileged button-state supervision. The learned audio encoder provides a contact-event representation used at inference with vision and audio only, maintaining success rates while consistently reducing contact forces.Link
Referring-Aware Visuomotor Policy Learning for Closed-Loop ManipulationIntroduces ReV, a closed-loop visuomotor policy that integrates sparse human- or planner-provided referring points to steer trajectories in real time. Dual diffusion heads generate global action anchors and locally interpolate them, enabling on-the-fly replanning from perturbed demonstrations without additional data or fine-tuning, improving success in dynamic scenes.Link
HiPolicy: Hierarchical Multi-Frequency Action Chunking for Policy LearningBeihang University; CS, School of CS, PKU; School of CS, PKU; Xi'an Jiaotong UniversityProposes HiPolicy, which predicts action chunks at multiple temporal frequencies to jointly capture long-horizon plans and fine-grained reactive control. An entropy-guided execution strategy adaptively balances reliance on coarse vs. fine actions, and plugging HiPolicy into existing generative policies yields higher performance and execution efficiency on simulated and real manipulation tasks.Link
A1: A Fully Transparent Open-Source, Adaptive and Efficient Truncated Vision-Language-Action ModelA1 is an open-source VLA framework optimized for low-latency manipulation control on commodity hardware via adaptive inference. By monitoring action consistency across VLM layers to terminate early and using inter-layer truncated flow matching to cut denoising iterations, it achieves state-of-the-art success on simulation and real robots while reducing per-episode latency by up to 72%.Link
