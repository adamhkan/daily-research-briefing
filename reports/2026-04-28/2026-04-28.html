Executive Summary

  • Multiple works advance motion planning and optimal control, from DRL-augmented parallel trajectory optimization (ATRS) to variable-time multi-vehicle coordination (V-STC) and energy-efficient multi-robot coverage (MRCPP).
  • New foundations for hybrid and constrained control are proposed, including MPC for general hybrid systems and RL policies that provably satisfy affine constraints under unknown hybrid dynamics.
  • Dexterous and dynamic manipulation see progress via palm-DoF analysis for thumb opposability, diverse trajectory primitives for articulated objects (QDTraj), and zero-shot dynamic rope manipulation through system identification (Wiggle and Go!).
  • Robot learning and foundation-model-based manipulation are extended with gaze-based intention modeling (GazeVLA), non-Markovian VLA with semantic graphs and code planners (CodeGraphVLP), and a closed-loop LLM-driven autonomous learning framework for uncovered tasks.
  • Simulation and tooling advances include LeHome for deformable household object manipulation and higher-order SE(3) tangent-operator derivatives for robust geometric optimization.
  • Real-world deployments and characterization span learning-augmented industrial automation on a live production line and precision torque coupling measurements on insect-scale flapping-wing robots.

ATRS: Adaptive Trajectory Re-splitting via a Shared Neural Policy for Parallel OptimizationProposes ATRS, which embeds a shared deep RL policy inside an ADMM-based parallel trajectory optimizer to adaptively re-split stagnating trajectory segments. The shared policy operates on solver-internal states, enabling size-invariant handling of varying segment counts and zero-shot generalization, yielding up to 26% fewer iterations and 19% less compute, with real-time replanning at 35 ms.Link
False Feasibility in Variable Impedance MPC for Legged LocomotionAnalyzes a structural mismatch between parameter-based feasible sets and physically realizable behaviors in variable-impedance MPC when stiffness is treated as an instantaneous decision variable. Introduces dimensionless thresholds on actuator bandwidth vs. task timescale that predict infeasibility and shows that augmenting the prediction state with stiffness eliminates the mismatch by construction.Link
V-STC: A Time-Efficient Multi-Vehicle Coordinated Trajectory Planning ApproachIntroduces variable-time-step spatio-temporal corridors where both spatial cubes and their durations are optimization variables for each autonomous vehicle. This flexible corridor construction reduces temporal occupancy while maintaining collision-free separation, enabling more time-efficient multi-vehicle trajectories than fixed-time STC baselines.Link

DM$^3$-Nav: Decentralized Multi-Agent Multimodal Multi-Object Semantic NavigationNortheastern UniversityDevelops DM$^3$-Nav, a fully decentralized semantic navigation system where robots coordinate via ad-hoc pairwise communication without a global map or central coordinator. An implicit task allocation mechanism based on intent broadcasting and distance-weighted frontier selection reduces redundant exploration, matching or surpassing centralized baselines in simulation and two-robot real-world tests.Link
Model Predictive Control of Hybrid Dynamical SystemsUniversity of California, Santa CruzFormulates MPC for general hybrid dynamical systems modeled by coupled continuous and discrete dynamics with constraints. Provides structural properties of the hybrid optimal control problem and checkable conditions guaranteeing asymptotic stability via stage/terminal costs and static feedback laws linked by a control Lyapunov function.Link
Learning Control Policies to Provably Satisfy Hard Affine Constraints for Black-Box Hybrid Dynamical SystemsProposes an RL-based method that enforces affine, repulsive policies near constraint boundaries to guarantee satisfaction of hard affine state constraints for hybrid systems with unknown nonlinear dynamics but affine resets. A second repulsive region accounts for resets, and experiments on constrained pendulum and paddle juggling show strict safety with higher policy quality than reward shaping or learned CBF baselines.Link
A Kinematic Analysis of Palm Degrees of Freedom for Enhancing Thumb Opposability in Robotic HandsAnalyzes how introducing palm degrees of freedom in a five-finger robotic hand affects thumb–finger overlap workspace, quantified via voxelized fingertip reach. Results show palm motion can significantly increase opposability, especially for ring and little fingers, but under fixed total DoF there are trade-offs between enlarged overlap and finger redundancy, offering design guidance for dexterous hands.Link
Wiggle and Go! System Identification for Zero-Shot Dynamic Rope ManipulationIntroduces a two-stage framework where a system identification module infers rope physical parameters from motion, which then condition a goal-directed action optimizer for dynamic rope tasks. The approach enables zero-shot execution of new tasks, improving 3D target-striking accuracy from 15.34 cm to 3.55 cm and achieving high fidelity between predicted and real rope dynamics.Link
