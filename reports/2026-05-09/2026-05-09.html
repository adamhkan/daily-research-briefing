Executive Summary

  • Dexterous manipulation advances focus on learning from sparse human demonstrations and improving sim-to-real transfer, achieving strong real-world performance across diverse contact-rich tasks.
  • World Action Models and VLA-based systems are rapidly evolving, with new methods for object-addressable state representations, parameter-efficient adaptation, cross-model knowledge transfer, and trust-aware execution.
  • Multiple works tackle motion planning and control under complex constraints, including visibility-aware trajectory search, resource- and safety-constrained planning, and structure-preserving dynamics learning.
  • New theoretical and algorithmic tools for whole-body and locomotion control include analysis of pendular structure in multi-contact locomotion and higher-order Lie-group dynamics for floating-base robots.
  • Benchmarks and sensing innovations (visually realistic manipulation simulation, contact-free grasp stability, electronics-free tactile feedback, deformable-object teleoperation) target more reliable and accessible real-world manipulation.
  • MIT contributes a cephalopod-inspired pulsed-jet swimming robot, while Stanford analyzes how pendular dynamics emerge from optimal control in legged locomotion.

On the Emergence of Pendular Structure in Multi-Contact LocomotionStanford UniversityAnalyzes how pendulum-like force patterns emerge directly from an optimal control problem that penalizes angular-momentum rates in multi-contact legged locomotion. The work explains conditions under which LIPM-like behavior appears, highlights limitations in two-contact stances, and validates results in simulation (point-mass quadruped) and on a Unitree Go1.Link
Cycle-resolved Cephalopod-Inspired Pulsed-Jet Robot With High-Volume Expulsion and Drag-Reduced GlidingMassachusetts Institute of TechnologyIntroduces a cephalopod-inspired pulsed-jet robot with a rigid–soft origami mantle enabling large cavity-volume reduction and drag-reduced gliding. Experiments dissect the contributions of expelled volume, glide duration, and refill path to overall locomotion, achieving fast, efficient jet-driven swimming as a platform for studying expulsion–glide–refill cycles.Link

DexSynRefine: Synthesizing and Refining Human-Object Interaction Motion for Physically Feasible Dexterous Robot ActionsProposes DexSynRefine, which turns sparse human-object interaction demonstrations into executable dexterous robot motions via a motion-manifold synthesizer, a task-space residual RL policy, and a contact-and-dynamics adaptation module. Across five dexterous tasks, it significantly outperforms prior action representations in simulation and sim-to-real transfer, improving success by 50–70 percentage points over pure kinematic retargeting.Link
DexSim2Real: Foundation Model-Guided Sim-to-Real Transfer for Generalizable Dexterous ManipulationIntroduces DexSim2Real, combining foundation-model-guided visual domain randomization, a tactile-visual cross-attention policy, and an LLM-based progressive curriculum for dexterous manipulation. On six contact-rich tasks with blinded evaluation it achieves a 78.2% real-world success rate and shrinks the sim-to-real gap to 8.3%, outperforming prior sim-to-real methods.Link
ReActor: Reinforcement Learning for Physics-Aware Motion RetargetingPresents a bilevel RL-based framework that simultaneously adapts human reference motions to new robot morphologies and trains a tracking controller in physics simulation. Using approximate gradients and sparse semantic rigid-body correspondences, it produces physically plausible motions without manual tuning and demonstrates challenging behaviors on disparate embodiments, including quadrupeds.Link
Lie Group Formulation of Recursive Dynamics Algorithms of Higher Order for Floating-Base RobotsDevelops higher-order Lie-group formulations of Newton–Euler, articulated-body inertia, and hybrid dynamics algorithms for floating-base trees, providing closed-form equations and an admissible Coriolis matrix. Demonstrated on a 12-DoF aerial manipulator, the methods scale quadratically with derivative order and support accurate high-order dynamics for planning and control.Link
Structure-Preserving Gaussian Processes Via Discrete Euler-Lagrange EquationsIntroduces Lagrangian Gaussian Processes that embed discrete Euler–Lagrange structure into GP dynamics learning, preserving energy behavior and physical consistency. The approach learns from position-only data, enabling data-efficient, uncertainty-aware long-term predictions on systems including a real soft robot with hysteresis.Link
Track A*: Fast Visibility-Aware Trajectory Planning for Active Target TrackingProposes Track A*, a visibility-aware trajectory planner on a 4D spatio-temporal grid that uses DAG layering, BVH-based distance caching, and beam pruning. Across large CARLA-based benchmarks it achieves near-baseline visibility with over 20× speedups and 100% convergence, providing a practical offline reference for tracking scenarios.Link
Resource-Constrained Robotic Planning in the face of Mixed UncertaintyChinese Academy of Sciences; University College London; University of Chinese Academy of SciencesModels robots under both probabilistic and nondeterministic uncertainty with resource limits using Consumption MDPs with Set-valued Transitions, combined with LTLf task specifications. Presents unrolling and optimized synthesis methods that compute robust strategies maximizing task satisfaction without resource exhaustion, validated on warehouse transport scenarios.Link
Maximal Controlled Invariant-MPC: Enhancing Feasibility and Reducing Conservatism through Terminal CBF Constraint in Safety-Critical ControlIntroduces an MPC formulation that uses a Control Barrier Function as a terminal constraint to reduce conservatism while guaranteeing safety. Theoretical results and simulations on a nonholonomic system show expanded reachable sets, fewer infeasible states (1.7–2.7× reduction), and improved tracking even for trajectories lying inside nominally unsafe regions.Link
OA-WAM: Object-Addressable World Action Model for Robust Robot ManipulationProposes OA-WAM, which represents scenes as persistent robot and object slots with addressable identifiers, decoupling object identity from appearance. The model jointly predicts slot-based world evolution and continuous action chunks, achieving near-SOTA results on LIBERO and strong robustness on geometric generalization and causal slot-intervention tests.Link
CKT-WAM: Parameter-Efficient Context Knowledge Transfer Between World Action ModelsIntroduces CKT-WAM, which transfers knowledge between World Action Models via compact text-space context vectors derived from compressed teacher hidden states and lightweight adapters. With only 1.17% trainable parameters, it attains 86.1% success on LIBERO-Plus and 83.3% in real long-horizon tasks, approaching full fine-tuning performance.Link
