## Executive Summary

- Dexterous manipulation advances focus on learning from sparse human demonstrations and improving sim-to-real transfer, achieving strong real-world performance across diverse contact-rich tasks.
- World Action Models and VLA-based systems are rapidly evolving, with new methods for object-addressable state representations, parameter-efficient adaptation, cross-model knowledge transfer, and trust-aware execution.
- Multiple works tackle motion planning and control under complex constraints, including visibility-aware trajectory search, resource- and safety-constrained planning, and structure-preserving dynamics learning.
- New theoretical and algorithmic tools for whole-body and locomotion control include analysis of pendular structure in multi-contact locomotion and higher-order Lie-group dynamics for floating-base robots.
- Benchmarks and sensing innovations (visually realistic manipulation simulation, contact-free grasp stability, electronics-free tactile feedback, deformable-object teleoperation) target more reliable and accessible real-world manipulation.
- MIT contributes a cephalopod-inspired pulsed-jet swimming robot, while Stanford analyzes how pendular dynamics emerge from optimal control in legged locomotion.
