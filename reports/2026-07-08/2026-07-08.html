Executive Summary

  • ETH Zurich proposes an anytime plug-and-play distributed MPC framework that maintains collision-free multi-robot driving under dynamic communication topologies.
  • A cluster of new VLA and world-model methods (Cortex, InternVLA-A1.5, DSWAM, XS-VLA, ACE, HiMe, ACE-Brain-0.5, WSA1, Worldscape-MoE) advance long-horizon, memory-aware, and 3D-centric manipulation and navigation control.
  • Multiple works target high-precision and contact-rich manipulation, including zero-shot sim-to-real dexterous grasping, tactile-residual correction and adaptation, unified dexterous grasp synthesis, and proprioception-based compliance.
  • Benchmarks such as RoboDojo, SoftVTBench, Deform360, LH-AVLN, and GigaWorld-1 expand systematic evaluation of manipulation, deformable and tactile interaction, long-horizon navigation, and world models for policy assessment.
  • Motion planning and control see advances in GPU-accelerated collision constraints, kinodynamic and belief-space planners, function-space diffusion, and occlusion-aware contingency planning for autonomous driving.
  • Several navigation and mapping works improve robustness under sensing uncertainty, including ego-centric octrees, LiDAR Gaussian Splatting SLAM, geometry-aware VO and underwater odometry, and multi-sensor heading refinement.

This design transfers dynamics priors without pixel-level generation, improving simulation and real-world manipulation performance, especially on compositional generalization and long-horizon tasks.Link
Closing the Reality Gap: Zero-Shot Sim-to-Real Deployment for Dexterous Force-Based Grasping and ManipulationPresents a sim-to-real RL pipeline for multi-fingered hands using dense tactile simulation, current-to-torque calibration, and randomized actuator dynamics.
