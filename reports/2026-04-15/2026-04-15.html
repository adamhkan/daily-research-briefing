Executive Summary

  • Institution highlights include CMU’s TAX-DPD for high-precision placement, UC Berkeley’s fast convex-set distance for planning, UW’s GPU-accelerated contact-implicit locomotion optimization, NYU’s safe human-to-humanoid imitation via CBFs, and UCSD’s OL–CL gap analysis in autonomous driving.
  • Motion planning advances feature BTIT* for faster kinodynamic optimal planning and COSMIK-MPPI for constraint-satisfying MPC in human–robot shared workspaces.
  • Manipulation and control papers emphasize affordance-aware data generation (AffordSim, AffordGen), precise placement (AnySlot, TAX-DPD), dexterous in-hand reorientation (ViserDex), and contact-rich control with multimodal sensing (OmniUMI).
  • Whole-body and humanoid control is tackled via safe imitation (Safe Human-to-Humanoid CBF-QP), language-annotated whole-body motion generation (CLAW), and advanced state estimation on Lie groups (NANO-L) for legged robots.
  • Learning frameworks trend toward richer world and action models (AIM, GWM, ProGAL-VLA, ScoRe-Flow), better human-to-robot transfer (WARPED, LIDEA), and device-efficient deployment of manipulation policies (DC-QFA).
  • Foundational methods for dexterous and micro-manipulation (ViserDex, micro-dexterity survey, HO-Flow, continuum grasp kinematics, Point2Pose) point to increasingly perception-rich, geometry-aware manipulation stacks.

Disentangled Point Diffusion for Precise Object PlacementCarnegie Mellon UniversityTAX-DPD introduces a hierarchical placement framework that first predicts a dense scene-level Gaussian-mixture prior over candidate placements, then refines object pose via a disentangled point-cloud diffusion that separately reasons over object geometry and placement frame. It achieves state-of-the-art precision and generalization in both rigid and deformable (cloth hanging) placement tasks, outperforming SE(3)-diffusion approaches.Link
A Ray Intersection Algorithm for Fast Growth Distance Computation Between Convex SetsUC BerkeleyThis work defines growth distance as a unified metric for intersection and separation between convex sets and reduces its computation to a ray–Minkowski-difference intersection problem. An iterative polyhedral approximation algorithm yields monotone convergence with strong primal/dual feasibility guarantees and state-of-the-art performance in benchmarks, with demonstrated applications to motion planning and rigid-body simulation.Link
GPU-Accelerated Continuous-Time Successive Convexification for Contact-Implicit Legged LocomotionUniversity of WashingtonThe authors extend continuous-time successive convexification to contact-implicit trajectory optimization via integral cross-complementarity constraints, avoiding missed contacts between time nodes while allowing mode changes. A JAX-based GPU implementation with homotopy on complementarity achieves order-of-magnitude speedups and lower-energy solutions versus existing SCP methods, validated on HalfCheetah locomotion tasks.Link
Safe Human-to-Humanoid Motion Imitation Using Control Barrier FunctionsNew York UniversityThis paper presents a vision-based pipeline that maps single-camera human skeleton keypoints to humanoid joint commands and enforces safety via a Control Barrier Function quadratic program layer. The CBF-QP filters imitation motions in real time to avoid both self-collisions and human–robot collisions, with simulations demonstrating collision-aware whole-body imitation.Link
BridgeSim: Unveiling the OL-CL Gap in End-to-End Autonomous DrivingUC San DiegoAnalyzing open-loop vs. closed-loop performance, the authors identify observational domain shift and objective mismatch as key causes of deployment failure in end-to-end driving policies. They propose a test-time adaptation framework that calibrates perception, reduces Q-value bias, and enforces temporal consistency, substantially improving closed-loop robustness over OL-optimized baselines.Link
PRoID: Predicted Rate of Information Delivery in Multi-Robot Exploration and RelayingCarnegie Mellon UniversityPRoID introduces a relay criterion for multi-robot exploration that uses learned map prediction to estimate future information gain along each robot’s path and compares it to immediate return. Its failure-aware variant PRoID-Safe incorporates survival probabilities, leading to better information delivery under time and failure constraints than fixed-schedule relaying in real-world floorplan simulations.Link
Towards Multi-Source Domain Generalization for Sleep Staging with Noisy LabelsETH ZurichThe work introduces NL-DGSS, a benchmark for multi-source domain-generalized sleep staging under label noise, and shows that standard noisy-label methods degrade when domain shift is present. FF-TRUST, a multimodal framework with joint time–frequency early-learning regularization and confidence-diversity constraints, achieves state-of-the-art robustness across multiple public datasets and noise regimes.Link

Optimal Kinodynamic Motion Planning Through Anytime Bidirectional Heuristic Search with Tight Termination ConditionBTIT* is a kinodynamic sampling-based planner that couples an anytime bidirectional heuristic search with informed trees while enforcing a meet-in-the-middle property and MM-optimality. Efficient tight termination conditions enable early stopping and significantly improve time-to-first-solution and convergence versus non-lazy informed batch planners on 4D and 10D benchmarks.Link
COSMIK-MPPI: Scaling Constrained Model Predictive Control to Collision Avoidance in Close-Proximity Dynamic Human EnvironmentsCzech Technical University in Prague; LAAS-CNRS; University of TrentoCOSMIK-MPPI integrates Model Predictive Path Integral control with a constraints-as-terminations formulation and markerless human motion estimation to guarantee collision avoidance for torque-controlled manipulators. It achieves 100% task success and reliable constraint satisfaction at ~22 ms control cycles, outperforming gradient-based MPC and vanilla MPPI in human–robot interaction tasks.Link
ViserDex: Visual Sim-to-Real for Robust Dexterous In-hand ReorientationViserDex leverages 3D Gaussian Splatting for domain-randomized visual data generation, enabling robust monocular RGB pose estimation for in-hand object reorientation. Coupled with curriculum RL and teacher–student distillation, it enables a multi-fingered hand with an onboard camera to reorient diverse objects reliably under challenging lighting.Link
AffordSim: A Scalable Data Generator and Benchmark for Affordance-Aware Robotic ManipulationAffordSim combines an open-vocabulary 3D affordance detector with Isaac Sim to generate large-scale, affordance-aware manipulation trajectories across multiple robot arms. The benchmark reveals strong performance on basic grasping but poor success on affordance-critical tasks like pouring and mug hanging, underscoring the need for semantically grounded data.Link
CLASP: Closed-loop Asynchronous Spatial Perception for Open-vocabulary Desktop Object GraspingCLASP introduces a closed-loop, VLM-driven grasping system with a dual-pathway perception module that separates semantic intent from geometric grounding to reduce spatial hallucinations. An asynchronous evaluator compares pre- and post-execution states via text feedback, enabling robust error correction and achieving 87% success with strong generalization in cluttered scenes.Link
OmniUMI: Towards Physically Grounded Robot Learning via Human-Aligned Multimodal InteractionOmniUMI provides a handheld, shared-embodiment interface that synchronously gathers RGB-D, trajectories, tactile sensing, grasp force, and external wrenches for contact-rich tasks. Extending diffusion policies with these modalities and deploying via impedance control yields strong performance on force-sensitive tasks like wiping and selective release.Link
AffordGen: Generating Diverse Demonstrations for Generalizable Object Manipulation with Afford CorrespondenceAffordGen uses 3D generative models and vision foundation models to establish semantic keypoint correspondences across large mesh collections, synthesizing new affordance-aligned manipulation trajectories. Policies trained on this enriched dataset achieve higher success and zero-shot generalization to unseen objects with improved data efficiency.Link
AnySlot: Goal-Conditioned Vision-Language-Action Policies for Zero-Shot Slot-Level PlacementAnySlot introduces a hierarchical VLA framework that first turns language into an explicit scene-marker image specifying the target slot, then executes it via a goal-conditioned policy. On the SlotBench benchmark, this decomposition dramatically improves zero-shot, sub-centimeter placement accuracy over monolithic VLA baselines.Link
MoRI: Mixture of RL and IL Experts for Long-Horizon Manipulation TasksUniversity of Science and Technology of China; University of TokyoMoRI dynamically switches between imitation and reinforcement learning experts using action-variance as a signal, handling coarse and fine-grained manipulation phases separately. With offline pretraining and IL-regularized RL fine-tuning, it achieves ~97.5% success on four real long-horizon tasks while reducing human intervention and convergence time relative to standard RL.Link
LIDEA: Human-to-Robot Imitation Learning via Implicit Feature Distillation and Explicit Geometry AlignmentLIDEA aligns human and robot data via a dual-stage transitive distillation pipeline in latent feature space and a 3D embodiment-agnostic geometric alignment for interaction geometry. Experiments show that human videos can replace up to 80% of robot demonstrations while improving out-of-distribution robustness in manipulation tasks.Link
