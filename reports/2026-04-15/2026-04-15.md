## Executive Summary

- Institution highlights include CMU’s TAX-DPD for high-precision placement, UC Berkeley’s fast convex-set distance for planning, UW’s GPU-accelerated contact-implicit locomotion optimization, NYU’s safe human-to-humanoid imitation via CBFs, and UCSD’s OL–CL gap analysis in autonomous driving.
- Motion planning advances feature BTIT* for faster kinodynamic optimal planning and COSMIK-MPPI for constraint-satisfying MPC in human–robot shared workspaces.
- Manipulation and control papers emphasize affordance-aware data generation (AffordSim, AffordGen), precise placement (AnySlot, TAX-DPD), dexterous in-hand reorientation (ViserDex), and contact-rich control with multimodal sensing (OmniUMI).
- Whole-body and humanoid control is tackled via safe imitation (Safe Human-to-Humanoid CBF-QP), language-annotated whole-body motion generation (CLAW), and advanced state estimation on Lie groups (NANO-L) for legged robots.
- Learning frameworks trend toward richer world and action models (AIM, GWM, ProGAL-VLA, ScoRe-Flow), better human-to-robot transfer (WARPED, LIDEA), and device-efficient deployment of manipulation policies (DC-QFA).
- Foundational methods for dexterous and micro-manipulation (ViserDex, micro-dexterity survey, HO-Flow, continuum grasp kinematics, Point2Pose) point to increasingly perception-rich, geometry-aware manipulation stacks.

| GPU-Accelerated Continuous-Time Successive Convexification for Contact-Implicit Legged Locomotion | University of Washington | The authors extend continuous-time successive convexification to contact-implicit trajectory optimization via integral cross-complementarity constraints, avoiding missed contacts between time nodes while allowing mode changes. A JAX-based GPU implementation with homotopy on complementarity achieves order-of-magnitude speedups and lower-energy solutions versus existing SCP methods, validated on HalfCheetah locomotion tasks. | https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.09993 |
| Safe Human-to-Humanoid Motion Imitation Using Control Barrier Functions | New York University | This paper presents a vision-based pipeline that maps single-camera human skeleton keypoints to humanoid joint commands and enforces safety via a Control Barrier Function quadratic program layer. | BridgeSim: Unveiling the OL-CL Gap in End-to-End Autonomous Driving | UC San Diego | Analyzing open-loop vs. closed-loop performance, the authors identify observational domain shift and objective mismatch as key causes of deployment failure in end-to-end driving policies. They propose a test-time adaptation framework that calibrates perception, reduces Q-value bias, and enforces temporal consistency, substantially improving closed-loop robustness over OL-optimized baselines. | https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.10856 |
| PRoID: Predicted Rate of Information Delivery in Multi-Robot Exploration and Relaying | Carnegie Mellon University | PRoID introduces a relay criterion for multi-robot exploration that uses learned map prediction to estimate future information gain along each robot’s path and compares it to immediate return. Its failure-aware variant PRoID-Safe incorporates survival probabilities, leading to better information delivery under time and failure constraints than fixed-schedule relaying in real-world floorplan simulations. | https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.10433 |
| Towards Multi-Source Domain Generalization for Sleep Staging with Noisy Labels | ETH Zurich | The work introduces NL-DGSS, a benchmark for multi-source domain-generalized sleep staging under label noise, and shows that standard noisy-label methods degrade when domain shift is present. FF-TRUST, a multimodal framework with joint time–frequency early-learning regularization and confidence-diversity constraints, achieves state-of-the-art robustness across multiple public datasets and noise regimes. | https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.10009 |
