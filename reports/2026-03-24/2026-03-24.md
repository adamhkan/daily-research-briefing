## Executive Summary

- Humanoid control and loco-manipulation advance via standardized RL workflows, physics-guided motion generation, and video-based whole-body interaction synthesis, all emphasizing sim-to-real reliability.
- Motion planning theory progresses with tighter separation guarantees for unlabeled multi-robot planning and new decentralized MPC schemes with safety certificates for dense multi-agent systems.
- Cloud and edge architectures for manipulation and navigation mature through speculative policy orchestration and cross-embodiment local planning frameworks that enhance robustness under latency and embodiment shifts.
- Certified and safety-critical control gains traction, from contraction-based RL for robust legged locomotion to closed-form CLF–CBF controllers and topological sim-to-real modeling for continuum and soft manipulators.
- LLM-based tools appear at both algorithmic and systems levels: benchmarking LLMs on path-planning optimality proofs and leveraging LLMs for multi-robot indoor R2X coordination with IoT-augmented perception.
- Human-centered navigation and assistive robotics are highlighted by co-ego guide dog navigation fusing human and robot viewpoints and shared-control teleoperation for large construction manipulators.
