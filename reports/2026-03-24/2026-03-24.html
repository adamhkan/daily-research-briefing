Executive Summary

  • Humanoid control and loco-manipulation advance via standardized RL workflows, physics-guided motion generation, and video-based whole-body interaction synthesis, all emphasizing sim-to-real reliability.
  • Motion planning theory progresses with tighter separation guarantees for unlabeled multi-robot planning and new decentralized MPC schemes with safety certificates for dense multi-agent systems.
  • Cloud and edge architectures for manipulation and navigation mature through speculative policy orchestration and cross-embodiment local planning frameworks that enhance robustness under latency and embodiment shifts.
  • Certified and safety-critical control gains traction, from contraction-based RL for robust legged locomotion to closed-form CLF–CBF controllers and topological sim-to-real modeling for continuum and soft manipulators.
  • LLM-based tools appear at both algorithmic and systems levels: benchmarking LLMs on path-planning optimality proofs and leveraging LLMs for multi-robot indoor R2X coordination with IoT-augmented perception.
  • Human-centered navigation and assistive robotics are highlighted by co-ego guide dog navigation fusing human and robot viewpoints and shared-control teleoperation for large construction manipulators.

IndoorR2X: Indoor Robot-to-Everything Coordination with LLM-Driven PlanningCarnegie Mellon UniversityIntroduces IndoorR2X, a benchmark and simulation framework for LLM-driven multi-robot task planning that fuses mobile robot and static IoT sensor observations into a global semantic state. The platform evaluates how R2X perception and language-based planners can reduce redundant exploration and improve efficiency and reliability in indoor multi-robot coordination.Link
A Closed-Form CLF-CBF Controller for Whole-Body Continuum Soft Robot Collision AvoidanceMassachusetts Institute of TechnologyProposes a closed-form CLF–CBF controller for tendon-driven continuum manipulators that embeds safety constraints analytically into the control law, eliminating online optimization. The method yields real-time whole-body collision avoidance with formal stability and safety guarantees, significantly outperforming QP-based CLF–CBF and sampling-based planners in speed.Link
Not an Obstacle for Dog, but a Hazard for Human: A Co-Ego Navigation System for Guide Dog RobotsETH ZurichAddresses viewpoint asymmetry in robotic guide dogs by fusing a quadruped's ground-level sensing with the human user's egocentric view. The Co-Ego system’s dual-branch obstacle avoidance substantially reduces collisions and cognitive load with overhead hazards that are invisible to the robot alone.Link
SOFTMAP: Sim2Real Soft Robot Forward Modeling via Topological Mesh Alignment and Physics PriorCarnegie Mellon UniversityPresents SOFTMAP, a sim-to-real learning framework for real-time 3D forward modeling of tendon-actuated soft fingers using ARAP-based mesh alignment, a simulation-pretrained MLP, and a residual correction network. The approach achieves millimeter-scale shape prediction and fingertip tracking, boosting teleoperation success while remaining data-efficient.Link
Real-Time Optical Communication Using Event-Based Vision with Moving TransmittersMassachusetts Institute of TechnologyDevelops an optical communication system for multi-robot settings using event cameras to track moving light transmitters and decode messages with >95% accuracy at kHz modulation rates. A geometry-aware unscented Kalman filter enables 7× faster processing than prior work while maintaining tracking accuracy under fast relative motion.Link

PhyGile: Physics-Prefix Guided Motion Generation for Agile General Humanoid Motion TrackingPhyGile unifies text-conditioned motion generation with general motion tracking by generating robot-native trajectories in a high-dimensional skeletal space using physics-guided prefixes. A curriculum-trained mixture-of-experts controller is adapted with physics-derived objectives, enabling stable, agile whole-body humanoid motions far beyond standard walking.Link
Unlabeled Multi-Robot Motion Planning with Improved Separation Trade-offsAnalyzes unlabeled motion planning for unit-disk robots in polygonal environments and presents algorithms that substantially relax previous separation assumptions while retaining polynomial-time guarantees. The work provides constant-factor approximations for total path length under new robot–robot and robot–obstacle separation regimes and characterizes necessary obstacle separations without robot separation.Link
Multi-Agent Motion Planning on Industrial Magnetic Levitation Platforms: A Hybrid ADMM-HOCBF approachKU LeuvenProposes a decentralized MPC framework for holonomic multi-agent motion planning that combines distributed ADMM optimization with a centralized high-order control barrier function layer for safety. Implemented efficiently in C++ and deployed on a magnetic levitation platform, the method scales better than centralized MPC while preserving collision avoidance guarantees.Link
CeRLP: A Cross-embodiment Robot Local Planning Framework for Visual NavigationShanghai Jiao Tong UniversityCeRLP abstracts heterogeneous visual inputs into height-adaptive laser-like scans using calibrated monocular depth and a visual-to-scan projection, enabling a single local planner to run across diverse robots and cameras. Experiments show robust obstacle avoidance and navigation, including vision-language navigation, without per-robot retraining.Link
ContractionPPO: Certified Reinforcement Learning via Differentiable Contraction LayersContractionPPO augments PPO with a differentiable, state-dependent contraction metric layer that is learned jointly with the policy to certify incremental exponential stability. Applied to quadruped locomotion, the method yields robust controllers with formal stability margins that transfer from simulation to hardware under strong disturbances.Link

Results show current LLMs struggle with full proofs but benefit markedly from targeted lemma conditioning, highlighting pathways for LLM-assisted algorithm verification.Link
