Executive Summary

  • Diffusion, video, and one-step generative policies continue to reshape motion planning and manipulation, targeting both mobile manipulators and high-DoF hands.
  • Whole-body and formation control advances span dancing humanoids, quadruped locomotion, and multi-robot formation/formation-MPC under safety constraints and latent risk.
  • New frameworks tackle data scarcity and imperfection in robot learning via taxonomy-guided dexterous grasping, copilot-style imitation, trajectory repair, and preference-based self-imitation.
  • Vision-Language(-Action) models are extended with adaptive chunking, event cameras, sketch-based instruction, and frontier video models as high-level planners, improving robustness and accessibility.
  • Autonomous driving gains hierarchical diffusion planners, video-action world models, and a full sim-to-real stack for VLM-guided RL policies on real vehicles.
  • Core tools and infrastructure emerge, including a high-performance JAX dynamics library and fast online adaptation of neural dynamics for MPC, plus large-scale data generation with video diffusion.

frax: Fast Robot Kinematics and Dynamics in JAXStanford UniversityIntroduces frax, a fully vectorized JAX-based library for rigid-body kinematics and dynamics that runs efficiently on CPU, GPU, and TPU. It achieves microsecond-level computation suitable for kHz control on manipulators and humanoids while supporting large-scale parallel rollouts and automatic differentiation for optimization and learning.Link
Adapting Neural Robot Dynamics on the Fly for Predictive ControlUC San DiegoProposes a hybrid dynamics learning scheme that combines an offline-trained incremental neural dynamics model with fast low-rank second-order online parameter adaptation. Demonstrated on a quadrotor, the method enables rapid on-the-fly model updates for robust model-predictive tracking in novel operating conditions without full retraining.Link
Learning-Based Fault Detection for Legged Robots in Remote Dynamic EnvironmentsMassachusetts Institute of TechnologyPresents an offline learning-based framework that detects single-limb faults in quadruped robots using proprioceptive sensing. The detected fault mode feeds a controller that selects appropriate tripedal gaits, enabling morphology-aware adaptation for operation in hazardous, remote environments.Link

Primitive-based Truncated Diffusion for Efficient Trajectory Generation of Differential Drive Mobile ManipulatorsDevelops a learning-enhanced motion planner for differential-drive mobile manipulators using a keypoint-based encoder and a primitive-based truncated diffusion model to generate diverse, feasible trajectories. The diffusion samples are refined via trajectory optimization, yielding higher success rates and trajectory diversity in cluttered 3D simulations compared to vanilla diffusion and classical planners.Link
Learning Dexterous Grasping from Sparse Taxonomy GuidanceSeoul National UniversityIntroduces GRIT, a two-stage framework where a model first predicts a taxonomy-level grasp specification and then a dexterous policy realizes it with continuous finger motions. By exploiting correlations between grasp taxonomies and object geometry, the method improves generalization to novel objects and achieves 87.9% success with controllable high-level grasp strategy selection.Link
Dynamic Whole-Body Dancing with Humanoid Robots -- A Model-Based Control ApproachHarbin Institute of Technology; Tsinghua University; University of Science and Technology of ChinaPresents an integrated pipeline for dynamic whole-body dance on humanoids, combining MoCap-based motion retargeting, trajectory optimization for dynamic feasibility, and a centroidal-dynamics MPC for online tracking with disturbance-responsive foot placement. Validated on the Kuavo 4Pro humanoids in simulation and a four-minute multi-robot live performance.Link
Build on Priors: Vision--Language--Guided Neuro-Symbolic Imitation Learning for Data-Efficient Real-World Robot ManipulationTufts UniversityProposes a neuro-symbolic framework that autonomously segments unannotated demonstrations into skills, labels them with a VLM, and synthesizes a PDDL domain via Answer Set Programming. The resulting symbolic plan constrains observation/action spaces for each skill policy, enabling data-efficient imitation on an industrial forklift and a Kinova arm from as few as 1–30 demos.Link
CRAFT: Video Diffusion for Bimanual Robot Data GenerationIntroduces CRAFT, which uses edge-conditioned video diffusion to generate photorealistic, action-consistent bimanual manipulation videos from simulator trajectories. The method supports large-scale augmentation over objects, viewpoints, lighting, and embodiments, boosting success rates for dual-arm policies beyond standard data scaling and augmentation.Link
RK-MPC: Residual Koopman Model Predictive Control for Quadruped Locomotion in Offroad EnvironmentsClemson UniversityProposes Residual Koopman MPC, which augments a nominal locomotion template with a compact Koopman-based residual model learned in lifted coordinates to correct terrain- and contact-induced mismatch. Embedded into a convex MPC running at 500 Hz, the controller enables robust blind locomotion of a Unitree Go1 across diverse off-road terrains like grass, gravel, snow, and ice.Link

Pickalo: Leveraging 6D Pose Estimation for Low-Cost Industrial Bin PickingIstituto Italiano di TecnologiaPresents Pickalo, a low-cost bin-picking system using a wrist RGB-D camera, synthetic-trained Mask-RCNN, and SAM-6D for zero-shot 6D pose estimation, fused over viewpoints in a pose buffer. Precomputed antipodal grasps and fast collision checking yield up to 600 picks/hour with 96–99% success in cluttered industrial bins on a UR5e.Link
