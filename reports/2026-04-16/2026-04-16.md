## Executive Summary

- New frameworks for whole-body mobile manipulation (AutoMoMa, WHOLE-MoMa, FastGrasp) emphasize scalable trajectory generation, data-efficient offline RL, and fast, impact-robust grasping with tactile feedback.
- Humanoid and dexterous manipulation advances (HTD, XRZero-G0, VGA) highlight touch-centered multimodal policies, large-scale robot-free VR demonstrations, and 3D vision-geometry backbones that outperform vision-language models.
- Legged loco-manipulation for cooperative transport (PAINT) shows that proprioceptive intent estimation can replace explicit force sensing and generalize across terrains, partners, and robot embodiments.
- New motion-planning and control methods (UGE-MPC, Marble, RL-based dynamics refinement) tackle exploration, contact-rich LCQPs, and bridging first-order hybrid plans to physically feasible execution.
- Continuum and long-horizon manipulation work (reconfigurable TDCM actuation, screw-theoretic lab skills, nanoparticle synthesis automation) focuses on model-free shape control and re-usable, constraint-aware skills from single demonstrations.
