Executive Summary

  • New frameworks for whole-body mobile manipulation (AutoMoMa, WHOLE-MoMa, FastGrasp) emphasize scalable trajectory generation, data-efficient offline RL, and fast, impact-robust grasping with tactile feedback.
  • Humanoid and dexterous manipulation advances (HTD, XRZero-G0, VGA) highlight touch-centered multimodal policies, large-scale robot-free VR demonstrations, and 3D vision-geometry backbones that outperform vision-language models.
  • Legged loco-manipulation for cooperative transport (PAINT) shows that proprioceptive intent estimation can replace explicit force sensing and generalize across terrains, partners, and robot embodiments.
  • New motion-planning and control methods (UGE-MPC, Marble, RL-based dynamics refinement) tackle exploration, contact-rich LCQPs, and bridging first-order hybrid plans to physically feasible execution.
  • Continuum and long-horizon manipulation work (reconfigurable TDCM actuation, screw-theoretic lab skills, nanoparticle synthesis automation) focuses on model-free shape control and re-usable, constraint-aware skills from single demonstrations.

PAINT: Partner-Agnostic Intent-Aware Cooperative Transport with Legged RobotsETH ZurichIntroduces PAINT, a hierarchical learning framework enabling legged robots to cooperatively transport payloads by inferring partner intent purely from proprioceptive signals. A high-level intent estimator is decoupled from a terrain-robust locomotion backbone, yielding compliant, partner-agnostic transport that scales to multi-robot settings and different robot embodiments without external force sensing.Link
Dynamic Multi-Robot Task Allocation under Uncertainty and Communication Constraints: A Game-Theoretic ApproachUC BerkeleyProposes Iterative Best Response (IBR), a decentralized task-allocation policy for fleets of robots operating under uncertain task completion, deadlines, and sparse communication. In large-scale drone delivery scenarios, IBR achieves competitive completion rates with lower computational cost compared to classical scheduling and conflict-based allocation baselines.Link
RMGS-SLAM: Real-time Multi-sensor Gaussian Splatting SLAMMassachusetts Institute of TechnologyPresents a tightly-coupled LiDAR–inertial–visual SLAM system that maintains a real-time 3D Gaussian splatting map while estimating robot pose in large-scale environments. A cascaded Gaussian initialization strategy and Gaussian-based loop closure enable a favorable balance of efficiency, localization accuracy, and photorealistic rendering.Link
Machine Learning-Based Real-Time Detection of Compensatory Trunk Movements Using Trunk-Wrist Inertial Measurement UnitsETH ZurichUses just two IMUs (trunk and wrist) with an XGBoost classifier to detect compensatory trunk movements during upper-limb tasks in real time. The approach matches optical-motion-capture performance on able-bodied subjects and shows promising, though variable, generalization to clinical populations, supporting sparse wearable monitoring in therapy.Link
3DRO: Lidar-level SE(3) Direct Radar Odometry Using a 2D Imaging Radar and a GyroscopeETH ZurichExtends planar direct radar odometry to full SE(3) by fusing 2D imaging radar velocity estimates with 3D gyroscope integration over SO(3). Evaluated over 643 km from Boreas-RT, the method attains lidar-comparable odometry accuracy with a simple, radar–IMU configuration.Link
ReefMapGS: Enabling Large-Scale Underwater Reconstruction by Closing the Loop Between Multimodal SLAM and Gaussian SplattingMassachusetts Institute of TechnologyIntegrates multimodal SLAM with 3D Gaussian splatting to incrementally reconstruct complex underwater reef environments without COLMAP. The system seeds reconstruction from high-certainty trajectory segments, alternates local 3DGS optimization with pose-graph refinement, and achieves accurate, large-scale AUV mapping over hundreds of meters.Link
Artificial Intelligence for Modeling and Simulation of Mixed Automated and Human TrafficNew York UniversitySurveys AI methods for simulating traffic with both autonomous and human-driven vehicles, organizing work into agent-level behavior, environment-level simulation, and cognitive/physics-informed models. It critiques current simulators’ reliance on simplistic drivers and outlines research directions, metrics, and datasets for more realistic mixed-autonomy traffic evaluation.Link

Learning Versatile Humanoid Manipulation with Touch DreamingDevelops an RL-based whole-body humanoid controller and a VR teleoperation pipeline to collect rich loco-manipulation demonstrations. The proposed Humanoid Transformer with Touch Dreaming treats tactile signals as a core modality, predicting future contact forces and tactile latents to enable robust, contact-aware execution on diverse real-world tasks like folding, insertion, and tool use.Link
FastGrasp: Learning-based Whole-body Control method for Fast Dexterous Grasping with Mobile ManipulatorsIntroduces FastGrasp, a two-stage RL framework that couples grasp candidate generation via a conditional VAE on point clouds with a learned whole-body controller for high-speed base–arm–hand coordination. Tactile sensing is used online to stabilize impacts and adapt to object variation, yielding robust fast grasping in both simulation and real-world tests.Link
Scalable Trajectory Generation for Whole-Body Mobile ManipulationPresents AutoMoMa, a GPU-accelerated trajectory optimization framework that unifies base–arm–object kinematics into a single chain and parallelizes planning for whole-body mobile manipulation. It generates over 500k physically valid trajectories across many scenes and robots, showing that large-scale, high-fidelity data is key for training generalizable imitation-learning policies.Link
Whole-Body Mobile Manipulation using Offline Reinforcement Learning on Sub-optimal ControllersProposes WHOLE-MoMa, which uses a randomized, sub-optimal whole-body controller to collect diverse data, then applies offline RL with a chunked diffusion policy and Q-chunking critic to improve coordination. On articulated-object manipulation tasks with a TIAGo++ mobile manipulator, policies surpass WBC, behavior cloning, and offline RL baselines and transfer to hardware without real-world training data.Link
XRZero-G0: Pushing the Frontier of Dexterous Robotic Manipulation with Interfaces, Quality and RatiosIntroduces XRZero-G0, a VR-based, robot-free data collection system with ergonomic dual grippers and a closed-loop pipeline for quality-controlled dexterous manipulation datasets. Experiments show that mixing a small amount of real-robot data with a much larger volume of robot-free demonstrations (e.g., 10:1) can match fully robot-collected datasets while reducing acquisition cost by ~20×.Link
Robotic Manipulation is Vision-to-Geometry Mapping ($f(v) \rightarrow G$): Vision-Geometry Backbones over Language and Video ModelsYat-sen UniversityArgues that manipulation policies should be built on 3D vision-geometry backbones rather than vision-language or video encoders, and proposes the Vision-Geometry-Action (VGA) model using a pretrained 3D world model. With progressive volumetric modulation and joint training, VGA outperforms strong VLA baselines and shows robust zero-shot viewpoint generalization in real-world tasks.Link
Actuation space reduction to facilitate insightful shape matching in a novel reconfigurable tendon driven continuum manipulatorUniversity of Illinois Urbana-ChampaignPresents a reconfigurable tendon-driven continuum manipulator where spacer disks can be rotated to reroute tendons even post-actuation, greatly expanding shape control. By working in curvature–torsion space, the method identifies influential disks and uses a sequential, model-free shape-matching strategy to achieve desired backbones without complex modeling.Link
From Kinematics to Dynamics: Learning to Refine Hybrid Plans for Physically Feasible ExecutionAddresses the gap between first-order hybrid temporal plans and dynamically feasible trajectories by framing plan refinement as an RL problem in continuous space with explicit second-order constraints. The learned policy adjusts the initial kinematic plan to satisfy physical limits like accelerations and deadlines, reliably recovering executable motions.Link
Robotic Nanoparticle Synthesis via Solution-based ProcessesAutomates long-horizon, solution-based nanoparticle synthesis by representing manipulation skills like pouring and knob-turning as sequences of constant screw motions learned from a single demonstration. This screw-theoretic, coordinate-invariant representation captures geometric constraints and supports robust reuse and adaptation of skills across lab setups.Link
Uncertainty Guided Exploratory Trajectory Optimization for Sampling-Based Model Predictive ControlRobotics, Sensing and Networks Laboratory (RSN).; University of Minnesota, Twin Cities; University of Texas at AustinIntroduces UGE-TO, which models trajectories as uncertainty ellipsoids and enforces separation in distribution space via Hellinger distance to promote diverse exploration during optimization. Embedded into MPC as UGE-MPC, it achieves faster convergence and higher success rates than standard sampling-based controllers under the same sample budget, particularly in cluttered environments.Link
