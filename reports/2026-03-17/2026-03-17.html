Executive Summary

  • Vision-Language-Action (VLA) work continues to dominate manipulation, with multiple papers adding structured actions, memory, tactile sensing, and visual feature injection to improve long-horizon and contact-rich control.
  • Several planning-focused methods target hard geometric settings: hybrid deformable–rigid objects, constant-time continuous-goal manipulation, non-prehensile planar contact, and decomposition-based narrow-passage motion planning.
  • New generative and flow-based visuomotor policies emphasize speed and structure, including one-step flow control, structured world-model planning, and rectified-flow navigation across static and dynamic tasks.
  • Navigation and multi-agent path planning advances address dynamic crowds, human motion flows, agricultural bottlenecks, and reactive halting over lightweight topological priors.
  • Humanoid and whole-body control is pushed forward by physically grounded preference optimization, athletic tennis skills from imperfect data, and socially aware multi-platform navigation policies.
  • Robustness, safety, and evaluation receive attention via cyberattack-resilient manipulator control, red-teaming of VLA instructions and CoT reasoning, VLM spatial reasoning benchmarks, and long-term predictive mapping.

COAD: Constant-Time Planning for Continuous Goal Manipulation with Compressed Library and Online AdaptationWorcester Polytechnic InstituteProposes COAD, a framework for constant-time motion planning across continuous goal-parameterized manipulation tasks by discretizing task space into coverage regions and storing only root solutions. At query time, root motions are retrieved in O(1) and rapidly adapted via interpolation, DMPs, or light trajectory optimization, yielding highly compressed libraries with sub-millisecond queries and strong real-world performance.Link
GNN-DIP: Neural Corridor Selection for Decomposition-Based Motion PlanningTechnical University of MunichCombines a graph neural network with a decomposition-informed planner to select promising corridors in a convex cell decomposition of free space, tackling narrow-passage motion planning. The GNN biases corridor search while preserving completeness, achieving 99–100% success and 2–280× speedups over sampling-based baselines in 2D and 3D benchmarks.Link
Coordinated Manipulation of Hybrid Deformable-Rigid Objects in Constrained EnvironmentsPresents a quasi-static optimization-based planner using a strain-based Cosserat rod model to handle hybrid deformable linear objects coupled with rigid parts in constrained spaces. Analytical gradients enable fast inverse kinetostatic solutions and trajectory optimization, validated on dual-arm manipulation of a three-link hybrid object with low deformation error.Link
Push, Press, Slide: Mode-Aware Planar Contact Manipulation via Reduced-Order ModelsMiddle East Technical University; S. Oguz is with the Dept. of Computer Engineering, Bilkent University, Ankara, Turkiye.Develops a mode-aware planner for non-prehensile planar manipulation that abstracts complex frictional contact into a library of reduced-order kinematic models (e.g., unicycle-like). By selecting contact topologies and distributing forces algebraically, it generates fast, optimization-free trajectories for single- and dual-arm push/press/slide tasks while respecting friction limits and arm kinematics.Link
One-Step Flow Policy: Self-Distillation for Fast Visuomotor PoliciesIntroduces One-Step Flow Policy, a generative flow-based visuomotor controller that distills multi-step sampling into a single action step using self-consistency and self-guided regularization. Across 56 manipulation tasks, it outperforms 100-step diffusion and flow baselines while being over 100× faster, and improves the π₀.₅ policy on real-robot benchmarks.Link
Beyond Dense Futures: World Models as Structured Planners for Robotic ManipulationProposes StructVLA, which converts a generative world model into an explicit structured planner by predicting sparse kinematically meaningful milestones instead of dense video rollouts. A two-stage token-based training pipeline aligns these structured frames with low-level actions, yielding strong performance on LIBERO and SimplerEnv-WidowX and robust real-world long-horizon manipulation.Link
Language-Grounded Decoupled Action Representation for Robotic ManipulationIntroduces LaDA, a language-grounded representation that decouples low-level actions into interpretable translation, rotation, and gripper primitives aligned with natural language semantics. A soft-label contrastive objective and adaptive curriculum-based loss weighting improve cross-task alignment and generalization, with strong results on LIBERO, MimicGen, and real-robot benchmarks.Link
RoboStream: Weaving Spatio-Temporal Reasoning with Memory in Vision-Language Models for RoboticsRoboStream augments VLM-based manipulation planners with training-free spatio-temporal memory via STF-Tokens that bind visual evidence to 3D attributes and a Causal Spatio-Temporal Graph capturing action-induced state changes. This persistent geometric anchoring and causal tracking drastically boosts long-horizon performance on RLBench and real-world block-building versus SoFar and VoxPoser.Link
AnchorVLA4D: an Anchor-Based Spatial-Temporal Vision-Language-Action Model for Robotic ManipulationPeking UniversityAnchorVLA4D augments VLA policies with an initial anchor image and a lightweight spatial encoder that jointly processes anchor and current frames to preserve scene context. Built on Qwen2.5-VL with a diffusion action head, it improves spatial reasoning and occlusion robustness, delivering notable gains on Simpler WidowX and 80% average success on real-robot tasks.Link
TacVLA: Contact-Aware Tactile Fusion for Robust Vision-Language-Action ManipulationIstituto Italiano di Tecnologia; Purdue UniversityTacVLA injects tactile sensing into transformer-based VLA policies via a contact-aware gating mechanism that activates tactile tokens only upon contact. Joint fusion of vision, language, and tactile signals significantly improves performance on disassembly and in-box picking and enhances robustness to occlusion in contact-rich manipulation.Link
