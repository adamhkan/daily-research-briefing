## Executive Summary

- Vision-Language-Action (VLA) work continues to dominate manipulation, with multiple papers adding structured actions, memory, tactile sensing, and visual feature injection to improve long-horizon and contact-rich control.
- Several planning-focused methods target hard geometric settings: hybrid deformable–rigid objects, constant-time continuous-goal manipulation, non-prehensile planar contact, and decomposition-based narrow-passage motion planning.
- New generative and flow-based visuomotor policies emphasize speed and structure, including one-step flow control, structured world-model planning, and rectified-flow navigation across static and dynamic tasks.
- Navigation and multi-agent path planning advances address dynamic crowds, human motion flows, agricultural bottlenecks, and reactive halting over lightweight topological priors.
- Humanoid and whole-body control is pushed forward by physically grounded preference optimization, athletic tennis skills from imperfect data, and socially aware multi-platform navigation policies.
- Robustness, safety, and evaluation receive attention via cyberattack-resilient manipulator control, red-teaming of VLA instructions and CoT reasoning, VLM spatial reasoning benchmarks, and long-term predictive mapping.
