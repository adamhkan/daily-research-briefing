Executive Summary

  • Strong emphasis on contact-rich and dexterous manipulation: whole-arm MPC with tactile sensing (TACTIC), tactile-guided dexterity benchmarking (TactiDex), and one-shot force-aware LfD highlight growing focus on physically grounded contact control.
  • Multiple works bridge vision/video priors to feasible robot motion (PhysV2A, GenVid2Robot, DemoBridge, AgenticFocus), tackling embodiment and kinematic feasibility in video-to-action pipelines.
  • Navigation and exploration advances span socially aware and pedestrian-safe mobile robots, crowd-free multi-UAV exploration without communication, underwater vehicle control with CFD surrogates, and permanence-aware autonomous driving.
  • New control and planning abstractions emerge: B-spline action parameterizations, shortcut trajectory planning for offline RL, adaptive MPPI with disturbance estimation, and shared-control teleoperation via probabilistic intent inference.
  • Foundational model and world-model work (CLAP, FlowDAgger, CD-LAM, STP) targets fast adaptation and controllability of large VLM/VLA and action-conditioned world models for both manipulation and navigation.
  • MIT’s Hydra++ demonstrates real-time hierarchical 3D scene graphs with object-level shape estimation, underscoring integration of richer geometry into online mapping pipelines.

TACTIC: Tactile and Vision Conditioned Contact-Centric Control for Whole-Arm ManipulationTACTIC is a receding-horizon controller for whole-arm manipulation that fuses RGB-D, distributed tactile sensing, and a proximity map into a hybrid predictive model. By coupling learned latent dynamics with analytical contact Jacobians inside a sampling-based MPC, it predicts future contact configurations/forces and steers actions to trade task progress against safe, regulated multi-link contact.Link
TactiDex: A Real-World Tactile-Guided Benchmark for Human-Like Dexterous ManipulationTactiDex provides a real-world dataset and benchmark that align dense whole-hand tactile signals with kinematic and object states for dexterous tasks. Building on this, the TactiSkill framework uses a tri-component tactile reward to supervise human-to-robot transfer, improving manipulation success and contact realism, including bimanual scenarios.Link
Validating Virtual Reality for Studying Multimodal Human-Robot Interaction in Socially Aware Robot NavigationThis work validates an immersive VR replica of a motion-capture arena for studying multimodal human-robot co-navigation with a PR2 mobile manipulator. User studies across crossing and pass-by interactions show VR preserves perceived social awareness and reproduces human trajectory and head-orientation behaviors consistent with real-world navigation.Link
Empirical Pedestrian Safety Assessment in a Mobile Robot Using a Predictive Social Force ModelNational Taiwan UniversityThe paper implements classical and PTTC-augmented social force models, plus new predictive variants, on a nonholonomic robot navigating toward a pedestrian in a facing scenario. Objective metrics and user studies confirm that PTTC-based models improve pedestrian safety, while the added benefit of predictive integration is modest in single-pedestrian interactions.Link
Dec-MARVEL: Decentralized Multi-Agent Exploration without Communication under Budget Constraints. of Artificial Intelligence, Sogang University.; . of Electronic Engineering, Sogang University.; National University of SingaporeDec-MARVEL enables teams of UAVs with limited sensing and no explicit communication to perform budget-aware exploration by coordinating implicitly through observed teammate trajectories. A graph-attention actor trained with phase-conditioned critics and a budget curriculum selects waypoints that respect return constraints, achieving higher coverage and lower overlap than baselines across multiple team sizes and budgets, with sim-to-real validation.Link
SplatCtrl: Perception-Action Coupling via Gaussian Scene Representations and Reactive Robot ControlMitsubishi Electric Research Laboratories; University of Cambridge; University of PennsylvaniaSplatCtrl tightly couples 3D Gaussian Splatting-based scene reconstruction with reactive arm control via signed-distance-like metrics derived from isotropic Gaussians. These continuous collision probabilities feed into control barrier functions, enabling smooth, safe arm motion in previously unseen, dynamic environments, including human-robot shared workspaces.Link
DemoBridge: A Simulation-in-the-Loop Toolkit for Single-View Human Demonstration RetargetingDemoBridge converts monocular RGB stereo recordings of human hand demonstrations into collision-aware, dynamically validated robot-arm trajectories. A single global trajectory optimizer reasons jointly over alternative grasps, arm and object collisions, and fidelity to the demonstration, backtracking through a physics simulator when needed to ensure executable, stable rollouts suitable for policy learning.Link
One-Shot Multimodal Learning from Demonstration with Force-Constrained Elastic MapsThis framework performs one-shot LfD that jointly models motion and force, segmenting demonstrations into force-aware primitives and extending elastic maps to encode external force constraints. A convex optimization procedure yields trajectory models that reproduce demonstrated motion and contact profiles, improving safety and robustness across different force-sensing setups and robot platforms.Link
PhysV2A: Reachability-Gated and Semantic-Mask-Constrained Feasibility Completion for Video-to-Robot ManipulationPhysV2A turns object-motion priors from video into robot-feasible trajectories by treating grasps as trajectory-conditioned and performing hierarchical reachability gating over grasp–trajectory candidates. A VLM-assisted semantic mask identifies which Cartesian components can be relaxed, enabling manipulability-aware refinement that preserves task semantics while improving kinematic feasibility and robustness in real-robot tasks.Link
GenVid2Robot: From Video Generation to Robot Manipulation via Rigid-Geometric ConsistencyShanghai Jiao Tong UniversityGenVid2Robot uses generated videos as hypotheses over object motion, accepting only those whose 2D anchor trajectories are consistent with first-frame RGB-D geometry under a sparse SE(3) model. The validated relative motion is bound to a physically grounded grasp pose and refined with bounded depth compensation, yielding grasp-conditioned trajectories that are geometrically consistent and more reliable in real manipulation.Link
