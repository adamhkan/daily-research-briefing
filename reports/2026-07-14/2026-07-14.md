## Executive Summary

- Strong emphasis on contact-rich and dexterous manipulation: whole-arm MPC with tactile sensing (TACTIC), tactile-guided dexterity benchmarking (TactiDex), and one-shot force-aware LfD highlight growing focus on physically grounded contact control.
- Multiple works bridge vision/video priors to feasible robot motion (PhysV2A, GenVid2Robot, DemoBridge, AgenticFocus), tackling embodiment and kinematic feasibility in video-to-action pipelines.
- Navigation and exploration advances span socially aware and pedestrian-safe mobile robots, crowd-free multi-UAV exploration without communication, underwater vehicle control with CFD surrogates, and permanence-aware autonomous driving.
- New control and planning abstractions emerge: B-spline action parameterizations, shortcut trajectory planning for offline RL, adaptive MPPI with disturbance estimation, and shared-control teleoperation via probabilistic intent inference.
- Foundational model and world-model work (CLAP, FlowDAgger, CD-LAM, STP) targets fast adaptation and controllability of large VLM/VLA and action-conditioned world models for both manipulation and navigation.
- MIT’s Hydra++ demonstrates real-time hierarchical 3D scene graphs with object-level shape estimation, underscoring integration of richer geometry into online mapping pipelines.
