Executive Summary

  • Navigation and planning see strong advances, from VLM-augmented sidewalk navigation and safe Ackermann steering in unmapped spaces to scalable self-play and safety-critical post-training for end-to-end driving.
  • Manipulation work focuses on improving long-horizon and dexterous control via memory-efficient world models, equivariant and structured VLA policies, action-chunked execution, and new low-cost dexterous hardware.
  • Multiple papers tackle data and model efficiency for control policies, including frequency-aware flow matching, minimal-layer VLA finetuning, reflection and action-view augmentations, and exploration-efficient finetuning.
  • Formal methods, STL-based planning, and uncertainty/failure estimation are increasingly integrated into navigation and control, improving safety guarantees, temporal logic satisfaction, and failure prediction in VLA and navigation agents.
  • New mapping, SLAM, and state estimation pipelines (Gaussian splatting with thermal/LiDAR, structure-aware 3DGS SLAM, UAV motor-preintegration, humanoid non-inertial estimation) enhance robustness in challenging environments.
  • Legged and whole-body locomotion gain from mixture-of-experts terrain adaptation, symmetry-equivariant parkour world models, sand-ground simulators, and analysis of toe-enabled biped agility and efficiency.
