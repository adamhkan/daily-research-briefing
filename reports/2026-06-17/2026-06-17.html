Executive Summary

  • Multi-scenario and multi-robot motion planning advances span hierarchical RL+MPPI control, generative multi-robot trajectory planners, and safety-guaranteed occlusion-aware navigation.
  • Navigation and exploration see strong new tools: map-free diffusion-based explorers, standardized urban sidewalk benchmarks, ultra-fast DRL navigation training, and task-aware environment augmentation.
  • Dexterous and contact-rich manipulation is pushed forward via tactile-reactive policies, interaction-preserving human-to-robot retargeting, compliant cross-hand grasp transfer, and articulated-object interaction frameworks.
  • Humanoid and whole-body control progresses through disturbance-aware locomotion, full-body motion tracking, energy-aware arm control, and scalable safety analysis and infeasible-robust QP solvers.
  • Vision-language(-action) and world models deepen their role in control, from geometric action models and latent world-action models to retrieval-augmented, memory-augmented, and steering methods for VLAs and diffusion/flow policies.
  • Core navigation infrastructure (SLAM, VO, state estimation, semantic mapping, TRN, radar and monocular pipelines) is strengthened for operation in challenging environments and under tight compute budgets.

HOLO-MPPI: Multi-Scenario Motion Planning via Hierarchical Policy OptimizationMassachusetts Institute of TechnologyIntroduces HOLO-MPPI, a hierarchical framework that learns a high-level, scenario-robust policy offline and uses it online as a data-driven prior for MPPI-based low-level control. Demonstrated on autonomous driving, it improves robustness and performance over vanilla MPPI and end-to-end RL across diverse scenarios while retaining real-time feasibility.Link
VANDERER: Map-Free Exploration using Future-Aware and Visual-Curiosity-Guided Diffusion PolicyNew York UniversityProposes VANDERER, a map-free exploration framework that steers a diffusion policy with a learned visual curiosity module using only monocular images. The method predicts and scores future observations to guide exploratory actions, outperforming prior approaches like NoMaD by covering significantly more area in diverse simulated environments.Link
Learning Context-Aware Neural ODE Dynamics for Adaptive Robotic ControlUC BerkeleyPresents a context-aware dynamics model based on neural ODEs that infers latent environmental factors from state–action histories and plugs into MPC-style controllers. Validated on quadrotor simulation and real Sphero and Fanuc platforms, the approach adapts to temporally and spatially varying dynamics without explicit environment sensing.Link
Robust Conformal CBF and CLF Controllers via Iterative Policy UpdatesETH ZurichDevelops an episodic framework combining adversarially robust conformal prediction with CBF/CLF control to maintain formal safety and stability guarantees under distribution shift. By explicitly budgeting distribution shift between episodes, it updates conformal bounds and policies while preserving probabilistic guarantees, demonstrated on several control case studies.Link

Robots as Tokens: Unified Diffusion Transformer for Coordinated Multi-Robot Trajectory GenerationIntroduces Roken, a diffusion transformer that treats each robot as a token and jointly generates collision-free, connectivity-constrained multi-robot trajectories in a single feed-forward pass. Trained on expert data across varying team sizes, it scales to different robot counts and unseen environments, outperforming the expert planner that generated its training set.Link
PolyMerge: Compressing 3D Gaussian Splats with Polytope Coverings for Provably Safe Resource-Constrained NavigationGeorgia Institute of TechnologyProposes PolyMerge, which converts dense 3D Gaussian Splatting scenes into a compact union of convex polytopes that over-approximate obstacles and integrate with control barrier functions. Demonstrations on a Crazyflie show real-time, resource-efficient safe navigation with formal collision-avoidance guarantees under tight onboard compute.Link
Exact, Efficient, and Safe Occlusion-Aware Planning Using AH-PolyhedronsGeorgia Institute of Technology; Honda Research Institute; Research Institute (HRI), Mountain View, CA.Introduces APRO, an occlusion-aware planning framework using AH-polyhedron reachability to compute exact safety sets under occlusions without added conservatism or road-structure assumptions. Validated in autonomous valet parking scenarios, it achieves 100% safety with more efficient and less conservative behavior than prior guarantee-based methods.Link
FlashNav: Ultra-Fast Policy Training for Robot Navigation within 20 SecondsPresents FlashNav, a fully GPU-resident DRL framework that mirrors the navigation MDP while stripping nonessential simulation details, enabling policy training in under 20 seconds. Policies learned in this bitmap-based simulator transfer to real TurtleBot2 and Unitree Go2 platforms, showing that seconds-level training can still yield deployable obstacle-avoidance behavior.Link
Task-Aware Environment Augmentation for Reliable Navigation via Shielded Conditional DiffusionSCoDA poses environment augmentation—placing a limited number of fiducials along a planned trajectory—as a conditional diffusion problem over marker layouts. A shielded sampler reasons over where uncertainty-critical pose corrections should occur, delivering hardware-validated improvements in trajectory reliability and completion time under localization uncertainty.Link
CrossMaps: Confidence-Aware Open-Vocabulary Semantic Mapping for Rover NavigationPlattner Institute for Digital Engineering, Potsdam, Germany.CrossMaps builds real-time, language-queryable semantic maps from RGB-D data using CLIP embeddings fused with geometric, semantic, and temporal confidence cues in short- and long-term memories. Designed for Jetson-powered UGVs, it provides semantic heatmaps that can be queried in natural language to guide navigation under partial observability.Link
SidewalkBench: Benchmarking Visual Navigation on Urban SidewalksIntroduces SidewalkBench, a GPU-accelerated Isaac Sim benchmark with procedurally generated and scanned urban sidewalk scenes populated by reactive pedestrians. Evaluating nine visual navigation models across unit tests, pedestrian interaction, and long-horizon scenarios, the work highlights robustness gaps and points to large-scale synthetic training as a promising direction.Link
SemGeoNav:A Safety-Guided Visual Navigation Approach with Semantic Reasoning and Geometric PlanningSemGeoNav combines high-level semantic reasoning from end-to-end models with a geometric local planner and trajectory smoothing to achieve safe visual navigation. Real-world tests on a Unitree Go2 show improved obstacle avoidance, higher success rates, and shorter navigation times versus ViNT and NoMaD baselines.Link
Bayesian Optimization for Learning Nonlinear MPC in Autonomous Agent NavigationPresents a LiDAR-based, map-free navigation framework that generates collision-free A*-based trajectories and tracks them with a nonlinear MPC featuring smooth obstacle barriers. Offline Bayesian optimization tunes controller parameters, which transfer effectively from simulation to a Unitree Go2, yielding high success rates and sizable metric improvements without on-robot retuning.Link
