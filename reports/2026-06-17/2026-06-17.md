## Executive Summary

- Multi-scenario and multi-robot motion planning advances span hierarchical RL+MPPI control, generative multi-robot trajectory planners, and safety-guaranteed occlusion-aware navigation.
- Navigation and exploration see strong new tools: map-free diffusion-based explorers, standardized urban sidewalk benchmarks, ultra-fast DRL navigation training, and task-aware environment augmentation.
- Dexterous and contact-rich manipulation is pushed forward via tactile-reactive policies, interaction-preserving human-to-robot retargeting, compliant cross-hand grasp transfer, and articulated-object interaction frameworks.
- Humanoid and whole-body control progresses through disturbance-aware locomotion, full-body motion tracking, energy-aware arm control, and scalable safety analysis and infeasible-robust QP solvers.
- Vision-language(-action) and world models deepen their role in control, from geometric action models and latent world-action models to retrieval-augmented, memory-augmented, and steering methods for VLAs and diffusion/flow policies.
- Core navigation infrastructure (SLAM, VO, state estimation, semantic mapping, TRN, radar and monocular pipelines) is strengthened for operation in challenging environments and under tight compute budgets.
