Executive Summary

  • Dexterous and long-horizon manipulation see multiple advances: privileged tactile distillation, massive bimanual grasp datasets, and improved diffusion/visuomotor memory architectures.
  • Motion planning for high-DoF manipulators and humanoids is pushed forward via GPU-native trajectory optimization, informed sampling, multi-goal RRT*, and uncertainty-aware collision avoidance.
  • Whole-body and mobile manipulation gain new frameworks for risk-aware control, omnidirectional 3D perception, and physics-aware VLA/tri-system architectures.
  • Digital twins and sim-to-real pipelines mature, with Gaussian-splatting-based physical twins and CT-based contact-aware planning for surgical insertion tasks.
  • Vision-language(-action) models are increasingly integrated into planning loops—for navigation, exploration, shared autonomy, skill acquisition, and interpretability/control.
  • Memory, robustness, and uncertainty handling emerge as cross-cutting themes, from evidential waypoint prediction and adversarial robustness to episodic memory in policies and feature-level VLA control.

PTLD: Sim-to-real Privileged Tactile Latent Distillation for Dexterous ManipulationCarnegie Mellon University; UC BerkeleyIntroduces PTLD, a sim-to-real framework that avoids tactile simulation by collecting real-world tactile data with privileged sensors and distilling it into a tactile state estimator. When combined with proprioception-trained sim policies, it yields large gains on in-hand rotation and reorientation tasks, substantially improving sim-to-real dexterous manipulation performance.Link
Observing and Controlling Features in Vision-Language-Action ModelsStanford UniversityAnalyzes internal feature representations in VLAs through linear observability and controllability, enabling targeted interventions that steer behavior without fine-tuning. Demonstrates on π0.5 and OpenVLA that lightweight linear controls can align robot behavior with user preferences in real time while preserving closed-loop capabilities.Link

cuRoboV2: Dynamics-Aware Motion Generation with Depth-Fused Distance Fields for High-DoF RobotsPresents a unified GPU-native stack for motion generation combining B-spline trajectory optimization with torque limits, fast TSDF/ESDF distance fields, and scalable whole-body kinematics/dynamics. Achieves high success and collision-free rates for manipulators and a 48-DoF humanoid, substantially outperforming prior planners in both quality and speed.Link
Direct Contact-Tolerant Motion Planning With Vision Language ModelsIntegrates VLMs into a direct contact-tolerant navigation pipeline by segmenting contact-permissible regions in image space and projecting them into a contact-aware point cloud. A learned controller then solves perception-to-control optimization, enabling efficient navigation through clutter with movable obstacles in sim and on a real car-like robot.Link
U-OBCA: Uncertainty-Aware Optimization-Based Collision Avoidance via Wasserstein Distributionally Robust Chance ConstraintsExtends the OBCA framework to handle uncertainties in robot and obstacle states using distributionally robust chance constraints over polygonal geometries. By avoiding primitive approximations (e.g., circles) and tightening constraints into tractable nonlinear forms, it reduces conservatism and improves navigation efficiency, especially in narrow cluttered spaces.Link
GIANT - Global Path Integration and Attentive Graph Networks for Multi-Agent Trajectory PlanningCombines global path plans with an attentive graph neural network for local collision avoidance among multiple robots. Training-time noise improves robustness, and evaluations show higher success and lower collision rates than NH-ORCA and learned baselines in dense, dynamic environments.Link
Risk-Aware Reinforcement Learning for Mobile ManipulationLearns distributional RL-based teacher policies with adjustable risk sensitivity and distills them into depth-based visuomotor students for mobile manipulation. The resulting policies exhibit tunable risk-aware whole-body behavior with improved worst-case performance in unmapped, dynamic settings.Link
Many-RRT*: Robust Joint-Space Trajectory Planning for Serial ManipulatorsAddresses multi-solution IK in joint-space planning by growing RRT*-Connect trees toward many goal configurations in parallel. This multi-goal strategy improves robustness and yields substantially lower-cost trajectories and higher success rates than prior RRT variants without added runtime.Link
GAIDE: Graph-based Attention Masking for Spatial- and Embodiment-aware Motion PlanningProposes GAIDE, a transformer-based neural informed sampler that encodes both scene geometry and robot embodiment as a graph with attention masking. Integrated into sampling-based planners, GAIDE improves sample efficiency and planning success for high-DoF manipulators over uniform and previous learned samplers.Link
PhysiFlow: Physics-Aware Humanoid Whole-Body VLA via Multi-Brain Latent Flow Matching and Robust TrackingIntroduces a physics-aware multi-brain VLA framework that couples semantic intent from vision-language inputs with low-level whole-body control for humanoids. Experiments indicate improved stability and coordination on dynamic tasks compared with prior VLA-based control approaches.Link
UltraDexGrasp: Learning Universal Dexterous Grasping for Bimanual Robots with Synthetic DataBuilds UltraDexGrasp-20M, a large synthetic dataset of multi-strategy bimanual dexterous grasps generated via optimization and planning. A point-cloud-based policy trained solely on this data achieves robust zero-shot sim-to-real transfer for diverse objects, enabling universal bimanual grasping with high real-world success.Link
Iterative On-Policy Refinement of Hierarchical Diffusion Policies for Language-Conditioned ManipulationProposes HD-ExpIt, which iteratively refines hierarchical diffusion-based planners and controllers via environment interaction, using self-discovered successful behaviors as new training data. This tightens the coupling between high-level subgoal generation and low-level capabilities, yielding state-of-the-art results on the long-horizon CALVIN manipulation benchmark.Link
