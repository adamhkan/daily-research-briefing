Executive Summary

  • Dexterous manipulation advances include sim-to-real tactile latent distillation and massive synthetic datasets for bimanual grasping, significantly boosting in-hand and universal grasp performance.
  • GPU-native and geometry-aware motion generation methods (cuRoboV2, GAIDE, Many-RRT*) improve planning speed, success, and optimality for high-DoF manipulators and humanoids.
  • Whole-body and mobile manipulation see progress in risk-aware visuomotor policies, omnidirectional 3D perception for large-workspace humanoid tasks, and physics-aware VLA frameworks.
  • New frameworks for long-horizon, language-conditioned, and memory-intensive manipulation (HD-ExpIt, VPWEM, SeedPolicy, SkillsCrafter, Critic-in-the-loop) improve robustness and horizon scaling.
  • Uncertainty- and risk-sensitive planning/control methods emerge for contact-tolerant navigation, collision avoidance, waypoint prediction, and medical insertion, enhancing safety under uncertainty.
  • Digital twin systems and interpretable VLA interventions (GaussTwin, feature-controllability in VLAs) support real-time adaptation, closed-loop planning, and alignment of robot behavior with user goals.

PTLD: Sim-to-real Privileged Tactile Latent Distillation for Dexterous ManipulationCarnegie Mellon University; UC BerkeleyIntroduces PTLD, a sim-to-real framework that learns tactile dexterous manipulation without tactile simulation by distilling privileged real-world tactile sensing into a tactile state estimator. Combining this estimator with proprioception substantially improves in-hand rotation and reorientation success over proprioception-only policies.Link
Observing and Controlling Features in Vision-Language-Action ModelsStanford UniversityAnalyzes internal representations in VLAs by defining feature-observability and feature-controllability, enabling linear classifiers and minimal interventions to probe and steer behavior. Demonstrates that targeted internal control can reliably adjust robot actions for π₀.₅ and OpenVLA without fine-tuning, supporting real-time alignment with user preferences.Link

Risk-Aware Reinforcement Learning for Mobile ManipulationPresents a distributional RL framework for learning whole-body, depth-conditioned visuomotor policies with tunable risk sensitivity in mobile manipulation. A privileged teacher is trained with distortion risk metrics and then distilled into depth-based student policies, yielding better worst-case performance and reactive behavior in unmapped environments.Link
cuRoboV2: Dynamics-Aware Motion Generation with Depth-Fused Distance Fields for High-DoF RobotsProposes a unified GPU-native stack for motion generation combining B-spline trajectory optimization with torque limits, fast TSDF/ESDF perception, and scalable whole-body dynamics for manipulators and humanoids. Achieves large speedups, near-perfect collision-free IK, and higher success on high-DoF tasks compared to prior planners.Link
PhysiFlow: Physics-Aware Humanoid Whole-Body VLA via Multi-Brain Latent Flow Matching and Robust TrackingIntroduces a multi-brain VLA framework that couples semantic intent from vision-language inputs with physics-aware whole-body control for humanoids. Experiments show improved stability and coordination in dynamic limb tasks under language and visual guidance.Link
UltraDexGrasp: Learning Universal Dexterous Grasping for Bimanual Robots with Synthetic DataBuilds a synthetic data pipeline combining optimization-based grasp synthesis and planning-based demonstrations to create a 20M-frame bimanual dexterous grasp dataset. A point-cloud policy trained only on this data transfers zero-shot to the real world, achieving over 80% success on diverse objects.Link
Iterative On-Policy Refinement of Hierarchical Diffusion Policies for Language-Conditioned ManipulationProposes HD-ExpIt, which iteratively refines hierarchical diffusion policies by using environment feedback to discover successful behaviors and distill them back into the planner and controller. This implicitly grounds high-level subgoals in low-level capability and reaches state-of-the-art results on CALVIN for long-horizon language-conditioned manipulation.Link
Direct Contact-Tolerant Motion Planning With Vision Language ModelsDevelops a direct contact-tolerant planner that uses VLMs to reason about contact-tolerable regions, projecting image-space masks into a contact-aware point cloud and optimizing a perception-to-control objective. Demonstrated on a car-like platform, it improves navigation efficiency and robustness in cluttered, movable environments.Link
Many-RRT*: Robust Joint-Space Trajectory Planning for Serial ManipulatorsExtends RRT*-Connect to plan simultaneously toward multiple IK-derived joint goals, addressing kinematic redundancy and goal ambiguity in joint space. Parallel goal trees yield substantially higher success and lower-cost trajectories without added runtime, especially in cluttered scenes.Link
GAIDE: Graph-based Attention Masking for Spatial- and Embodiment-aware Motion PlanningIntroduces GAIDE, a transformer-based informed sampler that encodes obstacles and robot embodiment as a graph and uses attention masking to bias sampling. Compared to uniform and prior learned samplers, it improves efficiency and success for sampling-based manipulator planners in high-dimensional spaces.Link
Omni-Manip: Beyond-FOV Large-Workspace Humanoid Manipulation with Omnidirectional 3D PerceptionProposes an end-to-end LiDAR-driven visuomotor policy that uses 360° panoramic point clouds and time-aware attention pooling to control humanoid manipulation over a large workspace. Reduces the need for base motion and outperforms egocentric depth-based baselines in cluttered, extended-reach tasks.Link
Curve-Induced Dynamical Systems on Riemannian Manifolds and Lie GroupsProposes CDSM, a framework that constructs stable dynamical systems along curves defined on manifolds such as SE(3) and SPD(n), combining tangential and normal components. Demonstrated on manipulators and a mobile manipulator, it enables real-time generation of geometry-consistent motions in structured spaces.Link
