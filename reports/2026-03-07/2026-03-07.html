Executive Summary

  • Dexterous and long-horizon manipulation see multiple advances, from sim-to-real tactile distillation (PTLD) to massive synthetic bimanual grasp datasets and new diffusion-based policy architectures with memory.
  • Motion planning for high-DoF systems progresses via GPU-native stacks (cuRoboV2), multi-goal RRT* variants, graph-structured neural samplers, and uncertainty-aware collision avoidance.
  • Whole-body and mobile manipulation are pushed toward real deployments with risk-aware RL, omnidirectional 3D perception for humanoids, and physics-/VLA-based full-body control.
  • Vision-language(-action) models are increasingly integrated into planning and control, powering contact-tolerant motion planning, long-horizon manipulation frameworks, and open-world navigation and search.
  • Interpretability, safety, and robustness receive attention through feature-level control of VLAs, adversarial analysis of visuomotor policies, evidential waypoint prediction, and perception-uncertainty-driven shared autonomy.
  • Tooling and infrastructure emerge around motion planning libraries and digital twins, including LLM-assisted Python bindings for OMPL and physically grounded Gaussian-splatting-based twins for manipulation.

PTLD: Sim-to-real Privileged Tactile Latent Distillation for Dexterous ManipulationCarnegie Mellon University; UC BerkeleyIntroduces PTLD, a sim-to-real framework that trains proprioceptive policies in simulation and then distills a real-world tactile state estimator from privileged sensor data without requiring tactile simulation. On in-hand rotation and reorientation benchmarks, incorporating PTLD-driven tactile sensing yields large performance gains over proprioception-only policies, enabling more challenging dexterous manipulation skills.Link
Observing and Controlling Features in Vision-Language-Action ModelsStanford UniversityAnalyzes internal representations in VLAs such as π0.5 and OpenVLA, defining feature observability via linear probes and feature controllability via minimal linear interventions framed as optimal control. Demonstrations show that targeted interventions can reliably steer robot behavior in closed loop without fine-tuning, enabling real-time alignment to user preferences and task constraints.Link

cuRoboV2: Dynamics-Aware Motion Generation with Depth-Fused Distance Fields for High-DoF RobotsPresents a unified GPU-native stack for dynamics-aware motion generation combining B-spline trajectory optimization with torque limits, fast TSDF/ESDF mapping, and scalable whole-body kinematics/dynamics. The system scales from arms to 48-DoF humanoids, achieving high collision-free success rates and substantial speedups over prior planners and GPU implementations.Link
PhysiFlow: Physics-Aware Humanoid Whole-Body VLA via Multi-Brain Latent Flow Matching and Robust TrackingIntroduces a physics-aware multi-brain VLA framework for humanoids that fuses semantic motion intent with whole-body control, using latent flow matching and tracking to stabilize dynamic, coordinated behaviors. Experiments show reliable language- and vision-guided full-body coordination for humanoid robots on real-world tasks.Link
UltraDexGrasp: Learning Universal Dexterous Grasping for Bimanual Robots with Synthetic DataProposes UltraDexGrasp, a pipeline that synthesizes high-quality, multi-strategy bimanual dexterous grasp trajectories by coupling optimization-based grasp synthesis with planning-based demonstration generation, yielding a 20M-frame dataset. A point-cloud-based grasp policy trained only on this synthetic data transfers zero-shot to real bimanual robots, achieving robust grasps on diverse unseen objects.Link
Direct Contact-Tolerant Motion Planning With Vision Language ModelsDevelops a contact-tolerant planner that uses a VLM to segment movable/deformable objects in images, projects these masks into point clouds, and plans directly over contact-aware point sets. The resulting system enables a car-like robot to navigate cluttered, movable environments more efficiently and robustly than existing CTMP baselines in sim and real.Link
U-OBCA: Uncertainty-Aware Optimization-Based Collision Avoidance via Wasserstein Distributionally Robust Chance ConstraintsExtends Optimization-Based Collision Avoidance to handle uncertainty in robot and obstacle states via distributionally robust chance constraints over Wasserstein ambiguity sets. Without simplifying polygons to circles/ellipses, U-OBCA remains computationally tractable and substantially reduces conservatism, improving navigation efficiency in narrow, cluttered environments.Link
GIANT - Global Path Integration and Attentive Graph Networks for Multi-Agent Trajectory PlanningIntroduces a multi-robot navigation framework that fuses pre-planned global paths with a local GNN-based policy that models agent interactions through attention. Noise-robust training yields improved success and lower collision rates compared to NH-ORCA and deep RL baselines across diverse dynamic scenarios.Link
Risk-Aware Reinforcement Learning for Mobile ManipulationProposes a two-stage method where a privileged teacher policy is trained with distributional RL and distortion risk metrics to produce varying risk-sensitive behaviors, which are then distilled into depth-based visuomotor student policies. The learned policies execute reactive whole-body motions for mobile manipulators while exhibiting tunable risk sensitivity and improved worst-case performance.Link
Many-RRT*: Robust Joint-Space Trajectory Planning for Serial ManipulatorsExtends RRT*-Connect to plan simultaneously toward multiple IK solutions by growing trees from many goal configurations in parallel, mitigating failures due to poor goal choice in joint space. Across robots and environments, Many-RRT* achieves dramatically higher success rates and lower-cost trajectories than prior RRT variants without extra runtime.Link
GAIDE: Graph-based Attention Masking for Spatial- and Embodiment-aware Motion PlanningPresents GAIDE, a neural informed sampler that encodes obstacles, goals, and robot embodiment as a graph and applies attention masking within a transformer to focus sampling on spatially relevant regions. When plugged into sampling-based planners, GAIDE improves planning efficiency and success in high-dimensional manipulator configuration spaces over uniform, heuristic, and prior learned samplers.Link
Accelerating Sampling-Based Control via Learned Linear Koopman DynamicsReplaces expensive nonlinear dynamics rollouts in MPPI with a learned deep Koopman operator that provides linear latent dynamics from interaction data. The resulting MPPI-DK controller maintains near-baseline control quality on pendulum, surface vehicle, and quadruped tasks while substantially reducing computational cost for real-time sampling-based control.Link
