Executive Summary

  • New sim-to-real and tactile methods (PTLD, GenTact-Prox) significantly boost dexterous and whole-body manipulation by leveraging privileged sensing and 3D-printed tactile skin.
  • GPU-native and learning-based motion generation (cuRoboV2, Many-RRT*, GAIDE, CDSM, U-OBCA) push scalable, risk-aware planning from manipulators to humanoids and mobile robots.
  • Multiple works target long-horizon, memory-intensive manipulation with hierarchical diffusion, recurrent and memory-augmented visuomotor policies, and VLA memory benchmarks (HD-ExpIt, SeedPolicy, VPWEM, RoboMME).
  • Vision-language(-action) systems increasingly act as high-level brains for manipulation and navigation, with tri-system architectures, critic-triggered replanning, and training-free frontier selection (Critic in the Loop, PhysiFlow, OpenFrontier).
  • Data efficiency and sim/digital twins remain central: new pipelines for massive synthetic bimanual grasp data, handheld collection with instant policy iteration, and physically grounded digital twins (UltraDexGrasp, RoboPocket, GaussTwin).
  • Robustness, safety, and interpretability are addressed via risk-aware mobile manipulation, shared autonomy under perception uncertainty, adversarial analysis of visuomotor policies, and controllable VLA features (Risk-Aware RL, SPIRIT, adversarial 3D objects, VLA feature control).

PTLD: Sim-to-real Privileged Tactile Latent Distillation for Dexterous ManipulationCarnegie Mellon University; UC Berkeley; University of WashingtonIntroduces PTLD, a sim-to-real framework that learns tactile state estimation from real-world privileged sensing and distills it into policies trained in simulation without requiring tactile simulators. On in-hand rotation and reorientation benchmarks, adding PTLD-derived tactile input yields large performance gains over proprioception-only dexterous manipulation.Link
RoboMME: Benchmarking and Understanding Memory for Robotic Generalist PoliciesStanford UniversityProposes RoboMME, a standardized benchmark of 16 manipulation tasks targeting temporal, spatial, object, and procedural memory in VLA models. The authors systematically compare 14 memory-augmented variants of a π0.5-based backbone, showing that memory design choices are highly task-dependent and revealing strengths/weaknesses of existing architectures.Link
Observing and Controlling Features in Vision-Language-Action ModelsStanford UniversityDevelops a framework to identify linearly encoded features in VLA internal representations and steer behavior via minimal linear interventions grounded in optimal control. Experiments on π0.5 and OpenVLA show that these targeted interventions can reliably adjust robot behavior online without full model fine-tuning, enabling real-time preference alignment.Link
OpenFrontier: General Navigation with Visual-Language Grounded FrontiersETH ZurichFrames open-world navigation as selecting and reaching semantically grounded frontiers using off-the-shelf vision–language priors. The training-free OpenFrontier system avoids dense mapping and policy training, achieving strong zero-shot navigation performance in simulation and on real mobile robots.Link

cuRoboV2: Dynamics-Aware Motion Generation with Depth-Fused Distance Fields for High-DoF RobotsNVIDIAPresents a unified GPU-native motion generation stack that combines B-spline trajectory optimization with torque limits, fast TSDF/ESDF-based perception, and scalable whole-body kinematics/dynamics for manipulators and humanoids. The system achieves near-perfect success and collision avoidance under payload, handles a 48-DoF humanoid, and substantially improves locomotion policy tracking while being up to 61× faster than prior GPU approaches.Link
PhysiFlow: Physics-Aware Humanoid Whole-Body VLA via Multi-Brain Latent Flow Matching and Robust TrackingIntroduces a physics-aware multi-brain VLA framework that couples semantic motion intent with whole-body humanoid control. Latent flow matching and robust tracking enable vision–language-guided coordinated limb motions for complex tasks, improving stability and semantic consistency over prior humanoid control methods.Link
UltraDexGrasp: Learning Universal Dexterous Grasping for Bimanual Robots with Synthetic DataShanghai AI Laboratory; The Chinese University of Hong KongProposes UltraDexGrasp, a pipeline that synthesizes physically plausible, multi-strategy bimanual dexterous grasps and trajectories, yielding a 20M-frame dataset over 1,000 objects. A point-cloud policy trained solely on this synthetic data achieves robust zero-shot sim-to-real bimanual grasping with over 81% success on diverse real novel objects.Link
Iterative On-Policy Refinement of Hierarchical Diffusion Policies for Language-Conditioned ManipulationHD-ExpIt iteratively refines hierarchical diffusion policies for language-conditioned manipulation by using environment rollouts to discover successful behaviors and distill them back into both planner and controller. This on-policy improvement grounds high-level subgoals in low-level capabilities and yields state-of-the-art performance on the long-horizon CALVIN benchmark.Link
Critic in the Loop: A Tri-System VLA Framework for Robust Long-Horizon Manipulationbionic Tri-System architecture com-Presents a tri-system architecture with a VLM "brain" for semantic planning, a fast VLA "cerebellum" for reactive control, and a visual critic that monitors execution and triggers replanning on anomalies. This dynamic scheduling reduces expensive VLM calls while significantly boosting robustness and autonomy on challenging long-horizon manipulation benchmarks.Link
Lifelong Language-Conditioned Robotic Manipulation LearningChinese Academy of Sciences; Shenyang Institute of Automation; State Key Laboratory of Robotics and Intelligent SystemsIntroduces SkillsCrafter, a framework for continual learning of language-conditioned manipulation skills that mitigates catastrophic forgetting. It adapts existing skills via shared representations and skill-semantic subspaces, and aggregates relevant prior skills for new tasks, improving retention and generalization across diverse manipulation skills.Link
SeedPolicy: Horizon Scaling via Self-Evolving Diffusion Policy for Robot ManipulationSichuan University; Dexmal Inc.Proposes SEGA, a gated-attention temporal module that compresses long observation histories into a compact latent state, enabling diffusion policies to scale to long horizons with modest overhead. Integrated as SeedPolicy, the approach substantially outperforms standard diffusion policies and other imitation learning methods on 50 RoboTwin 2.0 manipulation tasks, especially under randomized conditions.Link
Python Bindings for a Large C++ Robotics Library: The Case of OMPLimulation and learning frameworks in robotics research.Explores using LLMs to generate nanobind-based Python bindings for the large C++ OMPL motion planning library, with humans in the loop for verification. Risk-Aware Reinforcement Learning for Mobile ManipulationDevelops distributional RL-based teacher policies that optimize distortion risk metrics to produce varying degrees of risk-aware behavior in mobile manipulation, then distills them into depth-based visuomotor students. The resulting policies exhibit adjustable risk sensitivity and improved worst-case performance while executing reactive whole-body motions in unmapped environments.Link

This Many-RRT* strategy avoids committing to suboptimal IK branches and achieves dramatically higher success rates and lower trajectory costs than prior RRT variants in cluttered manipulator scenarios.Link
