Executive Summary

  • Dexterous and long-horizon manipulation sees multiple advances: tactile sim-to-real distillation (PTLD), massive synthetic bimanual grasp datasets (UltraDexGrasp), and several memory- and diffusion-based visuomotor policy architectures.
  • Motion planning progresses on several fronts: GPU-accelerated dynamics-aware planning for high-DoF robots (cuRoboV2), multi-goal RRT* in joint space (Many-RRT*), graph-structured neural informed sampling (GAIDE), and risk/uncertainty-aware planners for both manipulators and mobile robots.
  • Whole-body and humanoid control is extended with physics-aware VLA frameworks (PhysiFlow), risk-aware mobile manipulation policies, and LiDAR-driven 360° visuomotor control for large-workspace humanoid manipulation (Omni-Manip).
  • Vision-language(-action) models permeate navigation and manipulation, enabling contact-tolerant planning with VLM reasoning, training-free frontier-based VL navigation, tri-system LLM/VLA control, and interpretable/controllable VLA feature interventions.
  • Several works tackle robustness and uncertainty: evidential waypoint prediction (ELLIPSE), distributionally robust collision avoidance (U-OBCA), adversarial analysis of visuomotor policies, uncertainty-aware shared autonomy (SPIRIT), and CT-enabled contact-aware surgical planning.
  • Tooling and infrastructure for motion/manipulation advance via digital twins (GaussTwin, textile-sorting twin), OMPL Python bindings with LLM assistance, and benchmarks/datasets focused on memory and hyperbolic multiview pretraining for manipulation.

PTLD: Sim-to-real Privileged Tactile Latent Distillation for Dexterous ManipulationCarnegie Mellon University; UC BerkeleyIntroduces PTLD, a sim-to-real framework that avoids tactile simulation by collecting real-world data with privileged tactile sensing and distilling it into a tactile state estimator. When combined with proprioceptive policies trained in simulation, this substantially boosts in-hand rotation and reorientation performance, demonstrating large gains over proprioception-only baselines.Link
Observing and Controlling Features in Vision-Language-Action ModelsStanford UniversityAnalyzes internal representations of VLA models, defining feature observability and controllability via linear probes and minimal linear interventions. Shows that targeted manipulation of internal features can reliably steer robot behavior in VLAs like π0.5 and OpenVLA without fine-tuning, enabling lightweight online alignment with user preferences.Link

Risk-Aware Reinforcement Learning for Mobile ManipulationProposes a distributional RL framework to learn visuomotor policies for mobile manipulation with tunable risk sensitivity. A privileged teacher is trained with a distributional critic and distortion risk metrics, then distilled into depth-based student policies that exhibit better worst-case performance during reactive whole-body motion in unmapped environments.Link
cuRoboV2: Dynamics-Aware Motion Generation with Depth-Fused Distance Fields for High-DoF RobotsPresents a unified GPU-native stack for motion generation, combining B-spline trajectory optimization with torque limits, fast TSDF/ESDF-based perception, and scalable whole-body kinematics/dynamics for high-DoF systems including humanoids. Achieves near-perfect success and collision avoidance on industrial arms and humanoids with large speedups over existing solvers.Link
Many-RRT*: Robust Joint-Space Trajectory Planning for Serial ManipulatorsExtends RRT*-Connect to simultaneously plan to multiple IK-derived joint-space goals, mitigating the risk of committing to a poor configuration for a given end-effector pose. Experiments across robots and cluttered environments show dramatically higher success rates and substantially lower-cost trajectories compared to standard RRT variants.Link
GAIDE: Graph-based Attention Masking for Spatial- and Embodiment-aware Motion PlanningIntroduces GAIDE, a neural informed sampler that encodes both workspace geometry and robot embodiment as a graph, integrated into a transformer via attention masking. This spatially structured sampler improves sample efficiency and success rates of sampling-based motion planners for manipulators over uniform, hand-crafted, and prior learned samplers.Link
Direct Contact-Tolerant Motion Planning With Vision Language ModelsDevelops a contact-tolerant planner that uses a VLM to label image regions as contact-acceptable or not, projects this into a contact-aware point cloud, and feeds it to a learned navigation controller. The resulting system enables mobile robots to exploit safe contacts with movable objects for efficient navigation in cluttered environments, outperforming CTMP baselines.Link
PhysiFlow: Physics-Aware Humanoid Whole-Body VLA via Multi-Brain Latent Flow Matching and Robust TrackingProposes a multi-brain VLA architecture for humanoids that couples semantic intent with physics-aware whole-body control via latent flow matching. Experiments indicate stable, vision-language-guided full-body coordination on dynamic tasks, addressing prior trade-offs between semantic guidance and low-level robustness.Link
UltraDexGrasp: Learning Universal Dexterous Grasping for Bimanual Robots with Synthetic DataIntroduces a large-scale synthetic data pipeline for bimanual dexterous grasping, combining optimization-based synthesis and planning-based demonstrations into a 20M-frame dataset. A point-cloud-based grasp policy trained only in simulation achieves robust zero-shot sim-to-real transfer for diverse objects and grasp strategies with over 80% real-world success.Link
Omni-Manip: Beyond-FOV Large-Workspace Humanoid Manipulation with Omnidirectional 3D PerceptionPresents a LiDAR-driven 3D visuomotor policy for humanoid manipulation that leverages panoramic point clouds and a time-aware attention pooling mechanism. This 360° perception enables reliable whole-body manipulation across a large workspace without frequent base motions, outperforming egocentric RGB-D baselines in cluttered real-world tasks.Link
Curve-Induced Dynamical Systems on Riemannian Manifolds and Lie GroupsIntroduces CDSM, a framework for constructing stable dynamical systems directly on manifolds such as SE(3) and SPD(n) by defining a reference curve and combining tangential and normal components. Demonstrations on manipulators and a mobile manipulator show real-time, geometry-consistent motion generation that adapts poses and impedance matrices online.Link
U-OBCA: Uncertainty-Aware Optimization-Based Collision Avoidance via Wasserstein Distributionally Robust Chance ConstraintsExtends Optimization-Based Collision Avoidance to account for uncertainty in robot and obstacle states using distributionally robust chance constraints over Wasserstein ambiguity sets. Avoids simplistic shape approximations by handling polygonal geometries directly, yielding less conservative and more efficient trajectories in narrow, cluttered navigation scenarios.Link
