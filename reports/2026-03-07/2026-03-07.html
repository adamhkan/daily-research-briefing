Executive Summary

  • Dexterous and long-horizon manipulation see multiple advances: sim-to-real tactile distillation, large-scale bimanual grasp data, and powerful diffusion-based visuomotor policies with explicit memory and horizon scaling.
  • Motion planning progresses on several fronts, from GPU-native dynamics-aware stacks and multi-IK RRT* variants to graph-structured neural samplers and robust distributionally robust collision avoidance.
  • Humanoid and mobile manipulation benefit from physics-aware whole-body control, LiDAR-based 360° perception for large-workspace tasks, and risk-aware RL for depth-based mobile manipulation.
  • VLA and language-conditioned systems are pushed toward more robust, interpretable, and adaptable behavior via hierarchical VLA frameworks, critic-scheduled VLM/VLA control, and tools for observing and steering internal features.
  • Digital twins and medical/specialized planning—Gaussians-based manipulation twins and CT-driven cochlear insertion planning—highlight precise, contact-aware control in challenging domains.
  • Stanford, CMU/UC Berkeley/UW, ETH Zurich and collaborators contribute benchmark, interpretability, and navigation work that underpins more reliable generalist robotic policies.

PTLD: Sim-to-real Privileged Tactile Latent Distillation for Dexterous ManipulationCarnegie Mellon University; UC Berkeley; University of WashingtonIntroduces PTLD, a sim-to-real framework that learns tactile state estimators from real privileged tactile data, then uses them to enhance proprioception-trained dexterous manipulation policies without simulating tactile sensors. On in-hand rotation and reorientation benchmarks, adding PTLD-based tactile sensing yields 57–182% performance gains over proprioception-only baselines.Link
OpenFrontier: General Navigation with Visual-Language Grounded FrontiersETH ZurichFormulates open-world navigation as selecting and reaching semantic frontiers, using vision–language priors as high-level anchors instead of dense 3D maps or trained policies. The training-free system integrates multiple VL models to achieve strong zero-shot performance across navigation benchmarks and real-world mobile robot deployments.Link
RoboMME: Benchmarking and Understanding Memory for Robotic Generalist PoliciesStanford UniversityProposes RoboMME, a standardized 16-task benchmark targeting temporal, spatial, object, and procedural memory in VLA-based manipulation. Alongside, 14 memory-augmented variants of a π0.5 VLA backbone reveal that the best memory mechanism is highly task-dependent, clarifying trade-offs for long-horizon manipulation policies.Link
Observing and Controlling Features in Vision-Language-Action ModelsStanford UniversityDevelops tools to identify linearly encoded features within VLA representations and applies minimal linear interventions, framed as optimal control, to steer behavior in π0.5 and OpenVLA. The work demonstrates that internal VLA features can be both observed and reliably manipulated online, enabling preference alignment without fine-tuning.Link

UltraDexGrasp: Learning Universal Dexterous Grasping for Bimanual Robots with Synthetic DataShanghai AI Laboratory; The Chinese University of Hong KongProposes UltraDexGrasp, a synthetic pipeline that combines optimization-based grasp synthesis with planning-based demonstrations to generate 20M frames of bimanual dexterous grasps across multiple strategies. A point-cloud grasp policy trained solely on this data transfers zero-shot to real bimanual robots, achieving over 81% success on diverse unseen objects.Link
Risk-Aware Reinforcement Learning for Mobile ManipulationUses distributional RL with distortion risk metrics to train privileged teacher policies that express varying risk sensitivities for mobile manipulation, then distills them into depth-conditioned visuomotor students. The resulting policies perform reactive whole-body motion in unmapped environments while improving worst-case performance at runtime-adjustable risk levels.Link
Many-RRT*: Robust Joint-Space Trajectory Planning for Serial ManipulatorsExtends RRT*-Connect to plan simultaneously toward multiple IK solutions, addressing the ambiguity of mapping task-space goals to joint-space configurations. Across robots and cluttered scenes, Many-RRT* dramatically improves success (up to 100% vs. 1.6%) and reduces trajectory cost by 44.5% within comparable runtime.Link
GAIDE: Graph-based Attention Masking for Spatial- and Embodiment-aware Motion PlanningIntroduces GAIDE, a transformer-based neural informed sampler that encodes both workspace geometry and manipulator embodiment as a graph with attention masking. When plugged into sampling-based planners, GAIDE yields higher planning efficiency and success rates than uniform, hand-crafted informed, and prior neural samplers.Link
Omni-Manip: Beyond-FOV Large-Workspace Humanoid Manipulation with Omnidirectional 3D PerceptionProposes Omni-Manip, a LiDAR-driven 360° visuomotor policy using Time-Aware Attention Pooling over panoramic point clouds for humanoid manipulation in large, cluttered workspaces. A whole-body teleoperation system supports data collection, and experiments show superior performance over egocentric RGB-D baselines without frequent base repositioning.Link
CT-Enabled Patient-Specific Simulation and Contact-Aware Robotic Planning for Cochlear ImplantationCombines CT-based anatomical reconstruction with a differentiable Cosserat-rod model of the electrode to plan contact-aware cochlear implant insertions. An online direction-update law reduces lateral forces and locking/buckling risks, validated via simulations and benchtop tests showing improved insertion depth and safety.Link
Curve-Induced Dynamical Systems on Riemannian Manifolds and Lie GroupsIntroduces CDSM, a framework that constructs stable dynamical systems directly on Riemannian manifolds and Lie groups by combining tangential motion along a nominal curve with normal attraction.
