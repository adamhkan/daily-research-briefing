Executive Summary

  • Dexterous and long-horizon manipulation see notable advances, from privileged tactile sim-to-real transfer (PTLD) and massive bimanual grasp datasets (UltraDexGrasp) to memory- and horizon-scaled diffusion policies (VPWEM, SeedPolicy).
  • Motion planning progresses on multiple fronts, including GPU-accelerated dynamics-aware planning for high-DoF robots (cuRoboV2), multi-IK RRT* for joint space (Many-RRT*), and graph/learning-informed samplers (GAIDE) and uncertainty-aware OBCA.
  • Mobile and humanoid manipulation capabilities are expanded through risk-aware RL for mobile manipulation, omnidirectional LiDAR-based whole-body control (Omni-Manip), and physics-aware whole-body VLA frameworks (PhysiFlow).
  • Vision-language(-action) models are increasingly central in control stacks, enabling contact-tolerant planning (DCT), tri-system long-horizon manipulation (Critic in the Loop), neuro-symbolic skill growth from feedback (MEMO), and mechanistic control of VLA internal features.
  • Memory and representation learning for manipulation get systematic treatment via benchmarks and architectures (RoboMME, VPWEM, HyperMVP), improving robustness to occlusions, non-Markovian tasks, and geometric complexity.
  • Digital twins and simulation tools for manipulation, from Gaussian-splatting-based twins (GaussTwin) to CT-driven surgical planning and industrial textile sorting, illustrate a trend toward tightly coupled real-to-sim planning loops.

PTLD: Sim-to-real Privileged Tactile Latent Distillation for Dexterous ManipulationCarnegie Mellon University; UC Berkeley; University of WashingtonIntroduces PTLD, a sim-to-real framework that bypasses tactile simulation by using privileged real-world tactile sensing to train a tactile state estimator. This estimator augments proprioceptive policies trained in simulation, yielding large gains on in-hand rotation and challenging tactile reorientation tasks.Link
RoboMME: Benchmarking and Understanding Memory for Robotic Generalist PoliciesStanford UniversityProposes RoboMME, a standardized benchmark of 16 manipulation tasks targeting temporal, spatial, object, and procedural memory in VLA models. Evaluates 14 memory-augmented variants of a π0.5-based policy, showing that memory design choices are highly task-dependent and exposing trade-offs for long-horizon manipulation.Link
Observing and Controlling Features in Vision-Language-Action ModelsStanford UniversityDevelops linear tools to observe and minimally intervene on internal features of VLAs such as π0.5 and OpenVLA. By steering latent representations with optimal-control-inspired linear interventions, the method reliably adjusts robot behavior online without fine-tuning, aligning actions with user preferences.Link

cuRoboV2: Dynamics-Aware Motion Generation with Depth-Fused Distance Fields for High-DoF RobotsNVIDIAPresents a unified GPU-native stack for motion generation that couples B-spline trajectory optimization with fast TSDF/ESDF perception and scalable whole-body dynamics for manipulators and humanoids. Achieves high collision recall and large speedups over prior tools, enabling torque-limited, collision-free motion for up to 48-DoF systems.Link
UltraDexGrasp: Learning Universal Dexterous Grasping for Bimanual Robots with Synthetic DataChinese University of Hong Kong; e Chinese University of Hong KongIntroduces an optimization- and planning-based pipeline for generating 20M frames of bimanual dexterous grasp trajectories across multiple strategies, forming the UltraDexGrasp-20M dataset. A point-cloud-based grasp policy trained purely in simulation achieves 81.2% success in zero-shot real-world dexterous grasping on novel objects.Link
Direct Contact-Tolerant Motion Planning With Vision Language ModelsProposes a contact-tolerant planner that uses a VLM-driven point cloud partitioner to reason about where contact is permissible, forming a contact-aware point cloud. A neural controller then solves navigation as a perception-to-control optimization problem, enabling robust contact-rich motion in clutter on both simulated and real robots.Link
Risk-Aware Reinforcement Learning for Mobile ManipulationLearns risk-aware visuomotor policies for mobile manipulation by first training privileged distributional RL teachers with distortion risk metrics and then distilling them into depth-conditioned students. The resulting whole-body controllers exhibit adjustable risk sensitivity and improved worst-case performance in unmapped, dynamic environments.Link
Many-RRT*: Robust Joint-Space Trajectory Planning for Serial ManipulatorsExtends RRT*-Connect to plan simultaneously toward multiple IK solutions by growing parallel goal trees in joint space. This multi-goal strategy mitigates failures from poor IK choice, yielding substantially lower-cost trajectories and near-perfect success rates without increasing runtime.Link
GAIDE: Graph-based Attention Masking for Spatial- and Embodiment-aware Motion PlanningIntroduces GAIDE, a transformer-based neural informed sampler that encodes the spatial structure of the environment and robot embodiment as a graph with attention masking. Integrated with sampling-based planners for manipulators, GAIDE improves sample efficiency and planning success over uniform, hand-crafted, and prior learned samplers.Link
PhysiFlow: Physics-Aware Humanoid Whole-Body VLA via Multi-Brain Latent Flow Matching and Robust TrackingShanghai Jiao Tong UniversityProposes a multi-brain VLA framework that fuses semantic intent with physics-aware whole-body control for humanoids. Using latent flow matching and robust tracking, the system enables vision-language-guided, stable full-body coordination in dynamic tasks.Link
Omni-Manip: Beyond-FOV Large-Workspace Humanoid Manipulation with Omnidirectional 3D PerceptionUses LiDAR-based panoramic point clouds and a Time-Aware Attention Pooling architecture to endow humanoids with 360° perception for manipulation. Trained via whole-body teleoperation data, the resulting visuomotor policy manipulates across large workspaces without frequent base motions, outperforming RGB-D-based baselines.Link
CT-Enabled Patient-Specific Simulation and Contact-Aware Robotic Planning for Cochlear ImplantationBuilds a CT-to-simulation pipeline that models electrode arrays as Cosserat rods with frictional contact in patient-specific cochlear anatomies. A differentiable equilibrium-constrained planner computes insertion directions that limit lateral forces and reduce locking/buckling risk, validated via simulations and benchtop experiments.Link
