Executive Summary

  • Long-horizon, language-conditioned, and memory-augmented manipulation policies are a central theme, with multiple works proposing new diffusion, VLA, and memory architectures for robust, temporally extended control.
  • Dexterous and tactile manipulation advances include sim-to-real privileged distillation and large-scale synthetic data for bimanual grasping, significantly improving in-hand and dual-arm performance.
  • Motion planning for high-DoF manipulators and humanoids progresses via GPU-accelerated optimization (cuRoboV2), multi-goal sampling (Many-RRT*), spatially structured neural samplers (GAIDE), and risk-/uncertainty-aware formulations for navigation and contact.
  • Whole-body and mobile manipulation are addressed through physics-aware humanoid VLA, LiDAR-based 360° humanoid manipulation, risk-aware mobile manipulation RL, and manifold-based dynamical systems.
  • Institution-matched work from CMU, Berkeley, Washington, Stanford, and ETH Zurich spans tactile dexterous sim-to-real, memory benchmarking for VLAs, mechanistic control of VLA behaviors, and training-free vision-language grounded navigation.
  • Data and systems papers (RoboPocket, digital twins, OMPL bindings, textile sorting cells) focus on scaling data collection, simulation fidelity, and tooling for motion planning and manipulation research.

PTLD: Sim-to-real Privileged Tactile Latent Distillation for Dexterous ManipulationCarnegie Mellon University; UC Berkeley; University of WashingtonIntroduces PTLD, a sim-to-real framework that avoids simulating tactile sensors by distilling a state estimator from privileged real-world tactile data into a tactile policy head. When combined with proprioception-only policies trained in simulation, PTLD yields large gains on in-hand rotation and reorientation, demonstrating strong benefits of tactile sensing for dexterous manipulation.Link
RoboMME: Benchmarking and Understanding Memory for Robotic Generalist PoliciesStanford UniversityProposes RoboMME, a standardized benchmark of 16 manipulation tasks designed to stress temporal, spatial, object, and procedural memory in VLA policies. The authors evaluate 14 memory-augmented variants of a π0.5 backbone, showing that memory design is highly task-dependent and providing a common testbed for long-horizon, history-dependent manipulation.Link
OpenFrontier: General Navigation with Visual-Language Grounded FrontiersETH ZurichFormulates navigation as selecting and reaching sparse frontiers that are semantically grounded using off-the-shelf vision–language models, enabling training-free goal-conditioned navigation. OpenFrontier achieves strong zero-shot performance on multiple benchmarks and real robots without dense mapping or policy training.Link
Observing and Controlling Features in Vision-Language-Action ModelsStanford UniversityDevelops notions of feature-observability and feature-controllability for VLAs, using linear probes and optimal-control-based interventions to steer internal representations. Experiments on π0.5 and OpenVLA show that lightweight online interventions can reliably adjust robot behavior in closed loop without retraining, enabling real-time alignment with user goals.Link

cuRoboV2: Dynamics-Aware Motion Generation with Depth-Fused Distance Fields for High-DoF RobotsPresents a unified GPU-native stack for motion generation that combines B-spline trajectory optimization with torque limits, fast TSDF/ESDF distance fields, and scalable whole-body dynamics for high-DoF manipulators and humanoids. Achieves near-perfect success and collision-free rates on challenging benchmarks, significantly outperforming prior planners and enabling dynamics-aware whole-body motion.Link
PhysiFlow: Physics-Aware Humanoid Whole-Body VLA via Multi-Brain Latent Flow Matching and Robust TrackingHesheng Wang is with the Department of Automation, Key LaboratoryIntroduces a multi-brain VLA framework that couples semantic motion intent with physics-aware whole-body control for humanoids. The method focuses on efficient latent flow matching and robust tracking, enabling vision-language-guided full-body coordination in dynamic tasks.Link
Omni-Manip: Beyond-FOV Large-Workspace Humanoid Manipulation with Omnidirectional 3D PerceptionProposes a LiDAR-driven visuomotor policy using panoramic point clouds and a Time-Aware Attention Pooling module to give humanoids 360° situational awareness. The approach supports large-workspace, cluttered manipulation without frequent base motions, outperforming RGB-D baselines that suffer from narrow FOV and self-occlusion.Link
UltraDexGrasp: Learning Universal Dexterous Grasping for Bimanual Robots with Synthetic DataShanghai AI Laboratory; The Chinese University of Hong KongPresents UltraDexGrasp, a pipeline that combines optimization-based grasp synthesis and planning-based demonstrations to create a 20M-frame dataset for bimanual dexterous grasping. A simple point-cloud-based policy trained purely on synthetic data achieves robust zero-shot sim-to-real transfer with >80% real-world success on diverse objects and grasp strategies.Link
Iterative On-Policy Refinement of Hierarchical Diffusion Policies for Language-Conditioned ManipulationIntroduces HD-ExpIt, an iterative refinement loop for hierarchical diffusion policies where high-level planners and low-level controllers co-improve via environment feedback. Applied to the CALVIN benchmark, it grounds the planner in the controller’s capabilities and achieves state-of-the-art long-horizon language-conditioned manipulation from offline data.Link
Critic in the Loop: A Tri-System VLA Framework for Robust Long-Horizon Manipulationdistinctive hallmark of human intelligence is the seamless integration of high-Proposes a tri-system architecture with a VLM "brain", a fast VLA "cerebellum", and a lightweight visual critic that dynamically allocates control authority. The critic detects anomalies and triggers VLM replanning only when necessary, yielding state-of-the-art robustness and efficiency on challenging long-horizon manipulation benchmarks.Link
Lifelong Language-Conditioned Robotic Manipulation LearningState Key Laboratory of Robotics and Intelligent Systems, Shenyang Institute of Automation, Chinese Academy of Sciences; University of Chinese Academy of Sciences; North University of ChinaIntroduces SkillsCrafter, a continual learning framework for language-conditioned manipulation that preserves previous skills via adaptation and shared semantic subspaces. By aggregating and reusing semantically similar skills, the method mitigates catastrophic forgetting while enabling generalization to new instructions.Link
SeedPolicy: Horizon Scaling via Self-Evolving Diffusion Policy for Robot ManipulationSichuan University; Dexmal Inc.Augments diffusion policies with Self-Evolving Gated Attention (SEGA), a recurrent temporal module that compresses long observation histories into a fixed latent state. On a 50-task benchmark, SeedPolicy substantially improves long-horizon manipulation success over standard diffusion policies, approaching VLA-scale performance with far fewer parameters.Link
VPWEM: Non-Markovian Visuomotor Policy with Working and Episodic MemoryProposes VPWEM, a diffusion-policy-based controller equipped with short-term working memory and compressed episodic memory for non-Markovian tasks. A Transformer-based compressor maintains a fixed-size episodic representation, enabling near-constant compute while improving performance on memory-intensive manipulation and mobile manipulation benchmarks.Link
Risk-Aware Reinforcement Learning for Mobile ManipulationUses distributional RL with distortion risk metrics to train privileged, risk-aware mobile manipulation policies that trade off performance and safety. These are distilled via imitation learning into depth-based visuomotor policies that retain adjustable risk sensitivity and exhibit improved worst-case performance in unmapped, dynamic environments.Link
