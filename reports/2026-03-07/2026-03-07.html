Executive Summary

  • Strong push on long-horizon, language-conditioned manipulation and VLA control, with new architectures for memory, curricula, and hierarchical refinement.
  • Dexterous and whole-body manipulation advance via tactile sim-to-real distillation, large bimanual grasp datasets, and omnidirectional 3D perception.
  • Motion planning sees GPU-accelerated whole-body optimization, neural informed samplers, multi-goal RRT*, and robust collision avoidance under uncertainty.
  • Risk and uncertainty are modeled more explicitly in mobile manipulation, waypoint prediction, shared autonomy, and adversarial robustness for visuomotor policies.
  • Digital twins, Gaussian splatting, Koopman dynamics, and curve-induced manifold DSs tighten the loop between geometric modeling, simulation, and control.
  • Stanford, ETH Zurich, CMU, UC Berkeley, and University of Washington contribute notable work on memory benchmarks, navigation, VLAs, and tactile dexterity.

PTLD: Sim-to-real Privileged Tactile Latent Distillation for Dexterous ManipulationCarnegie Mellon University; UC Berkeley; University of WashingtonIntroduces PTLD, which learns a tactile state estimator in the real world using privileged sensing and distills it into a tactile policy without requiring tactile simulation. This significantly boosts sim-trained proprioceptive policies for in-hand rotation and reorientation, closing a key gap in tactile dexterous manipulation sim-to-real transfer.Link
OpenFrontier: General Navigation with Visual-Language Grounded FrontiersETH ZurichProposes OpenFrontier, a training-free navigation framework that treats frontiers as semantic anchors and uses off-the-shelf vision–language priors to select and reach subgoals. It avoids dense 3D mapping and policy training yet achieves strong zero-shot performance across navigation benchmarks and on real robots.Link
RoboMME: Benchmarking and Understanding Memory for Robotic Generalist PoliciesStanford UniversityIntroduces RoboMME, a standardized benchmark of 16 manipulation tasks designed to probe temporal, spatial, object, and procedural memory in VLA policies. The authors also implement 14 memory-augmented variants of a π0.5 backbone, revealing strong task-dependence in what memory designs work best.Link
Observing and Controlling Features in Vision-Language-Action ModelsStanford UniversityDefines feature-observability and feature-controllability for VLAs, using linear classifiers and optimal-control-based interventions to read out and steer internal representations. Demonstrates that lightweight interventions on π0.5 and OpenVLA can reliably bias robot behavior online without fine-tuning, enabling real-time preference alignment.Link

cuRoboV2: Dynamics-Aware Motion Generation with Depth-Fused Distance Fields for High-DoF RobotsNVIDIAPresents a unified GPU-native stack for motion generation that couples B-spline trajectory optimization with fast TSDF/ESDF mapping and scalable whole-body dynamics for high-DoF arms and humanoids. Achieves large speedups, high collision-free success, and better tracking quality, showing a practical path to real-time, dynamics-aware planning at humanoid scale.Link
PhysiFlow: Physics-Aware Humanoid Whole-Body VLA via Multi-Brain Latent Flow Matching and Robust TrackingIntroduces a physics-aware, multi-brain VLA framework that fuses semantic intent with whole-body control for humanoids. By combining latent flow matching with robust tracking, the system enables vision-language-guided, dynamically stable full-body tasks.Link
UltraDexGrasp: Learning Universal Dexterous Grasping for Bimanual Robots with Synthetic DataShanghai AI Laboratory; The Chinese University of Hong KongBuilds UltraDexGrasp-20M, a 20M-frame synthetic dataset of physically plausible bimanual dexterous grasps generated via optimization and planning. A point-cloud grasp policy trained only on this data zero-shots to real hardware with ~81% success on diverse novel objects, advancing universal bimanual dexterous manipulation.Link
Iterative On-Policy Refinement of Hierarchical Diffusion Policies for Language-Conditioned ManipulationProposes HD-ExpIt, which iteratively refines hierarchical diffusion policies by letting a planner discover successful behaviors and then distilling them back into both high and low levels. This closes the planner–controller mismatch for language-conditioned manipulation and achieves state-of-the-art on the long-horizon CALVIN benchmark.Link
Critic in the Loop: A Tri-System VLA Framework for Robust Long-Horizon Manipulationbionic Tri-System architecture com-Introduces a tri-system architecture with a fast VLA “cerebellum,” a slower VLM “brain,” and a visual Critic that monitors execution and triggers replanning when anomalies arise. This adaptive scheduling reduces expensive VLM calls while improving robustness and autonomy on long-horizon, OOD manipulation tasks.Link
Lifelong Language-Conditioned Robotic Manipulation LearningChinese Academy of Sciences; Shenyang Institute of Automation; State Key Laboratory of Robotics and Intelligent SystemsPresents SkillsCrafter, a lifelong learning framework that preserves prior language-conditioned manipulation skills while adding new ones. It factors skill adaptation via shared semantic subspaces and aggregates related skills, mitigating catastrophic forgetting and improving generalization to new instructions.Link
SeedPolicy: Horizon Scaling via Self-Evolving Diffusion Policy for Robot ManipulationSichuan University; Dexmal Inc.Augments diffusion policies with Self-Evolving Gated Attention, a recurrent temporal module that compresses long observation histories into a fixed latent state. The resulting SeedPolicy scales to long horizons and outperforms standard diffusion and VLA baselines across 50 RoboTwin 2.0 manipulation tasks, especially under challenging perturbations.Link
Direct Contact-Tolerant Motion Planning With Vision Language ModelsIntroduces a direct contact-tolerant planner that uses a VLM to segment movable/deformable regions in images and propagates this into a contact-aware point cloud. A learned perception-to-control module then plans motions that safely exploit contact in cluttered environments, outperforming baselines in sim and on a car-like robot.Link
U-OBCA: Uncertainty-Aware Optimization-Based Collision Avoidance via Wasserstein Distributionally Robust Chance ConstraintsExtends Optimization-Based Collision Avoidance to handle uncertainty using distributionally robust chance constraints over full polygonal robot and obstacle shapes. The method yields less conservative, more efficient trajectories than circle/ellipse approximations, particularly in narrow cluttered spaces, and is validated in theory and hardware.Link
Risk-Aware Reinforcement Learning for Mobile ManipulationLearns risk-aware whole-body visuomotor policies for mobile manipulators by training distributional RL teachers with distortion risk metrics and distilling them to depth-based students. Policies exhibit tunable risk sensitivity and improved worst-case performance while reacting in real time in unmapped environments.Link
