Executive Summary

  • Dexterous and bimanual manipulation see strong sim-to-real advances via tactile latent distillation and massive synthetic grasp datasets, significantly boosting in-hand and dual-arm performance.
  • GPU-native motion generation and new sampling-based/planning algorithms (cuRoboV2, Many-RRT*, GAIDE, CDSM) push high-DoF and joint-space motion planning toward real-time, higher success, and lower-cost trajectories.
  • Long-horizon and memory-intensive manipulation is tackled with new diffusion-policy variants and VLA frameworks (SeedPolicy, VPWEM, HD-ExpIt, RoboMME, Tri-System, Risk-aware mobile manipulation).
  • Whole-body humanoid control and large-workspace manipulation benefit from physics-aware VLA control and omnidirectional LiDAR-based visuomotor policies.
  • Safety, robustness, and uncertainty handling see progress through risk-aware RL, uncertainty-aware shared autonomy, distributionally robust collision avoidance, and contact-tolerant planning.
  • Stanford and ETH contribute tools for interpreting and benchmarking VLA policies and for general, language-grounded navigation in open environments.

PTLD: Sim-to-real Privileged Tactile Latent Distillation for Dexterous ManipulationCarnegie Mellon University; UC Berkeley; University of WashingtonIntroduces PTLD, a sim-to-real framework that avoids tactile simulation by using privileged real-world tactile data to train a latent state estimator, which is then fused with proprioceptive policies trained in simulation. On in-hand rotation and reorientation benchmarks, incorporating PTLD yields large performance gains (up to 182% over proprioception-only baselines), demonstrating a practical path to tactile dexterous manipulation without high-fidelity tactile simulators.Link
RoboMME: Benchmarking and Understanding Memory for Robotic Generalist PoliciesStanford UniversityProposes RoboMME, a standardized benchmark of 16 manipulation tasks structured around temporal, spatial, object, and procedural memory requirements for VLA models. The authors systematically compare 14 memory-augmented variants of a π0.5-based policy, showing that memory design is highly task-dependent and providing a principled testbed for long-horizon, history-dependent manipulation.Link
Observing and Controlling Features in Vision-Language-Action ModelsStanford UniversityDevelops tools to probe and steer internal representations of VLA policies such as π0.5 and OpenVLA via linear feature observability and minimal optimal-control-based interventions. Experiments show that targeted modifications of internal features can reliably alter robot behavior online while preserving closed-loop performance, enabling real-time alignment without full fine-tuning.Link
OpenFrontier: General Navigation with Visual-Language Grounded FrontiersETH ZurichIntroduces OpenFrontier, a training-free navigation framework that selects frontier regions as visual anchors and scores them using off-the-shelf vision–language models to satisfy high-level language goals. The method avoids dense 3D mapping and policy training yet achieves strong zero-shot performance across benchmarks and real-world runs on a mobile robot.Link

cuRoboV2: Dynamics-Aware Motion Generation with Depth-Fused Distance Fields for High-DoF RobotsPresents cuRoboV2, a GPU-native motion generation stack combining B-spline trajectory optimization, fast TSDF/ESDF distance field construction, and scalable whole-body kinematics/dynamics for high-DoF arms and humanoids. It achieves large speedups over CPU and prior GPU baselines, high success and collision-free rates under payload and humanoid constraints, and demonstrates that well-structured codebases can be effectively extended using LLM-assisted kernel authoring.Link
Risk-Aware Reinforcement Learning for Mobile ManipulationIntroduces a distributional RL framework that trains privileged teacher policies with distortion risk metrics to realize tunable risk sensitivity, then distills them into depth-based visuomotor policies for mobile manipulators. The resulting controllers adjust behavior based on risk level and demonstrate improved worst-case performance while executing reactive whole-body motions in unmapped environments.Link
Many-RRT*: Robust Joint-Space Trajectory Planning for Serial ManipulatorsExtends RRT*-Connect to plan simultaneously toward multiple inverse-kinematics goal configurations, treating joint-space redundancy as parallel goal trees rather than a single arbitrary IK solution. Across diverse manipulators and obstacle fields, Many-RRT* yields substantially lower-cost trajectories and near-100% success without extra runtime, mitigating failures due to poor IK goal selection.Link
GAIDE: Graph-based Attention Masking for Spatial- and Embodiment-aware Motion PlanningProposes GAIDE, a neural informed sampler that encodes the spatial structure of the environment and the manipulator’s embodiment as a graph, integrated into a Transformer via attention masking. When plugged into sampling-based planners, GAIDE improves sampling efficiency and planning success over uniform, hand-crafted, and prior learned samplers in high-DoF manipulation scenarios.Link
Curve-Induced Dynamical Systems on Riemannian Manifolds and Lie GroupsIntroduces Curve-induced Dynamical Systems on Smooth Manifolds (CDSM), which build stable dynamical systems directly on manifolds like SE(3) and SPD(n) by combining tangential motion along a nominal curve with normal attraction toward it. Benchmarks on S² and demonstrations on manipulators and a mobile manipulator show improved path adherence, responsiveness, and real-time performance for geometry-aware motion generation.Link
PhysiFlow: Physics-Aware Humanoid Whole-Body VLA via Multi-Brain Latent Flow Matching and Robust TrackingPresents a physics-aware VLA framework for humanoid whole-body control that uses multi-brain latent flow matching to couple semantic motion intent with dynamically consistent control. Experiments indicate improved stability and coordinated limb behavior in vision–language-guided humanoid tasks that require full-body motion.Link
Omni-Manip: Beyond-FOV Large-Workspace Humanoid Manipulation with Omnidirectional 3D PerceptionIntroduces Omni-Manip, a LiDAR-driven 3D visuomotor policy for humanoids that processes panoramic point clouds with a time-aware attention pooling module. This 360° perception enables large-workspace manipulation without frequent base motion, outperforming egocentric RGB-D baselines in cluttered and wide-area tasks.Link
UltraDexGrasp: Learning Universal Dexterous Grasping for Bimanual Robots with Synthetic DataShanghai AI Laboratory; The Chinese University of Hong KongProposes UltraDexGrasp, a pipeline that synthesizes physically plausible, multi-strategy bimanual grasps via optimization and planning, yielding a 20M-frame dataset over 1,000 objects. A point-cloud-based policy trained purely on this synthetic data transfers zero-shot to real dual-arm platforms, achieving around 81% success on diverse, previously unseen objects.Link
PTLD: Sim-to-real Privileged Tactile Latent Distillation for Dexterous ManipulationCarnegie Mellon University; UC Berkeley; University of WashingtonPTLD sidesteps tactile simulation by using privileged real-sensor data to train a tactile latent estimator, which is then combined with sim-trained proprioceptive policies. It substantially improves in-hand rotation and reorientation performance relative to proprioception-only baselines, offering a practical route to tactile dexterous manipulation.Link
Iterative On-Policy Refinement of Hierarchical Diffusion Policies for Language-Conditioned ManipulationHD-ExpIt iteratively refines hierarchical diffusion policies for language-conditioned manipulation by using online environment feedback: the planner discovers successful behaviors that are distilled back into the hierarchical agent. On the CALVIN benchmark, this self-reinforcing loop boosts performance over offline-trained hierarchies and achieves new state-of-the-art results.Link
