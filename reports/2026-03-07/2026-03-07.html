Executive Summary

  • Strong cluster on long-horizon, language-conditioned, and memory-augmented manipulation (RoboMME, HD-ExpIt, SeedPolicy, VPWEM, SkillsCrafter) targeting robustness and temporal scalability.
  • Dexterous and tactile manipulation advances show significant sim-to-real gains, especially via privileged tactile latent distillation and large-scale bimanual grasp datasets (PTLD, UltraDexGrasp).
  • Motion planning and whole-body control see notable progress in GPU-accelerated dynamics-aware planning, risk-aware mobile manipulation, and improved sampling for high-DoF manipulators (cuRoboV2, Risk-Aware RL for Mobile Manipulation, Many-RRT*, GAIDE).
  • Humanoid and mobile-manipulator work emphasizes whole-body coordination and large workspace perception using VLA frameworks and omnidirectional 3D sensing (PhysiFlow, Omni-Manip, Risk-Aware RL for Mobile Manipulation).
  • Multiple works probe the structure and reliability of VLA and visuomotor policies, including mechanistic feature control, adaptive inference, robustness to adversarial objects, and critic-in-the-loop architectures (Observing and Controlling VLA Features, Act-Think-or-Abstain, adversarial 3D objects, Critic in the Loop).
  • Simulation, digital twins, and tooling for planning (GaussTwin, OMPL Python bindings, GaussTwin, U-OBCA) continue to push toward more accurate, uncertainty-aware, and developer-friendly motion generation stacks.

RoboMME: Benchmarking and Understanding Memory for Robotic Generalist PoliciesStanford UniversityIntroduces RoboMME, a standardized benchmark of 16 manipulation tasks designed to probe temporal, spatial, object, and procedural memory in vision-language-action policies. Builds 14 memory-augmented variants of the π0.5 backbone, revealing that optimal memory representations are highly task-dependent and highlighting gaps in current long-horizon manipulation capabilities.Link
PTLD: Sim-to-real Privileged Tactile Latent Distillation for Dexterous ManipulationCarnegie Mellon University; UC Berkeley; University of WashingtonProposes PTLD, a sim-to-real approach where privileged real-world tactile sensing is used to distill a robust tactile state estimator, avoiding the need for tactile simulation. Demonstrates large performance gains on in-hand rotation and reorientation tasks over proprioceptive-only baselines, advancing practical tactile dexterous manipulation.Link
Observing and Controlling Features in Vision-Language-Action ModelsStanford UniversityApplies mechanistic-interpretablity ideas to VLAs, defining feature observability and controllability via linear probes and minimal interventions. Shows that internal VLA representations can be steered online to adjust robot behavior in closed loop without fine-tuning, enabling real-time alignment to user preferences and tasks.Link

cuRoboV2: Dynamics-Aware Motion Generation with Depth-Fused Distance Fields for High-DoF RobotsNVIDIAPresents a unified GPU-native motion generation stack combining B-spline trajectory optimization with torque limits, fast TSDF/ESDF depth fusion, and scalable whole-body dynamics for high-DoF manipulators and humanoids. Achieves large speedups and near-perfect success/collision rates on challenging planning, IK, and retargeting benchmarks, enabling dynamics-feasible motions across platforms.Link
PhysiFlow: Physics-Aware Humanoid Whole-Body VLA via Multi-Brain Latent Flow Matching and Robust TrackingShanghai Jiao Tong UniversityIntroduces a physics-aware multi-brain VLA framework for humanoid robots that integrates semantic intent with whole-body control. Experiments show improved stability and coordinated limb motions in dynamic, language-guided tasks, addressing efficiency and semantic grounding limitations of prior humanoid VLAs.Link
Omni-Manip: Beyond-FOV Large-Workspace Humanoid Manipulation with Omnidirectional 3D PerceptionProposes Omni-Manip, a LiDAR-driven 360° visuomotor policy for humanoid manipulation in large workspaces, using time-aware attention pooling over panoramic point clouds. Coupled with a whole-body teleoperation data pipeline, the system outperforms RGB-D baselines in cluttered, wide-area manipulation without frequent base repositioning.Link
UltraDexGrasp: Learning Universal Dexterous Grasping for Bimanual Robots with Synthetic DataPeking University; The Chinese University of Hong Kong; The University of Hong Kong; Zhejiang UniversityDevelops UltraDexGrasp, a synthetic data pipeline combining grasp optimization and planning to generate 20M-frame trajectories for bimanual dexterous grasping across strategies and objects. A point-cloud-based policy trained solely on this dataset achieves strong zero-shot sim-to-real performance with over 81% success on novel real-world objects.Link
Iterative On-Policy Refinement of Hierarchical Diffusion Policies for Language-Conditioned ManipulationIntroduces HD-ExpIt, which iteratively refines hierarchical diffusion policies by using environment feedback to discover successful behaviors that are distilled back into the planner and controller. On the CALVIN benchmark, this self-improvement loop substantially boosts performance of offline-trained, language-conditioned manipulation policies.Link
Critic in the Loop: A Tri-System VLA Framework for Robust Long-Horizon ManipulationChinese Academy of Sciences; University of Chinese Academy of SciencesProposes a tri-system architecture with a VLM planner, fast VLA executor, and lightweight visual critic that dynamically hands off control based on detected anomalies. The critic-triggered replanning reduces reliance on expensive VLM calls while improving robustness and autonomy on long-horizon manipulation benchmarks, especially under OOD conditions.Link
Lifelong Language-Conditioned Robotic Manipulation LearningChinese Academy of Sciences; Mohamed bin Zayed University of Artificial IntelligencePresents SkillsCrafter, a continual learning framework for language-conditioned manipulation that preserves old skills while acquiring new ones. It decomposes skill adaptation and uses SVD-based semantic subspaces plus skill similarity aggregation to mitigate forgetting and support generalization to unseen skills.Link
SeedPolicy: Horizon Scaling via Self-Evolving Diffusion Policy for Robot ManipulationDexmal Inc.; Sichuan UniversityAugments diffusion policies with Self-Evolving Gated Attention (SEGA), a recurrent temporal module that compresses long observation histories into a fixed latent state. On the RoboTwin 2.0 benchmark with 50 tasks, SeedPolicy significantly outperforms standard diffusion and VLA baselines, particularly under challenging randomized conditions, while remaining parameter-efficient.Link
SPIRIT: Perceptive Shared Autonomy for Robust Robotic Manipulation under Deep Learning UncertaintyTechnical University of MunichIntroduces perceptive shared autonomy, where uncertainty-aware point cloud registration regulates autonomy level between semi-autonomous manipulation and haptic teleoperation. In aerial manipulation user studies and mock industrial tasks, SPIRIT improves both manipulation performance and reliability despite failures in deep-learning perception modules.Link
Direct Contact-Tolerant Motion Planning With Vision Language ModelsProposes a Direct Contact-Tolerant planner that uses a VLM-based point cloud partitioner to reason about where contact is permissible, generating contact-aware point clouds for navigation. A learned perception-to-control module then performs contact-tolerant motion planning, yielding more efficient navigation in cluttered, movable environments than existing CTMP baselines.Link
