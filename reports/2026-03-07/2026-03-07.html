Executive Summary

  • Dexterous and long-horizon manipulation see multiple advances, from privileged tactile distillation (PTLD) to massive synthetic bimanual grasp datasets (UltraDexGrasp) and improved diffusion-based policies (SeedPolicy, VPWEM).
  • Motion planning progresses on several fronts: GPU-accelerated dynamics-aware planning for high-DoF robots (cuRoboV2), multi-goal joint-space RRT* (Many-RRT*), graph-informed sampling (GAIDE), and uncertainty-aware collision avoidance (U-OBCA).
  • Whole-body and mobile manipulation receive new frameworks for risk-aware visuomotor control, physics-aware humanoid VLA (PhysiFlow), and large-workspace humanoid manipulation leveraging omnidirectional LiDAR (Omni-Manip).
  • Vision-language(-action) systems are being rethought via memory benchmarks (RoboMME), hierarchical and tri-system controllers (HD-ExpIt, Critic-in-the-Loop), interpretability and online interventions in VLAs, and code-based policy rewriting (Act-Observe-Rewrite).
  • Safety, robustness, and uncertainty are addressed through contact-tolerant planning with VLMs, robust waypoint prediction with evidential learning (ELLIPSE), perceptive shared autonomy (SPIRIT), and semantic-aware safety filters (Safe-SAGE).
  • Tooling and infrastructure evolve with LLM-assisted GPU-native motion stacks, OMPL Python bindings, digital twins for manipulation (GaussTwin), and textile-sorting cells that tightly couple VLM perception with collision-aware planning.

PTLD: Sim-to-real Privileged Tactile Latent Distillation for Dexterous ManipulationCarnegie Mellon University; UC Berkeley; University of WashingtonIntroduces PTLD, a sim-to-real framework that leverages privileged real-world tactile sensing to distill a latent tactile state estimator, enhancing proprioception-trained dexterous manipulation policies without requiring tactile simulation. On in-hand rotation and reorientation benchmarks, incorporating PTLD yields large gains over proprioception-only baselines, demonstrating the value of learned tactile state representations for challenging in-hand tasks.Link
RoboMME: Benchmarking and Understanding Memory for Robotic Generalist PoliciesStanford UniversityPresents RoboMME, a standardized benchmark of 16 manipulation tasks designed to probe temporal, spatial, object, and procedural memory in vision-language-action policies. The authors evaluate 14 memory-augmented variants of a π0.5-based VLA, showing that memory design is highly task-dependent and highlighting open challenges in building generalist manipulation policies with robust long-horizon memory.Link
Observing and Controlling Features in Vision-Language-Action ModelsStanford UniversityStudies linear feature observability and controllability inside VLAs (π0.5, OpenVLA), showing that internal representations can be probed with linear classifiers and minimally steered via optimal-control-style interventions. The approach enables online modification of robot behavior—e.g., aligning with user preferences—without fine-tuning, while preserving closed-loop manipulation capabilities.Link

cuRoboV2: Dynamics-Aware Motion Generation with Depth-Fused Distance Fields for High-DoF RobotsNVIDIAcuRoboV2 unifies perception, dynamics-aware trajectory optimization, and whole-body GPU computation for manipulators and humanoids. It fuses TSDF/ESDF depth fields with smooth, torque-limited B-spline trajectories and fast kinematics/dynamics kernels, achieving large speedups and higher success/collision-free rates compared to prior motion generation stacks.Link
Direct Contact-Tolerant Motion Planning With Vision Language ModelsProposes a Direct Contact-Tolerant (DCT) planner that uses a VLM-driven point-cloud partitioner to reason about which objects are safe to contact and embeds this into a contact-aware navigation policy. The method operates directly on point clouds and outperforms baselines in cluttered environments with movable obstacles in both simulation and real-robot tests.Link
U-OBCA: Uncertainty-Aware Optimization-Based Collision Avoidance via Wasserstein Distributionally Robust Chance ConstraintsExtends Optimization-Based Collision Avoidance to handle localization and prediction uncertainty through distributionally robust chance constraints over polygonal robots and obstacles. By avoiding crude geometric approximations and tightening constraints into tractable nonlinear forms, U-OBCA reduces conservatism and improves navigation efficiency, especially in narrow, cluttered settings.Link
Risk-Aware Reinforcement Learning for Mobile ManipulationIntroduces a distributional RL framework to learn risk-aware visuomotor policies for mobile manipulation from egocentric depth, with tunable risk sensitivity at runtime. A privileged teacher is trained with distortion risk metrics and then distilled into student policies, yielding whole-body behaviors with improved worst-case performance in unmapped environments.Link
Many-RRT*: Robust Joint-Space Trajectory Planning for Serial ManipulatorsMany-RRT* extends RRT*-Connect by planning simultaneously toward multiple inverse-kinematics goal configurations, mitigating the risk of committing to a poor joint-space goal in cluttered scenes. Experiments across robot morphologies show substantially higher success rates and lower-cost trajectories versus standard RRT variants at similar runtimes.Link
GAIDE: Graph-based Attention Masking for Spatial- and Embodiment-aware Motion PlanningGAIDE is a neural informed sampler that encodes robot embodiment and workspace structure as a graph and injects this into a transformer via attention masking. Plugged into sampling-based planners, GAIDE improves sampling efficiency and planning success in high-dimensional manipulator configuration spaces compared to uniform and prior learned samplers.Link
UltraDexGrasp: Learning Universal Dexterous Grasping for Bimanual Robots with Synthetic DataPeking University; The Chinese University of Hong Kong; The University of Hong Kong; Zhejiang UniversityPresents UltraDexGrasp, a pipeline that synthesizes high-quality bimanual dexterous grasp trajectories via optimization and planning, yielding a 20M-frame dataset across 1,000 objects and multiple grasp strategies. A point-cloud-based policy trained solely on this synthetic data achieves robust zero-shot sim-to-real universal grasping with over 81% success on novel real-world objects.Link
PhysiFlow: Physics-Aware Humanoid Whole-Body VLA via Multi-Brain Latent Flow Matching and Robust TrackingShanghai Jiao Tong UniversityProposes a physics-aware multi-brain VLA framework for humanoid robots that couples semantic intent from vision-language modules with whole-body motion control. Through latent flow matching and robust tracking, the system achieves stable, semantically guided full-body coordination in dynamic tasks.Link
Risk-Aware Reinforcement Learning for Mobile ManipulationUses a distributional critic and distortion risk metrics to train teacher policies that explicitly trade off risk and performance, then distills them into depth-conditioned visuomotor students. The learned mobile manipulation policies exhibit adjustable risk profiles and better worst-case behavior during reactive whole-body operation.Link
Curve-Induced Dynamical Systems on Riemannian Manifolds and Lie GroupsIntroduces Curve-induced Dynamical Systems (CDSM), a framework to define stable dynamical systems along task-space curves directly on manifolds such as SE(3) and SPD(n). The method combines tangential and normal components to generate real-time, geometry-consistent behaviors, validated on benchmarks and demonstrated on both a manipulator and a mobile manipulator.Link
