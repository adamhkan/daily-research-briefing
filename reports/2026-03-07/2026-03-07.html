Executive Summary

  • Memory, horizon scaling, and interpretability for VLA-based manipulation see significant advances, including new long-horizon benchmarks, memory architectures, and feature-level control.
  • Dexterous and bimanual manipulation progresses via sim-to-real tactile distillation, large synthetic grasp datasets, and improved visuomotor pretraining and augmentation.
  • Motion planning and control see stronger performance and scalability, from GPU-accelerated dynamics-aware generation to improved sampling-based planners and informed neural samplers.
  • Whole-body and mobile manipulation are tackled with risk-aware policies, omnidirectional humanoid manipulation, and physics-aware VLA frameworks.
  • New human-in-the-loop and rapid iteration tools (coding agents, smartphone-based data collection, neuro-symbolic skill expansion) streamline policy improvement without heavy retraining.
  • Safety, robustness, and uncertainty handling are emphasized across manipulation and navigation through risk-aware RL, shared autonomy, adversarial robustness, and evidential waypoint prediction.

RoboMME: Benchmarking and Understanding Memory for Robotic Generalist PoliciesStanford UniversityIntroduces RoboMME, a standardized benchmark of 16 manipulation tasks designed to probe temporal, spatial, object, and procedural memory in vision-language-action (VLA) models. Builds 14 memory-augmented variants on the π0.5 backbone, showing that optimal memory representations are highly task-dependent and enabling systematic comparison of long-horizon, history-dependent policies.Link
PTLD: Sim-to-real Privileged Tactile Latent Distillation for Dexterous ManipulationCarnegie Mellon University; UC Berkeley; University of WashingtonProposes PTLD, a sim-to-real framework that avoids simulating tactile sensors by first learning privileged real-world tactile policies and then distilling them into a tactile state estimator. Augmenting proprioceptive sim-trained policies with this estimator substantially improves in-hand rotation and reorientation performance, highlighting a practical route to tactile dexterous skills.Link
Observing and Controlling Features in Vision-Language-Action ModelsStanford UniversityApplies mechanistic interpretability concepts to VLAs by defining feature-observability and feature-controllability in their internal representations. Demonstrates that linear probes and minimal optimal-control-based interventions can steer π0.5 and OpenVLA behaviors online without fine-tuning, enabling real-time preference alignment and behavior correction in robotic control.Link

cuRoboV2: Dynamics-Aware Motion Generation with Depth-Fused Distance Fields for High-DoF RobotsNVIDIAPresents a unified GPU-native motion generation stack combining B-spline trajectory optimization with torque limits, fast dense TSDF/ESDF perception, and scalable whole-body kinematics and dynamics. Achieves large speedups and near-perfect success rates from manipulators to 48-DoF humanoids, enabling collision-aware, dynamics-feasible motions suitable for both manipulation and locomotion.Link
PhysiFlow: Physics-Aware Humanoid Whole-Body VLA via Multi-Brain Latent Flow Matching and Robust TrackingShanghai Jiao Tong UniversityIntroduces a physics-aware, multi-brain VLA framework that links semantic intent to whole-body humanoid control. The system couples latent flow matching with robust tracking to produce stable, vision-language-guided full-body coordinated behaviors on humanoid robots.Link
Omni-Manip: Beyond-FOV Large-Workspace Humanoid Manipulation with Omnidirectional 3D PerceptionProposes Omni-Manip, a LiDAR-centric 360° visuomotor policy for humanoid manipulation in large workspaces where base motion is constrained. A time-aware attention pooling mechanism encodes panoramic point clouds over time, enabling robust full-body manipulation across wide areas and clutter compared to narrow-FOV RGB-D baselines.Link
UltraDexGrasp: Learning Universal Dexterous Grasping for Bimanual Robots with Synthetic DataPeking University; The Chinese University of Hong Kong; The University of Hong Kong; Zhejiang UniversityDevelops UltraDexGrasp, a pipeline that synthesizes diverse, physically plausible bimanual dexterous grasps via optimization and planning, yielding a 20M-frame dataset over 1,000 objects. A simple point-cloud-based policy trained solely on this synthetic data transfers zero-shot to real bimanual grasping with over 81% success on unseen objects.Link
Iterative On-Policy Refinement of Hierarchical Diffusion Policies for Language-Conditioned ManipulationIntroduces HD-ExpIt, which iteratively refines hierarchical diffusion-based language-conditioned manipulation policies using environment feedback. By letting the planner and controller co-improve through self-discovered successful behaviors, it grounds high-level subgoals in actual low-level capabilities and achieves state-of-the-art performance on the long-horizon CALVIN benchmark.Link
Critic in the Loop: A Tri-System VLA Framework for Robust Long-Horizon ManipulationChinese Academy of Sciences; University of Chinese Academy of SciencesProposes a tri-system architecture with a VLM "brain" for global reasoning, a fast VLA "cerebellum" for execution, and a visual Critic for anomaly detection and routing. The Critic adaptively triggers high-level replanning when failures or stagnation appear, boosting long-horizon manipulation robustness while minimizing expensive VLM calls.Link
Lifelong Language-Conditioned Robotic Manipulation LearningChinese Academy of Sciences; Mohamed bin Zayed University of Artificial IntelligenceIntroduces SkillsCrafter, a continual learning framework for language-conditioned manipulation that mitigates catastrophic forgetting. It adapts manipulation skills while extracting a shared semantic subspace over instructions and aggregates related prior skills, improving retention and generalization to new or unknown skills.Link
SeedPolicy: Horizon Scaling via Self-Evolving Diffusion Policy for Robot ManipulationDexmal Inc.; Sichuan UniversityExtends diffusion policies for long-horizon manipulation with Self-Evolving Gated Attention (SEGA), a recurrent temporal module that compresses long observation histories into a fixed latent state. The resulting SeedPolicy scales to longer horizons with moderate overhead and substantially outperforms standard diffusion policies and other IL baselines on 50 RoboTwin 2.0 tasks.Link
Direct Contact-Tolerant Motion Planning With Vision Language ModelsPresents a direct contact-tolerant planner that leverages VLM reasoning to segment environment points into contact-tolerant versus forbidden regions, forming a contact-aware point cloud. A learned navigation module then optimizes control under these constraints, enabling efficient, robust navigation through clutter with movable obstacles in both simulation and real robots.Link
VPWEM: Non-Markovian Visuomotor Policy with Working and Episodic MemoryProposes VPWEM, a visuomotor policy architecture that combines a short-term working memory window with a learned episodic memory compressor to handle long, non-Markovian manipulation tasks. Integrated with diffusion policies, it maintains near-constant per-step compute while improving performance on memory-intensive benchmarks like MIKASA and MoMaRT.Link
