## Executive Summary

- Dexterous and long-horizon manipulation see multiple advances, from sim-to-real tactile distillation (PTLD) to massive synthetic bimanual grasp datasets and new diffusion-based policy architectures with memory.
- Motion planning for high-DoF systems progresses via GPU-native stacks (cuRoboV2), multi-goal RRT* variants, graph-structured neural samplers, and uncertainty-aware collision avoidance.
- Whole-body and mobile manipulation are pushed toward real deployments with risk-aware RL, omnidirectional 3D perception for humanoids, and physics-/VLA-based full-body control.
- Vision-language(-action) models are increasingly integrated into planning and control, powering contact-tolerant motion planning, long-horizon manipulation frameworks, and open-world navigation and search.
- Interpretability, safety, and robustness receive attention through feature-level control of VLAs, adversarial analysis of visuomotor policies, evidential waypoint prediction, and perception-uncertainty-driven shared autonomy.
- Tooling and infrastructure emerge around motion planning libraries and digital twins, including LLM-assisted Python bindings for OMPL and physically grounded Gaussian-splatting-based twins for manipulation.

| cuRoboV2: Dynamics-Aware Motion Generation with Depth-Fused Distance Fields for High-DoF Robots | Presents a unified GPU-native stack for dynamics-aware motion generation combining B-spline trajectory optimization with torque limits, fast TSDF/ESDF mapping, and scalable whole-body kinematics/dynamics. | U-OBCA: Uncertainty-Aware Optimization-Based Collision Avoidance via Wasserstein Distributionally Robust Chance Constraints | Extends Optimization-Based Collision Avoidance to handle uncertainty in robot and obstacle states via distributionally robust chance constraints over Wasserstein ambiguity sets. Without simplifying polygons to circles/ellipses, U-OBCA remains computationally tractable and substantially reduces conservatism, improving navigation efficiency in narrow, cluttered environments. | https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.04914 |
| GIANT - Global Path Integration and Attentive Graph Networks for Multi-Agent Trajectory Planning | Introduces a multi-robot navigation framework that fuses pre-planned global paths with a local GNN-based policy that models agent interactions through attention. | Risk-Aware Reinforcement Learning for Mobile Manipulation | Proposes a two-stage method where a privileged teacher policy is trained with distributional RL and distortion risk metrics to produce varying risk-sensitive behaviors, which are then distilled into depth-based visuomotor student policies. | Many-RRT*: Robust Joint-Space Trajectory Planning for Serial Manipulators | Extends RRT*-Connect to plan simultaneously toward multiple IK solutions by growing trees from many goal configurations in parallel, mitigating failures due to poor goal choice in joint space.
