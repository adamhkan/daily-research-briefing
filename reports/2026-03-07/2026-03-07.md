## Executive Summary

- Several works push long-horizon, memory-intensive manipulation forward via new diffusion, memory, and hierarchical policy designs (SeedPolicy, VPWEM, HD-ExpIt, Critic in the Loop, RoboMME).
- Motion planning advances span GPU-accelerated dynamics-aware planning (cuRoboV2), multi-goal joint-space RRT* (Many-RRT*), graph-aware neural samplers (GAIDE), and manifold-based dynamical systems (CDSM).
- Whole-body and mobile manipulation see new risk-aware visuomotor RL, physics-aware VLA control, and omnidirectional 3D perception for large-workspace humanoid manipulation.
- Dexterous and bimanual manipulation are strengthened via sim-to-real tactile latent distillation (PTLD) and a 20M-frame synthetic dataset plus policy for universal bimanual dexterous grasping (UltraDexGrasp).
- VLAs and LLM-based agents are probed and leveraged for interpretability, online control, and code-based policy evolution, including feature-level control, neuro-symbolic skill expansion, and in-context code rewriting.
- Digital-twin, uncertainty-aware, and contact-tolerant methods address robustness: from Gaussian-splatting twins and risk-aware or distributionally robust planners to shared autonomy and adversarial robustness of visuomotor policies.
