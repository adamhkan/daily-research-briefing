## Executive Summary

- Dexterous manipulation advances include sim-to-real tactile latent distillation and massive synthetic datasets for bimanual grasping, significantly boosting in-hand and universal grasp performance.
- GPU-native and geometry-aware motion generation methods (cuRoboV2, GAIDE, Many-RRT*) improve planning speed, success, and optimality for high-DoF manipulators and humanoids.
- Whole-body and mobile manipulation see progress in risk-aware visuomotor policies, omnidirectional 3D perception for large-workspace humanoid tasks, and physics-aware VLA frameworks.
- New frameworks for long-horizon, language-conditioned, and memory-intensive manipulation (HD-ExpIt, VPWEM, SeedPolicy, SkillsCrafter, Critic-in-the-loop) improve robustness and horizon scaling.
- Uncertainty- and risk-sensitive planning/control methods emerge for contact-tolerant navigation, collision avoidance, waypoint prediction, and medical insertion, enhancing safety under uncertainty.
- Digital twin systems and interpretable VLA interventions (GaussTwin, feature-controllability in VLAs) support real-time adaptation, closed-loop planning, and alignment of robot behavior with user goals.

| Risk-Aware Reinforcement Learning for Mobile Manipulation | Presents a distributional RL framework for learning whole-body, depth-conditioned visuomotor policies with tunable risk sensitivity in mobile manipulation.
