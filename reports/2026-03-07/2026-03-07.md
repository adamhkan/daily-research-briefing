## Executive Summary

- Dexterous and long-horizon manipulation see multiple advances, from privileged tactile distillation (PTLD) to massive synthetic bimanual grasp datasets (UltraDexGrasp) and improved diffusion-based policies (SeedPolicy, VPWEM).
- Motion planning progresses on several fronts: GPU-accelerated dynamics-aware planning for high-DoF robots (cuRoboV2), multi-goal joint-space RRT* (Many-RRT*), graph-informed sampling (GAIDE), and uncertainty-aware collision avoidance (U-OBCA).
- Whole-body and mobile manipulation receive new frameworks for risk-aware visuomotor control, physics-aware humanoid VLA (PhysiFlow), and large-workspace humanoid manipulation leveraging omnidirectional LiDAR (Omni-Manip).
- Vision-language(-action) systems are being rethought via memory benchmarks (RoboMME), hierarchical and tri-system controllers (HD-ExpIt, Critic-in-the-Loop), interpretability and online interventions in VLAs, and code-based policy rewriting (Act-Observe-Rewrite).
- Safety, robustness, and uncertainty are addressed through contact-tolerant planning with VLMs, robust waypoint prediction with evidential learning (ELLIPSE), perceptive shared autonomy (SPIRIT), and semantic-aware safety filters (Safe-SAGE).
- Tooling and infrastructure evolve with LLM-assisted GPU-native motion stacks, OMPL Python bindings, digital twins for manipulation (GaussTwin), and textile-sorting cells that tightly couple VLM perception with collision-aware planning.
