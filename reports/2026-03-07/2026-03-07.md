## Executive Summary

- GPU-native motion generation and distance-field perception (cuRoboV2) push high-DoF planning from arms to humanoids with strong whole-body guarantees.
- Dexterous manipulation advances span massive synthetic bimanual grasp datasets and sim-to-real tactile latent distillation for in-hand control.
- New hierarchy, memory, and diffusion policy designs significantly strengthen long-horizon, language-conditioned manipulation and mobile manipulation.
- Safety- and uncertainty-aware motion planning and control are tackled from microrobotic contact tasks to mobile and multi-agent navigation under uncertainty.
- Humanoid and large-workspace manipulation benefit from omnidirectional 3D perception, whole-body VLA control, and whole-body tactile/proximity sensing.
- Tooling and infrastructure—digital twins, OMPL bindings, handheld data collection, and benchmarks for memory and VLA interpretability—aim to make advanced planners and policies more deployable.
