Executive Summary

  • ELMP introduces an analytical-gradient fine-tuning framework that slashes data and compute requirements for adapting neural motion planners, with strong real-robot results on a Franka arm.
  • Multiple works advance safe and scalable motion planning: new conformal distance fields, neural reachability for multi-arm systems, spatiotemporal convex-set graph search, and optimal any-angle planners for dynamic environments.
  • World and action modeling continues to mature, with new unified mobile manipulation WAMs, 3D point and 4D latent world models, and a concise taxonomy of world (action) models for control.
  • Dexterous and contact-rich manipulation sees progress via large-scale human tactile datasets, contact-wrench-guided learning from demonstrations, and symbol discovery for effect-based manipulation planning.
  • Navigation research spans robust bio-inspired policies, social and communication-enabled navigation, semantic map grounding with VLMs, and personalized household object search policies.
  • Control and safety advances include robust operational-space control with conformal disturbance bounds, GPU-parallel robust optimal control with certified linearization error bounds, and decentralized geometric control for cooperative aerial transport.

ELMP: Efficient Learning for Motion Planning via Analytical Policy GradientsETH ZurichELMP adapts neural motion planners without recollecting expert trajectories by directly optimizing policies through a differentiable kinematic layer with dense collision, target, and smoothness costs. It achieves large gains in success rate and adaptation speed, generalizes across changing kinematics via point-cloud tool encodings, and runs at millisecond latency, validated on a Franka Panda.Link

From Prediction Uncertainty to Conformalized Distance Fields for Safe Motion PlanningSeoul National UniversityThis work introduces functional conformal prediction for entire distance fields, yielding distribution-free lower bounds that guarantee safety for any trajectory satisfying the constraint. Embedded in an MPC framework, it scales well with obstacle density and improves the safety–feasibility–efficiency trade-off in dense pedestrian and 3D quadrotor scenarios.Link
Search-Based Spatiotemporal and Multi-Robot Motion Planning on Graphs of Space-Time Convex SetsThe paper proposes a planning framework where collision-free regions are modeled as convex sets in space-time, with trajectories corresponding to graph paths plus continuous optimization within sets. An exact convex decomposition scheme enables unified handling of dynamic obstacles and multi-robot interactions, solving up to 100-robot instances efficiently with high-quality solutions.Link
NeHMO: Neural Hamilton-Jacobi Reachability Learning for Decentralized Safe Multi-Arm Motion PlanningPurdue UniversityNeHMO learns an approximation of a Hamilton–Jacobi reachability safety value function that captures worst-case inter-arm interactions in multi-manipulator systems. This learned representation is used inside a decentralized trajectory optimizer, enabling scalable, data-efficient, and provably safe multi-arm motion planning that outperforms existing decentralized baselines.Link
Optimal any-angle path planning in static and dynamic environmentsThe authors present Zeta* and Zeta*-SIPP, optimal any-angle grid-based planners that use elliptical forward expansion and a novel field-of-view visibility check to reduce search while maintaining optimality. Zeta*-SIPP handles dynamic obstacles and is over 20× faster than the prior optimal dynamic any-angle method TO-AA-SIPP, making it attractive for navigation in open spaces.Link
Trajectory Learning with Graph Representations for Social Robot NavigationThis imitation learning framework uses a graph-based auxiliary network to encode crowd interactions and a trajectory-level objective to capture temporal dynamics, addressing error accumulation in social navigation. It outperforms existing data-driven baselines on simulated and real-world datasets across multiple social compliance metrics.Link
FLYNN: Robust Neural Network for Robot Navigation using Fly Brain TopologyFLYNN is a recurrent neural network whose architecture mirrors the Drosophila brain connectome and is trained for vision-based navigation. It matches the performance of similarly sized hand-crafted networks but is markedly more robust to out-of-distribution inputs and sensor degradation, remaining functional even under complete vision loss.Link
Path Planning in Physically Viable World ModelsThe paper augments 3D Gaussian splat reconstructions with physics-based simulation to predict how terrain changes (e.g., flooding) affect route feasibility before deployment. A terrain-aware planner then reasons over these “physically viable” world models, revealing long-horizon route failures that standard static-map planners miss in real outdoor field tests.Link
Trajectory Learning with Graph Representations for Social Robot NavigationThis imitation learning framework uses a graph-based auxiliary network to encode crowd interactions and a trajectory-level objective to capture temporal dynamics, addressing error accumulation in social navigation. It outperforms existing data-driven baselines on simulated and real-world datasets across multiple social compliance metrics.Link
Robust Operational Space Control with Conformal Disturbance Bounds for Safe Redundant ManipulationUniversity of HoustonThis work combines an operational-space extended state observer with conformal prediction to estimate disturbance bounds online and construct robust control barrier functions. The resulting controller achieves millimeter-level tracking and real-time safe control on a 7-DoF Franka arm under diverse disturbances, with probabilistic safety guarantees that reduce conservatism.Link
Human-Centric Transferable Tactile Pre-Training for Dexterous Robotic ManipulationPeking University; Tsinghua UniversityH-Tac is a 160-hour egocentric human tactile-action dataset spanning 300+ tasks and 135k episodes, used to pre-train a tactile model via unified tactile and action spaces for human-to-robot transfer. The proposed Transferable Tactile Pre-Training explicitly predicts future tactile signals to capture contact dynamics, yielding strong gains in dexterous robotic manipulation in simulation and on hardware.Link
