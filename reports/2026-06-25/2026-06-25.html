Executive Summary

  • Inference-time optimization and simulator-in-the-loop refinement are emerging as powerful ways to impose physics and safety constraints on generative control policies and cloth manipulation in deployment.
  • World models are being leveraged across navigation, locomotion, and manipulation for foresight-driven planning, dynamics-aware representations, and value estimation from mixed-quality data.
  • Generalist manipulation policies increasingly rely on geometry-aware visual tokens, cross-robot action abstractions, and video-based representation alignment to scale across embodiments and data sources.
  • New control and planning advances span GPU-accelerated differentiable MPC and parallel LQ control, as well as safe trajectory planning for multi-floor ground vehicles and topology-adaptive multi-robot formation control.
  • Dexterous and fault-tolerant manipulation is pushed forward via tactile exploration without vision, pseudo-tactile contact estimation, compliant PDS joints with proprioception, and fault-aware whole-body loco-manipulation.
  • Structured scene and observation representations, semantic maps, and robust VPR pipelines are improving navigation, exploration, and context-aware planning in intralogistics, maritime, and indoor environments.

Grounding Generative Policies in Physics: Optimization-Guided Diffusion for Robot ControlETH ZurichIntroduces an inference-time optimization scheme that replaces diffusion noise with optimized corrections, enforcing reachability, collision-avoidance, and controller trackability constraints without retraining. Demonstrated on dexterous grasp synthesis and dynamic manipulation, the approach improves feasibility, grasp quality preservation, and task success by up to 20–23 percentage points over guidance baselines.Link
TACTFUL: Tactile-Driven Exploration For Object Localization and Identification in Confined EnvironmentsCarnegie Mellon UniversityPresents a vision-free framework where a multi-fingered robot autonomously explores confined workspaces using tactile sensing to discover and localize objects and reconstruct their geometry. Trained fully on real hardware with a dynamic reward schedule, the single learned policy achieves 77% success and 1.5 cm reconstruction error, outperforming baselines on real objects.Link
MinInter: Minimizing Trajectory Interpolation During Data Augmentation for Imitation LearningCarnegie Mellon UniversityProposes MinInter, a trajectory selection strategy for demonstration recombination that explicitly minimizes non-expert interpolation segments when generating augmented rollouts. On 12 MimicGen manipulation tasks with 26 variants, MinInter consistently boosts both data-generation and policy success rates, surpassing SkillGen particularly on long-horizon, contact-rich settings.Link
ArtiTwinSplat: Interactable Digital Twin Reconstruction via Gaussian Splatting from RGB-D videosETH ZurichIntroduces a pipeline that turns real RGB-D videos into articulated, photorealistic digital twins using 3D Gaussian Splatting plus unsupervised articulation discovery. The resulting models expose part structure and kinematics for real-time rendering and interactive manipulation, offering a scalable route to digital assets for articulated object manipulation and planning.Link

FT-WBC: Learning Fault-Tolerant Whole-Body Control for Legged Loco-ManipulationDevelops FT-WBC, a fault-tolerant whole-body controller for legged manipulators that decouples upper and lower body policies and adds a learned fault estimator plus a posture adaptation module. The system synthesizes compensatory gaits under actuator failures while preserving arm workspace, improving survival and task performance in both simulation and real-world transfer.Link

Using latent world tokens and anchor-based multimodal trajectory forecasting, it generates diverse candidate paths and uses visual foresight to select actions, achieving state-of-the-art future prediction and zero-shot navigation success across datasets.Link

TurboMPC: Fast, Scalable, and Differentiable Model Predictive Control on the GPUToyota Research InstitutePresents TurboMPC, a fully GPU-based differentiable MPC solver supporting rich constraints, implicit integration, and cross-time costs via SQP with an ADMM inner loop and implicit differentiation.
