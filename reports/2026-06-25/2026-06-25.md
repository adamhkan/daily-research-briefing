## Executive Summary

- Inference-time optimization and simulator-in-the-loop refinement are emerging as powerful ways to impose physics and safety constraints on generative control policies and cloth manipulation in deployment.
- World models are being leveraged across navigation, locomotion, and manipulation for foresight-driven planning, dynamics-aware representations, and value estimation from mixed-quality data.
- Generalist manipulation policies increasingly rely on geometry-aware visual tokens, cross-robot action abstractions, and video-based representation alignment to scale across embodiments and data sources.
- New control and planning advances span GPU-accelerated differentiable MPC and parallel LQ control, as well as safe trajectory planning for multi-floor ground vehicles and topology-adaptive multi-robot formation control.
- Dexterous and fault-tolerant manipulation is pushed forward via tactile exploration without vision, pseudo-tactile contact estimation, compliant PDS joints with proprioception, and fault-aware whole-body loco-manipulation.
- Structured scene and observation representations, semantic maps, and robust VPR pipelines are improving navigation, exploration, and context-aware planning in intralogistics, maritime, and indoor environments.
