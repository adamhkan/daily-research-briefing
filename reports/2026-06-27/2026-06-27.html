Executive Summary

  • GPU-batched nonlinear optimization and new belief-space planners are pushing motion planning and control toward higher throughput and stronger theoretical guarantees.
  • Humanoid and whole-body loco-manipulation see rapid progress via diffusion and RL-based skill acquisition, contact- and pressure-aware imitation, and contact-flow meta-skill chaining.
  • Dexterous and contact-rich manipulation is advancing through tactile and vibro-acoustic sensing, play-based pretraining, structured supervision, and behavior composition frameworks.
  • Navigation research spans robust visual encoders, human-aware simulation benchmarks, and disturbance-robust multi-waypoint and agile flight controllers.
  • New data, benchmarks, and world/action models (behavior cloning stacks, WatchAct, WAM-based continual learning, Fast-LeWM) are standardizing training and evaluation for complex manipulation and planning.
  • Semantic voxel maps and embodied agent architectures are emerging to support edge mobile manipulation and unified cyber-physical autonomy.

Scaling Nonlinear Optimization: Many Problems One GPUETH Zurich; UC BerkeleyIntroduces jaxipm, the first GPU-batched nonlinear programming solver based on IPOPT and implemented in JAX, enabling simultaneous solution of many constrained optimal control and motion-planning problems. Through heterogeneous iteration fusion and iteration-level batching, it delivers up to 32.85× higher throughput than IPOPT on quadrotor MPC tasks including obstacle avoidance and multi-robot navigation.Link
PRISM: Efficient and Locally Optimal Probabilistic Planning with Reachability GuaranteesMassachusetts Institute of TechnologyProposes PRISM, a constrained belief-space motion planner that decomposes planning into deterministic mean control and covariance shrinking, with a new result on covariance controllability under constraints. The method achieves near-complete coverage and lower-cost trajectories than prior belief-space planners and offers completeness guarantees under mild start/goal distribution assumptions.Link

BOWConnect: Parallel Bayesian Optimization over Windows with Learned Local Cost Maps for Sample-Efficient Kinodynamic Motion Planning. Bobadilla are with the Knight Foundation SchoolBOWConnect is a bidirectional kinodynamic motion planner that embeds Bayesian Optimization over local trajectory windows into parallel tree expansion, learning cost maps and constraints to guide sampling. It achieves 100% success and competitive or superior planning times in high-dimensional, narrow-passage, and non-convex scenarios, with successful deployment on a ground vehicle and quadrotor.Link
Ordinal Neural Collapse as a Representation Prior for Visual NavigationORION enforces an ordinal structure in the visual representation space aligned with ordered steering commands, arranging action classes along a discriminative axis while suppressing intra-class variance. Integrated into a diffusion-based navigation policy, it yields higher success and goal progress than standard end-to-end and neural-collapse baselines, especially at visually ambiguous intersections.Link
NavIsaacLab: Generating Realistic Crowd via Parallel Robot Learning for Benchmarking Human-aware NavigationNational University of SingaporeNavIsaacLab is a GPU-parallel simulation framework built on Isaac Lab that generates physics-based, photo-realistic human crowds using trajectory diffusion and adversarial motion controllers. It provides diverse, sensor-rich scenes for training and benchmarking human-aware navigation policies, addressing the lack of realistic crowd simulators.Link
KRVF: A Source-Aware Semantic Voxel World Representation for Edge Mobile ManipulationUniversity of ManchesterKRVF defines a task-focused voxel memory that encodes occupancy, color, semantic evidence, temporal freshness, and measurement source while separating measured geometry from semantic priors. Implemented in ROS 2, it supports edge-deployable mobile manipulators with semantics-aware queries, grasp candidate extraction, and depth-repair via rendered map priors.Link
A System for Fast, Resilient, and Adaptable Loco-Manipulation Behaviors on Humanoid RobotsPresents a behavior authoring and execution system for humanoid loco-manipulation that combines object-centric Affordance Templates, behavior-tree-like organization, and a whole-body controller capable of arm motion during walking. Deployed on multiple humanoids including Atlas, Valkyrie, and Nadia, it enables operators to compose and adapt door opening, obstacle clearing, and table-to-table tasks in minutes to hours.Link
Humanoid-DART: Humanoid Loco-Manipulation using Diffusion-guided Augmentation through Relabeling and TrackingHumanoid-DART bootstraps humanoid loco-manipulation from sparse demonstrations using a diffusion model to generate goal-conditioned trajectories and RL to track them, progressively expanding the skill repertoire. The framework learns a unified goal-conditioned policy covering diverse loco-manipulation behaviors with minimal ongoing expert intervention.Link
OmniContact: Chaining Meta-Skills via Contact Flow for Generalizable Humanoid Loco-ManipulationHong Kong University of Science and TechnologyOmniContact introduces contact flow, a compact representation of body trajectories and binary contacts, used both for a unified low-level loco-manipulation policy (CF-Track) and a high-level generator (CF-Gen) that synthesizes future contact sequences. On long-horizon box-carrying and stacking tasks, it significantly outperforms baselines in both individual meta-skill robustness and skill chaining, and can be driven by VLM-based task decomposition.Link
PressMimic: Pressure-Guided Motion Capture and Control for Humanoid Robot ImitationTsinghua University; University of Science and Technology of ChinaPressMimic augments humanoid imitation with pressure sensing, using a multimodal RGB–pressure perception model (FRAPPE++) and a pressure-supervised RL controller to capture and reproduce contact patterns. The approach reduces artifacts like foot sliding and improves stability by explicitly grounding motion in contact forces, supported by the MotionPRO RGB–pressure–mocap dataset.Link
MPC-Injection: Biasing Off-Policy Locomotion RL Toward Controller-Induced Behavior BasinsMPC-Injection steers locomotion RL toward designer-preferred gaits by inserting transitions from a model predictive controller into the replay buffer, without reward redesign or adversarial training. On simulated walkers and a Go2 quadruped, it yields deployable gaits comparable to heavily engineered rewards or motion-prior methods using simple reward functions.Link
