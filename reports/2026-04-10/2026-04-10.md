## Executive Summary

- Differentiable and diffusion-based planners push multi-agent navigation and multi-robot path planning toward gradient-based, train-small-deploy-large formulations.
- Generative and flow-matching motion planners for manipulators capture multi-modal path distributions, improving success and inference-time optimization.
- New datasets, hardware, and frameworks for bimanual dexterous and tactile-aware manipulation significantly boost performance on contact-rich tasks.
- Skill adaptation and transfer methods, including residual RL and reachability-aware policy transfer, improve robustness and cross-embodiment reuse of manipulation skills.
- Rich reachability maps and fast panoramic scene reconstruction tools strengthen the infrastructure for scalable manipulation planning and simulation.
- Adaptive perception pipelines for embedded platforms close the loop between navigation requirements and depth-estimation compute, improving energy efficiency.
