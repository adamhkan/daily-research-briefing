Executive Summary

  • Foundation and world-action models for embodied control see rapid advances, with new work on drift-based world models, long-context robot policies, and analysis of WAM vulnerabilities and steering.
  • Motion planning and navigation methods increasingly leverage flow/diffusion-style generative models, constraint integration, and adaptive scheduling to improve safety, speed, and constraint satisfaction.
  • Whole-body and dexterous manipulation research emphasizes tactile/force grounding, contact-rich control, and new affordable hardware platforms for hands and grippers.
  • Vision-Language-Action (VLA) policies are being shaped for robustness, steerability, lifelong learning, and real-time deployment, including navigation-oriented interfaces and teleoperation aids.
  • Safe and robust operation under uncertainty is addressed via control barrier functions, risk-aware safety filters, causal diagnosis of failures, and network-aware QoC for collaborative navigation.
  • Mapping, memory, and scenario-generation tools—from object-level change detection and stigmergic memories to DSL scenario generators—support long-term autonomy and validation in complex environments.

Action QFormer: Structured Representation Shaping under Action Supervision in Vision-Language-Action ModelsUC BerkeleyIntroduces Action QFormer, a query-based action interface that reorganizes pretrained multimodal VLA representations into action-centric features before action generation. This reduces harmful overwriting of language/grounding representations, yielding large gains in zero-shot sim-to-real navigation and more reliable instruction-following action generation.Link
G$^2$SR: Geometric Methods for Fast and Memory-Efficient Gaussian-based Surface ReconstructionMassachusetts Institute of TechnologyProposes G2SR, a hybrid geometric–learning pipeline that reconstructs 3D Gaussian splats from few RGB views using a lightweight 2D splat tracker plus analytic multi-view triangulation. It matches or surpasses heavier end-to-end methods in geometric accuracy while running tens of reconstructions per second with dramatically lower memory, enabling practical online mapping for mobile robots.Link

Curvature-Constrained and Constant-Speed Distributed Simultaneous Arrival Control for Multi-Robot SystemsNational University of Defense TechnologyDevelops a distributed hybrid controller for Dubins-type robots with curvature and constant-speed constraints to achieve simultaneous arrival at a target point. Using a virtual time variable and maximum-consensus-based switching, the method is scalable, communication-light, and in some cases provably time-optimal.Link
BridgeFlow: Fast and Robust SE(2)-Equivariant Motion Planning with Flow MatchingShanghai Jiao Tong UniversityBridgeFlow achieves exact SE(2) equivariance via task canonicalization instead of heavy equivariant networks, then uses a Brownian-bridge prior and optimal transport for efficient flow-matching-based trajectory generation. It yields up to 15× faster inference and higher valid-trajectory rates than diffusion baselines in cluttered 2D and 7-DoF manipulator tasks.Link
Motion Planning with Model-Based Diffusion via Constraint Optimization and Adaptive SchedulingMD-COAS unifies soft constraint handling via an inexact augmented Lagrangian prior with hard Convex Feasible Set projections in a model-based diffusion framework for single-robot motion planning. An adaptive scheduler co-optimizes safety enforcement and diffusion steps, improving safety, convergence speed, and final cost in highly non-convex 2D and 7-DoF arm benchmarks.Link
ConFlow: Constraints-Guided Learning with Flow Matching for Motion GenerationConFlow injects constraint information directly into the flow-matching training objective using differentiable barrier/cost functions and a conditional Gaussian Process source distribution. It also leverages infeasible demonstrations as negative supervision, reducing collisions and improving trajectory quality over standard flow-matching baselines in multi-robot navigation.Link
Risk-Aware Belief Control Barrier Functions over Random Finite SetsDelft University of Technology; KTH Royal Institute of Technology; TH Royal InstituteIntroduces a belief-level control barrier function framework where multi-object uncertainty is modeled as a Random Finite Set and estimated via an SMC-PHD filter. Safety constraints are enforced directly on the particle-based belief, with theoretical forward-invariance guarantees and validation in simulation and underwater robot experiments.Link
Modeling and Validation of Quality of Control for Edge-Offloaded Collaborative NavigationExtends the Quality of Control (QoC) framework to realistic mobile robots whose navigation and collision avoidance rely on edge-offloaded control over 5G. Through analytical modeling and testbed experiments, it characterizes control–communication trade-offs and shows how ROS2 reliability settings impact QoC, with RELIABLE QoS outperforming BEST-EFFORT in key regimes.Link
Safe Execution of RL Policies Via Acceleration-Based CBF-QP Constraint Enforcement for Real-World Robotic DeploymentsCNRS–AIST Joint Robotics Laboratory (JRL), IRL3218, AIST, Tsukuba, Japan; IST Joint Robotics Laboratory; University of TokyoAcc-CBF-QP wraps any RL policy in an acceleration-based QP that enforces joint, torque, velocity, and collision constraints via control barrier functions at runtime. On a Kinova arm and Unitree H1 humanoid, it drastically reduces or eliminates constraint violations while preserving nominal performance in safe regimes and preventing shutdowns under aggressive commands.Link
