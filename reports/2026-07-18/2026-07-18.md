## Executive Summary

- Foundation and world-action models for embodied control see rapid advances, with new work on drift-based world models, long-context robot policies, and analysis of WAM vulnerabilities and steering.
- Motion planning and navigation methods increasingly leverage flow/diffusion-style generative models, constraint integration, and adaptive scheduling to improve safety, speed, and constraint satisfaction.
- Whole-body and dexterous manipulation research emphasizes tactile/force grounding, contact-rich control, and new affordable hardware platforms for hands and grippers.
- Vision-Language-Action (VLA) policies are being shaped for robustness, steerability, lifelong learning, and real-time deployment, including navigation-oriented interfaces and teleoperation aids.
- Safe and robust operation under uncertainty is addressed via control barrier functions, risk-aware safety filters, causal diagnosis of failures, and network-aware QoC for collaborative navigation.
- Mapping, memory, and scenario-generation tools—from object-level change detection and stigmergic memories to DSL scenario generators—support long-term autonomy and validation in complex environments.
