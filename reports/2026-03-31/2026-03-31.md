## Executive Summary

- Dexterous manipulation hardware and sensing advance with a new open-source tendon-driven hand (Ruka-v2) and an 800 Hz data glove (T-800) enabling richer teleoperation and learning data.
- Multiple works push unified or longer-horizon motion generation, including diffusion-based in-image navigation + pre-grasp planning and emergent neuro-symbolic automaton policies from visuomotor data.
- Task-oriented and bimanual manipulation benefit from geometry-centric grasp planning and online adaptation via natural language feedback for dual-arm skills.
- VLA-based manipulation pipelines are refined with new training (VLA-OPD, DFM-VLA) and deployment (Realtime-VLA V2) strategies for higher robustness, sample efficiency, and control-rate execution.
- Mobile and whole-body manipulation see progress in legged cart pushing, UAV-mounted manipulators under disturbance, and fast, controllable humanoid running with perception-integrated autonomy.
