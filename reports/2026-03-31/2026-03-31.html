Executive Summary

  • Dexterous manipulation hardware and sensing advance with a new open-source tendon-driven hand (Ruka-v2) and an 800 Hz data glove (T-800) enabling richer teleoperation and learning data.
  • Multiple works push unified or longer-horizon motion generation, including diffusion-based in-image navigation + pre-grasp planning and emergent neuro-symbolic automaton policies from visuomotor data.
  • Task-oriented and bimanual manipulation benefit from geometry-centric grasp planning and online adaptation via natural language feedback for dual-arm skills.
  • VLA-based manipulation pipelines are refined with new training (VLA-OPD, DFM-VLA) and deployment (Realtime-VLA V2) strategies for higher robustness, sample efficiency, and control-rate execution.
  • Mobile and whole-body manipulation see progress in legged cart pushing, UAV-mounted manipulators under disturbance, and fast, controllable humanoid running with perception-integrated autonomy.

Ruka-v2: Tendon Driven Open-Source Dexterous Hand with Wrist and Abduction for Robot LearningIntroduces Ruka-v2, an open-source, tendon-driven humanoid hand adding a 2-DOF wrist and finger ab/adduction to improve human-like dexterity for confined and fine-grained manipulation. Teleoperation user studies show large gains in task speed and success over the prior design, and the platform is demonstrated on a range of bimanual teleoperation and autonomous learning tasks with full CAD, control, and build resources released.Link
Partial Motion Imitation for Learning Cart Pushing with Legged ManipulatorsGeorgia Institute of TechnologyProposes a partial imitation learning scheme for loco-manipulation, transferring only lower-body motion style from a robust locomotion policy to a cart-pushing policy for legged robots. Using a partial adversarial motion prior, the method yields more stable and accurate cart pushing across varied terrains and transfers across simulators, highlighting effective whole-body mobile manipulation.Link
Generalizable task-oriented object grasping through LLM-guided ontology and similarity-based planningPresents a geometry-centric pipeline for task-oriented grasping that avoids brittle semantic recognition, using an LLM-built object-part-task ontology combined with point-cloud-based part identification. A similarity-based planner transfers grasp strategies from known objects to novel ones, achieving accurate functional part selection and robust task-oriented grasps on unseen categories.Link
DiffusionAnything: End-to-End In-context Diffusion Learning for Unified Navigation and Pre-Grasp MotionIntelligent Space Robotics Laboratory, Center for Digital Engineering, Skolkovo Institute of Science and Technology.Introduces an RGB-only diffusion policy that jointly handles meter-scale navigation and centimeter-scale pre-grasp motion in image space using multi-scale FiLM modulation and trajectory-aligned depth prediction. With about 5 minutes of self-supervised data per task, the system achieves goal-directed navigation and pre-grasp behaviors with strong zero-shot generalization and onboard-suitable compute and memory footprints.Link
Meta-Adaptive Beam Search Planning for Transformer-Based Reinforcement Learning Control of UAVs with Overhead Manipulators under Flight DisturbancesDevelops a transformer-based DDQN framework with an adaptive beam-search planner for aerial manipulators, performing short-horizon lookahead using the critic to anticipate end-effector motion under base disturbances. On a 3-DoF UAV-mounted arm, the method improves reward, halves mean tracking error, and maintains low tip tracking error under drift relative to DDQN and transformer-only baselines.Link
Adapt as You Say: Online Interactive Bimanual Skill Adaptation via Human Language Feedback-Stone Robotics Institute; Istituto Italiano di Tecnologia; The Chinese University of Hong Kong; University of California, Los AngelesBiSAIL enables zero-shot online adaptation of offline-learned bimanual skills through human language feedback, using a hierarchical reason-then-modulate scheme. It infers high-level adaptation objectives from multimodal task cues and verbally provided corrections, then modulates a diffusion-based bimanual motion generator, significantly improving adaptability and cross-robot transfer across six tasks and two dual-arm platforms.Link
T-800: An 800 Hz Data Glove for Precise Hand Gesture TrackingChinese Academy of Sciences; Peking University; Tsinghua University; University of Chinese Academy of SciencesIntroduces T-800, a high-bandwidth data glove with 18 IMUs and synchronized 800 Hz tracking to capture fast, contact-rich hand motions without occlusion. Experiments show substantial high-frequency content in human dexterity and demonstrate accurate kinematic retargeting to robotic hands, providing rich data for future dexterous manipulation policy learning.Link
Realtime-VLA V2: Learning to Run VLAs Fast, Smooth, and AccurateDetails a practical stack for deploying VLA-driven manipulation at high control rates, covering calibration, planning and control, and learning-based selection of execution speed. The system achieves task execution speeds comparable to casual human operation and near hardware limits on a lightweight arm, with unaccelerated logs validating real-time performance.Link
DFM-VLA: Iterative Action Refinement for Robot Manipulation via Discrete Flow MatchingProposes DFM-VLA, which uses discrete flow matching to iteratively refine tokenized action sequences for manipulation, enabling correction of early token errors. With a velocity-field-based decoder and a two-stage refinement-plus-validation scheme, it outperforms autoregressive, discrete diffusion, and continuous diffusion baselines on CALVIN, LIBERO, and real-robot tasks while keeping inference efficient.Link
Emergent Neural Automaton Policies: Learning Symbolic Structure from Visuomotor TrajectoriesENAP infers a Mealy state machine from visuomotor demonstrations via adaptive clustering and an L*-style algorithm, yielding an interpretable high-level planner over latent task modes. A low-level residual policy then handles continuous control, leading to improved sample efficiency and performance on complex, long-horizon manipulation tasks compared to end-to-end VLA baselines.Link
