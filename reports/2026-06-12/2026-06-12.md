## Executive Summary

- Multi-robot and informative planning methods advance motion planning and navigation for ground, aerial, surface, and marine robots, emphasizing safety, belief sharing, and long-horizon trajectory optimization.
- New learning-based planners and world models (ILD, Fibration-RRT, ConsistencyPlanner, VLGA) focus on scaling to high dimensions and complex driving scenarios while retaining real-time performance and safety.
- Dexterous and contact-rich manipulation see progress via new VLA adaptations, world models, tactile sensing, and Real2Sim2Real pipelines that better handle morphology gaps and dynamics uncertainty.
- Vision-Language(-Action) architectures are being refined with better action pretraining, multimodal timing, language steering, and test-time compute allocation to improve robustness and instruction/OOD generalization.
- Real-world deployment concerns—sim-to-real gaps, safety, adversarial robustness, and human-in-the-loop efficiency—are addressed in social navigation, marine and aerial systems, surgical and industrial manipulation, and HiL-RL.
- Benchmarks, sensors, and hardware frameworks (DuoBench, HiPi, novel tactile and piezoresistive arrays, AerialClaw, MARUS extensions) aim to standardize evaluation and make advanced capabilities more reproducible.
