## Executive Summary

- Navigation and motion planning see strong advances in safety-aware, socially compliant, and long-horizon settings, including adaptive safety margins for visual navigation, multimodal terrain mapping, and disturbance-aware aerial planning.
- Whole-body and manipulation control progress via new VLA-based frameworks, contact- and force-aware policies, compliant LfD, and interpretable high-level skill selection for long-horizon tasks.
- Foundation-style models and operating systems for embodied agents mature, with unified task-planning benchmarks, policy orchestration frameworks, and runtime platforms that decouple cognition from low-level execution and emphasize verification and safety.
- Multi-agent and distributed control methods improve robustness and coordination under communication loss and shared-payload constraints, with theoretical safety guarantees via control barrier functions and covariance steering under distributional ambiguity.
- Sim-to-real transfer and controller robustness are repeatedly stressed across locomotion, grasping, path tracking, and INDI-based aerial control, supported by new analysis tools, calibration pipelines, and depth-regularized world models.
- Topic-relevant work from major institutions (NYU, CMU, Stanford) includes adaptive safety critics for diffusion navigation, long-range traversability priors for off-road planning, and value-aware predictors that stabilize multi-agent coordination under degraded communication.

| Learning Adaptive Safety Margins for Visual Navigation | New York University | Proposes a context-conditioned safety critic that learns adaptive clearance preferences to rank diffusion-based path proposals from RGB-D input.
