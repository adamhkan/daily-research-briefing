1) Executive Summary
- The filtered set highlights strong progress in motion planning and control, including GPU-accelerated whole-body motion generation for high-DoF robots, Koopman-operator-accelerated sampling-based MPC, and graph-based neural samplers for configuration-space planning.
- Mobile manipulation and whole-body control feature prominently, with new LiDAR-based 360° visuomotor policies for large-workspace humanoid manipulation, risk-aware visuomotor policies for mobile manipulators, and physics-aware VLA frameworks for humanoid whole-body behavior.
- Dexterous manipulation and tactile reasoning advance via large-scale synthetic bimanual grasp datasets/policies and a sim-to-real privileged tactile latent distillation framework that makes tactile sensing practical for in-hand control.
- Several works probe memory, hierarchy, and interpretability in vision-language(-action) models and diffusion policies, enabling feature-level control of VLAs, long-horizon hierarchical manipulation, and standardized benchmarks for memory in robotic generalist policies.
- Safety and uncertainty handling are central themes, spanning semantic-aware safety filters for legged robots, evidential waypoint prediction, uncertainty-aware collision avoidance, and risk-aware rulebooks and RL for embodied systems.

| Loop Closure via Maximal Cliques in 3D LiDAR-Based SLAM | Massachusetts Institute of Technology | Proposes CliReg, a deterministic loop-closure validation algorithm that replaces RANSAC with maximal-clique search over a compatibility graph of 3D feature correspondences. By finding the largest mutually consistent correspondence set, it avoids random sampling failures and improves robustness to noise and outliers. Integrated into a real-time LiDAR SLAM pipeline with binary 3D descriptors and Hamming-embedding BST matching, it yields lower pose error and more reliable loop closures, including in sparse and ambiguous environments. The method also generalizes to 2D projection-based maps. | https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.05397 |
| Accelerating Sampling-Based Control via Learned Linear Koopman Dynamics | ETH Zurich, Massachusetts Institute of Technology | Introduces MPPI-DK, which accelerates Model Predictive Path Integral control by replacing nonlinear dynamics rollouts with a learned deep Koopman operator that yields linear dynamics in a lifted latent space. The Koopman model is trained purely from interaction data, avoiding analytical modeling, then used for fast trajectory propagation within MPPI. Simulated pendulum and surface-vehicle tasks and quadruped hardware experiments show similar control performance to full nonlinear MPPI but with substantially reduced computation, enabling real-time sampling-based control on robots. | https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.05385 |
| OpenFrontier: General Navigation with Visual-Language Grounded Frontiers | ETH Zurich | Formulates navigation as selecting and reaching sparse visual frontiers that act as semantic anchors for diverse vision–language priors (e.g., CLIP/VLMs). The framework achieves strong zero-shot performance across several VLN benchmarks and is demonstrated on a real mobile robot, showing efficient open-world, goal-conditioned navigation. | https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.05377 |
| Omni-Manip: Beyond-FOV Large-Workspace Humanoid Manipulation with Omnidirectional 3D Perception | Massachusetts Institute of Technology | Presents a LiDAR-driven, 360° 3D visuomotor policy for humanoid manipulation in large, cluttered workspaces where base motion is constrained. Omni-Manip processes panoramic point clouds via a Time-Aware Attention Pooling module that encodes sparse 3D geometry and temporal context into control actions. A whole-body teleoperation framework is used to collect full-body coordination data for training. Experiments in sim and real show robust, dexterous manipulation across wide areas, outperforming egocentric RGB-D policies that suffer from FOV and occlusion issues. | https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.05355 |

Trained only in simulation, the policy transfers zero-shot to real bimanual hands and achieves ~81% success on diverse real-world dexterous grasps, demonstrating scalable data-driven bimanual manipulation. | https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.05312 |

A short-term “working memory” retains a sliding window of recent tokens, while a Transformer-based compressor converts older history into a fixed set of episodic memory tokens via self-/cross-attention over cached summaries and observations. Integrated with diffusion policies, VPWEM maintains near-constant per-step compute while exploiting long-horizon context, outperforming diffusion and VLA baselines by >20% on MIKASA and improving MoMaRT mobile manipulation by ~5%. | https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.04910 |

A learned intent predictor maps past trajectories to probabilistic “parking spot belief maps”, which inform the ego vehicle’s spot selection and negotiation strategy under occlusions and ambiguous maneuvers. Simulation with realistic sensing and prediction noise shows improved prediction accuracy, social acceptance, and task completion compared to methods that embed intent implicitly or rely on predicted future motion. | https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.04695 |

The process mirrors the C++ structure, scaffolds empty wrapper files, and uses LLMs to populate binding code, followed by expert review. The study catalogs common LLM failure modes (e.g., shared-pointer ownership, overloads, trampolines) and shows how in-context examples and prompting mitigate them. Performance of the generated bindings matches legacy solutions, offering a template for LLM-assisted bindings in large robotics codebases. | https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.04668 |
| ELLIPSE: Evidential Learning for Robust Waypoints and Uncertainties | Massachusetts Institute of Technology | Introduces ELLIPSE, which uses multivariate deep evidential regression to output both waypoint predictions and a multivariate Student‑t predictive distribution in a single forward pass for mobile robots. A synthetic augmentation strategy perturbs viewpoints/poses around expert trajectories to reduce overconfidence under covariate shift. Additionally, isotonic recalibration on probability integral transform values ensures calibrated uncertainty under environment/domain shifts. In real-world staircase navigation, ELLIPSE improves both success rate and uncertainty coverage compared to standard IL and probabilistic baselines. | https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.04585 |
| Risk-Aware Reinforcement Learning for Mobile Manipulation | University of Oxford | Learns risk-aware visuomotor policies for mobile manipulators that perform whole-body motion in clutter from egocentric depth. A privileged teacher is first trained with distributional RL and a risk-neutral critic, then distortion risk metrics are applied to the return distribution to obtain risk-adjusted advantages, yielding a family of risk-sensitive teachers. These are distilled via imitation learning into depth-based student policies with runtime-adjustable risk levels. Experiments show improved worst-case performance and reactive, risk-aware behaviors in unmapped, dynamic environments. | https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.04579 |
| From Local Corrections to Generalized Skills: Improving Neuro-Symbolic Policies with MEMO | University of Massachusetts Amherst | Proposes MEMO, which augments neuro-symbolic manipulation policies by turning local natural-language corrections into generalized, re-usable skill templates. The system clusters and rephrases user textual feedback across tasks into generic guidance (e.g., “go higher”) and synthesizes new code-level skill templates that can be invoked by a high-level neuro-symbolic policy. At runtime, a retrieval-augmented “skillbook” of text and code enables the robot to ground language reasoning into novel primitive skills. Experiments show that aggregating feedback into skill templates significantly improves generalization to new manipulation tasks where baselines fail. | https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.04560 |
| Many-RRT*: Robust Joint-Space Trajectory Planning for Serial Manipulators | U.S. Army Engineer Research and Development Center | Extends RRT*-Connect to handle the multi-goal nature of manipulator joint space, where a single end-effector pose has many IK solutions. The method retains asymptotic optimality and convergence, and across diverse robot morphologies and obstacle layouts yields dramatically higher success (100% vs ≤1.6% for baselines) and ~44% lower trajectory cost within comparable runtime. | https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.04547 |
| PTLD: Sim-to-real Privileged Tactile Latent Distillation for Dexterous Manipulation | Carnegie Mellon University, UC Berkeley | Introduces PTLD, a sim-to-real framework to exploit tactile sensing for dexterous in-hand manipulation without simulating tactile signals. Policies are trained in simulation using proprioception only, while in the real world, “privileged” sensing ( motion capture/tactile-rich supervision) is used to collect data to train a tactile state estimator that maps real tactile inputs into a compact latent. This latent augments the proprioceptive policy, yielding a tactile-enhanced controller. On in-hand rotation and reorientation benchmarks, PTLD improves success by 182% and 57% respectively over proprioceptive-only baselines. | https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.04531 |

After one mapping run, it supports arbitrary goal navigation in the known map without learned components. Field trials over >700 m in an experimental mine achieve 100% success over 20 runs with SPL 0.73±0.09. | https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.04470 |

Plugged into planners like RRT*, GAIDE significantly improves planning efficiency and success compared to uniform, hand-crafted informed, and prior neural samplers, especially in high-DoF, cluttered scenarios. | https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.04463 |
