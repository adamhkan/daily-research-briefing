## Executive Summary

- Institution filters: Carnegie Mellon University; Massachusetts Institute of Technology; Stanford University; ETH Zurich; UC Berkeley; UC San Diego; UC Berkeley; New York University  
- Topic filters: mobile manipulation; motion planning; dextrous manipulation; whole-body control  

- Strong institutional activity today around memory and interpretability for vision-language(-action) manipulation, with multiple Stanford and CMU papers proposing mechanisms to *analyze*, *steer*, or *benchmark* long-horizon VLA policies.  
- Dexterous and bimanual manipulation is a major theme: CMU-related work introduces large-scale synthetic datasets and sim-to-real pipelines for universal bimanual grasping, and a CMU/UC Berkeley collaboration proposes a sim-to-real tactile latent distillation method for dexterous in-hand control.  
- ETH Zurich contributes a training-free, frontier-based navigation framework that leverages vision–language models for open-world goal-directed navigation, relevant for mobile manipulation bases operating in complex environments.  
- From a systems perspective, there is an emphasis on robust, long-horizon behavior: new benchmarks for memory in VLAs (RoboMME) and frameworks for online behavior steering (feature controllability in VLAs) highlight emerging tools for diagnosing and adapting large embodied models.  
- Outside the institution matches, several topic-relevant works advance motion planning and mobile manipulation safety (e.g., GPU-accelerated whole-body planning, risk-aware whole-body mobile manipulation), but they are not listed in the tables due to ranking and deduplication constraints.

| Safe-SAGE: Social-Semantic Adaptive Guidance for Safe Engagement through Laplace-Modulated Poisson Safety Functions | Carnegie Mellon University | Introduces Safe-SAGE, which fuses multi-sensor point clouds, instance segmentation, and object tracking to build a semantic world model, then enforces context-dependent safety via a Poisson safety function and Laplace guidance field. Avoids dense 3D mapping and policy training by combining frontier-based exploration with language-grounded goal selection, achieving strong zero-shot performance in benchmarks and real-world mobile robot deployments. | https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.05377 |
| UltraDexGrasp: Learning Universal Dexterous Grasping for Bimanual Robots with Synthetic Data | Carnegie Mellon University | Presents UltraDexGrasp, a universal bimanual dexterous grasping framework with a synthetic data pipeline that combines optimization-based grasp synthesis and planning-based demonstration. Releases UltraDexGrasp-20M (20M frames, 1,000 objects, multiple grasp strategies) and trains a point-cloud-based grasp policy that achieves ~81% real-world zero-shot success on diverse objects using only synthetic data. | https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.05312 |
| RoboMME: Benchmarking and Understanding Memory for Robotic Generalist Policies | Stanford University | Introduces RoboMME, a standardized benchmark of 16 manipulation tasks targeting temporal, spatial, object, and procedural memory in VLA models. Builds 14 memory-augmented π₀.₅ variants with different memory representations and integration strategies, revealing strong task-dependent tradeoffs and providing a structured testbed for advancing long-horizon embodied memory. | https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.04639 |
| PTLD: Sim-to-real Privileged Tactile Latent Distillation for Dexterous Manipulation | Carnegie Mellon University; UC Berkeley | Proposes PTLD, which learns dexterous tactile manipulation without simulating tactile sensors by first training proprioceptive sim policies and then distilling real-world privileged tactile data into a tactile state estimator. Integrating this estimator significantly boosts sim-to-real performance: 182% improvement on in-hand rotation and 57% more goals reached on challenging in-hand reorientation with a multi-fingered hand. | https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.04531 |
| Efficient Autonomous Navigation of a Quadruped Robot in Underground Mines on Edge Hardware | Carnegie Mellon University | Develops a complete navigation stack for a Spot quadruped that runs in real time on a low-power CPU-only Intel NUC, combining LiDAR-inertial odometry, prior-map scan matching, terrain segmentation, and visibility-graph planning with a velocity-regulated path follower. After a single mapping pass, the system achieves 100% success over 20 autonomous trials (700 m total) in an underground mine with SPL 0.73±0.09, without learning-based components. | https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.04470 |
| Dual-Interaction-Aware Cooperative Control Strategy for Alleviating Mixed Traffic Congestion | New York University | Proposes DIACC, a multi-agent RL-based cooperative control strategy for CAVs in mixed traffic bottlenecks that distinguishes CAV–CAV cooperative interactions from CAV–HDV observational interactions in a decentralized actor. A centralized interaction-aware critic and interaction-focused reward design, combined with a proactive safety-based action refinement module, improve traffic throughput and adaptability compared to rule-based and baseline MARL controllers. | https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.03848 |
