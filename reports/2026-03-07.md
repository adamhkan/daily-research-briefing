## Executive Summary

- Dexterous and long-horizon manipulation see multiple advances: sim-to-real tactile distillation, large-scale bimanual grasping, and improved diffusion/memory architectures for language-conditioned control.
- Motion planning progresses on several fronts, from GPU-accelerated dynamics-aware whole-body planning to multi-goal RRT*, graph-informed sampling, and distributionally robust collision avoidance.
- Humanoid and mobile manipulation gain new whole-body VLA frameworks, omnidirectional 3D perception, and risk-aware RL for safer operation in unstructured environments.
- Vision-language(-action) systems are pushed toward more robust, interpretable, and adaptive behavior via tri-system control, complexity-aware routing, feature-level steering, and neuro-symbolic skill expansion.
- Digital twins and simulation methods (for manipulation, medical insertion, textiles sorting) increasingly integrate differentiable physics and perception for contact-aware planning and closed-loop control.
