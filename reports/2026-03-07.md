# Daily Robotics Briefing

Submission date covered: **2026-03-06**
Papers analyzed: **66**

## Executive Summary

- Vision-Language(-Action) systems are being made more reliable and practical through interpretability and control (feature steering in VLAs), hierarchical scheduling between slow VLM “brains” and fast VLA “cerebellums”, adaptive inference that can abstain, and memory benchmarks for long-horizon manipulation.
- Imitation and reinforcement learning for manipulation continue to scale: new diffusion-policy variants tackle long horizons and non‑Markovian tasks; sim‑to‑real pipelines incorporate tactile sensing; and offline/latent RL methods improve stability and performance with minimal tuning.
- Motion generation and planning see major GPU-native and learning‑guided advances, including a unified dynamics-aware stack for high‑DoF robots, Koopman-accelerated sampling-based control, graph-based informed sampling, and multi‑IK RRT* for robust joint‑space planning.
- Whole-body and mobile manipulation are pushed toward real-world deployment via physics-aware humanoid VLAs, 360° LiDAR-based visuomotor policies for beyond-FOV manipulation, risk-aware visuomotor policies, and ergonomic teleoperation/AR and shared autonomy under perception uncertainty.
- Mapping, localization, and navigation are made more robust in challenging settings through semantic safety functions, clique-based loop closure, foundation-model-based dense monocular SLAM, agricultural semantic mapping, uncertainty-aware collision avoidance, and resource-constrained underground navigation.
- New tools and frameworks emerge around digital twins, robotic testbeds, and software infrastructure (Gaussian-splatting twins, ViL/digital-twin AD testing, ROS-based multirotor autopilot, LLM-assisted bindings), along with domain-specific systems in agriculture, healthcare rehab, mines, and cochlear surgery.
