## 1) Executive Summary

- Strong motion-planning and control contributions for manipulators and humanoids: cuRoboV2 delivers a high-performance, GPU-native motion generation stack for high-DoF robots, while GAIDE and Many‑RRT* tackle sampling efficiency and multi-goal IK issues in high-dimensional planning. PhysiFlow and Omni‑Manip focus on whole-body control and large-workspace humanoid manipulation.
- Mobile manipulation and whole-body behavior are increasingly VLA-driven and memory-aware: PhysiFlow, Critic in the Loop, VPWEM, SeedPolicy, and RoboMME all address long-horizon, history-dependent control via VLAs, diffusion policies, and explicit memory modules, often with hierarchical or tri-system architectures.
- Dextrous and bimanual manipulation is scaling in data and sensing: UltraDexGrasp introduces a 20M-frame synthetic dataset and universal bimanual dexterous grasp policy; PTLD shows a practical sim-to-real tactic for tactile dexterous hands without tactile simulation.
- Planning and safety under uncertainty are getting more principled: U‑OBCA, ELLIPSE, and risk-aware rulebooks / RL for mobile manipulation focus on chance constraints, evidential uncertainty, and risk-sensitive decision-making, especially in tight spaces and safety-critical tasks.
- Multi-robot and field robotics systems are maturing: work spans decentralized exploration with LLM guidance, heterogeneous-sensor cooperative localization, cooperative slung-load transport, and edge-only quadruped navigation in mines; several papers show industrial inspection (aerial NDT, textile sorting, crane-mounted LiDAR).
- Tooling and infrastructure for robotics research are improving: new bindings for OMPL, a ROS2 multirotor autopilot, and GaussTwin’s physically grounded digital twin highlight efforts to make high-performance planning and sim–real workflows more accessible.
