## Executive Summary

- Institution filters: Carnegie Mellon University; Massachusetts Institute of Technology; Stanford University; ETH Zurich; UC Berkeley; New York University; UC San Diego  
- Topic filters: mobile manipulation; motion planning; dextrous manipulation; whole-body control  

- Multiple works advance motion planning and control for high-DoF manipulators and humanoids, including dynamics-aware GPU planners, Koopman-accelerated sampling-based control, and uncertainty-aware collision avoidance.
- Several papers target whole-body or large-workspace manipulation and navigation, e.g., humanoid VLA control, omnidirectional 3D perception for humanoid manipulation, and curve-induced dynamical systems on manifolds.
- There is strong activity in dexterous and bimanual manipulation, including large synthetic grasp datasets and sim-to-real privileged tactile learning for in-hand manipulation.
- Vision-language(-action) methods are increasingly tied to low-level control and safety, with work on memory in VLA policies, semantic-safe legged navigation, and interpretability/steering of VLA internals.
- Tools, benchmarks, and infrastructure (e.g., OMPL Python bindings, RoboMME memory benchmark, ROS-based autopilots) continue to mature the motion-planning and robotic-learning ecosystem.

| Observing and Controlling Features in Vision-Language-Action Models | Stanford University | Introduces feature-observability and feature-controllability for VLAs by learning linear probes over internal representations and applying optimal-control-based linear interventions to steer activations. Demonstrates that small online interventions can reliably adjust robot behavior without fine-tuning across π₀.₅ and OpenVLA, preserving closed-loop capabilities while aligning actions with user preferences. | https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.05487 |
| OpenFrontier: General Navigation with Visual-Language Grounded Frontiers | ETH Zurich; University of Bonn | Proposes OpenFrontier, a training-free navigation framework that detects image-space frontiers, ranks them with VLMs using a set-of-marks formulation, and grounds selected frontiers into 3D for long-horizon language-conditioned navigation. Avoids dense 3D mapping and policy training, achieving strong zero-shot navigation in simulation and on robots with lightweight geometry–semantics coupling. | https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.05377 |
| Curve-Induced Dynamical Systems on Riemannian Manifolds and Lie Groups | Delft University of Technology; Idiap/EPFL | Presents CDSM, a framework that builds stable dynamical systems directly on manifolds like SE(3) and SPD(n) by combining tangential motion along a nominal curve with normal attraction toward it. Provides stability analysis and shows applications to manipulators and mobile manipulators where both end-effector poses and impedance parameters are adapted online in a geometry-consistent way. | https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.05268 |
| Rethinking the Role of Collaborative Robots in Rehabilitation | Idiap/EPFL | Conceptual paper arguing for broader roles of cobots in rehabilitation beyond repetitive motion training, including pre-, intra-, and post-session assistance to both therapists and patients. Identifies challenges in user-state estimation, safety, and workflow integration, and maps opportunities for HRI-driven design of assistive behaviors and shared autonomy. | https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.05252 |
| Python Bindings for a Large C++ Robotics Library: The Case of OMPL | Rice University | Describes a human-in-the-loop workflow that uses LLMs plus nanobind to generate and maintain Python bindings for the OMPL motion-planning library. Analyzes common LLM failure modes around ownership, overloads, and trampolines, and shows the resulting bindings match legacy performance while greatly easing maintenance of a large C++ motion-planning stack. | https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.04668 |

| cuRoboV2: Dynamics-Aware Motion Generation with Depth-Fused Distance Fields for High-DoF Robots | NVIDIA | Presents a unified motion-generation stack combining B-spline trajectory optimization with torque limits, GPU-native TSDF/ESDF mapping, and whole-body kinematics/dynamics for manipulators and humanoids. | Curve-Induced Dynamical Systems on Riemannian Manifolds and Lie Groups | Delft University of Technology; Idiap/EPFL | Develops a general method to construct stable vector fields on manifolds (e.g., SE(3) for poses, SPD(n) for damping) from a reference curve, separating tangential progression from normal attraction. | Accelerating Sampling-Based Control via Learned Linear Koopman Dynamics | Purdue University | Integrates a deep Koopman operator model with MPPI to replace expensive nonlinear dynamics rollouts with linear evolution in a learned lifted space. Shows in simulation and quadruped hardware that MPPI-DK closely matches true-dynamics MPPI performance while significantly lowering compute, enabling faster real-time model-predictive control. | https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.05385 |
| U-OBCA: Uncertainty-Aware Optimization-Based Collision Avoidance via Wasserstein Distributionally Robust Chance Constraints | Shanghai Jiao Tong University | Extends optimization-based collision avoidance to handle localization, prediction, and disturbance uncertainty for polygonal robots and obstacles using distributionally robust chance constraints. Derives deterministic nonlinear constraints from OBCA plus Wasserstein ambiguity sets, yielding less conservative, more efficient navigation in cluttered spaces and validated in simulation and real-world trials. | https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.04914 |
| Many-RRT*: Robust Joint-Space Trajectory Planning for Serial Manipulators | USACE ERDC; Virginia Tech | Proposes Many-RRT*, which grows trees toward multiple IK solutions in parallel to address multi-goal ambiguity arising from non-invertible kinematics. | GAIDE: Graph-based Attention Masking for Spatial- and Embodiment-aware Motion Planning | Texas A&M University | Introduces a transformer-based neural informed sampler that encodes robot embodiment and workspace structure as a graph and injects it via attention masks. When plugged into sampling-based planners, GAIDE focuses sampling on relevant subspaces, improving success rates and sample efficiency over uniform, hand-crafted, and prior neural samplers. | https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.04463 |
| Risk-Aware Reinforcement Learning for Mobile Manipulation | University of Oxford; Dyson Institute; UWE Bristol | Trains distributional RL teacher policies for mobile manipulators with a risk-neutral critic, then applies distortion risk metrics to produce risk-sensitive behaviors, and distills to depth-based visuomotor students. Demonstrates runtime-adjustable risk attitudes and improved worst-case performance in reactive whole-body motion in unmapped environments. | https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.04579 |
| PTLD: Sim-to-Real Privileged Tactile Latent Distillation for Dexterous Manipulation | Carnegie Mellon University; UC Berkeley; Meta FAIR | Presents PTLD, a sim-to-real framework where privileged-sensor policies are trained in simulation, then executed on a tactile-equipped hand to collect real tactile trajectories from which a tactile state estimator is distilled. Integrating this estimator into the control loop substantially boosts performance on in-hand rotation and reorientation versus proprioception-only baselines. | https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.04531 |
