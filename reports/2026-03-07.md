## Executive Summary

- Dexterous and long-horizon manipulation see multiple advances: sim-to-real tactile skill distillation, massive bimanual grasp datasets, and improved diffusion / memory-based visuomotor policies.
- Motion planning is strongly represented, from GPU-accelerated dynamics-aware stacks and Riemannian dynamical systems to robust joint-space RRT*, informed sampling, and uncertainty-aware collision avoidance.
- Mobile and mobile manipulation safety are tackled via risk-aware RL, evidential waypoint prediction, and contact-tolerant navigation that leverages vision-language reasoning.
- Whole-body humanoid control progresses with physics-aware VLA frameworks and omnidirectional LiDAR-driven policies for large-workspace manipulation.
- Neuro-symbolic and VLA-based manipulation architectures increasingly integrate memory, interpretability, and human feedback, while new benchmarks probe memory and robustness.
- Digital-twin-based pipelines emerge for both medical (cochlear implantation) and industrial (textile sorting) manipulation, tightening the loop between perception, simulation, and planning.
