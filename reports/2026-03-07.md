1) Executive Summary
- No papers in today’s cs.RO set involve authors from the specified institutions (CMU, MIT, Stanford, ETH Zurich, UC Berkeley, NYU, UC San Diego), based on best-effort affiliation extraction from the PDFs.
- A focused subset of papers substantially advances motion planning and whole-body control, especially for high-DoF manipulators and humanoids (e.g., GPU-accelerated dynamics-aware planning, curve-induced manifold D.S., uncertainty-aware collision avoidance, informed sampling).
- Several works target mobile manipulation and long-horizon visuomotor control, including risk-aware RL for mobile manipulators, LiDAR-based large-workspace humanoid manipulation, and integrated navigation + object search frameworks that couple locomotion with manipulation.
- Dexterous manipulation is addressed through large-scale bimanual dexterous grasp datasets and sim-to-real tactile latent distillation, pushing toward more general and robust in-hand and bimanual skills.
- There is a clear trend toward integrating richer perception (3D LiDAR, whole-body tactile skins, language/gesture/VLM reasoning) with planning and control stacks for safer, more adaptable interaction in complex environments.

| cuRoboV2: Dynamics-Aware Motion Generation with Depth-Fused Distance Fields for High-DoF Robots | NVIDIA | Presents a unified GPU-native motion generation framework that couples B-spline trajectory optimization with torque limits, dense TSDF/ESDF mapping, and scalable whole-body kinematics/dynamics for high-DoF robots. Demonstrates 99.6% collision-free IK on a 48-DoF humanoid and high success rates under payload, and shows generated motions can seed locomotion policies with lower tracking error and variance. | Curve-Induced Dynamical Systems on Riemannian Manifolds and Lie Groups | TU Delft & Idiap | Introduces CDSM, a framework that constructs dynamical systems directly on Riemannian manifolds and Lie groups by defining a nominal curve and decomposing motion into tangential (along-curve) and normal (to-curve) components. Provides stability analysis and efficient real-time computation on manifolds like S², SE(3), and SPD(n). Benchmarks show improved trajectory accuracy, reduced path deviation, and faster generation/query times vs. prior manifold-based motion generation. Demonstrates online adaptation of poses and damping matrices for a manipulator and a mobile manipulator, enabling geometry-aware motion planning and control. | https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.05268 |
| U-OBCA: Uncertainty-Aware Optimization-Based Collision Avoidance via Wasserstein Distributionally Robust Chance Constraints | Shanghai Jiao Tong Univ. | Extends Optimization-Based Collision Avoidance (OBCA) to account for uncertainty in robot and obstacle states using distributionally robust chance constraints in Wasserstein ambiguity sets. Explicitly models polygonal robots and obstacles without circular/elliptic approximations, tightening chance constraints into tractable nonlinear deterministic constraints. The method yields less conservative trajectories and higher success in narrow, cluttered environments relative to existing uncertainty-aware planners. Validated via theory, simulation, and real-robot experiments, showing improved navigation efficiency under localization and prediction errors. | https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.04914 |

When plugged into sampling-based planners, GAIDE improves planning success rates and reduces computation time compared to uniform, hand-crafted informed, and prior neural samplers. Evaluations across diverse manipulator and environment settings show better exploitation of spatial structure for sample efficiency. | https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.04463 |
| Many-RRT*: Robust Joint-Space Trajectory Planning for Serial Manipulators | Brown Univ. | Addresses the multi-solution IK issue in joint-space planning by planning simultaneously to multiple configuration-space goals corresponding to a single task-space target. Extends RRT*-Connect to grow trees from multiple IK goal states in parallel toward a common start tree, framing goal selection as a multi-arm bandit problem. Maintains asymptotic optimality while avoiding wasted effort on poor IK branches, yielding 44.5% lower cost solutions at equal runtime and vastly higher success rates than prior RRT variants. Demonstrated across various robot morphologies and cluttered task environments. | https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.04547 |
| Risk-Aware Reinforcement Learning for Mobile Manipulation | Univ. of Oxford | Trains privileged teacher policies for mobile manipulators using Distributional RL, then shapes risk-aware behavior via distortion risk metrics over the return distribution. Experiments show student policies execute reactive whole-body motions that improve worst-case performance and exhibit explicit risk-aware trade-offs in unmapped environments. The framework is one of the first to demonstrate risk-sensitive visuomotor policies for mobile manipulation. | https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.04579 |

Experiments on humanoid platforms show improved stability and task success in vision-language-guided full-body coordination. | https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.05410 |

| Risk-Aware Rulebooks for Multi-Objective Trajectory Evaluation under Uncertainty | Iowa State Univ. | Extends the rulebooks framework to a risk-aware setting where system trajectories induce distributions over environment responses rather than treating the environment as exogenous noise. An autonomous driving example illustrates how the formalism clarifies the rationale for selecting one trajectory over another under risk. | https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.04603 |

A VPP-Guided Navigation (VGN) module then optimizes control under these constraints using a dedicated DNN, avoiding explicit map construction.
