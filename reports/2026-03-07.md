| PTLD: Sim-to-Real Privileged Tactile Latent Distillation for Dexterous Manipulation | Carnegie Mellon University | Proposes PTLD, a sim-to-real framework for tactile dexterous manipulation that **avoids tactile simulation**. Policies are first trained in simulation with privileged state (“privileged sensor policies”), then executed on a real, instrumented setup to collect tactile trajectories. A latent state estimator is distilled to map real tactile inputs into the privileged latent space, enabling tactile policies at test time. On in-hand rotation benchmarks, PTLD improves success by 182% over proprioception-only policies and yields a 57% increase in goal-reaching for in-hand reorientation, showing that tactile sensing can substantially enhance robustness to slip, mass variation, and wrist reorientation. | https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.04531 |

| cuRoboV2: Dynamics-Aware Motion Generation with Depth-Fused Distance Fields for High-DoF Robots | NVIDIA Research | Introduces cuRoboV2, a unified GPU-native motion generation stack for arms and humanoids. It combines B-spline trajectory optimization with torque limits, a TSDF/ESDF pipeline that builds dense signed distance fields up to 10× faster and 8× more memory-efficient than prior work, and GPU-accelerated whole-body kinematics, differentiable inverse dynamics, and self-collision checking. | UltraDexGrasp: Learning Universal Dexterous Grasping for Bimanual Robots with Synthetic Data | Shanghai AI Laboratory | Presents UltraDexGrasp, a framework and a 20M-frame synthetic dataset (UltraDexGrasp‑20M) for **bimanual dexterous grasping** with multiple strategies (two-finger pinch, tripod, whole-hand, bimanual). Grasps are generated via optimization-based grasp synthesis plus planning-based trajectory demonstrations, ensuring physical plausibility and wrench robustness. A point-cloud policy with unidirectional attention predicts control commands and, trained purely in simulation, achieves 81.2% average success zero-shot in real-world dexterous grasping across varied shapes and weights, highlighting the power of large-scale synthetic data for dexterous manipulation. | https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.05312 |
| Omni-Manip: Beyond-FOV Large-Workspace Humanoid Manipulation with Omnidirectional 3D Perception | HKUST (Guangzhou) | Proposes Omni-Manip, an end-to-end LiDAR-driven visuomotor policy for **large-workspace humanoid manipulation**. A 360° panoramic LiDAR provides omnidirectional point clouds, processed with a Time-Aware Attention Pooling module to encode sparse 3D geometry and temporal context. This avoids frequent base repositioning and camera FOV limitations, allowing a humanoid to manipulate objects in cluttered and out-of-view regions. A whole-body teleoperation system is used to collect full-body demonstrations, and experiments show superior robustness and success rates versus egocentric RGB-D baselines in cluttered, workspace-wide tasks. | https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.05355 |
| PhysiFlow: Physics-Aware Humanoid Whole-Body VLA via Multi-Brain Latent Flow Matching and Robust Tracking | Shanghai Jiao Tong University | Introduces PhysiFlow, a **whole-body Vision-Language-Action (VLA)** framework for humanoid control on a Unitree G1. A “multi-brain” hierarchy uses semantic latent vectors to coordinate three functional subsystems (e.g., semantics, motion intent, and physics-aware low-level control), learned via latent flow matching. The system achieves real-time, vision-language-guided full-body behaviors such as walking to objects, sitting, circling, and coordinated arm motions, improving stability in limb-coordinated tasks relative to naive VLA control by tightly coupling semantics, motion generation, and physics-aware tracking. | https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.05410 |
| U-OBCA: Uncertainty-Aware Optimization-Based Collision Avoidance via Wasserstein Distributionally Robust Chance Constraints | Shanghai Jiao Tong University | Extends Optimization-Based Collision Avoidance (OBCA) to an **uncertainty-aware** setting (U‑OBCA) for polygonal robots and obstacles, avoiding circular/elliptic approximations that cause conservatism. It incorporates localization, prediction, and disturbance uncertainties via Wasserstein distributionally robust chance constraints, which are tightened into deterministic nonlinear constraints solvable by standard optimizers. Real and simulated experiments show less conservative trajectories and better navigation efficiency in narrow, cluttered environments compared to uncertainty-aware planners using simple geometric buffers. | https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.04914 |
| Many-RRT*: Robust Joint-Space Trajectory Planning for Serial Manipulators | USACE ERDC; Virginia Tech | Proposes Many‑RRT*, an extension of RRT*‑Connect for manipulators that **plans to multiple IK goal configurations in parallel**. Recognizing that a single end-effector pose admits many joint states, it treats each IK solution as a separate goal tree and grows them simultaneously from the start tree, effectively searching both configuration space and the preimage of the forward kinematics. This improves success rate and path optimality—achieving 44.5% lower cost for the same runtime and much higher success (100% vs. 1.6% for the next best) across diverse robots and environments—addressing a core failure mode of joint-space planners. | https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.04547 |
| Accelerating Sampling-Based Control via Learned Linear Koopman Dynamics | Purdue University | Presents MPPI-DK, which replaces expensive nonlinear dynamics in **Model Predictive Path Integral (MPPI)** control with a learned Deep Koopman Operator (DKO), yielding linear dynamics in a lifted space. The nonlinear lifting is learned from interaction data, then rollouts during sampling propagate via matrix multiplications, greatly reducing per-sample cost while preserving MPPI’s ability to handle nonlinear costs. Experiments on pendulum, surface vessel, and a quadruped show performance close to MPPI with true dynamics but with significantly reduced computation, enabling higher-frequency sampling-based control for complex robots. | https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.05385 |
| Efficient Autonomous Navigation of a Quadruped Robot in Underground Mines on Edge Hardware | Missouri University of Science and Technology | Develops a complete navigation stack for a Boston Dynamics Spot quadruped operating in **GPS-denied underground mines** using only a low-power Intel NUC (no GPU, no connectivity). The system combines LiDAR–inertial odometry, scan-matching localization against a prior map, terrain segmentation, a visibility-graph global planner, and a velocity‑regulated local controller. After a single mapping pass, it handles arbitrary goal points in the known map and achieves 100% success across 20 fully autonomous trials totaling over 700 m, with SPL ≈ 0.73±0.09, demonstrating robust mobile manipulation base navigation under extreme sensing and compute constraints. | https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.04470 |
| Risk-Aware Reinforcement Learning for Mobile Manipulation | University of Oxford; Dyson Institute; UWE Bristol | Learns **risk-aware visuomotor policies** for mobile manipulators from egocentric depth. A privileged teacher is trained with Distributional RL (Distributional PPO) and a risk‑neutral critic; distortion risk measures are then applied to the return distribution to obtain risk-adjusted advantages for different risk attitudes (risk‑seeking, risk‑averse, etc.). These behaviors are distilled into student visuomotor policies via imitation learning, resulting in depth-based controllers whose risk sensitivity can be tuned at runtime. Evaluations show improved worst‑case performance and more cautious whole‑body motions in unmapped environments, leveraging live depth for safe mobile manipulation. | https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.04579 |
| GAIDE: Graph-based Attention Masking for Spatial- and Embodiment-aware Motion Planning | Texas A&M University | Proposes GAIDE, a neural **informed sampler** for sampling-based motion planning that encodes both workspace geometry and robot embodiment as a graph. A transformer-based sampler uses attention masking driven by this graph (e.g., joint–link structure, obstacle proximity) to bias samples toward promising regions of configuration space. Integrated into RRT-style planners, GAIDE outperforms uniform, hand-crafted heuristic, and prior neural samplers (MPNet, SIMPNet) in planning time and success rate across multi-DoF manipulator benchmarks, showing that graph-structured attention can significantly improve learning-based motion planning. | https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.04463 |
