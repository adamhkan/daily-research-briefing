Executive Summary

  • UC San Diego proposes an object-informed MPPI framework that significantly boosts success and control rates for long-horizon non-prehensile pushing with obstacle avoidance.
  • Carnegie Mellon introduces a globally optimal factor-graph estimator that automatically builds chordal-sparse SDPs, improving reliability for SLAM and localization backends.
  • Multiple works advance navigation and motion planning: TSDF+Gaussian-splat semantic planning, geometry-aware CBFs for complex shapes, bidirectional hybrid A* for kinodynamics, and learning-based indoor navigation.
  • Whole-body and loco-manipulation capabilities are extended via terrain-aware RL policies for legged manipulators and scalable magnetic feet for quadruped wall-climbing.
  • Dexterous and contact-rich manipulation see progress in haptics-driven planning, surface-constrained diffusion policies, in-hand vibratory transport, and novel hardware such as hyperextending tactile hands.
  • Vision-language(-action) models are increasingly integrated into localization, navigation, and manipulation stacks, with new methods for semantic MCL, deformable manipulation, feature-grounded RL, and safety/runtime monitoring.

Object-Informed Model Predictive Path Integral Control for Non-Prehensile Robot ManipulationUC San DiegoIntroduces a hierarchical MPPI framework where an object-only planner first computes a desired object trajectory, which then guides a full robot–object MPPI for non-prehensile pushing with obstacle avoidance. On a 6-DoF xArm6, the method yields up to 40% higher task success and 26% faster control in simulation, and 20% higher success on hardware, with similar compute as standard MPPI.Link
Exploiting Chordal Sparsity for Globally Optimal Estimation with Factor GraphsCarnegie Mellon UniversityPresents an automatic procedure within GTSAM to construct convex SDP relaxations for generic factor graphs and exploit Bayes-tree-induced chordal sparsity for efficient solution. Demonstrated on 3D pose-graph SLAM and 2D localization, the approach scales favorably relative to standard local solvers while providing global optimality or certificates, strengthening estimation for navigation stacks.Link

Learning Terrain-Aware Whole-Body Control for Perceptive Legged Loco-ManipulationHong Kong University of Science and TechnologyProposes TA-WBC, an RL-based unified whole-body controller for legged manipulators that fuses proprioception with a hybrid exteroceptive terrain encoder. A novel end-effector sampling strategy and dual-policy distillation enable stable loco-manipulation with larger reachable workspace, lower tracking error, and fewer stumbles across diverse terrains.Link
LiftNav: Path Planning via Semantic Lifting in TSDF-Guided Gaussian SplattingM University Hospital, Clinic for Orthopedics and Sports Orthopedics; Technical University of Munich; UM University HospitalIntroduces LiftNav, a hybrid navigation framework combining TSDF geometry with Gaussian Splatting appearance plus YOLO-based detection and B-spline trajectory optimization. A hinge-loss collision penalty improves smoothness and safety, achieving 100% feasible trajectories and shorter paths versus a radiance-field baseline in Replica simulations.Link
Geometry-Aware Control Barrier Functions for Collision Avoidance via Bernstein Polynomial ApproximationsProposes a geometry-aware CBF formulation built on Bernstein-polynomial signed distance fields that uniformly represent robot and obstacle geometry, including irregular shapes. The differentiable BP-SDF allows closed-loop safety constraints and is validated for single- and multi-robot collision avoidance with improved safety and computational efficiency.Link
Bidirectional Incremental Generalized Hybrid A*Extends Incremental Generalized Hybrid A* to a bidirectional variant (Bi-IGHA*) for anytime kinodynamic planning without precomputed motion primitives. By searching from start and goal over a hierarchy of resolutions, it preserves monotonic cost improvement while substantially reducing node expansions and maintaining closed-loop performance on high-speed off-road tasks.Link
Learning-Based Navigation for Indoor Mobile RobotsCombines a supervised neural global planner trained from cost-aware A* with a learning-based DWA local planner refined via PPO. The framework produces feasible global routes and robust local commands, enabling safe goal-directed indoor navigation around obstacles in both simulation and real-world tests.Link
Trajectory Planning for Non-Communicating Mobile Robots using Inverse Optimal ControlUses inverse optimal control to infer other robots’ goals from past trajectories, then performs joint prediction that includes self-prediction from each agent’s perspective. The resulting predictions inform trajectory planning, yielding up to 9.8% faster multi-robot goal completion times without planner failures in 2–8 robot scenarios.Link
Haptic Sorter: A Unified Planning Framework for Online Shape Estimation and Real-Time Pose InferenceNanyang Technological University; National Taiwan UniversityPresents a unified model-based framework that couples haptic exploration, Bayesian optimization-driven shape estimation with superellipses, and an online ODE for real-time pose inference. Deployed on a 2D multi-arm sorting task, the system robustly manipulates objects with varying geometries in simulation and hardware despite limited prior shape knowledge.Link
Surface Constraint Policy for Learning Surface-Constrained and Dynamically Feasible Robot SkillsIntroduces a Surface Constraint Policy that encodes free-form surface geometry via weighted Gaussian kernels learned from demonstrations and couples it with diffusion-based intent prediction. These intents are mapped to surface-constrained dynamic movement primitives, enabling smooth, dynamically feasible manipulation along complex surfaces with improved success and contact stability.Link
Two Degree-of-Freedom Vibratory Transport in a GraspAnalyzes and experimentally validates how asymmetric stick–slip vibrations within a grasp can produce controlled 2-DoF in-hand translation and rotation of objects. A custom 2-DoF vibratory surface in a parallel jaw gripper demonstrates bidirectional transport and rotation of various parts, illustrating a non-prehensile route to dexterous manipulation.Link

The system increases tool-swapping reliability and demo efficiency, enabling long-horizon tasks like sandwich making via sequential deployment of multiple tools and effectors.Link
