Executive Summary

  • New control and planning frameworks push robustness in challenging navigation settings: from dynamic, human-populated spaces (HCSG, DynoJEPP) to deformable microrobots and wheeled–legged platforms (3D RL-DWA, MUJICA).
  • Multiple works advance manipulation robustness and generalization under data scarcity, including few-demo motion-field guidance (SID), single-demo construction manipulation, and semantics-aware dexterous grasping (SECOND-Grasp).
  • Navigation safety under uncertainty is a recurring focus, with distributionally robust safety filters, belief-space residual risk, and multi-UAV localization refinement all targeting reliable behavior in ambiguous environments.
  • Autonomous driving research emphasizes richer intent and causality modeling (CaAD, DIAL, MindVLA-U1, SI) to overcome mode collapse and improve preference alignment in long-tail scenes.
  • Perception and mapping for embodied agents see advances from monocular open-vocabulary 3D scene graphs (LEXI-SG) to calibration-free gas source localization and bathymetry-aware coverage planning.
  • No highly relevant motion-planning/manipulation papers this cycle come from the specified key institutions, but NYU contributes a notable on-device gesture detection framework (Scale-Gest) relevant to human-robot interaction pipelines.

Scale-Gest: Scalable Model-Space Synthesis and Runtime Selection for On-Device Gesture DetectionNew York UniversityIntroduces Scale-Gest, a runtime-adaptive framework that spans a dense family of tiny-YOLO gesture detectors and selects operating points via device-calibrated Accuracy-Complexity-Energy profiles. A motion-aware ROI gate further reduces compute, achieving up to 4× energy savings on a battery-powered laptop while maintaining 0.8–0.9 event-level F1 and ≈6 ms latency in driving-like scenarios.Link

TinySDP: Real Time Semidefinite Optimization for Certifiable and Agile Edge RoboticsPresents TinySDP, a semidefinite programming solver tailored for embedded systems that enables real-time MPC with nonconvex obstacle constraints. By integrating PSD cone projections into a cached-Riccati ADMM scheme and adding rank-1 certificates, it achieves shorter, provably collision-free paths in cluttered navigation scenarios and is validated on a Crazyflie quadrotor.Link
Asymptotically Optimal Ergodic Coverage on Generalized Motion FieldsExtends ergodic coverage to time-varying, flow-driven domains using a maximum mean discrepancy (MMD)–based metric that explicitly incorporates ambient flow. The method preserves ergodic guarantees and enables effective open-loop and underactuated exploration, validated in ocean-like fields and on aerial and legged robots in non-convex, flow-restricted environments.Link
SID: Sliding into Distribution for Robust Few-Demonstration ManipulationProposes SID, which learns an object-centric motion field from canonicalized demonstrations to iteratively drive the system back toward the demonstration manifold, paired with a lightweight egocentric policy trained via conditioned flow matching. This decomposes large corrective motion from fine manipulation, yielding ~90% success on six real tasks from just two demonstrations, robust to pose shifts and disturbances.Link
Manipulation Planning for Construction Activities with Repetitive TasksStony Brook UniversityUses VR demonstrations and screw geometry to extract constant screw motions from human examples, then applies ScLERP and RMRC to generate joint-space plans for arbitrarily long repetitive construction tasks. A 7-DoF arm builds brick walls and installs ceiling tiles in simulation and hardware from a single demonstration per primitive, maintaining precision over long horizons.Link
SECOND-Grasp: Semantic Contact-guided Dexterous GraspingIntroduces a unified framework that fuses vision–language reasoning with multi-view geometry to produce semantically meaningful 3D contact maps for dexterous hands. A Semantic-Geometric Consistency Refinement stage enforces cross-view alignment and feasibility, and the resulting contact-supervised policy achieves >97% lifting success and sizable gains in intent-aware grasping on DexGraspNet.Link
Object Manipulation of the Variable Topology Truss systemDevelops a hybrid force–position control framework for a Variable Topology Truss (VTT) robot, whose actuated truss members are joined by passive spherical joints. Sensor-based member-level force control and a static model of the truss enable accurate end-effector force synthesis, allowing consistent object manipulation with concurrent tracking of position and interaction forces.Link
MUJICA: Multi-skill Unified Joint Integration of Control Architecture for Wheeled-Legged RobotsPresents MUJICA, a unified proprioceptive control policy that integrates multiple locomotion skills—omnidirectional driving, high-platform climbing, and fall recovery—for wheeled–legged robots. Skills are trained jointly under accurate DC-motor constraints with a learned high-level selector, achieving robust, adaptive locomotion and seamless mode transitions on a Unitree Go2-W in unstructured terrain.Link
DynoJEPP: Joint Estimation, Prediction and Planning in Dynamic EnvironmentsUniversity of Manchester; University of SydneyIntroduces DynoJEPP, a factor-graph framework that jointly optimizes state estimation, dynamic-object prediction, and planning, while enforcing directional information flow via a novel directed factor. This prevents prediction/planning factors from corrupting localization, dramatically improving safety in dynamic navigation tasks and enabling an extended cooperative variant for interaction-aware planning.Link
What Limits Vision-and-Language Navigation ?Proposes StereoNav, a VLN framework that tackles sim-to-real degradation by introducing Target-Location Priors for stable spatial grounding and stereo-based semantic–geometric fusion for depth-aware action prediction. It achieves SOTA RGB-only performance on R2R-CE and RxR-CE and shows markedly improved real-robot navigation under lighting changes, motion blur, and underspecified instructions.Link
HCSG: Human-Centric Semantic-Geometric Reasoning for Vision-Language NavigationHong Kong University of Science and Technology; National University of Singapore; Shanghai Jiao Tong University; The Chinese University of Hong Kong; The University of Hong Kong; Tsinghua UniversityIntroduces HCSG, a human-centric VLN framework that explicitly reasons about pedestrian trajectories and intentions rather than treating humans as generic obstacles. A Human Understanding Module fuses geometric forecasting with VLM-derived action semantics into the navigation map, and a social distance loss enforces comfort distances, yielding large gains in success rate and collision reduction on HA-VLNCE.Link
