## Executive Summary

- New control and planning frameworks push robustness in challenging navigation settings: from dynamic, human-populated spaces (HCSG, DynoJEPP) to deformable microrobots and wheeled–legged platforms (3D RL-DWA, MUJICA).
- Multiple works advance manipulation robustness and generalization under data scarcity, including few-demo motion-field guidance (SID), single-demo construction manipulation, and semantics-aware dexterous grasping (SECOND-Grasp).
- Navigation safety under uncertainty is a recurring focus, with distributionally robust safety filters, belief-space residual risk, and multi-UAV localization refinement all targeting reliable behavior in ambiguous environments.
- Autonomous driving research emphasizes richer intent and causality modeling (CaAD, DIAL, MindVLA-U1, SI) to overcome mode collapse and improve preference alignment in long-tail scenes.
- Perception and mapping for embodied agents see advances from monocular open-vocabulary 3D scene graphs (LEXI-SG) to calibration-free gas source localization and bathymetry-aware coverage planning.
- No highly relevant motion-planning/manipulation papers this cycle come from the specified key institutions, but NYU contributes a notable on-device gesture detection framework (Scale-Gest) relevant to human-robot interaction pipelines.
