Executive Summary

  • Dexterous and contact-rich manipulation sees multiple advances, from exploration-driven contact coverage and zero-shot dexterous grasping in dense clutter to tool-mediated haptic SLAM and few-shot data augmentation for arm–hand coordination.
  • Mobile and humanoid whole-body control is pushed forward via hierarchical task MPC for sequential mobile manipulation, perceptive HT-MPC for semi-static environments, and a language-conditioned humanoid loco-manipulation framework.
  • Vision-language-action models for manipulation are being strengthened with embodiment-invariant latent action spaces, autoregressive action experts, guidance fields, and training-time or inference-time boosters for precision in clutter.
  • Tactile and deformable-object handling improves through global tactile pose registration, fine-grained tactile-language pretraining, and a single-arm vision-tactile gripper enabling bimanual-style cloth unfolding.
  • Motion planning and safe control research spans unified multi-robot planning benchmarks, GPU-native nonlinear optimal control, belief-space safety under partial observability, and safety-augmented diffusion planners for autonomous driving.
  • Institutional highlights include MIT work on hyper-redundant arm shape control and AUV context-aware search planning, and University of Washington work on conformal risk–aware safe control for human-robot interaction.

Hierarchical Task Model Predictive Control for Sequential Mobile Manipulation TasksTechnical University of Munich; University of TorontoIntroduces a hierarchical-task MPC framework based on nonlinear lexicographic optimization to execute sequential tasks on a 9-DoF mobile manipulator. Compared to task-prioritized inverse kinematics and single-task architectures, it improves hierarchical tracking by 42% and achieves over 2× faster execution by better exploiting redundancy during task changes and singularities.Link
Perceptive Hierarchical-Task MPC for Sequential Mobile Manipulation in Unstructured Semi-Static EnvironmentsMunich and the Munich Institute; Simon Fraser University; Technical University of Munich; University of TorontoExtends hierarchical-task MPC to unstructured, semi-static environments by embedding a Bayesian object-level change model into the control loop. The approach maintains an up-to-date 3D world representation without prior maps and systematically handles moved and phantom obstacles, improving efficiency and robustness in long-horizon sequential mobile manipulation.Link
Contact Coverage-Guided Exploration for General-Purpose Dexterous ManipulationProposes CCGE, an exploration method that represents hand-object contacts as intersections between object surface points and hand keypoints, with a learned hash-based state discretization. A count-based contact-coverage reward and energy-based reaching term drive discovery of diverse contact patterns, boosting training efficiency and success on tasks from cluttered singulation to bimanual manipulation, with demonstrated sim-to-real transfer.Link
Cybo-Waiter: A Physical Agentic Framework for Humanoid Whole-Body Locomotion-ManipulationPresents an agentic humanoid framework that converts VLM-generated plans into typed JSON subtasks with explicit pre- and postconditions, grounded via multi-object 3D perception. A supervisory layer performs condition-level verification and replanning, while whole-body locomotion-manipulation primitives are selected under reachability and balance constraints, improving robustness on tabletop and long-horizon loco-manipulation tasks.Link

A distance-based RL policy enables zero-shot dexterous grasping across varying objects, and the new Clutter-Bench benchmark plus sim-to-real tests show substantial gains in grasp success in dense clutter.Link
