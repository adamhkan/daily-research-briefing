## Executive Summary

- Strong emphasis on world and world-action models for manipulation and navigation, including WEAVER, NavWAM, MaskWAM, EA-WM, EWAM, and several certified or equivariant modeling approaches.
- Mobile manipulation and long-horizon control receive substantial attention via GeoHAT, SERF, SERF-enhanced VLAs, and GPU-accelerated planning for reactive motion (G-MAPP).
- Dexterous and contact-rich manipulation advances span articulated tools (Mana), anatomically grounded hand design (MCR-Bionic), SE(3)-equivariant grasp generation (EquiDexFlow), and egocentric-to-robot demonstration pipelines (EgoEngine).
- Whole-body and humanoid control are pushed through tactile whole-body manipulation (WT-UMI), humanoid-object interaction from generated videos (GenHOI), and unified RL for motion tracking and fall recovery (Stubborn).
- Navigation and localization in complex, open-world environments are advanced by FlowPilot for sidewalk navigation, Foresight for language-guided cue reasoning, and new LiDAR/vision place-recognition methods for unstructured and forested terrains.
- Representation and policy-level tools (AEM, Sparse2Act, FTP-1, μVLA, EmbodiSteer, Flow Reversal Steering) systematically improve manipulation robustness under partial observability, embodiment changes, and low-data regimes.
