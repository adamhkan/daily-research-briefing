Executive Summary

  • Strong emphasis on world and world-action models for manipulation and navigation, including WEAVER, NavWAM, MaskWAM, EA-WM, EWAM, and several certified or equivariant modeling approaches.
  • Mobile manipulation and long-horizon control receive substantial attention via GeoHAT, SERF, SERF-enhanced VLAs, and GPU-accelerated planning for reactive motion (G-MAPP).
  • Dexterous and contact-rich manipulation advances span articulated tools (Mana), anatomically grounded hand design (MCR-Bionic), SE(3)-equivariant grasp generation (EquiDexFlow), and egocentric-to-robot demonstration pipelines (EgoEngine).
  • Whole-body and humanoid control are pushed through tactile whole-body manipulation (WT-UMI), humanoid-object interaction from generated videos (GenHOI), and unified RL for motion tracking and fall recovery (Stubborn).
  • Navigation and localization in complex, open-world environments are advanced by FlowPilot for sidewalk navigation, Foresight for language-guided cue reasoning, and new LiDAR/vision place-recognition methods for unstructured and forested terrains.
  • Representation and policy-level tools (AEM, Sparse2Act, FTP-1, μVLA, EmbodiSteer, Flow Reversal Steering) systematically improve manipulation robustness under partial observability, embodiment changes, and low-data regimes.

GeoHAT: Geometry-Adaptive Hybrid Action Transformer for Mobile ManipulationGeoHAT is a diffusion-based mobile manipulation policy that injects 3D geometry only where depth is reliable and needed, using lightweight Fourier encodings and gated fusion into vision-foundation features. A hybrid whole-body decoder separately reasons about base and arm while coordinating them temporally, achieving a 79.3% success rate on ManiSkill-HAB and strong real-world gains.Link
SERF: Spatiotemporal Environment and Robot Feature Map for Long-Horizon Mobile ManipulationSERF maintains a unified neural point map of both environment and articulated robot body, updated online via tracking and forward kinematics. Map tokens provide multi-scale, multi-frame context to a VLA policy, improving long-horizon mobile manipulation on BEHAVIOR-1K in terms of success, subgoal efficiency, and robustness to configuration shifts.Link
NavWAM: A Navigation World Action Model for Goal-Conditioned Visual NavigationNavWAM turns navigation world-model prediction into a directly executable diffusion-transformer policy by representing future observations, goal progress, and action chunks in a shared latent sequence. Pretrained in simulation and adapted to real robots, it outperforms planning-based world-model and direct policy baselines for image-goal navigation without online action search.Link
From Imitation to Alignment: Human-Preference Flow Policies for Long-Horizon Sidewalk NavigationFlowPilot uses anchored flow matching as an action representation to pretrain a monocular, mapless sidewalk navigation policy on fleet data, then refines it via human preference learning to improve social compliance and counterfactual reasoning. It significantly increases route completion and reduces interventions in both simulation and real-world sidewalk deployments.Link
Foresight: Iterative Reasoning About Clues that Matter for NavigationForesight uses a finetuned VLM in a test-time loop that alternates between proposing image-space motion plans and critiquing them relative to language instructions and visual context. A human-feedback-trained reward model steers critiques so the robot discovers and exploits previously unknown cues (e.g., ramps, signs), improving real-world instruction-following navigation success by 37%.Link
Mana: Dexterous Manipulation of Articulated ToolsMana reframes dexterous manipulation of articulated tools as an animation problem, using keyframe-based motion planning followed by RL refinement in simulation. With minimal human input to specify affordances, it achieves zero-shot sim-to-real transfer on four varied articulated tools for both functional grasping and in-hand manipulation.Link
MCR-Bionic Hand: Anatomical Structural Priors for Dexterous ManipulationMCR-Bionic Hand implements a 1:1 musculoskeletal architecture with tendon routing, extensor hood, and intrinsic muscle analogs, embedding human-like structural priors into the hardware. This design couples low-dimensional postural inputs to rich default grasps and distal modulation, enabling contact-rich tasks like coin rotation and pen transfer with reduced control complexity.Link
EquiDexFlow: Contact-Grounded SE(3)-Equivariant Dexterous Grasp Generative FlowsEquiDexFlow is an SE(3)-equivariant flow-matching model that jointly predicts wrist pose, joint angles, surface contacts, normals, and forces for dexterous grasps, enforcing friction-cone and surface constraints by design. Trained on Allegro Hand grasps, it produces physically valid, force-closure grasps that transfer to a different 16-DoF hand and succeed in real pick-and-hold trials.Link
EgoEngine: From Egocentric Human Videos to High-Fidelity Dexterous Robot DemonstrationsEgoEngine converts raw egocentric human manipulation videos into robot-ready data by generating aligned robot observation videos and feasible action trajectories that respect kinematic constraints. It supports zero-shot visuomotor policies for dexterous hands without real-robot demonstrations, opening a scalable path from human video corpora to robot training sets.Link
FTP-1: A Generalist Foundation Tactile Policy Across Tactile Sensors for Contact-Rich ManipulationFTP-1 is a foundation tactile policy that unifies heterogeneous tactile inputs (images, arrays, states) into shared morphology-aware tokens modeled by a transformer expert. Pretrained on ~3,000 hours from 21 sensors and both human and robot data, it improves downstream contact-rich manipulation by 17.2% on seen sensors and transfers effectively to previously unseen tactile hardware.Link
