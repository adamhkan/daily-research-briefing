Executive Summary

  • Humanoid and dexterous control advance via large-scale motion models (Humanoid-GPT), physics-aware style transfer/retargeting, and new RL tracking frameworks for contact-rich skills.
  • Navigation and motion planning see progress in zero-shot neural navigation functions, world-model-based drone/UAV control, sketch- and diffusion-aided path planners, and glare-/semantics-aware mapping and odometry.
  • Vision-language(-action) foundations mature with unified VLN simulators, geometry- and phase-aware VLA training, action-gated memories, prompt adaptation, and security analysis of CoT-based policies.
  • New planning and control theory spans Riemannian informed sampling, certified repair of vetoed maneuvers in driving, stable flow-matching dynamical systems, and matrix-valued heuristics for complex manipulators.
  • Embodied platforms, simulation tools, and sensing – from an open VLA+arm stack and Isaac Sim survey to tactile/event-based sensing and underwater electrosense – broaden the experimentation toolbox.
  • One paper from Carnegie Mellon University focuses on a hydroponic digital-twin architecture for plant growth forecasting, illustrating robotics-adjacent modeling in agriculture.

Neural Navigation Functions for Zero-Shot Generalizable Motion PlanningIntroduces Neural-NF, which learns parameters of a structured elliptic navigation PDE from geometric features while preserving formal guarantees of collision-free, globally convergent navigation. This hybrid of analytic navigation functions and learning achieves strong zero-shot transfer across unseen environments and outperforms direct value-function learners by up to 5×.Link
Direct Informed Sampling on Riemannian Manifolds via Loewner Order Lower BoundsUniversity of TorontoDevelops a matrix-valued admissible heuristic for informed sampling in Riemannian motion planning that preserves the directional structure of configuration-dependent metrics. By mapping with a tight Loewner-order lower bound, the planner samples directly in an equivalent Euclidean hyperspheroid, tightening informed sets and accelerating convergence for high-DOF manipulators.Link
Motion Planning in Dynamic Environments: A Survey from Classical to Modern MethodsChinese Academy of Sciences; Shanghai Jiao Tong University; University of Chinese Academy of Sciences; University of Connecticut; University of Science and Technology of ChinaSurveys 138 works on motion planning in dynamic environments, covering sampling-based, graph-search, MPC, learning-based, and classical local methods. Highlights key challenges such as prediction uncertainty, human–robot interaction, and the freezing robot problem, providing a structured map of current approaches and open problems.Link
SPADE: Sketch-guided Path Planning Augmented with Diffusion ExpertsPresents a ROS 2 framework where human sketch-based preferences are incorporated into mobile-robot path planning via behavior cloning enhanced with diffusion-model augmentation. The method yields substantially lower pose error and better trajectory realism than prior imitation-based planners, while remaining lightweight enough for real-time onboard use.Link
Self-Refining Agentic Reinforcement Learning for Vision-Conditioned UAV NavigationAgenticRL uses a multimodal GPT agent to iteratively design and refine reward functions, diagnose policy failures, and select policies for UAV navigation tasks. Across gate traversal, obstacle avoidance, and landing, the self-refining loop improves behavior by 71% over initial rewards and achieves high sim-to-real transfer with ~91% real success.Link

The framework achieves higher success rates and robust sim-to-real transfer without deployment-time tuning, exhibiting emergent behaviors like escaping local minima and controlling yaw for better situational awareness.Link
Fixed-Time Dynamic Landing of Quadrotors using Adaptive Unscented Kalman Filtering and Nonlinear Model Predictive Control. Faieghi are with the Autonomous Vehicles Laboratory; University of TorontoProposes a dynamic-landing framework that couples NMPC with minimum-jerk reference generation to enforce fixed touchdown timing on moving platforms. Autonomous Navigation System for Library Service Robot Based on Unitree Go2 EduImplements a ROS 2-based navigation stack on a Unitree Go2 quadruped for quiet, safe motion in cluttered library aisles using 4D LiDAR and depth sensing.
