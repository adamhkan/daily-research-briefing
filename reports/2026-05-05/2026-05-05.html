Executive Summary

  • Stereo and multistage spatial attention architectures are pushing robustness in mobile and fine manipulation, improving localization stability and task success under visual disturbances.
  • Interleaved vision–language reasoning and latent world-action models are emerging as strong paradigms for long-horizon manipulation without expensive pixel-space rollouts.
  • Experience-aware data selection and fleet-scale offline-to-online RL are improving learning efficiency and robustness for generalist manipulation policies in the wild.
  • Model-based contact localization and visual force sensing for soft grippers enable precise, low-cost force estimation to support delicate dexterous manipulation.
  • Uncertainty-aware costmaps and safety-constrained DRL planners are advancing motion planning reliability for legged locomotion and UAV navigation in challenging environments.
  • New XR-based simulation and cross-source LiDAR pose refinement benchmarks are enriching data generation and mapping infrastructure that underpins field robotic systems.

Paired-CSLiDAR: Height-Stratified Registration for Cross-Source Aerial-Ground LiDAR Pose RefinementStanford UniversityIntroduces Paired-CSLiDAR, a benchmark for refining ground LiDAR poses using aerial scans despite limited geometric overlap. TitleInstitutionOverviewLink
Stereo Multistage Spatial Attention for Real-Time Mobile Manipulation Under Visual Scale Variation and DisturbancesProposes a stereo multistage spatial attention and hierarchical recurrent model for real-time mobile manipulation with onboard vision. By extracting task-relevant 3D attention points and integrating them with robot state, the method improves robustness to changing viewpoints and visual disturbances across several mobile manipulation tasks.Link
MSACT: Multistage Spatial Alignment for Stable Low-Latency Fine ManipulationAugments action-chunking policies with a multistage spatial attention module that tracks stable 2D keypoints for fine, including bimanual, manipulation. A self-supervised temporal alignment loss reduces attention drift without explicit keypoint labels, improving localization stability and task success on the ALOHA platform while maintaining low latency.Link
Thinking in Text and Images: Interleaved Vision--Language Reasoning Traces for Long-Horizon Robot ManipulationIntroduces Interleaved Vision–Language Reasoning (IVLR), which plans using traces that interleave textual subgoals and visual keyframes over an entire manipulation task. A multimodal transformer generates these traces and conditions an action decoder, yielding large gains on LIBERO long-horizon benchmarks versus text-only, vision-only, or no-trace baselines.Link
Task-Conditioned Uncertainty Costmaps for Legged LocomotionLearns a foothold prediction model that also estimates epistemic uncertainty conditioned on terrain and commanded motion, distinguishing in- from out-of-distribution operating regimes. This uncertainty is injected into costmap generation, enabling legged robots to plan safer paths and reducing feasibility error on challenging, unstructured terrains.Link
Dynamic-TD3: A Novel Algorithm for UAV Path Planning with Dynamic Obstacle Trajectory PredictionModels UAV navigation with dynamic obstacles as a constrained MDP and extends TD3 with trajectory intent prediction and a physics-aware Kalman filter. The dual-criterion policy enforces hard safety constraints while maintaining maneuverability, yielding better collision avoidance and smoother, more energy-efficient paths in aggressive threat scenarios.Link
A Model-based Visual Contact Localization and Force Sensing System for Compliant Robotic GrippersPresents a model-based pipeline that infers contact location and grasp forces for fin-ray soft grippers using RGB-D wrist cameras and inverse finite element analysis. Structural keypoints parameterize a simulation-driven estimator, achieving low force errors across objects and conditions, and enabling indirect, real-time force sensing for compliant dexterous grasps.Link
Learning while Deploying: Fleet-Scale Reinforcement Learning for Generalist Robot PoliciesIntroduces Learning While Deploying, an offline-to-online RL framework that continually improves a generalist VLA policy using autonomous rollouts and human interventions collected from a 16-robot fleet. Combining distributional value learning with specialized Q-learning for flow-based actions, the system raises success to about 95% on diverse, including long-horizon, manipulation tasks.Link
E$^2$DT: Efficient and Effective Decision Transformer with Experience-Aware Sampling for Robotic ManipulationEnhances Decision Transformers for manipulation by coupling them with k-DPP-based, experience-aware sampling that favors diverse, high-return, uncertain, and underrepresented trajectories. A quality–diversity joint kernel uses DT latent embeddings and composite quality metrics to drive data selection, improving long-horizon performance in simulation and real-robot evaluations.Link
Lucid-XR: An Extended-Reality Data Engine for Robotic ManipulationDescribes Lucid-XR, an XR-native physics environment running directly on headsets with human-to-robot pose retargeting and a physics-guided video generation pipeline. It produces large-scale, language-steerable synthetic datasets that enable zero-shot transfer of visual manipulation policies, including for soft and particulate materials, to cluttered and poorly lit real settings.Link
Being-H0.7: A Latent World-Action Model from Egocentric VideosProposes a latent world-action model that inserts learnable latent queries between perception and action in VLA-style policies, trained with a dual-branch prior/posterior scheme that injects future information at training time only. This yields future-aware, dynamics-sensitive representations without visual rollouts, achieving state-of-the-art or comparable results on several simulated and real manipulation tasks.Link
