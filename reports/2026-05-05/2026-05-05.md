## Executive Summary

- Stereo and multistage spatial attention architectures are pushing robustness in mobile and fine manipulation, improving localization stability and task success under visual disturbances.
- Interleaved vision–language reasoning and latent world-action models are emerging as strong paradigms for long-horizon manipulation without expensive pixel-space rollouts.
- Experience-aware data selection and fleet-scale offline-to-online RL are improving learning efficiency and robustness for generalist manipulation policies in the wild.
- Model-based contact localization and visual force sensing for soft grippers enable precise, low-cost force estimation to support delicate dexterous manipulation.
- Uncertainty-aware costmaps and safety-constrained DRL planners are advancing motion planning reliability for legged locomotion and UAV navigation in challenging environments.
- New XR-based simulation and cross-source LiDAR pose refinement benchmarks are enriching data generation and mapping infrastructure that underpins field robotic systems.
