## Executive Summary

- New motion-planning theory (PISTO, Voro-POMCPOW) improves success, smoothness, and offers finite-time guarantees for stochastic and POMDP planners.
- Multiple works advance safe navigation: resilient AV motion planning with V2X (MORPH-U), Hamiltonian risk-aware fields, BEV-guided PathPainter, and ultra-lightweight topological UAV exploration.
- Embodied manipulation sees progress in long-horizon brick assembly (BrickCraft), wet-lab protocol execution (BioProVLA-Agent), and aerial manipulation for pollination and deformable-object transport.
- VLA-based manipulation is refined with better data strategies (ACA), tactile integration (AT-VLA), and utilization of failure demonstrations for more robust imitation learning.
- New benchmarks and systems (TAVIS active-vision benchmark, AERO-VIS event-based SLAM) target perception and gaze control as key levers for manipulation and agile flight.
- Advanced whole-body and flexible-manipulator control is addressed via Lie-algebraic PDE control and robust NMPC for close-proximity aerial inspection.

| PISTO: Proximal Inference for Stochastic Trajectory Optimization | Georgia Institute of Technology | PISTO reframes STOMP-style stochastic trajectory optimization as variational inference with an explicit KL-regularized proximal update, yielding a trust-region interpretation and closed-form mean updates. The derivative-free method handles non-differentiable costs and outperforms CHOMP and STOMP in arm motion planning, as well as CEM and MPPI in contact-rich MuJoCo locomotion and manipulation. | https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.07215 |
| MORPH-U: Multi-Objective Resilient Motion Planning for V2X-Enabled Autonomous Driving in High-Uncertainty Environments via Simulation |  | MORPH-U is a CARLA-based closed-loop stack that fuses onboard sensing with V2X into a Local Dynamic Map and triggers Hybrid-A* replanning upon validated hazards or map changes. A multi-objective formulation with Pareto-front analysis balances safety, tracking, responsiveness, and comfort, while a Byzantine-inspired gate filters faulty V2X, improving safety and robustness under adversarial message injection. | https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.07370 |
| Learning Material-Aware Hamiltonian Risk Fields for Safe Navigation |  | This work augments port-Hamiltonian navigation with a learned context-energy term that induces selective lateral forces toward genuinely safer directions, gated to avoid hallucinated maneuvers when no safe escape exists. Using a CVaR objective, the approach improves success, reduces premature evasive actions, and cuts catastrophic failures across synthetic benchmarks, off-road terrain, semantic maps, and highway traffic scenarios. | https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.07038 |
