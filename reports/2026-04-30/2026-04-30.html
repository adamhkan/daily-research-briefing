Executive Summary

  • Dexterous manipulation sees advances in risk-aware grasp planning, sequential resource-aware hand use, and robust control under multimodal uncertainty.
  • Mobile manipulation robustness is boosted by physically grounded symbolic planning that tightly couples navigation, reachability, and layered recovery.
  • New motion-planning formalisms and UGV–UAV cooperation algorithms address path planning under uncertainty with formal guarantees and empirical gains.
  • Whole-body safety and perception are improved via reinforcement learning for collision avoidance with egocentric tactile/proximity priors and new multi-modal artificial skins.
  • Safe visuomotor control from images is made more practical at scale through learned visual abstractions combined with System Level Synthesis and efficient solvers.
  • Simulation, execution, and policy architectures advance via high-throughput photorealistic simulators, friction-aware sim-to-real for ballbots, and discrete diffusion for asynchronous control.

VISION-SLS: Safe Perception-Based Control from Learned Visual Representations via System Level SynthesisETH ZurichVISION-SLS combines learned low-dimensional visual representations with System Level Synthesis to design nonlinear output-feedback controllers that guarantee constraint satisfaction under uncertainty. Using state-dependent error bounds and a scalable solver, it enables safe visuomotor control from high-resolution images in simulated car, quadrotor, humanoid tasks, and a real ground vehicle, outperforming baselines in safety and solve time.Link

ANCHOR: A Physically Grounded Closed-Loop Framework for Robust Home-Service Mobile ManipulationANCHOR aligns symbolic task planning with continually revalidated geometric predicates to maintain consistency between plans and the evolving physical world in home-service mobile manipulation. It couples operability-aware base alignment with layered failure recovery, boosting real-robot success from 53.3% to 71.7% and achieving high recovery under perturbations in unseen homes.Link
Logic of Fuzzy PathsThis work introduces a temporal logic that treats paths as first-class objects and uses fuzzy, time-varying constraints to express motion-planning specifications and preferences. Compared to Signal Temporal Logic, it yields simpler formulas, richer satisfaction notions, and is better suited both for human-written specifications and learning specifications from demonstrations.Link
Variational Neural Belief Parameterizations for Robust Dexterous Grasping under Multimodal Uncertainty. James Clark School; University of Maryland, College ParkThe authors cast grasp acquisition as variational inference over latent contact and pose variables, representing belief as a differentiable Gaussian mixture and optimizing a CVaR-based risk objective via pathwise gradients. Compared to particle-filter MPC, the method achieves more robust dexterous grasps under contact and force uncertainty with an order-of-magnitude faster planning and better-calibrated risk both in simulation and on a multi-fingered hand.Link
HANDFUL: Sequential Grasp-Conditioned Dexterous Manipulation with Resource AwarenessNorthwestern University; University of Southern CaliforniaHANDFUL treats finger availability as a limited resource, learning grasp strategies that preserve degrees of freedom for subsequent manipulation subtasks such as pushing, pulling, and pressing. Through a new HANDFUL-Bench benchmark and real experiments on a LEAP hand, resource-aware grasps yield higher success and robustness than grasps optimized greedily for the initial task.Link
Egocentric Tactile and Proximity Sensors as Observation Priors for Humanoid Collision AvoidanceUniversity of Colorado BoulderUsing a dodgeball-inspired benchmark on a humanoid H1-2, this work studies how distributed tactile and proximity sensors shape learned whole-body collision-avoidance policies. It finds that raw, sparse, non-directional proximity measurements with sufficient range can effectively replace explicit object localization and offer better sample efficiency than dense directional sensing for RL-based avoidance.Link
Improving Sensing Coverage and Compliance of 3D-Printed Artificial Skins Through Multi-Modal Sensing and Soft MaterialsUniversity of Colorado BoulderThe paper presents 3D-printed artificial skin modules that combine time-of-flight and self-capacitance sensing under a soft, compliant covering to enhance impact absorption and tactile capability. Deployed over an FR3 arm, the skins provide whole-arm contact detection, local scene reconstruction, and pressure-correlated responses with a streamlined electrical interface for scalable whole-body sensing.Link
GS-Playground: A High-Throughput Photorealistic Simulator for Vision-Informed Robot LearningGS-Playground integrates a parallel physics engine with batch 3D Gaussian Splatting to deliver photorealistic simulation at up to 10^4 FPS at 640x480, enabling large-scale visual RL. An automated Real2Sim pipeline reconstructs efficient, physically consistent environments, supporting locomotion, navigation, and manipulation tasks and narrowing both perceptual and physical sim-to-real gaps.Link
Optimal UGV-UAV Cooperative Partitioning and Inspection of Shortest PathsExtending the Canadian Traveller Problem, this work analyzes cooperative shortest-path planning where a UGV is aided by a UAV inspecting uncertain road segments. It derives worst-case competitive ratios showing how UAV speed improves performance and proposes an optimal partitioning strategy for inspection, achieving up to 30% UGV travel-time reduction on real-world road networks.Link
Dynamic UGV-UAV Cooperative Path Planning in Uncertain EnvironmentsDepartment of Computer Science, University ofThe paper formulates dynamic cooperative path planning where UAVs scout an uncertain road network to guide a UGV safely and efficiently to its destination, relevant to disaster and emergency logistics. Several strategies, including a bidirectional scheme and multi-UAV extensions, are evaluated on 100 urban networks, showing notable UGV travel-time reductions at the cost of higher computation.Link
DiscreteRTC: Discrete Diffusion Policies are Natural Asynchronous ExecutorsDiscreteRTC leverages discrete diffusion policies, whose native unmasking operation supports real-time chunking and inpainting, to execute actions asynchronously while thinking ahead. On dynamic simulation and real-world manipulation tasks, it outperforms flow-matching-based RTC with 50% higher success in a dynamic pick task, reduced computation (≈0.7×), and no extra fine-tuning for asynchronous execution.Link
