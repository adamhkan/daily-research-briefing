## Executive Summary

- Mobile and whole-body manipulation see advances from lightweight VLAs (AnoleVLA), real-time 3D-aware policies (R3DP), and humanoid loco‑manipulation/RL fine‑tuning (REFINE-DP).
- Dexterous and contact-rich manipulation is a major focus, spanning new grasp synthesis/learning (DGS-Net, GraspADMM), visuo‑tactile policies (TransDex, TacFiLM), and hybrid force/tactile control frameworks (ForceVLA2, M2-ResiPolicy, HapticVLA).
- Mixture-of-experts and geometry/action-latent abstractions (MoE-ACT, OPFA, KoopmanFlow) improve multi-task, cross-embodiment, and spectrally decoupled control for complex manipulation.
- Model-based and optimization-centric motion planning progresses via task-oriented MPC (ToMPC), STL-guided variational trajectory optimization (STL-SVPIO), and fast generative MPC alternatives to diffusion (KDP, IMLE, IMLE-MPC).
- Benchmarks and datasets expand coverage to non-prehensile, dynamic, long-horizon, and collaborative industrial manipulation (DOMINO, RoCo, LDHP, GroundedPlanBench, Dream2Fix).
- Institutional highlights: CMU, MIT, ETH Zurich, and NYU contribute on dual-arm agricultural mobile manipulation, underwater opti-acoustic fusion, microgravity RPO simulation, transformer LQR controllers, and egocentric HOI world models.
