## Executive Summary

- Institutional highlights span UC San Diego’s above/below-water multi-robot SLAM, Stanford’s provably safe constraint tightening for vehicle planning, UC Berkeley’s certifiable speedups for optimal control, and ETH Zurich’s RGB-only multi-agent Gaussian-SLAM and large-scale 3D scene graph alignment.
- Mobile manipulation and motion-planning papers are dominated by advances in generative VLA control, trajectory optimization, and diffusion/flow-based policies that emphasize long-horizon robustness, recovery, and efficiency.
- Multiple works push fast and reliable motion planning: from optimal multi-agent path finding via optimal transport and Schrödinger bridges to translation-invariant edge bundles and terminal-cost-aware kinodynamic planning.
- Whole-body and legged locomotion control advances include diffusion-guided quadruped priors, neuromorphic RL for on-robot adaptation, explicit stair-geometry conditioning for humanoids, and curriculum learning with external forces.
- Safety, fault tolerance, and formal guarantees recur across topics, with contributions in safe Koopman learning, Gaussian-process-based safe exploration, deterministic protection levels for odometry, and symbolic constraint learning for embodied planning.
- Benchmarks and datasets such as OmniNavBench, RoboMemArena, ObjView-Bench, M2Bench, SABER, and others aim to standardize evaluation for navigation, memory, view planning, monitoring, and retail manipulation.
