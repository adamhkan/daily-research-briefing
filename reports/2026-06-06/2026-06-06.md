## Executive Summary

- Strong emphasis on whole-body and dexterous manipulation: several works (HANDOFF, MotionDisco, LadderMan, DexFuture, MoDex, RealDexUMI) push humanoid and hand-level control for long-horizon, contact-rich tasks.
- Vision-Language-Action foundations continue to mature, with new affordance-aware (AffordanceVLA), speed-controllable (TempoVLA), and latent-reasoning (MPCoT, one-step VLA, FlowPRO, Flash-WAM) architectures aimed at practical robot deployment.
- Robust motion generation and adaptation receive attention via torque adaptation (TAM), amortized nonlinear MPC, MPC-guided RL, transfer-boosted iCEM, and resilient policy adaptation (GLOVES).
- Navigation and motion planning advances span underwater 3D path planning with learned flow surrogates, waypoint design for sampling-based planners, continuous-time SLAM, and uncertainty-aware sensor fusion.
- Safety and verification are treated more systematically through a cross-layer survey of safe long-horizon manipulation, adversarial safety-policy training, and formally verifiable self-evolving skill contracts (VASO).
- Institution-aligned work from MIT and Stanford highlights hardware-focused advances in high-speed vine robot navigation, visuotactile robotic ultrasound, and cross-view geo-localization in GNSS-denied environments.
