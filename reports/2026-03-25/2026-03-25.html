Executive Summary

  • Dexterous and bimanual manipulation sees major advances, from in-hand drumming and universal hand control to energy-based unimanual-to-bimanual policy composition.
  • Whole-body and humanoid control progresses via neural motion retargeting and robustness-oriented sim-to-real methods, improving dynamic skill transfer from humans.
  • Multiple works focus on perception-action integration for manipulation, leveraging visuo-tactile sensing, differentiable simulation, and affordance-based reasoning in clutter.
  • VLA and diffusion-based visuomotor policies are being rethought around robustness, planning, visual prompting, chain-of-thought reasoning, and speed/latency.
  • New motion and trajectory planning tools—geometry-aware push-grasping, directional mollification, disturbance-aware reachability, and optimal routing with obstacles—expand safe control in complex environments.
  • Institution-matched labs (MIT, CMU, ETH Zurich, Stanford) contribute on precision optical assembly, robust estimation, simulator calibration, expert-guided printing, and dexterous performance testbeds.

DexDrummer: In-Hand, Contact-Rich, and Long-Horizon Dexterous Robot DrummingStanford UniversityIntroduces drumming as a challenging benchmark for in-hand, contact-rich, long-horizon dexterous manipulation, using a hierarchical bimanual policy trained in simulation with sim-to-real transfer. Combines trajectory planning with residual RL and a dexterous control layer to stabilize the stick, manage repeated impacts, and coordinate fast transitions between drums, achieving strong simulated and real-world performance.Link
A Framework for Closed-Loop Robotic Assembly, Alignment and Self-Recovery of Precision Optical SystemsMassachusetts Institute of TechnologyPresents a closed-loop robotic framework for autonomous assembly, alignment, and maintenance of high-precision free-space optical systems. Uses hierarchical vision, optimization, and custom tools to build and maintain a tabletop laser cavity from scattered components, including beam centering, resonator alignment, mode selection, and automatic self-recovery from misalignments.Link
Programming Manufacturing Robots with Imperfect AI: LLMs as Tuning Experts for FDM Print Configuration SelectionCarnegie Mellon UniversityUses FDM 3D printing as a testbed where an LLM serves as a tuning expert embedded in a Bayesian optimization loop for selecting print configurations. The LLM interprets diagnostics from an approximate evaluator to iteratively refine settings, yielding higher-quality, safer prints than single-shot AI recommendations and suggesting a broader pattern for LLM-in-the-loop robot process tuning.Link
Implementing Robust M-Estimators with Certifiable Factor Graph OptimizationMassachusetts Institute of TechnologyProposes a way to implement adaptively reweighted M-estimators using certifiable factor graph optimization for robust parameter estimation under outliers. The method solves sequences of weighted least-squares problems with global optimality certificates on manifolds, improving solution quality over local search in pose-graph and landmark SLAM scenarios while remaining scalable.Link
Swim2Real: VLM-Guided System Identification for Sim-to-Real TransferETH ZurichIntroduces a VLM-guided pipeline to calibrate a 16-parameter robotic fish simulator directly from swimming videos, eliminating hand-designed identification stages. The VLM proposes parameter updates that are validated by backtracking line search, yielding significantly better match to real velocities and enabling zero-shot transfer of RL swimming policies to hardware with improved distance traveled.Link
Optimal Solutions for the Moving Target Vehicle Routing Problem with Obstacles via Lazy Branch and PriceCarnegie Mellon UniversitySolves a moving-target vehicle routing problem with obstacles via a Lazy Branch-and-Price with Relaxed Continuity (Lazy BPRC) algorithm. The approach defers expensive motion-planning-based tour cost evaluations by using lower bounds from relaxed continuity planning and only computing exact costs as needed, yielding order-of-magnitude speedups over ablations while preserving optimality.Link

UniDex: A Robot Foundation Suite for Universal Dexterous Hand Control from Egocentric Human VideosProposes UniDex, a foundation suite for dexterous hand control that includes a large multi-hand egocentric dataset, a unified action space (FAAS) for cross-hand transfer, and a VLA policy. Human video is retargeted to diverse robot hands via pointcloud-based alignment, enabling a single policy to generalize tools, spaces, objects, and hand embodiments with strong performance on challenging tasks.Link
Make Tracking Easy: Neural Motion Retargeting for Humanoid Whole-body ControlIntroduces NMR, a neural motion retargeting framework that replaces non-convex optimization-based retargeting with a learned, dynamics-aware process for humanoids. A clustered-expert reinforcement learning pipeline cleans human motion, then trains a CNN-Transformer that produces smooth, feasible whole-body motions on Unitree G1, reducing joint jumps and self-collisions while accelerating downstream control learning.Link
BiPreManip: Learning Affordance-Based Bimanual Preparatory Manipulation through Anticipatory CollaborationDefines collaborative preparatory manipulation tasks where one arm configures the environment so the other can complete a goal-directed action (e.g., lifting or reorienting objects before grasp). Uses visual affordances to envision the final action and guide preparatory steps, improving success and generalization in sequential bimanual manipulation in both simulation and hardware.Link
HyReach: Vision-Guided Hybrid Manipulator Reaching in Unseen Cluttered EnvironmentsUniversity of Illinois Urbana-ChampaignPresents a hybrid rigid–soft continuum arm with vision-based 3D reconstruction and shape-aware motion planning for reaching in clutter without retraining. A learning-based controller exploits the soft segment's compliance while maintaining precision, achieving sub-2 cm accuracy across diverse, unseen cluttered scenes.Link
Bayesian Active Object Recognition and 6D Pose Estimation from Multimodal Contact SensingEindhoven University of TechnologyCombines force/torque, GelSight tactile sensing, and free-space constraints in a Bayesian framework for joint object class and 6D pose estimation during active exploration. A particle filter maintains beliefs and guides next-best touches under reachability constraints, with motion planning and tactile servoing enabling efficient and accurate real-robot exploration.Link
GAPG: Geometry Aware Push-Grasping Synergy for Goal-Oriented Manipulation in ClutterRen are with the CollegeIntroduces a geometry-aware framework that jointly evaluates pushes and grasps using pointcloud geometry to handle cluttered scenes with occlusions and stacked objects. The system predicts how pushes change future graspability and selects actions that safely convert non-graspable states into graspable ones, improving efficiency and robustness in simulation and real-world tests.Link
Affordance-Guided Enveloping Grasp Demonstration Toward Non-destructive Disassembly of Pinch-Infeasible Mating PartsUniversity of OsakaProposes an affordance-guided teleoperation scheme that pre-computes enveloping grasp candidates via physics simulation and visualizes them with grasp-quality color gradients. This augmentation helps operators teach robust multi-finger grasps for disassembly tasks where pinch grasps are infeasible and visual occlusions are severe, validated across simulated and physical components.Link
Cortical Policy: A Dual-Stream View Transformer for Robotic ManipulationIntroduces a dual-stream view transformer inspired by cortical pathways, combining a static-view stream grounded in 3D keypoints with a dynamic-view stream trained for gaze-like egocentric prediction. Fusing these streams yields improved spatial reasoning and adaptation, leading to superior performance over existing multi-view policies on RLBench, COLOSSEUM, and real-world language-conditioned manipulation tasks.Link
Geometrically Plausible Object Pose Refinement using Differentiable SimulationHonda Research Institute; Monash University; Research Institute, USA; Technical University of MunichProposes a pose refinement method that fuses differentiable physics, differentiable rendering, and visuo-tactile sensing to enforce geometric and physical plausibility in estimated object poses. The approach drastically reduces hand–object intersection volumes and pose errors compared to ICP, particularly under high initial uncertainty, targeting robust in-hand manipulation.Link
EnergyAction: Unimanual to Bimanual Composition with Energy-Based ModelsUses energy-based models to treat pretrained unimanual manipulation policies as composable primitives for constructing bimanual policies. An energy-based temporal–spatial coordination mechanism and adaptive denoising strategies ensure coherent dual-arm actions, achieving strong bimanual performance in simulation and real robots with minimal bimanual demonstration data.Link
