## Executive Summary

- Dexterous and bimanual manipulation sees major advances, from in-hand drumming and universal hand control to energy-based unimanual-to-bimanual policy composition.
- Whole-body and humanoid control progresses via neural motion retargeting and robustness-oriented sim-to-real methods, improving dynamic skill transfer from humans.
- Multiple works focus on perception-action integration for manipulation, leveraging visuo-tactile sensing, differentiable simulation, and affordance-based reasoning in clutter.
- VLA and diffusion-based visuomotor policies are being rethought around robustness, planning, visual prompting, chain-of-thought reasoning, and speed/latency.
- New motion and trajectory planning tools—geometry-aware push-grasping, directional mollification, disturbance-aware reachability, and optimal routing with obstacles—expand safe control in complex environments.
- Institution-matched labs (MIT, CMU, ETH Zurich, Stanford) contribute on precision optical assembly, robust estimation, simulator calibration, expert-guided printing, and dexterous performance testbeds.
