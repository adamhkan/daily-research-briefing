Executive Summary

  • CMU proposes visually aligned planning for long-horizon clay sculpting, highlighting both the promise and difficulty of planning directly in image space for deformable manipulation.
  • UC Berkeley demonstrates a hydraulically actuated, compliant gripper for Astrobee that improves microgravity dexterous manipulation while reducing base disturbance.
  • Multiple works advance general-purpose VLA-based manipulation, including dual-arm dexterous control, soft-arm vision-language benchmarks, and externalized linguistic memory for compositional tasks.
  • Navigation-focused papers span formation control for ground robots, topologically grounded planning in 3D environments, and language-informed, norm-compliant and metacognitive navigation.
  • Autonomous driving research emphasizes safety and robustness, from safety-guided diffusion planners and latent prompt adaptation to robustness benchmarks under deployment perturbations.
  • New datasets and benchmarks (e.g., CosFly-Track, WorldArena 2.0, 4DLidarOpen, ESI-Bench) broaden evaluation of motion planning, navigation, and embodied world models across sensing modalities and platforms.

Visual Sculpting: Visually-Aligned Planning Representations for Long-Horizon Robot Clay SculptingCarnegie Mellon UniversityFormulates clay sculpting as a long-horizon shape-matching problem and proposes a visually aligned dynamics model that operates in the image domain, capturing lighting and texture. The robot executes parameterized pushes into deformable media over >100-step horizons, achieving state-of-the-art-like performance while enabling visual planning and analyzing why such representations are harder to plan in than 3D state spaces.Link
A Dexterous and Compliant Gripper With Soft Hydraulic Actuation for Microgravity ManipulationUC BerkeleyIntegrates DexCoHand, a 6-DOF, soft-hydraulic, two-finger gripper, with NASA’s Astrobee free-flying robot for microgravity manipulation. Simulation and earth-based experiments show improved disturbance rejection and preserved commanded motions compared to Astrobee’s stock gripper, enabling more dexterous and compliant interaction with ISS infrastructure.Link

Dexora: Open-source VLA for High-DoF Bimanual DexterityPeking University; Shanghai Jiao Tong University; The University of Hong Kong; Tsinghua UniversityIntroduces Dexora, an open-source VLA system targeting dual-arm, dual-hand high-DoF dexterous manipulation, combining exoskeleton-based arm teleop with Vision Pro-based finger tracking. A large mixed real-sim dataset and a discriminator-weighted diffusion-transformer policy yield strong performance, robustness to OOD settings, and cross-embodiment generalization on dexterous tasks.Link
ManiSoft: Towards Vision-Language Manipulation for Soft Continuum RoboticsProposes ManiSoft, a benchmark and simulator coupling realistic soft-body dynamics with contact-rich interactions for vision-language-controlled soft arms. Defines four task families with 6,300 scenes and expert trajectories, showing that current policies perform well in nominal conditions but degrade sharply under domain randomization, mainly due to poor proprioceptive estimation and underuse of deformability.Link
Key-Gram: Extensible World Knowledge for Embodied ManipulationIntroduces Key-Gram, an external linguistic memory that decomposes instructions into key-grams and retrieves world knowledge via hashed lookup, then injects it into VLA backbones through gated fusion. Decoupling language knowledge from visual-action reasoning improves compositional grounding, zero-shot transfer, and long-horizon real-world manipulation across multiple benchmarks.Link
REACT: Environment-Adaptive Architecture for Continuous Formation Navigation of Wheeled Mobile RobotsNational University of Singapore; Tsinghua University; University of MichiganPresents REACT, a hierarchical framework where a centralized layer generates environment-adaptive formations and conflict-free robot-to-target assignments, while a distributed layer performs joint spatio-temporal trajectory planning. Sim and real experiments show continuous, collision-free formation navigation through cluttered and dynamic environments with efficient reconfiguration.Link
Dynamic robotic cloth folding with efficient Koopman operator-based model predictive controlIstituto Italiano di TecnologiaCombines a high-fidelity cloth simulator with kernel-based Koopman operator regression to learn a linear surrogate model for dynamic cloth behavior. Embedding this in an MPC scheme enables fast, accurate folding trajectories for more compliant garments, achieving efficient dynamic folding in sim and on real robots without sacrificing final fold quality.Link
TacSE3: Equivariant SE(3) Motion Estimation from Low-Texture Visuotactile Images for In-Gripper Tracking and CompensationBay University, Dongguan, China.; Hong Kong University of Science and Technology; The University of Hong KongProposes TacSE3, a tactile motion-estimation pipeline that converts low-texture visuotactile images into a 3D force field and estimates object motion in SE(3). Using dual DM-Tac fingertip sensors, it disambiguates translation and rotation and provides lightweight disturbance compensation signals that improve in-hand manipulation robustness without retraining base policies.Link
Virtues of Ordered Chaos: Planning with Topple Actions in Tabletop Stack RearrangementAustralian National UniversityStudies tabletop stack rearrangement with non-prehensile toppling actions to compress long sequences of pick-and-place moves. A directed graph abstraction models toppling as an aggregating gadget, reducing the task to a pebble-motion-like problem and yielding significantly faster rearrangement plans in IsaacSim benchmarks compared to pick-and-place-only strategies.Link
Optimal Knock-Pick Planning for Tightly Packed Tabletop Blocks With Parallel GrippersIntroduces a directional knock primitive to handle densely packed blocks where standard parallel-gripper picks are infeasible. Using minimal constraining gadgets and a maximum-weight perfect matching over a graphical abstraction, the method computes optimal knock-and-pick plans in polynomial time, minimizing action count and demonstrating efficient mixed prehensile/non-prehensile manipulation.Link
From a Single Demonstration to a General Policy for Contact-Rich ManipulationProposes a four-stage LfD pipeline that abstracts a single demonstration into environmental-constraint primitives, refines them via exploration and human corrections, and recovers instance-specific details at execution time. By following constraint structures instead of raw trajectories, the learned policies achieve >90% success and robustly generalize across poses, geometries, and unmodeled contact dynamics.Link
RoboFlow4D: A Lightweight Flow World Model Toward Real-Time Flow-Guided Robotic ManipulationIntroduces RoboFlow4D, an end-to-end world model that predicts multi-frame 3D motion flows from visual observations and language, serving as a unified perception-planning module. Coupled with a slow-fast execution scheme, it enables real-time, flow-guided manipulation that improves success rates and reduces compute versus modular predictive-flow pipelines.Link
