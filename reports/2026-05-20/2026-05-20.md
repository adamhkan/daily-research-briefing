## Executive Summary

- CMU proposes visually aligned planning for long-horizon clay sculpting, highlighting both the promise and difficulty of planning directly in image space for deformable manipulation.
- UC Berkeley demonstrates a hydraulically actuated, compliant gripper for Astrobee that improves microgravity dexterous manipulation while reducing base disturbance.
- Multiple works advance general-purpose VLA-based manipulation, including dual-arm dexterous control, soft-arm vision-language benchmarks, and externalized linguistic memory for compositional tasks.
- Navigation-focused papers span formation control for ground robots, topologically grounded planning in 3D environments, and language-informed, norm-compliant and metacognitive navigation.
- Autonomous driving research emphasizes safety and robustness, from safety-guided diffusion planners and latent prompt adaptation to robustness benchmarks under deployment perturbations.
- New datasets and benchmarks (e.g., CosFly-Track, WorldArena 2.0, 4DLidarOpen, ESI-Bench) broaden evaluation of motion planning, navigation, and embodied world models across sensing modalities and platforms.
