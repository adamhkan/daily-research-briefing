Executive Summary

  • Strong focus on safety-aware motion planning and navigation this cycle, including chance-constrained planning under unknown disturbances and predictive near-miss avoidance in dynamic scenes.
  • Multiple works advance dynamic and social navigation in challenging environments (maritime COLREGs, residential hallways, dark confined spaces, and collaborative mapping with heterogeneous robots).
  • Mobile manipulation and multi-robot cooperation see progress via decentralized role-based box transport and physics-guided property estimation from single pushes.
  • Manipulation-learning papers emphasize data collection and sim-to-real: VR teleoperation for dexterous demos, holistic synthetic-data pipelines, and keypoint-based imitation learning.
  • Control and RL contributions target safe, efficient whole-body behavior, from Lyapunov/Lagrangian tracking to thermally-aware quadruped locomotion and Koopman-based safety filters.
  • Fine-grained language conditioning and lightweight perception/vision models emerge as key enablers for steerable VLA policies and practical agricultural harvesting.

Provably Safe Motion Planning Under Unknown DisturbancesDelft University of Technology; University of Colorado Boulder; Virginia TechIntroduces a sampling-based motion planner that enforces chance-constrained safety for systems with unknown disturbance distributions. By learning Wasserstein ambiguity tubes from trajectory data and combining them with a bandit-based validity checker, the method achieves probabilistically complete, less conservative planning in cluttered environments.Link
TCBiRRT: Rapid Motion Planning for Tightly Coupled Dual-arm Space Manipulator Using Task-space Random ExpansionProposes TCBiRRT, a task-space constrained BiRRT for dual-arm space manipulators under closed-chain constraints. By sampling and expanding directly in object pose space and mapping to joint paths via path IK with regrasping, it substantially improves success rates and planning times over configuration-space planners in complex on-orbit assembly scenarios.Link
RCSP: Risk-Sensitive Conjectural Scenario Planning for Safe Dynamic Robot NavigationAddresses near-miss failures in dynamic environments with a risk-sensitive planning layer that samples short-horizon obstacle motion conjectures and penalizes high-risk tail outcomes. Integrated as a module atop standard navigation stacks, RCSP improves safety and path quality in bottleneck scenarios while maintaining compatibility with existing local planners.Link
Trust, Geometry, and Rules: A Credibility-Aware Reinforcement Learning Framework for Safe USV Navigation under UncertaintyCombines credibility-weighted value learning, covariance-inflated velocity obstacles, and continuous COLREGs embeddings to train USV navigation policies robust to miscalibrated perception. The framework dynamically downweights low-credibility samples, geometrically shields unsafe actions, and smooths rule-encoding to improve collision avoidance and rule compliance.Link
Look Further: Socially-Compliant Navigation System in Residential BuildingsShows that reacting to humans at distances beyond typical personal-space ranges improves perceived safety, smoothness, and politeness for delivery robots in hallways. Introduces a Proactive Lane-Changing pattern that laterally shifts the robot at about 8 meters from oncoming pedestrians, validated via a 42-participant user study.Link
NightSight: Passive Computation for Navigation in Dark Using EventsDemonstrates a lightweight perception pipeline for small aerial robots to navigate in complete darkness using an event camera, coded aperture, and IR dot projector. A CNN trained purely on synthetic data decodes depth-dependent blur patterns into dense depth maps in real time on an embedded GPU, enabling accurate short-range navigation.Link
Collaborative Navigation and Exploration with β-Sparse Gaussian ProcessesIntroduces β-Sparse GPs for task-aware inducing point selection in a collaborative navigation setting with a lead robot and a mobile sensor platform. The sensor jointly chooses what map points to transmit and where to move, yielding lower path cost and substantially reduced communication compared to raw-data sharing in simulated terrains.Link
Multi-Robot Box Transport over Different Surfaces with Decentralized Role-based Proportional ControlUniversity at BuffaloPresents R2P2, an asynchronous decentralized scheme for teams of robots to collaboratively push boxes with varying mass over flat and inclined surfaces. Robots assume roles such as push, support, and prevent and apply rule-based or proportional velocity control, outperforming virtual leader–follower baselines in simulation and physical TurtleBot experiments.Link
Learning to Balance Motor Thermal Safety and Quadrupedal Locomotion Performance with Residual PolicyPOSTECH; School of Mechanical Science and Engineering; University of Science and Technology of ChinaIntegrates a whole-body thermal model into quadruped locomotion RL via a two-stage training process: a nominal gait policy plus a residual policy conditioned on motor temperatures. On a Unitree A1, the method extends operation under payloads from about 5 to over 13 minutes while maintaining terrain traversal, preventing motor overheating.Link
L-Learning : A Lyapunov-Based Approach Leveraging Lagrangian Mechanics for Efficient and Stable Robot TrackingBeihang UniversityProposes L-Learning, a data-driven control framework that learns an energy-based Lyapunov function consistent with Lagrangian dynamics for trajectory tracking. By embedding stability constraints into the learning objective, it targets improved tracking accuracy and sample efficiency while guaranteeing closed-loop stability.Link
