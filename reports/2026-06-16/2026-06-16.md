## Executive Summary

- New control-theoretic and optimization tools push motion planning and MPC forward, from semidefinite relaxations for collision-free planning to robust MPC for deformable objects.
- Vision-Language-Action (VLA) research continues to mature, with multiple works on real-time execution, robustness, physics grounding, and fine-tuning stability for manipulation and driving.
- Navigation advances span floorplan-guided embodied navigation, reliability-aware and agentic UAV planners, multi-agent lifelong exploration, and BIM- and Gaussian-based mapping/localization.
- Dexterous and contact-rich manipulation receive significant infrastructure upgrades, including open-source hand platforms, exoskeleton and haptic interfaces, and synergy-inspired hand hardware.
- Safety and reliability are central themes across domains, with new frameworks for provably safe RL, safe highway driving RL, SOTIF-style safety envelopes, and auditing/repairing spurious policies.
- Teleoperation and data-collection pipelines evolve with richer haptics, egocentric guidance, and robot-free interfaces, directly supporting scalable learning for mobile and whole-body manipulation.
