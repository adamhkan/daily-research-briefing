Executive Summary

  • New control-theoretic and optimization tools push motion planning and MPC forward, from semidefinite relaxations for collision-free planning to robust MPC for deformable objects.
  • Vision-Language-Action (VLA) research continues to mature, with multiple works on real-time execution, robustness, physics grounding, and fine-tuning stability for manipulation and driving.
  • Navigation advances span floorplan-guided embodied navigation, reliability-aware and agentic UAV planners, multi-agent lifelong exploration, and BIM- and Gaussian-based mapping/localization.
  • Dexterous and contact-rich manipulation receive significant infrastructure upgrades, including open-source hand platforms, exoskeleton and haptic interfaces, and synergy-inspired hand hardware.
  • Safety and reliability are central themes across domains, with new frameworks for provably safe RL, safe highway driving RL, SOTIF-style safety envelopes, and auditing/repairing spurious policies.
  • Teleoperation and data-collection pipelines evolve with richer haptics, egocentric guidance, and robot-free interfaces, directly supporting scalable learning for mobile and whole-body manipulation.

Semidefinite Relaxations for Collision-Free Motion PlanningMassachusetts Institute of TechnologyFormulates point-robot motion planning with spherical obstacles as a polynomial-curve optimization and analyzes a semidefinite relaxation that is provably equivalent to a related higher-dimensional planning problem. The symmetry-reduced relaxation scales linearly with polynomial degree, is dimension-independent, and runs 10–100× faster than SNOPT/IPOPT while serving effectively as a convex steering function in RRT for high-order quadrotor trajectory planning.Link
The N2D Haptic Glove: A Multi-Finger Glove for 2D Directional Force Feedback for Contact Rich ManipulationUC San DiegoIntroduces a multi-finger haptic glove that renders planar fingertip forces via capstan transmissions, providing high-transparency, 2D directional feedback during manipulation. Bench tests and teleoperation user studies show reduced contact-force error, improved consistency, and better user experience versus visual-only and single-axis haptics, supporting more precise contact-rich teleoperation and learning from demonstration.Link

Whole-Body Impedance Model Predictive Control for Safe Physical Human--Robot Interaction on Floating-Base PlatformsPresents a three-level control architecture for floating-base robots that combines centroidal MPC, priority-based whole-body control, and a null-space impedance MPC with disturbance estimation. The approach guarantees zero steady-state error under bounded constant physical interaction loads and runs at ≥1 kHz via contact-consistent feedback linearization and offline QP precomputation, enabling safe, compliant pHRI during locomotion.Link
Impedance MPC with Disturbance Estimation for Dexterous Hand ControlDevelops an actuator-agnostic Impedance MPC framework that reduces tendon-driven fingers to constant-coefficient double integrators, enabling 500 Hz constrained MPC with encoder-only disturbance estimation. On a hydraulic finger and a 16-DOF simulated hand, it enforces contact and actuation limits while dramatically improving tracking accuracy and stiffness control over classical impedance in contact-rich tasks.Link
ORCA: A Platform for Open-Source Dexterity ResearchIntroduces the ORCA stack, an open-source ecosystem that unifies anthropomorphic hand hardware control, simulation, teleoperation, and retargeting behind a single interface tightly integrated with robot-learning frameworks like LeRobot. Demonstrations show end-to-end pipelines from VR-based teleoperation data collection to learned in-hand reorientation policies, lowering barriers for reproducible dexterous manipulation research.Link
Spatially Conditioned Diffusion Policy: Learning Precise and Robust Manipulation with a Single RGB CameraProposes SCDP, a diffusion-based visuomotor policy that uses multi-scale visual features and spatial conditioning along end-effector trajectories to focus attention in single-camera setups. The method matches or surpasses multi-camera baselines in simulation and shows precise, distraction-robust manipulation on real robots without wrist-mounted views.Link
ForestBack: Breadcrumb-Based Pedestrian Dead Reckoning for Infrastructure-Free Return NavigationIntroduces ForestBack, an infrastructure-free return-navigation system that records reversible breadcrumb nodes using IMU, magnetometer, and barometric data to reconstruct and invert walking paths. On indoor obstacle-avoidance routes it cuts RMSE and final-position error by ~16–22% over standard PDR while achieving near-perfect turn-event detection, providing a lightweight navigation aid in GPS-denied settings.Link
FloVerse: Floor Plan-Guided Multi-Modal NavigationDefines FloVerse, a unified task that extends floorplan-guided navigation to PointNav, ObjectNav, and ImageNav, and introduces a 1.6K-scene dataset with 240K trajectories. The ThreeDiff policy combines a planner, a multimodal diffusion goal reasoner, and a depth-based refiner, showing that floorplan priors significantly boost navigation performance and can be implicitly exploited via diffusion-based models.Link
BIM-Loc: BIM-Integrated Discrepancy-Aware LiDAR-based Indoor LocalizationPresents BIM-Loc, which localizes robots indoors by directly registering LiDAR scans to Building Information Models while jointly estimating discrepancies between as-designed and as-built structures. A multi-hit ray-casting data association, BIM-integrated pose-graph optimization, and hierarchical Bayesian discrepancy detection yield higher localization accuracy and robustness than existing map-based methods.Link
Universal Manipulation Exoskeleton: Learning Compliant Whole-body Policies with Real-time Torque FeedbackIntroduces UME, a low-cost upper-limb exoskeleton that records joint torques and poses while providing real-time haptic feedback for high-fidelity teleoperation across multiple robot arms. The system enables learning of bimanual and whole-body compliant policies that succeed in long-horizon mobile manipulation, force-mediated tasks, and highly constrained environments, including visually occluded scenarios.Link
Robustness without Wrinkles: Parallel Simulation and Robust MPC for Certified Deformable ManipulationCORD-SLS combines a GPU-parallel differentiable simulator with contact smoothing and an output-feedback robust MPC scheme to control ropes and cloth under uncertainty. Conformal prediction calibrates perception-error bounds, yielding safe reachable tubes; experiments in rope routing and cloth folding show millisecond-level planning, higher safety, and better task success than prior methods.Link
Guided Diffusion with Distilled Vision-Language Reliability for Aerial NavigationProposes a diffusion-based UAV trajectory planner that conditions on both observations and a reliability heatmap distilled from a vision-language model to flag untrustworthy regions like glass or glare. A differentiable ESDF-based guidance treats unreliable areas as virtual obstacles, cutting obstacle violations from ~40% to ~10% and increasing average reliability of traversed regions, while running up to 2× faster than using the full VLM online.Link
