Executive Summary

  • New control and planning methods advance safety and reliability in dexterous manipulation, bimanual skills, and high-uncertainty driving scenarios.
  • Multiple works strengthen navigation and coverage: from scout-guided heterogeneous teams and humanoid scene memory to ergodic multi-robot coverage and legged odometry.
  • Humanoid and whole-body interaction see progress in microgravity locomotion design and zero-shot humanoid–object interaction via generative video priors.
  • Vision-Language(-Action) systems gain robustness through spatial memory, gesture conditioning, latent scene evolution, and preemptive runtime verification.
  • Several papers tackle multi-robot coordination without reliable comms, VR-guided shared control of drone teams, and distributed multicoverage for swarms.
  • New evaluation and adaptation tools emerge for tactile sensing, contact-rich assembly, sim-to-real micromanipulation, and reward/metric learning for robust planning.

Safe and Steerable Geometric Motion Policies for Robotic Dexterous ManipulationStanford UniversityIntroduces SafePBDS, a pullback bundle dynamical system framework that maps task-space safety and objectives into certified-safe accelerations on a robot’s configuration manifold. It unifies geometric control, control barrier functions, and a residual action interface, enabling high-level policies to steer complex dexterous behaviors while preserving safety on a 23-DOF Panda–Allegro platform, including over-360° in-hand reorientation.Link
PGDG: Physically Grounded Data Generation for Robust Bimanual Policy Learning from a Single DemonstrationCarnegie Mellon UniversityProposes PGDG, a physics-grounded data engine that expands a single bimanual demonstration into a compact set of diverse, successful recovery trajectories without extra human labels. By iterating between a sampler, curator, and short-horizon corrective control, it significantly boosts robustness and zero-shot sim-to-real transfer for contact-rich dual-arm tasks and improves fine-tuning of foundation manipulation models.Link

Learning to Evolve: Multi-modal Interactive Fields for Robust Humanoid Navigation in Dynamic EnvironmentsPresents Multi-modal Interactive Fields (MIF), a humanoid-centric scene memory that fuses uncertainty-aware 3D Gaussian splatting, topological spatial memory, and safety-aware geometry reconstruction. On a Unitree-G1 in dynamic offices, it sharply improves relocation success and memory efficiency, enabling safer navigation for manipulation-capable humanoids amid locomotion-induced perceptual distortions.Link
AwareVLN: Reasoning with Self-awareness for Vision-Language NavigationIntroduces AwareVLN, a vision-language navigation framework that equips agents with explicit self-awareness of pose and task progress without explicit 3D mapping. A structural reasoning module and an automatic data engine enable end-to-end training that outperforms prior VLN methods in Habitat, improving grounded instruction-following and path selection.Link
Scout-Assisted Planning for Heterogeneous Robot Teams under Partially Known EnvironmentsProposes Scout-Assisted Planning where UAV scouts collect targeted information to reduce UGV backtracking in partially known maps. An information-gain-based action pruning strategy, approximated via a graph neural network for real-time use, cuts ground travel cost by over 30% versus Canadian Traveler baselines and outperforms proximity-guided scouting.Link
Branch-Stochastic Model Predictive Control for Motion Planning under Multi-Modal Uncertainty with Scenario ClusteringTechnical University of MunichCombines stochastic MPC with a branching structure to plan distinct ego trajectories for different possible intentions of surrounding vehicles. A scenario clustering scheme and adaptive branching time keep the problem tractable while reducing conservatism and improving safety in multi-modal highway scenarios.Link
Motion Design for Grasp-Based Dynamic Locomotion in MicrogravityUniversity of Illinois Urbana-ChampaignAnalyzes grasp-based multi-limbed locomotion in microgravity, focusing on gait pattern, stride length, speed, and posture for robots that pull themselves between sparse anchors. A parameterized planning framework and simulations with quadruped morphologies reveal how enlarging feasible contact wrench space and mitigating impulsive dynamics improve whole-body stability and actuation demands.Link
Higher Order Reasoning for Collaborative Communicationless Mobile Robot OperationsUniversity of VirginiaDevelops a dynamic epistemic planning framework where robots reason over higher-order beliefs about teammates to coordinate tasks without explicit communication. Coupled with an MPPI controller, this allows robots to anticipate others’ choices, plan intercepts, and adapt paths under partial observability, reducing task completion times compared to first-order baselines.Link
A Visitation Grid for Complete Coverage Foraging in Robot SwarmsIntroduces a lightweight, centrally maintained visitation grid that stores exploration counts and biases swarm robots toward under-visited regions when searching for sparse resources. The method significantly speeds late-stage collection and overall mission time relative to canonical CPFA baselines, improving coverage efficiency under tight onboard resource constraints.Link
Mind the Gaps: Multi-Robot Feedback-Driven Ergodic Coverage in Unknown EnvironmentsBrown UniversityExtends ergodic coverage by using an online-updated parametric environmental model to shape the target information distribution for multiple robots. This feedback-driven approach shifts sampling toward high-interest regions in unknown or slowly varying environments, improving coverage quality and resource allocation in simulation.Link
Distributed Multi-Coverage for Robot SwarmsAddresses the problem of ensuring that critical assets are simultaneously observed by multiple robots using only local sensing and communication. Proposes a fully distributed multicoverage algorithm suitable for drone swarms that must remain resilient to failures and limited global coordination while maintaining redundancy-aware coverage.Link
Flying Together: Human-Guided Immersive Shared Control for Aerial Robot Teams in Unknown EnvironmentsPresents a VR-based shared-control system in which a human operator guides a team of drones through migration points while a motion-primitive planner and admittance controller ensure collision-free trajectories. Experiments with mixed-reality drones show improved obstacle avoidance, inter-agent spacing, and reduced operator workload compared to purely autonomous or manually piloted strategies.Link
