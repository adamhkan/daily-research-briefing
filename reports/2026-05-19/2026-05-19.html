Executive Summary

  • Navigation and motion planning see multiple advances: sim-to-real RL navigation pipelines, geometry-driven local planners, MPC for ultra-narrow passages, and safe CBF-based navigation in constrained dynamic environments.
  • Dexterous and fine manipulation are targeted via new kinematic metrics for in-hand dexterity, a rich MuJoCo benchmark (DexJoCo), bimanual rope manipulation, and design optimization of robotic hands.
  • Visuomotor and VLA policy learning continues to accelerate, with several works on faster trajectory generation (FLASH, FocalPolicy, SkiP), feedback-enhanced world models, and compute-efficient VLA RL (PCM).
  • Mobile manipulation and whole-body control progress with dynamic quadruped pick-and-place, dual-arm + base door traversal via diffusion policy, humanoid navigation policies, and large-scale humanoid motion tracking (HoloMotion-1).
  • New tools and representations appear for long-horizon manipulation and planning: symbolic–low-level bilevel policies (BISON), functional 3D scene graphs, and task-conditioned tabletop scene/layout generation.
  • Multi-agent and aerial systems are addressed with practical AGV pathfinding adaptations, wind-aware glider trajectory planning, underwater AUV MARL tracking, and optimized drone inspection routing.

NavRL++: A System-Level Framework for Improving Sim-to-Real Transfer in Reinforcement Learning-Based Robot NavigationCarnegie Mellon UniversityPresents an end-to-end RL navigation framework with a detailed analysis of sim-to-real gaps such as sensor noise, latency, and perception failures. Introduces perturbation-aware fine-tuning and a Transformer-based temporal policy that achieve robust zero-shot transfer on aerial and legged platforms, matching optimization-based planners in static settings.Link
KaRMA: A Kinematic Metric for Fine Manipulation Ability in Robotic HandsMassachusetts Institute of TechnologyDefines KaRMA, a kinematic-only metric that quantifies in-hand rolling manipulation of a spherical object under joint limits, collisions, and force feasibility. Evaluations on 16 robotic hands show it distinguishes designs that static metrics rank identically and exposes translation–rotation trade-offs relevant to dexterous manipulation.Link

Field experiments on a quadruped in cluttered, dynamic underground settings show robust, minimally invasive safety enforcement.Link
