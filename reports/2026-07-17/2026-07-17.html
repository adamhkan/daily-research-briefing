Executive Summary

  • Strong push on high-speed, reliable motion generation: SIMD-accelerated multi-arm planning (VAMP-MR) and robust LiDAR/SLAM/localization pipelines for demanding environments.
  • Mobile and open-world manipulation see new data and agentic frameworks, from synthetic “one-demo” engines (WANDA) to fully embodied, interactive systems (REAL).
  • Navigation work spans language-driven goal setting, zero-shot object-goal exploration, risk-aware mapping, and unified decision-making/trajectory planning for complex traffic and UAV scenarios.
  • Manipulation research emphasizes data efficiency and robustness: reversed easy-task supervision, one-shot constrained skills, deformable-object models, and robust execution/representation learning for VLA policies.
  • Safety, uncertainty, and human interaction are recurrent themes: risk-layered cost maps, barrier-function IRL, expressive manipulator motions for communicating uncertainty, and scene-graph/memory systems to combat perceptual saturation.
  • Benchmarks and surveys (industrial dexterity benchmark, UAV coverage review, electrostatic haptics survey) signal maturing subfields and clearer problem definitions in dexterous manipulation, coverage planning, and haptic actuation.

VAMP-MR: Vector-Accelerated Motion Planning and Execution for Multi-Robot-ArmsCarnegie Mellon UniversityIntroduces a family of multi-robot-arm motion planners that exploit CPU SIMD vectorization for collision checking, yielding up to two orders of magnitude speedup in planning and postprocessing. The approach enables near real-time generation of collision-free trajectories for industrial multi-arm manipulation and is released as an open implementation for broader use.Link
Power from Potential: A Survey of Electrostatic Actuators for HapticsStanford UniversitySurveys high-voltage electrostatic actuators for haptic interfaces, covering switchable adhesives, dielectric elastomers, soft electrohydraulic devices, and electrokinetic pumps. The paper compares bandwidth, force density, scalability, and integration constraints, and outlines open challenges for translating these soft, low-power technologies into practical haptic systems.Link

Worlds in One Demo: A Synthetic Data Engine for Learning Open-World Mobile ManipulationWANDA reconstructs scenes and robot-object interactions from a single real RGB-D demonstration, then synthesizes large numbers of new mobile manipulation trajectories via whole-body motion planning and environment randomization. Policies trained on this synthetic data show strong long-horizon robustness and cross-scene and cross-embodiment generalization, substantially reducing real data needs for open-world mobile manipulation.Link
Exploratory, Communicative, and Deployable: Vision-Driven Embodied Agents for Open-World Mobile ManipulationREAL defines a sim-to-real-consistent framework for mobile manipulators that must explore, ground language/vision, and interact with users to disambiguate intent. The REAL-Bench suite and hierarchical training pipeline yield an agent that outperforms strong VLM baselines in interactive tasks and transfers zero-shot to a dual-arm mobile robot with high success on long-horizon household tasks.Link
From Language to Navigation Goals: A Vision-Language Approach for Semantic Navigation of Mobile Robots Using RGB-D PerceptionPresents a ROS 2-based pipeline that converts natural language commands (e.g., “go to the mail box”) into navigation goals by detecting and localizing referenced objects with RGB-D sensors. The system interfaces with Nav2, supports multiple robot platforms, and demonstrates successful semantic navigation in both simulated and real environments.Link
Joint On-and-Off Policy Learning for Vision-and-Language NavigationJOP-VLN unifies imitation learning, DAgger-based data aggregation, and on-policy reinforcement learning in a three-stage pipeline for vision-language navigation. By combining off-policy error-recovery trajectories with on-policy exploration, it achieves state-of-the-art success rates on VLN-CE R2R and strong performance on RxR benchmarks.Link
HRO: Hierarchical Room-to-Object Framework for Zero-Shot Object Goal Navigation with Large Language ModelsInternational Research Laboratory of Silk Road Multilingual Cognitive Computing, Xinjiang University.; Research Laboratory for Embodied Intelligence, Xinjiang University.; University of Science and Technology of ChinaProposes a room-to-object hierarchical navigation strategy where LLM priors first guide exploration at the room level, then refine search to specific objects. This coarse-to-fine scheme improves sample efficiency and success in zero-shot object-goal navigation on Gibson and HM3D without object-specific training.Link
Layered Risk Mapping for Autonomous Patient Transport in Expeditionary Medical FacilitiesNortheastern UniversityIntroduces a risk-aware mapping framework for autonomous wheelchairs that fuses slope, static/dynamic obstacles, and semantic traversability into a probabilistic cost map using a Noisy-OR model. Monte Carlo evaluations and real wheelchair deployments show large reductions in collision rates and improved clearances relative to risk-unaware planners in cluttered medical environments.Link
Min-Max Regret Task Allocation and Planning of Heterogeneous Multi-Robot System in Partially Known EnvironmentsFormulates heterogeneous multi-robot task allocation under temporal-logic constraints in partially known environments as a min-max regret problem. The proposed Extended Planning Decision Tree with regret-based branch-and-bound efficiently balances exploration and exploitation, scaling near-linearly in robot count and outperforming MILP baselines in both quality and runtime.Link
An Empirical Study on Stage-Information Interfaces for VLA Fine-TuningAnalyzes how to feed stage information into vision-language-action policies for long-horizon manipulation using segmented annotations and progress tracking. Results on LIBERO-10 show that a compact ordinal stage index in the robot state can outperform text-based stage cues under continuation fine-tuning, but explicit staging is not universally beneficial.Link
Generalizable VLA Finetuning via Representation Anchoring and Language-Action AlignmentAnchor-Align augments behavior cloning for VLA policies with two auxiliary objectives: anchoring intermediate visual-language representations to a frozen VLM and aligning language and discrete motion-direction targets on the same observations. On physical robots and large-scale simulators, this substantially boosts in-distribution and OOD manipulation success without sacrificing pretrained generalization.Link
Industrial Dexterity Benchmark: A Hardware-Software Benchmarking Platform for Industrial Dexterous ManipulationIntroduces the Industrial Dexterity Benchmark boards targeting cable routing, harness manipulation, and precision assembly, along with DAG-ROS and a multimodal diffusion policy framework (AG-iDP3). Multiview, multimodal diffusion policies trained from ~100 demos per phase significantly outperform classical pipelines and single-camera baselines on a representative cable-cleaning task.Link
