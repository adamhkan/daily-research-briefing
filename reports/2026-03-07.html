Executive Summary

  • Dexterous and long-horizon manipulation see multiple advances: sim-to-real tactile skill distillation, massive bimanual grasp datasets, and improved diffusion / memory-based visuomotor policies.
  • Motion planning is strongly represented, from GPU-accelerated dynamics-aware stacks and Riemannian dynamical systems to robust joint-space RRT*, informed sampling, and uncertainty-aware collision avoidance.
  • Mobile and mobile manipulation safety are tackled via risk-aware RL, evidential waypoint prediction, and contact-tolerant navigation that leverages vision-language reasoning.
  • Whole-body humanoid control progresses with physics-aware VLA frameworks and omnidirectional LiDAR-driven policies for large-workspace manipulation.
  • Neuro-symbolic and VLA-based manipulation architectures increasingly integrate memory, interpretability, and human feedback, while new benchmarks probe memory and robustness.
  • Digital-twin-based pipelines emerge for both medical (cochlear implantation) and industrial (textile sorting) manipulation, tightening the loop between perception, simulation, and planning.

PTLD: Sim-to-real Privileged Tactile Latent Distillation for Dexterous ManipulationCarnegie Mellon University; UC BerkeleyIntroduces PTLD, a sim-to-real framework that sidesteps tactile simulation by collecting real-world privileged tactile data and distilling it into a robust tactile state estimator. Augmenting proprioceptive policies with this estimator yields large performance gains on in-hand rotation and reorientation, demonstrating that learned tactile latents significantly enhance dexterous control.Link
Observing and Controlling Features in Vision-Language-Action ModelsStanford UniversityAnalyzes internal representations in VLAs via feature observability and controllability, using linear classifiers and optimal-control-based interventions on hidden features. Demonstrates that lightweight, online adjustments to internal activations can steer VLA-driven robot behavior in real time without full fine-tuning.Link

cuRoboV2: Dynamics-Aware Motion Generation with Depth-Fused Distance Fields for High-DoF RobotsPresents a unified GPU-native motion generation stack combining B-spline trajectory optimization with torque limits, fast TSDF/ESDF distance fields, and scalable whole-body kinematics/dynamics for manipulators and humanoids. Achieves near-perfect success and collision-free rates on high-DoF systems while greatly accelerating computation, enabling dynamics-aware planning across complex workspaces.Link
Risk-Aware Reinforcement Learning for Mobile ManipulationProposes a distributional RL framework to learn teacher policies with tunable risk sensitivity, then distills them into depth-based visuomotor policies for mobile manipulators. The resulting whole-body controllers adjust risk online and show improved worst-case performance in unmapped, dynamic environments.Link
UltraDexGrasp: Learning Universal Dexterous Grasping for Bimanual Robots with Synthetic DataIntroduces UltraDexGrasp, a pipeline that synthesizes 20M-frame trajectories for physically plausible, multi-strategy bimanual grasps across 1,000 objects. A point-cloud-based policy trained purely on this synthetic dataset achieves robust zero-shot sim-to-real dexterous grasping with over 80% success on diverse real objects.Link
Many-RRT*: Robust Joint-Space Trajectory Planning for Serial ManipulatorsExtends RRT*-Connect to plan simultaneously toward multiple IK solutions, treating each as a parallel goal tree in joint space. This multi-goal strategy avoids committing to poor IK branches, delivering markedly higher success rates and lower-cost trajectories for high-DoF manipulators without added runtime.Link
GAIDE: Graph-based Attention Masking for Spatial- and Embodiment-aware Motion PlanningProposes GAIDE, a neural informed sampler that encodes the spatial structure of the workspace and the robot’s embodiment as a graph, integrated into a transformer via attention masking. When plugged into sampling-based planners, it reduces sample inefficiency and improves success and planning speed in high-dimensional manipulation tasks.Link
Direct Contact-Tolerant Motion Planning With Vision Language ModelsDevelops a contact-tolerant planner that uses a VLM to segment images into contact-permissible vs. forbidden regions, then projects this into contact-aware point clouds for control. The resulting perception-to-control DNN enables efficient navigation through clutter, explicitly reasoning about movable objects and outperforming CTMP baselines.Link
Curve-Induced Dynamical Systems on Riemannian Manifolds and Lie GroupsIntroduces CDSM, a framework to construct dynamical systems directly on manifolds such as SE(3) and SPD(n) by generating motion along and toward a nominal curve. Provides stability guarantees and shows improved accuracy and responsiveness for trajectory generation on manipulators and mobile manipulators compared to existing manifold-based methods.Link
U-OBCA: Uncertainty-Aware Optimization-Based Collision Avoidance via Wasserstein Distributionally Robust Chance ConstraintsExtends optimization-based collision avoidance to handle uncertainty via distributionally robust chance constraints defined over Wasserstein balls. Directly models polygonal robots and obstacles without conservative shape simplifications, yielding less conservative and more efficient navigation in narrow, cluttered spaces.Link
Omni-Manip: Beyond-FOV Large-Workspace Humanoid Manipulation with Omnidirectional 3D PerceptionPresents an end-to-end LiDAR-based visuomotor policy for humanoids that consumes panoramic point clouds via a time-aware attention pooling mechanism. This 360° perception supports robust whole-body manipulation over large workspaces without frequent base motion, outperforming egocentric RGB-D baselines in cluttered real-world scenes.Link
PhysiFlow: Physics-Aware Humanoid Whole-Body VLA via Multi-Brain Latent Flow Matching and Robust TrackingIntroduces a multi-brain VLA framework that fuses semantic motion intent with physics-aware whole-body control for humanoids. Through latent flow matching and robust tracking, the system executes language-guided tasks with coordinated full-body motions, improving stability in dynamic, limb-coordinated behaviors.Link
