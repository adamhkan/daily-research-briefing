Executive Summary

  • Humanoid whole-body control and loco-manipulation continue to advance, with new multi-modal controllers and large-scale virtual data pipelines enabling high sim-to-real success on Unitree G1.
  • Navigation and motion planning under uncertainty are a major focus, spanning agile quadrotor world models, unified visual navigation with latent imagination, and long-horizon online POMDP solvers.
  • Risk-aware and safety-first frameworks emerge across MPC, multi-agent exploration, and safe RL, emphasizing distribution-free guarantees and structured handling of multi-objective uncertainty.
  • New benchmarks and datasets target challenging domains such as underground exploration, deformable object and DLO manipulation, and visuotactile, language-conditioned control.
  • Semantic and world-model-based representations (occupancy grids, semantic SLAM, action-conditioned video models) are increasingly used to close the loop between perception, planning, and policy evaluation.
  • LLM- and VLA-centric tools are being systematically upgraded via better data curation, 3D and focus priors, and simulation-based verification to improve robustness and physical feasibility in manipulation and navigation.

GRAIL: Generating Humanoid Loco-Manipulation from 3D Assets and Video PriorsGRAIL is a fully virtual pipeline that composes 3D assets, simulator scenes, and video foundation model priors to synthesize metric 4D human–object interaction trajectories for humanoid loco-manipulation. These motions are retargeted to a Unitree G1 humanoid to train egocentric visual loco-manipulation policies, achieving high sim-to-real success on diverse object pick-up and stair-climbing without teleoperation data.Link
M3imic: Learning a Versatile Whole-Body Controller for Multimodal Motion MimickingMohamed bin Zayed University of Artificial Intelligence; Tsinghua UniversityM3imic learns a single whole-body control policy for humanoids that can track heterogeneous motion references, including joint trajectories, human poses, and end-effector paths, via modality-specific encoders into a shared latent space. Trained with large-scale RL in simulation and deployed on a Unitree G1, it achieves strong sim-to-real performance and high success rates on unseen motion references for locomotion and loco-manipulation.Link
CoRe-MoE: Contrastive Reweighted Mixture of Experts for Multi-Terrain Humanoid Locomotion with Gait AdaptationCoRe-MoE introduces a two-stage RL framework where a base gait policy for walking/running is augmented with a terrain-aware Mixture-of-Experts trained via contrastive objectives. The resulting controller enables a Unitree G1 to perform robust, natural gait transitions and adapt to stairs, slopes, obstacles, and unstructured terrains in zero-shot deployment.Link
Generalization of World Models under Environmental Variability for Vision-based Quadrotor NavigationThis work systematically studies how DreamerV3-style world models trained for quadrotor navigation generalize under varying environmental randomness. Cross-environment SSL validation is shown to predict sim-to-real robustness better than RL policy scores, and key architectural factors like discrete latent size and training sequence length strongly influence real-world navigation success, including fully open-loop imagined flight.Link
MAD: Mapping-Aware World Models for Agile Quadrotor FlightHarbin Institute of TechnologyMAD augments Dreamer-style world models to reconstruct local occupancy and visibility maps, forcing latent states to encode geometry and history relevant for collision avoidance. Policies trained on these mapping-aware latents achieve faster, safer quadrotor flight in cluttered navigation and racing tasks, with successful sim-to-real transfer up to 5 m/s in forested environments.Link
WAM-Nav: Asymmetric Latent World-Action Modeling for Unified Visual NavigationWAM-Nav jointly learns long-horizon action sequences and short-horizon visual foresight with a shared diffusion transformer, enabling anticipatory yet efficient visual navigation. A dual-stream conditioning on ego-motion history and observations plus unified goal encoding allows a single policy to handle Image-Goal, Point-Goal, and exploration tasks, with strong generalization and 85% average success in real-world deployments.Link
MineXplore: An Open-Source Reinforcement Learning Exploration Benchmark for GNSS-Denied Underground EnvironmentMineXplore provides a MuJoCo-based exploration benchmark derived from a real Chilean copper mine survey, modeling a large tunnel network with realistic geometry, friction zones, incline, and lighting. PPO baselines demonstrate stable, reproducible learning for coverage-focused navigation, enabling controlled RL research on underground, GPS-denied exploration.Link
Think Fast and Far: Long-Horizon Online POMDP Planning via Rapid State SamplingAustralian National University; Purdue University; Rice UniversityROP-RAS3 is an approximate online POMDP solver that uses extremely fast sampling-based motion planning to generate diverse macro-actions, biasing belief-space search without enumerating large action spaces. It scales to problems with thousands of lookahead steps and high-dimensional continuous or hybrid state spaces, outperforming existing solvers on long-horizon planning tasks and demonstrating hardware feasibility.Link
Distribution-Free Risk-Aware Planning and Control Under Uncertainty Using Conformal Spectral Risk ControlUniversity of MichiganThis work extends conformal risk control to general spectral risk measures and integrates the resulting prediction sets into a risk-aware MPC framework. The approach provides distribution-free safety guarantees for navigation under uncertainty and yields safer, more computationally efficient obstacle-avoidance behavior than baseline RA-MPC in vehicle simulations.Link
Multi-Agent Next-Best-View Optimization for Risk-Averse PlanningAmazon Robotics; Lehigh UniversityThe authors propose a distributed multi-robot next-best-view scheme where each agent maintains a private 3D Gaussian Splatting map and collaboratively maximizes information gain via consensus ADMM. Collision risk is modeled through AV@R and used to both shape exploration and score trajectories, achieving mapping and safety comparable to centralized methods with drastically reduced communication.Link
