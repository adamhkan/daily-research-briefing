Executive Summary

  • ETH Zurich proposes a sampling-based safe RL algorithm with provable safety and efficiency guarantees, directly applicable to safety-critical robot control and motion planning.
  • NYU and UC Berkeley introduce COBALT, a scalable cloud-based teleoperation platform that crowdsources high-quality manipulation data using everyday devices, enabling rapid dataset growth for robot learning.
  • Multiple navigation works push beyond planar or single-floor assumptions, including cross-floor 3D exploration, semantic object-goal navigation, and depth-aware fine-tuning of navigation foundation models.
  • New frameworks for zero-shot and semantic navigation (TravExplorer, CLUE, MCNav, Dual-Heatmap grounding) combine language, vision, and traversability reasoning for robust goal-directed exploration without prior maps.
  • Advances in SLAM and odometry (PRISM-SLAM, minimalist VIO, ground-texture multi-session SLAM) focus on metric scale consistency, reduced sensing, and robustness in low-dynamic environments.
  • Whole-body and aerial control advances span compliant loco-manipulation for humanoids, neuromorphic and RL-based quadrotor control, and learning-accelerated multi-robot trajectory planning.

COBALT: Crowdsourcing Robot Learning via Cloud-Based Teleoperation with SmartphonesNew York University; UC BerkeleyCOBALT is a cloud-based teleoperation stack that supports dozens to hundreds of simultaneous operators controlling simulated or real robots over the internet, using commodity devices such as smartphones or VR headsets. The platform achieves sub-100 ms latency for multiple concurrent users per GPU, logs rich quality metrics for automatic filtering of poor demonstrations, and enables scalable collection of high-quality manipulation datasets, as demonstrated by a 7,500-trajectory crowdsourced pilot.Link
Sampling-Based Safe Reinforcement LearningETH ZurichThis work introduces Sampling-Based Safe Reinforcement Learning (SBSRL), a model-based algorithm that enforces safety by optimizing over a finite set of sampled dynamics models, approximating worst-case behavior under uncertainty. It provides high-probability safety guarantees and finite-time sample complexity bounds, and empirically achieves safe, efficient exploration in both simulation and real robot hardware, scaling via deep ensemble implementations.Link
Hamilton--Jacobi Reachability for Spacecraft Collision AvoidanceUC BerkeleyThe paper applies Hamilton–Jacobi reachability to two-satellite collision avoidance in a shared circular orbit using Hill–Clohessy–Wiltshire dynamics. By computing backward reachable sets for unsafe relative states and integrating them with supervisory control logic, it identifies when evasive maneuvers are necessary, yielding mathematically grounded safety guarantees for orbital separation.Link

CEER: Compliant End-Effector and Root Control as a Unified Interface for Hierarchical Humanoid Loco-ManipulationDuke UniversityCEER proposes an end-effector–root task-space abstraction that unifies whole-body compliant control and high-level planning for humanoid loco-manipulation. A teacher–student scheme distills a general motion-tracking controller into a low-level policy driven only by EE-root commands, enabling modular integration of heterogeneous planners and stable contact-rich manipulation in simulation and on hardware.Link
TravExplorer: Cross-Floor Embodied Exploration via Traversability-Aware 3-D PlanningShanghai Jiao Tong UniversityTravExplorer tackles zero-shot object navigation across multiple floors by maintaining a unified volumetric map that separates support surfaces from obstacles and explicitly models stairs and landings. A hierarchical planner performs target-aware frontier touring with 3D traversability-aware motion generation, achieving strong performance in large-scale simulation and on a Unitree Go2 without prior maps.Link
D-CLING: Prior-Preserving Depth-Conditioned Fine-Tuning for Navigation Foundation ModelsFrontier Research Center, Toyota Motor CorporationD-CLING augments navigation foundation models with a depth-conditioned, zero-initialized residual branch that learns geometric cues while preserving pre-trained priors. This ControlNet-inspired design improves long-horizon goal-reaching and obstacle avoidance in real-world navigation, maintaining or enhancing generalization beyond the fine-tuning dataset.Link
CLUE: Adaptively Prioritized Contextual Cues by Leveraging a Unified Semantic Map for Effective Zero-Shot Object-Goal NavigationKorea Advanced Institute of Science and TechnologyCLUE addresses zero-shot object-goal navigation by adaptively weighting room-type versus object co-occurrence cues using commonsense knowledge from an offline LLM. It builds a unified semantic value map that fuses these context signals to guide exploration, achieving higher success and SPL than prior ZSON baselines in both simulation and real-world runs.Link
MCNav: Memory-Aware Dynamic Cognitive Map for Zero-shot Goal-oriented NavigationMCNav introduces a dynamic cognitive map that stores queryable object information from explored regions, enabling goal re-validation and missed-goal re-exploration in zero-shot navigation. With blacklist and double-check mechanisms to stabilize revisits, it significantly improves instance-level target navigation performance on HM3D benchmarks.Link
Beyond Waypoints: Dual-Heatmap Grounding for Cross-Embodiment Semantic NavigationThis work replaces single-point waypoint regression with a dual-heatmap representation: one heatmap for reachable regions and another for desired facing direction. The dense affordance output integrates as a differentiable potential field for local planners and generalizes across robot embodiments, markedly improving affordance rates over standard waypoint prediction.Link
PRISM-SLAM: Probabilistic Ray-Grounded Inference for Scale-aware Metric SLAMPRISM-SLAM fuses monocular RGB with depth priors from vision foundation models in a Bayesian factor graph, using a novel Plücker Ray-Distance factor to make metric scale identifiable. A dynamic scene uncertainty gating mechanism down-weights unreliable depth in dynamic regions, enabling real-time, scale-consistent SLAM at 30 FPS with metric ATE matching oracle-aligned performance.Link
Minimalist Visual Inertial OdometryColumbia University; University of PaduaThis paper demonstrates planar odometry for differential-drive robots using just four downward-facing photodiodes with Gabor masks plus an IMU. A TCN trained in simulation decodes speed from the minimal visual signals, producing accurate, continuous trajectories across diverse terrains without real-world fine-tuning, highlighting an ultra-lightweight alternative to camera-based VIO.Link
KIO-planner: Attention-Guided Single-Stage Motion Planning with Dual Mapping for UAV NavigationKIO-planner is a single-stage trajectory planner for UAVs in dense, confined environments that integrates CBAM-based attention for better geometric feature extraction from depth and a Dual Mapping mechanism enforcing kinodynamic and collision constraints directly in pixel space. It achieves lower latency, smoother trajectories, and larger safety margins compared to optimization-based and end-to-end baselines.Link
Graph Neural Planning and Predictive Control for Multi-Robot Communication-Constrained Unlabeled Motion PlanningThe authors propose a hierarchical scheme combining a Graph Attention Planner (GATP) with decentralized nonlinear MPC for unlabeled multi-robot motion planning. GATP coordinates subgoal assignment under limited communication, while NMPC ensures dynamic feasibility and safety, showing good scalability, robustness to delays, and successful quadrotor deployments.Link
