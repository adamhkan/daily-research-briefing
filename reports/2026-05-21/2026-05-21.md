## Executive Summary

- ETH Zurich proposes a sampling-based safe RL algorithm with provable safety and efficiency guarantees, directly applicable to safety-critical robot control and motion planning.
- NYU and UC Berkeley introduce COBALT, a scalable cloud-based teleoperation platform that crowdsources high-quality manipulation data using everyday devices, enabling rapid dataset growth for robot learning.
- Multiple navigation works push beyond planar or single-floor assumptions, including cross-floor 3D exploration, semantic object-goal navigation, and depth-aware fine-tuning of navigation foundation models.
- New frameworks for zero-shot and semantic navigation (TravExplorer, CLUE, MCNav, Dual-Heatmap grounding) combine language, vision, and traversability reasoning for robust goal-directed exploration without prior maps.
- Advances in SLAM and odometry (PRISM-SLAM, minimalist VIO, ground-texture multi-session SLAM) focus on metric scale consistency, reduced sensing, and robustness in low-dynamic environments.
- Whole-body and aerial control advances span compliant loco-manipulation for humanoids, neuromorphic and RL-based quadrotor control, and learning-accelerated multi-robot trajectory planning.

| COBALT: Crowdsourcing Robot Learning via Cloud-Based Teleoperation with Smartphones | New York University; UC Berkeley | COBALT is a cloud-based teleoperation stack that supports dozens to hundreds of simultaneous operators controlling simulated or real robots over the internet, using commodity devices such as smartphones or VR headsets. The platform achieves sub-100 ms latency for multiple concurrent users per GPU, logs rich quality metrics for automatic filtering of poor demonstrations, and enables scalable collection of high-quality manipulation datasets, as demonstrated by a 7,500-trajectory crowdsourced pilot. | https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.19138 |
| Sampling-Based Safe Reinforcement Learning | ETH Zurich | This work introduces Sampling-Based Safe Reinforcement Learning (SBSRL), a model-based algorithm that enforces safety by optimizing over a finite set of sampled dynamics models, approximating worst-case behavior under uncertainty. It provides high-probability safety guarantees and finite-time sample complexity bounds, and empirically achieves safe, efficient exploration in both simulation and real robot hardware, scaling via deep ensemble implementations. | https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.19469 |
| Hamilton--Jacobi Reachability for Spacecraft Collision Avoidance | UC Berkeley | The paper applies Hamilton–Jacobi reachability to two-satellite collision avoidance in a shared circular orbit using Hill–Clohessy–Wiltshire dynamics. By computing backward reachable sets for unsafe relative states and integrating them with supervisory control logic, it identifies when evasive maneuvers are necessary, yielding mathematically grounded safety guarantees for orbital separation. | https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.20138 |
