## Executive Summary

- Vision-language-action and world models for manipulation continue to mature, with new frameworks targeting mobile manipulation (SG-VLA), physics-consistent video world models (ABot-PhysWorld), and tactile-augmented policies (VTAM).
- Equivariant and diffusion-based control policies are being pushed toward higher efficiency and robustness for manipulation (E3Flow, DiSCo), including edge-deployable VLA variants for industrial pose rectification (Agile-VLA).
- New dexterous and soft-manipulation hardware and models span underactuated hands (PHANTOM Hand), continuum and soft manipulators (caSSM, DER-based trajectory generation), and aerial continuum manipulators with coupled dynamics and visual servoing.
- Hybrid and hierarchical control architectures appear across domains: TO+RL for free-flying multi-arm space robots, decomposition of grinding into geometric planning plus local force adaptation, and multi-agent RL for collaborative carrying with coupled quadrupeds.
- Safety, benchmarking, and shared autonomy are emphasized via stoppability monitors for humanoids, CaP-X for code-as-policy manipulation agents, and diffusion-based copilots that correct user inputs in high-DOF tasks.
- Institution-aligned work features ETH Zurich on coupled quadruped carrying and continuum control, Stanford co-authoring actuator-aware SSMs, and CMU advancing active mobile perception in agriculture and large-scale forest/invasive-species mapping.
