Executive Summary

  • Vision-language-action and world models for manipulation continue to mature, with new frameworks targeting mobile manipulation (SG-VLA), physics-consistent video world models (ABot-PhysWorld), and tactile-augmented policies (VTAM).
  • Equivariant and diffusion-based control policies are being pushed toward higher efficiency and robustness for manipulation (E3Flow, DiSCo), including edge-deployable VLA variants for industrial pose rectification (Agile-VLA).
  • New dexterous and soft-manipulation hardware and models span underactuated hands (PHANTOM Hand), continuum and soft manipulators (caSSM, DER-based trajectory generation), and aerial continuum manipulators with coupled dynamics and visual servoing.
  • Hybrid and hierarchical control architectures appear across domains: TO+RL for free-flying multi-arm space robots, decomposition of grinding into geometric planning plus local force adaptation, and multi-agent RL for collaborative carrying with coupled quadrupeds.
  • Safety, benchmarking, and shared autonomy are emphasized via stoppability monitors for humanoids, CaP-X for code-as-policy manipulation agents, and diffusion-based copilots that correct user inputs in high-DOF tasks.
  • Institution-aligned work features ETH Zurich on coupled quadruped carrying and continuum control, Stanford co-authoring actuator-aware SSMs, and CMU advancing active mobile perception in agriculture and large-scale forest/invasive-species mapping.

SG-VLA: Learning Spatially-Grounded Vision-Language-Action Models for Mobile ManipulationSG-VLA targets household mobile manipulation with a 13D action space for base, arm, and gripper by enriching inputs (multi-view RGB-D and short histories) and co-training auxiliary decoders for poses, grasps, and segmentation. The auxiliary spatial tasks yield strongly grounded latent representations that significantly improve performance on pick-place and door-like operations over direct imitation learning.Link
Efficient Hybrid SE(3)-Equivariant Visuomotor Flow Policy via Spherical Harmonics for Robot ManipulationE3Flow is a rectified-flow-based visuomotor policy that enforces SO(3) equivariance via spherical harmonics and fuses RGB and point clouds through an invariant feature enhancement module. Across 8 MimicGen tasks and real robots, it modestly surpasses Spherical Diffusion Policy in success rate while achieving a 7× inference speedup, improving the performance–efficiency trade-off in equivariant manipulation policies.Link
PHANTOM HandThe PHANTOM hand is a 6-actuator, 15-DoF tendon-driven, underactuated hand that couples analytic kinematic modeling with compliant grasping. A sparse geometry-based mapping and mechanics-based compensation suppress kinematic drift, enabling sub-degree repeatability for free-motion shaping while preserving robust, predictable force output across diverse grasps.Link
Grounding Sim-to-Real Generalization in Dexterous Manipulation: An Empirical Study with Vision-Language-Action ModelsThrough 10k+ real trials, the authors systematically evaluate how domain randomization, photorealistic rendering, accurate physics, and RL updates affect sim-to-real transfer of VLA-based dexterous manipulation policies. Agile-VLA: Few-Shot Industrial Pose Rectification via Implicit Affordance AnchoringAgile-VLA decouples slow semantic inference from high-rate control on edge devices via implicit affordance anchoring that maps geometric cues to parametric action primitives. An asynchronous dual-stream design (10 Hz perception, 50 Hz control) enables robust few-shot (5 demos) pose rectification of irregular industrial workpieces on a 6-DoF arm running on Jetson-class hardware.Link
CaP-X: A Framework for Benchmarking and Improving Coding Agents for Robot ManipulationCaP-X introduces CaP-Gym and CaP-Bench to study code-as-policy agents that control robots by writing executable programs over perception and control primitives. Evaluations of 12 LLM/VLMs show dependence on human-designed abstractions, but also that richer agentic strategies (multi-turn feedback, visual differencing, automatic skill synthesis) can recover near-human reliability and enable RL-based sim-to-real transfer.Link
VTAM: Video-Tactile-Action Models for Complex Physical Interaction Beyond VLAsVTAM augments video-based action models with tactile streams through lightweight cross-modal finetuning and a tactile-regularized attention objective. On contact-rich manipulation, including fragile potato-chip pick-and-place, it sustains ~90% success and exceeds a pi 0.5 baseline by 80% in particularly force-sensitive tasks, highlighting the value of tactile grounding for foundation action models.Link
Strain-Parameterized Coupled Dynamics and Dual-Camera Visual Servoing for Aerial Continuum ManipulatorsThe paper unifies Cosserat-rod strain-based modeling of a tendon-driven continuum arm with UAV rigid-body dynamics into an SE(3) Lagrangian ODE, explicitly accounting for the underactuated aerial base. A dual-camera IBVS controller with adaptive low-level compensation mitigates limited FoV and attitude-induced image motion, validated on a custom aerial-continuum prototype in simulation and experiments.Link
Path Planning and Reinforcement Learning-Driven Control of On-Orbit Free-Flying Multi-Arm RobotsA hybrid framework uses trajectory optimization to generate dynamically feasible motions for thruster-equipped free-flying multi-arm robots and RL to robustly track them under disturbances. Simulated case studies—initial contact and fully free-floating surface interactions—show smoother, safer, and more efficient maneuvers than traditional strategies, with RL effectively compensating model mismatches.Link
