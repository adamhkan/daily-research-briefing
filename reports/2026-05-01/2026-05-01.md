## Executive Summary

- Motion planning sees multiple advances: STAR-Filter and NeRF-based reachable-set navigation both target faster, safer free-space reasoning in cluttered, noisy environments.
- Learning-based control progresses with Phase-varying Neural Potential Functions and STARRY, improving reactive, disturbance-robust manipulation and long-horizon action generation.
- Grasping/manipulation hardware and planning co-design appears via an asymmetrical GET gripper with 2D/3D planners and an origami tentacle gripper leveraging stochastic entanglement.
- Legged and quadruped platforms gain new capabilities through bi-level contact/motion planning for rope-assisted climbing and posture-adaptive navigation in unstructured 3D terrain.
- Institution-aligned work includes a fast single-view grasp planner for an asymmetric gripper from MIT and UC Berkeley, demonstrating sizable empirical gains over standard baselines.

| STAR-Filter: Efficient Convex Free-Space Approximation via Starshaped Set Filtering in Noisy Environments |  | Proposes STAR-Filter, a star-shaped set–based filter for fast convex free-space region generation that treats obstacle points as active constraints to cut redundant computation. The method is robust to sensor noise, less conservative than prior polytope inflation approaches, and demonstrated for safe flight corridor generation and agile quadrotor planning. | https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.26626 |
| Bi-Level Optimization for Contact and Motion Planning in Rope-Assisted Legged Robots | INRIA; University of Trento | Formulates rope-assisted climbing for legged robots as a bi-level mixed-integer optimization that jointly selects feasible landing regions and optimizes rope tensions, leg forces, and foothold locations. An outer Cross-Entropy Method searches terrain regions while an inner nonlinear optimizer enforces dynamic feasibility, validated on the ALPINE climbing platform. | https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.26910 |
| Safe Navigation using Neural Radiance Fields via Reachable Sets |  | Combines reachable-set representations of a robot’s dynamics with NeRF-based volumetric obstacle models to enforce safety in cluttered environments via constrained optimal control. The approach casts path planning with safety as LMIs in an optimal control problem and demonstrates safe navigation in scenarios with numerous obstacles. | https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.26899 |
| Reactive Motion Generation via Phase-varying Neural Potential Functions | Chalmers University of Technology; EPFL; University College London | Introduces Phase-varying Neural Potential Functions, which learn a phase-conditioned potential field where phase is inferred from state progression rather than time. This enables stable, reactive vector fields that handle trajectory intersections and state revisits, outperforming existing LfD baselines and showing robustness in 6D manipulation under disturbances. | https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.26450 |
| HiPAN: Hierarchical Posture-Adaptive Navigation for Quadruped Robots in Unstructured 3D Environments | Korea Advanced Institute of Science and Technology | Presents HiPAN, a hierarchical navigation framework where a high-level policy outputs planar velocity and body posture commands from onboard depth images, executed by a posture-adaptive locomotion controller. Path-Guided Curriculum Learning extends the planning horizon, yielding higher success rates and better path efficiency than reactive and end-to-end baselines in 3D terrain. | https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.26504 |
| STARRY: Spatial-Temporal Action-Centric World Modeling for Robotic Manipulation |  | Proposes STARRY, a manipulation policy that jointly denoises future spatial-temporal latents and action sequences while using geometry-aware selective attention over predicted depth and end-effector geometry. On RoboTwin 2.0 and real robots it substantially boosts success rates over prior VLA and world-model baselines, especially for spatial-temporally demanding tasks. | https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.26848 |
| Stochastic Entanglement of Deterministic Origami Tentacles For Universal Robotic Gripping |  | Develops a tendon-driven origami tentacle gripper where each tentacle’s deterministic coiling combines into stochastic entanglement for robust multi-tentacle grasping of varied object shapes. A Cosserat-rod-based simulation links design and performance, and experiments under gravity, in water, and with stow-and-release deployment highlight simple yet versatile universal gripping. | https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.26897 |
