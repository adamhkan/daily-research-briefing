Executive Summary

  • ETH Zurich features prominently with scalable motion planning for large robot fleets and high-speed dense tactile force estimation, alongside a demonstration of precise control using low-cost ToF sensing.
  • Whole-body humanoid loco-manipulation and locomotion control see major advances, with unified motion/world-action models, terrain-aware behavior foundations, foothold-tracking controllers, and multi-terrain fall recovery.
  • Dexterous and contact-rich manipulation remains a core frontier, with new pipelines for human-like grasps, video-to-skill transfer, tactile/force-aware world models, RGB-aligned tactile saliency, and data/skill acquisition from egocentric human demonstrations.
  • Navigation and motion planning progress spans monocular metric-aware visual navigation, UAV and tiny-robot optical-flow navigation, multi-UAV trajectory optimization, high-speed FPV flight, and end-to-end motion planning for underwater vehicles.
  • Vision-Language(-Action) and world-action models continue to mature with improved temporal modeling, safety and failure-recovery layers, geometry-aware action representations, and efficient runtimes, increasingly targeting real-time deployment.
  • New simulators, benchmarks, and datasets (e.g., IR-SIM, SIMPLE, Real-IKEA, Real/SO-101 evaluations) emphasize physically realistic interaction and reproducible evaluation for navigation, humanoid loco-manipulation, and robust manipulation.

Motion planning for hundreds of floating robotsETH ZurichIntroduces a scalable multi-robot motion-planning pipeline for omnidirectional floating robots that builds a collision graph, decomposes it into interaction clusters, and solves them in parallel with robustness mechanisms. Demonstrated in simulation up to 500 robots and deployed on real fleets in Lake Zürich and the Venice Biennale, enabling rapid choreography generation from sparse keyframes.Link
Dense Force Estimation with an Event-based Optical Tactile SensorETH ZurichPresents the first framework for reconstructing dense 3D force fields from event-based optical tactile sensors by estimating surface displacements and mapping them to forces via inverse FEM. Achieves sub-Newton accuracy at 100 Hz, offering high-temporal-resolution force feedback suited to grasping and dexterous manipulation control.Link
Can we stabilize an inverted pendulum with feedback from a time-of-flight camera?ETH ZurichDemonstrates that a low-cost, low-resolution time-of-flight depth camera can provide sufficiently precise feedback to reliably balance a classic inverted pendulum on a cart. This challenges assumptions about the limitations of ToF sensing for fast, unstable dynamics and informs vision-based feedback design for underactuated systems.Link

TitleInstitutionOverviewLink
MotionWAM: Towards Foundation World Action Models for Real-Time Humanoid Loco-ManipulationProposes MotionWAM, a real-time world-action model that unifies locomotion and manipulation for humanoids via a single motion latent and whole-body action tokens from egocentric video. On a Unitree G1 robot across nine tasks, it outperforms VLA baselines by over 30% success and enables task-driven foot interaction typically unreachable with decoupled upper/lower-body controllers.Link
VAIC: Vision-Guided Humanoid Agile Object Interaction Control via Decoupled CommandsIntroduces VAIC, a vision-guided whole-body control framework for humanoids that learns agile object interactions using a privileged teacher and distills to a deployable student using only onboard depth and proprioception. A recurrent object-adaptation module infers unobserved object dynamics, enabling tasks such as box carrying, cart pushing, and skateboarding without dense reference trajectories.Link
PTDL:Multi-Terrain Fall Recovery via Phase-Terrain Decoupled LearningPTDL trains a single proprioceptive policy for humanoid fall recovery and locomotion that generalizes across flat, gravel, and sloped terrains by decoupling supervision along phase (recovery vs. walking) and terrain axes. On a Unitree G1, it achieves stable cross-terrain get-up behaviors and smooth probe-to-walk transitions with terrain-specific strategies despite no terrain labels at deployment.Link
Mind Your Steps: A General Learning Framework for Accurate Humanoid Foothold TrackingProposes a general-purpose 3D foothold-tracking controller for humanoids that takes target foot poses and robustly tracks them under noisy pose and contact estimation. A goal-sampling scheme and new target representation allow the low-level policy to remain terrain-agnostic and plug-and-play with diverse high-level foothold planners for precise loco-manipulation.Link
Perceptive Behavior Foundation Model: Adapting Human Motion Priors to Robot-Centric TerrainIntroduces Perceptive BFM, a terrain-aware humanoid behavior model that adapts raw human motion references to local terrain using robot perception. A terrain-conformal reference synthesis pipeline and teacher–student distillation yield a controller that preserves expressive human-like motions while adjusting footholds, posture, and timing to the robot’s environment.Link
OASIS: From Simulation Data Collection to Real-World Humanoid Loco-ManipulationOASIS provides a sim-first pipeline for humanoid loco-manipulation by reconstructing realistic object assets from images, teleoperating trajectories in simulation, and heavily augmenting them for policy training. A hierarchical visuomotor policy trained purely on this data transfers zero-shot to a real humanoid and often outperforms policies trained on real-robot teleoperation thanks to broader domain randomization.Link
SIMPLE: Simulation-Based Policy Learning and Evaluation for Humanoid Loco-manipulationSIMPLE is a large-scale simulation testbed for humanoid loco-manipulation, combining MuJoCo dynamics with IsaacSim rendering across 60 tasks, 50 scenes, and 1,000+ objects. It supports automated motion-planned and VR-teleoperated trajectory collection and shows that performance in SIMPLE correlates with real-world humanoid results, enabling reproducible benchmarking of whole-body controllers and foundation models.Link
Safe Polytope-in-Polytope Motion Planning and Control with Control Barrier FunctionsKU LeuvenDevelops a local motion-planning and control scheme that guarantees a polytopic robot remains inside a convex free-space polytope using discrete-time control barrier functions within MPC. The formulation scales with free-space and robot-shape complexity rather than obstacle count, needs no explicit obstacle segmentation, and runs at 10 Hz onboard for both surface vehicles and ground robots in dynamic environments.Link
VGP-Nav: Metric-Aware Visual Geometric Perception for Robot NavigationMohamed bin Zayed University of Artificial IntelligenceVGP-Nav is a monocular RGB-only framework that jointly provides metric-consistent localization and dense obstacle perception by anchoring visual geometry to ground-plane-derived scale constraints. It resolves monocular scale online to output metric obstacle maps suitable for planning and is validated across varied environments and real mobile robot deployments.Link
Autonomous FPV Flight with Translational Optical Flow and Uncertainty MaskShanghai Jiao Tong UniversityAchieves high-speed monocular FPV quadrotor navigation by decomposing optical flow into translational and rotational components and using only translational flow plus an uncertainty mask derived from forward–backward flow inconsistencies. A differentiable sim-trained policy reaches nearly 14 m/s in simulation and 11.8 m/s in forested real-world tests with over 93% success, doubling prior monocular-flow systems’ speeds.Link
