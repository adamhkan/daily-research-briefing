Executive Summary

  • Mapping and planning tools advance with Kernel-SDF for real-time, uncertainty-aware SDFs and a 6 mW 3D Gaussian mapping SoC, targeting scalable, edge-capable motion planning.
  • Several works push manipulation and dexterous control, from long-reach lunar manipulators and safe DMP-based motion generation to kilohertz-safe retargeting and affordance-driven grasping.
  • Whole-body and legged control see progress via depth-conditioned humanoid locomotion, slip-robust bipedal walking, and flexible motion adaptation for physics-based characters.
  • New policy-learning frameworks (CLaD, DIAL, GenSplat, IMPASTO, hybrid RL+LLM) improve long-horizon, view-robust, and language-conditioned manipulation and control.
  • Multi-agent and temporal-logical planning methods (GraSP-STL, TAPF-PC LNS, CREST, distributed CBFs) improve safety, scalability, and execution efficiency in multi-robot and vehicle systems.
  • Perception for navigation and safety is strengthened by bootstrap depth fusion under sensor failures and VLM-based semantic monitoring and hazard communication layers.

Kernel-SDF: An Open-Source Library for Real-Time Signed Distance Function Estimation using Kernel RegressionUC San DiegoKernel-SDF combines a kernel-regression occupancy front-end with a GP-based SDF back-end to deliver real-time, uncertainty-aware signed distance fields from streaming sensor data. It improves SDF accuracy and provides gradients, calibrated uncertainty, and mesh extraction, directly supporting collision checking and trajectory optimization for planning and manipulation.Link
Long-Reach Robotic Manipulation for Assembly and Outfitting of Lunar StructuresStanford UniversityThis work presents a compact long-reach manipulator using a deployable composite boom for cable routing and assembly over meter-scale lunar structures. The authors characterize boom deflection and vibration and design a manipulation controller that maintains sub-centimeter endpoint accuracy up to 1.8 m reach for large-workspace tasks.Link
Long-Reach Robotic Cleaning for Lunar Solar ArraysStanford UniversityBuilding on long-reach manipulation, this paper equips a mobile robot with a deployable boom and compliant, force-sensing wrist for gentle cleaning of large lunar solar arrays. A velocity-based admittance controller regulates contact forces (~2 N) across varying boom lengths, indicating feasibility for maintenance tasks on expansive infrastructure.Link
Bootstrap Perception Under Hardware Depth Failure for Indoor Robot NavigationNew York UniversityThe authors propose a failure-aware perception hierarchy that uses surviving depth pixels to metrically calibrate monocular depth, fusing it with LiDAR to fill large depth gaps. The system boosts obstacle coverage by up to 110% over LiDAR alone and runs at over 200 FPS on embedded hardware, enabling robust indoor navigation under severe depth sensor failures.Link
Distributed Predictive Control Barrier Functions: Towards Scalable Safety Certification in Modular Multi-Agent SystemsETH Zurich; UC BerkeleyThis work introduces structured control barrier functions extended to a distributed predictive setting (D-PCBF) for safety in modular, networked multi-agent systems. The approach enables plug-and-play joining or leaving of agents while guaranteeing recoverable safety, validated on simulated and real miniature race-car platoons.Link
See Something, Say Something: Context-Criticality-Aware Mobile Robot Communication for Hazard MitigationsNew York UniversityA VLM/LLM-driven framework assesses contextual criticality of perceived hazards and adapts robot-to-human messaging accordingly. Experiments with a patrolling robot show faster mitigation responses and an 82% user trust rating compared to fixed-priority baselines, emphasizing communication as part of safe mobile robot deployment.Link
A Semantic Observer Layer for Autonomous Vehicles: Pre-Deployment Feasibility Study of VLMs for Low-Latency Anomaly DetectionNew York UniversityThe paper evaluates a quantized VLM as a low-rate semantic observer layer for AVs, detecting context-dependent hazards beyond pixel-level perception. With aggressive quantization and attention optimizations, the system reaches ~500 ms inference at 1–2 Hz, demonstrating feasible latency while highlighting recall limits and safety tradeoffs.Link

CReF: Cross-modal and Recurrent Fusion for Depth-conditioned Humanoid LocomotionZhejiang UniversityCReF learns humanoid locomotion policies that directly fuse proprioception and raw depth via cross-modal attention and gated recurrent fusion, avoiding explicit terrain heightmaps. A terrain-aware foothold reward guides contact selection, yielding robust traversal of complex 3D terrains in simulation and successful zero-shot transfer to real humanoids.Link
CLaD: Planning with Grounded Foresight via Cross-Modal Latent DynamicsCLaD jointly models proprioceptive and semantic state evolution using asymmetric cross-attention to generate grounded latent foresight for manipulation planning. These predictions condition a diffusion policy, enabling high success (94.7% on LIBERO-LONG) with far fewer parameters than large VLAs on long-horizon manipulation tasks.Link
IMPASTO: Integrating Model-Based Planning with Learned Dynamics Models for Robotic Oil Painting ReproductionIMPASTO combines learned pixel-level dynamics of brush–canvas interaction with MPC to plan stroke trajectories, forces, and colors for oil painting reproduction. Using only self-play data, a 7-DoF arm executes force-sensitive, contact-rich strokes that approximate human artwork more accurately than baseline methods.Link
SuperGrasp: Single-View Object Grasping via Superquadric Similarity Matching, Evaluation, and RefinementSuperGrasp addresses single-view parallel-jaw grasping by matching observed point clouds to a superquadric primitive library to propose grasps, then refining them with the E-RNet evaluation network. Trained on a large synthetic grasp dataset, the approach shows strong generalization and stable grasping on novel objects and cluttered scenes.Link
Kilohertz-Safe: A Scalable Framework for Constrained Dexterous RetargetingUniversity of Science and Technology of ChinaKilohertz-Safe reformulates dexterous hand motion retargeting as a convex quadratic program in joint differential space with systematically linearized kinematic and collision constraints. By integrating control barrier functions, it delivers high-frequency (~9 ms latency) safe retargeting on the Wuji Hand, outperforming prior optimization and learning baselines.Link
Stable Walking for Bipedal Locomotion under Foot-Slip via Virtual Nonholonomic ConstraintsUniversity of MichiganThis work augments standard virtual holonomic constraints with virtual nonholonomic constraints on stance-foot tangential velocity to explicitly model slip in bipedal locomotion. A nonlinear feedback controller enforces both constraint sets, yielding a slip-compatible hybrid zero dynamics manifold and stabilizing periodic gaits under low-friction conditions.Link
Hybrid Framework for Robotic Manipulation: Integrating Reinforcement Learning and Large Language ModelsThe authors propose a two-layer architecture where an LLM handles high-level task decomposition and language understanding, while RL policies execute low-level manipulation with a Franka arm. In simulation benchmarks, the hybrid system reduces completion time and improves accuracy and adaptability over RL-only baselines, indicating benefits of language-guided control.Link
DIAL: Decoupling Intent and Action via Latent World Modeling for End-to-End VLADIAL separates high-level intent from low-level actuation by having a VLM-based module predict latent future visual features that encode intent, which a lightweight policy decodes into actions via latent inverse dynamics. A staged training procedure maintains VLM semantics, enabling state-of-the-art performance on RoboCasa GR1 with 10× fewer demos and robust zero-shot real-world manipulation.Link
SafeDMPs: Integrating Formal Safety with DMPs for Adaptive HRIIndian Institute of ScienceSafeDMPs fuse Dynamic Movement Primitives with a closed-form safety controller derived from Spatio-Temporal Tubes, eliminating online optimization while enforcing obstacle avoidance. On a 7-DoF manipulator, the method yields fast, adaptable, and provably safe motions around static and dynamic obstacles, outperforming optimization-based safety baselines in speed and accuracy.Link
GraSP-STL: A Graph-Based Framework for Zero-Shot Signal Temporal Logic Planning via Offline Goal-Conditioned Reinforcement LearningShanghai Jiao Tong UniversityGraSP-STL learns a goal-conditioned value function from offline transitions to build a directed graph abstraction of state reachability, then performs graph search to satisfy arbitrary unseen STL tasks. By decoupling reusable reachability learning from STL-specific planning, it enables zero-shot satisfaction of complex temporal logic goals without additional environment interaction.Link
