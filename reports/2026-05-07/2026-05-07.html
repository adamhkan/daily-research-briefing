Executive Summary

  • MIT leads two contributions: a hierarchical RL+QP framework for reactive dexterous grasping with robust sim-to-real transfer, and an optimization-based method for accurate, energy-aware jumping in wheeled-bipedal robots.
  • New frameworks target complex whole-body and loco-manipulation: SigLoMa enables onboard vision-based quadrupedal pick-and-place, while BifrostUMI unlocks robot-free data collection for humanoid whole-body manipulation.
  • Several works push motion planning and control under complex constraints, including feasibility-aware hybrid control with temporal logic, and Neural Control for equilibrium-constrained deformable object manipulation.
  • Sampling-based and predictive control advances include risk-aware domain randomization for contact-rich MPC, residual-learning-enhanced MPPI for robust path tracking, and diffusion-based long-horizon trajectory composition.
  • Generative policies see more practical finetuning via OGPO, improving sample-efficient adaptation of diffusion/flow policies for dexterous and multi-task manipulation.

Learning Reactive Dexterous Grasping via Hierarchical Task-Space RL Planning and Joint-Space QP ControlMassachusetts Institute of TechnologyProposes a hybrid hierarchical framework where arm and hand RL agents output task-space velocities that are enforced by a GPU-parallel QP controller with hard safety constraints. The approach improves training convergence, enables operator-tunable safety margins, and transfers zero-shot to a 7-DoF arm with a 20-DoF anthropomorphic hand for robust reactive grasping of unseen objects.Link
Height Control and Optimal Torque Planning for Jumping With Wheeled-Bipedal RobotsMassachusetts Institute of TechnologyIntroduces a wheeled-bipedal jumping dynamics model combined with a Bayesian optimization-based torque planning method to achieve accurate jump heights with reduced energy use. Simulations show large reductions in height error and energy cost, yielding continuous torque profiles suitable for real-world whole-body jumping control.Link

SigLoMa: Learning Open-World Quadrupedal Loco-Manipulation from Ego-Centric VisionPresents SigLoMa, a fully onboard quadrupedal loco-manipulation system that relies on a 5 Hz ego-centric open-vocabulary detector and a Sigma-Points representation for scalable exteroception. An ego-centric Kalman filter bridges slow perception and fast floating-base control, while active sampling and temporal encoding enable dynamic pick-and-place performance comparable to expert teleoperation.Link
Feasibility-aware Hybrid Control for Motion Planning under Signal Temporal LogicsKTH Royal Institute of Technology; TH Royal InstituteProposes a hybrid task-and-motion planning architecture that encodes local constraint satisfaction via a discrete variable and uses control barrier functions on a transformed disk workspace. The method handles multiple overlapping spatio-temporal tasks under STL specifications, mitigating deadlocks and coping with input saturation in planar robotic workspaces.Link
BifrostUMI: Bridging Robot-Free Demonstrations and Humanoid Whole-Body ManipulationIntroduces BifrostUMI, a VR-based framework that collects human keypoint trajectories and wrist-mounted visual data without requiring a physical robot in the loop. A high-level policy predicts future keypoint trajectories which are retargeted to humanoid morphology and executed via whole-body control, enabling diverse humanoid whole-body manipulation skills from natural demonstrations.Link
On Surprising Effects of Risk-Aware Domain Randomization for Contact-Rich Sampling-based Predictive ControlCalifornia Institute of Technology; School of Computing, DePaul University, Chicago, IL, USA.; Technology Innovation InstituteStudies how risk-aware domain randomization affects sampling-based predictive control in a contact-rich pushing task, comparing average, optimistic, and pessimistic rollout aggregation. Findings indicate that randomization reshapes the effective cost landscape and the basin of attraction for contact-producing actions, suggesting new levers for robust contact-rich MPC under model uncertainty.Link
Neural Control: Adjoint Learning Through Equilibrium ConstraintsProposes a boundary-control framework for systems governed by implicit equilibrium, such as deformable linear objects, using adjoint-based differentiation through equilibrium conditions to obtain proxy gradients. Coupled with receding-horizon MPC, the method improves robustness in multi-stable regimes and outperforms gradient-free baselines for contact-rich deformable object manipulation on real robots.Link
Robust Path Tracking for Vehicles via Continuous-Time Residual Learning: An ICODE-MPPI ApproachIntroduces ICODE-MPPI, which augments MPPI control with Input Concomitant Neural ODEs to model residual dynamics in continuous time. High-fidelity simulations show substantial reductions in path-tracking error and smoother control under persistent disturbances, illustrating a principled way to fuse learning-based residual models with sampling-based optimal control.Link
Refining Compositional Diffusion for Reliable Long-Horizon PlanningProposes Refining Compositional Diffusion, a guidance scheme for composing overlapping short-horizon diffusion plans into long-horizon trajectories without mode-averaging. By using self-reconstruction error and overlap consistency as guidance signals, it concentrates sampling on globally coherent, high-density plans and improves performance on long-horizon locomotion and manipulation benchmarks.Link
OGPO: Sample Efficient Full-Finetuning of Generative Control PoliciesIntroduces Off-policy Generative Policy Optimization, which finetunes diffusion/flow-based control policies using off-policy critics and a modified PPO objective that backpropagates through the full generative process. OGPO achieves strong performance on multi-task and dexterous manipulation, effectively steering and refining generative policies with stabilizers that mitigate critic over-exploitation.Link
