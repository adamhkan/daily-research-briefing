## Executive Summary

- Stanford introduces a compact world-model-based runtime monitor that reliably detects failures in complex bimanual manipulation, outperforming larger anomaly detectors.
- MIT contributes residual control for fast dynamics-shift recovery and a formally safe control barrier–based framework for surgical robot policies, both improving reliability under uncertainty.
- MIT also presents a scalable probabilistic human motion predictor and an underwater robot modeling framework, targeting safer and more accurate interaction in challenging environments.
- NYU demonstrates that a single, time-varying optimality principle can explain human reaching via efficient inverse RL, informing future human-robot interaction models.
- UC Berkeley shows that rotating equilibria can cut power consumption by up to 20% for multi-UAV collaborative payload transport via centrifugal-force-based load sharing.
