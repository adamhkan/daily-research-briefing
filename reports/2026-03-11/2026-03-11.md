## Executive Summary

- Two institution-aligned works from CMU and Stanford focus on pretraining from casual human videos (3PoinTr) and test-time-scaled in-context imitation with VLMs (SAIL), both improving data efficiency and generalization for manipulation.
- Multiple papers push dexterous and bimanual manipulation via symmetry-equivariant policies (EquiBim), triadic interaction-aware diffusion (RoTri-Diff), tactile-force-aware control (TacDexGrasp, Multifingered force-aware control), and human-like VLA-based dexterous skills (MoDE-VLA).
- Hierarchical and graph-based planners for mobile manipulation and loco-manipulation (MoMaStage, MetaWorld-X, ACLM, Two-Stage Path Following) tackle long-horizon tasks, compositional whole-body skills, and distributed MPC for collaborative payload transport.
- New VLA-centric frameworks (AtomicVLA, AtomVLA, SaiVLA-0, TempoFit, Seed2Scale, LoRA-SP) emphasize scalable skill libraries, temporal memory, adaptive capacity, and self-evolving data engines for long-horizon and multi-task manipulation.
- Contact-rich and force-sensitive manipulation sees advances in phase-scheduled visual-force control (PhaForce), tactile-guided disassembly (CONTACT), compliance-focused grasping (TacDexGrasp), and pose-centered affordance learning from human interaction (RoboPCA).
- Robustness and safety in embodied systems are strengthened through uncertainty-aware interventions (TRIAGE), world-model-based failure detection (Foundational World Models...), real-time executability feedback during demo collection (FeasibleCap), and task-aware observation interfaces (Choose What to Observe).
