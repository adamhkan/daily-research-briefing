## Executive Summary

- Only one selected paper matches the requested institutions: a Stanford work on world-model-based failure detection for bimanual manipulators.
- Mobile and whole-body manipulation is advanced via graph-guided planning for long-horizon indoor tasks and a two-stage path-following planner for mobile manipulators.
- Dextrous and bimanual manipulation sees extensive progress, including symmetry-equivariant policies, tactile-force-aware controllers, robust multifingered grasping, and knotting deformable bags.
- Vision-Language-Action and imitation-learning methods are pushed toward long-horizon, multi-skill manipulation through atomic skill libraries, temporal KV memory, mixture-of-experts routing, and training-free policy routing.
- Contact-rich and tactile manipulation is strengthened through phase-scheduled vision–force policies, tactile disassembly, and pre-grasp force prediction from prior experience.
- Frameworks for better data, teleoperation, and affordance learning (e.g., gripper-in-hand constraint guidance, human-video pretraining, pose-centered affordances) aim to reduce demonstration cost and improve generalization.
