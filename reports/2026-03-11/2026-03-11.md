## Executive Summary

- Whole-body and dexterous manipulation see strong advances via hierarchical world models, symmetry-aware bimanual policies, and tactile/force-aware control for humanoids and multifingered hands.
- Vision-Language-Action models are being re-architected around atomic skills, mixtures-of-experts, temporal memory, and efficient fine-tuning/quantization/edge-cloud inference for robust long-horizon manipulation.
- Several works tackle long-horizon mobile and bimanual manipulation with structured task graphs, pose-centered affordances, and robust planning/execution under clutter, occlusion, and contact-rich dynamics.
- Safety, monitoring, and robustness emerge as key themes, with conformal world-model monitors for bimanual failures, safety-guaranteed surgical policies, and residual control for dynamics shifts.
- Tactile and visuo-tactile sensing are leveraged for contact-rich tasks, grasp stability, disassembly, and force selection, while new gripper and teleoperation platforms broaden practical deployment.
- Surveys and data/learning engines assess and scale robotic foundation models, highlighting gaps between benchmark success and industry-grade readiness in manipulation contexts.
