Executive Summary

  • Strong focus on dexterous and bimanual manipulation: several works introduce symmetry-aware policies, tactile and force-aware control, and diffusion-based planners for complex contact-rich tasks.
  • Vision-Language-Action (VLA) and related architectures continue to mature, with new frameworks for atomic skill libraries, long-horizon memory, subtask-aware post-training, and hierarchy for cluttered or long-horizon scenes.
  • Mobile and aerial manipulation see advances in long-horizon MoMA planning, robust grasping under occlusion, and language-conditioned aerial placement with explicit geometric reasoning.
  • Tactile and visuo-tactile perception are leveraged both for grasp stability and for challenging disassembly or shape/material recognition, emphasizing the importance of force-field and predictive tactile representations.
  • Safety, monitoring, and robustness are addressed via failure-detecting world models, uncertainty decomposition, safety-guaranteed surgical control, and residual adaptation to dynamics shifts.
  • Institution-matched work from Stanford, MIT, NYU, and UC Berkeley spans failure detection for bimanual manipulators, safe surgical policies, residual control under dynamics shifts, human motion intent inference, and energy-efficient collaborative aerial transport.

Foundational World Models Accurately Detect Bimanual Manipulator FailuresStanford UniversityTrains a probabilistic world model in the latent space of a vision foundation tokenizer and uses its uncertainty as a non-conformity score within a conformal prediction framework to detect anomalous failures of bimanual manipulators. Demonstrates superior failure detection on simulated and real bimanual datasets with far fewer parameters than competing learning-based methods, enabling practical runtime monitoring for safe deployment.Link
Toward Global Intent Inference for Human Motion by Inverse Reinforcement LearningNew York UniversityUses Minimal Observation Inverse Reinforcement Learning to infer time-varying cost weights explaining human reaching motions from a compact set of candidate terms. Shows that a single subject- and posture-independent time-varying cost function can accurately predict trajectories, suggesting a unified optimality principle for human reaching that can inform robot motion planning and collaboration.Link
Residual Control for Fast Recovery from Dynamics ShiftsMassachusetts Institute of TechnologyProposes a stability-aligned residual control architecture that leaves a nominal RL policy frozen and adds a bounded residual channel regulated by a Stability Alignment Gate. Across quadruped, biped, humanoid, and wheeled platforms, the method markedly shortens recovery time from mid-episode dynamics shifts (e.g., actuator degradation or mass changes) while preserving near-nominal steady-state behavior.Link
SSP: Safety-guaranteed Surgical Policy via Joint Optimization of Behavioral and Spatial ConstraintsMassachusetts Institute of TechnologyIntroduces the SSP framework, which learns an uncertainty-aware Neural ODE dynamics model from demonstrations and uses it to build a robust Control Barrier Function safety filter around black-box surgical policies. The controller enforces both behavioral and spatial constraints, achieving near-zero constraint violations on simulated and physical dVRK tasks while maintaining high task success.Link
Unified Structural-Hydrodynamic Modeling of Underwater Underactuated Mechanisms and Soft RobotsMassachusetts Institute of TechnologyPresents a trajectory-driven global optimization framework, inspired by CMA-ES, to jointly identify structural and hydrodynamic parameters for underwater multibody and soft robotic systems. Achieves low end-effector error on underactuated mechanisms and transfers a single identified model to an octopus-inspired soft arm and multi-arm swimmer without retuning, indicating strong modeling fidelity and scalability.Link
Towards Scalable Probabilistic Human Motion Prediction with Gaussian Processes for Safe Human-Robot CollaborationMassachusetts Institute of TechnologyDevelops a structured multitask variational Gaussian Process model for full-body human motion prediction with calibrated uncertainty and kinematically consistent 6D rotations. On Human3.6M, it delivers competitive accuracy with well-behaved confidence intervals using far fewer parameters than deep baselines, making it attractive for real-time, safety-critical HRC applications.Link
Energy-Efficient Collaborative Transport of Tether-Suspended Payloads via Rotating EquilibriumUC BerkeleyAnalyzes collaborative aerial transport of tethered payloads and proposes operating at a rotating equilibrium where centrifugal forces provide horizontal tension, allowing each quadrotor to use purely vertical thrust. Demonstrates up to 20% power savings relative to static lifting, broadening the feasible design space for energy-efficient multi-UAV payload transport.Link

MetaWorld-X: Hierarchical World Modeling via VLM-Orchestrated Experts for Humanoid Loco-ManipulationUniversity of Alberta; University of Science and Technology of ChinaProposes MetaWorld-X, a hierarchical world model for humanoid loco-manipulation that decomposes control into specialized expert policies trained with human-motion priors. MoMaStage: Skill-State Graph Guided Planning and Closed-Loop Execution for Long-Horizon Indoor Mobile ManipulationIntroduces MoMaStage, which grounds a VLM in a hierarchical skill library and topology-aware Skill-State Graph to plan long-horizon indoor mobile manipulation without explicit mapping. A closed-loop execution layer monitors proprioception and triggers graph-constrained replanning, improving logical consistency and robustness in complex multi-stage MoMA tasks.Link
Two-Stage Path Following for Mobile Manipulators via Dimensionality-Reduced Graph Search and Numerical OptimizationAuthors are with College of Mechanical and Vehicle Engineering, ChongQing UniversityPresents a two-stage configuration planning method that reduces an 8-DoF mobile manipulator path-following problem to a 2-DoF base optimization under a yaw-fixed assumption. A discrete IRM-based graph search followed by continuous L-BFGS refinement achieves sub-millimeter accuracy and robust physical performance while respecting reachability constraints.Link
RoboPCA: Pose-centered Affordance Learning from Human Demonstrations for Robot ManipulationChinese Academy of Sciences; University of Chinese Academy of SciencesProposes RoboPCA, a pose-centered affordance predictor that jointly estimates contact regions and poses from RGB-D and language, avoiding inconsistencies between region and pose predictions. A Human2Afford pipeline extracts 3D scene context and maps human hand interactions to robot end-effector orientations, yielding strong generalization across tasks, categories, and sim-to-real.Link
Viewpoint-Agnostic Grasp Pipeline using VLM and Partial ObservationsTohoku UniversityBuilds a language-guided grasping pipeline for mobile legged manipulators that combines open-vocabulary detection, robust point cloud completion under occlusion, and collision-filtered 6-DoF grasp synthesis. On a quadruped with an arm, the method achieves 90% grasp success in clutter, far surpassing a view-dependent baseline.Link
Low-Cost Teleoperation Extension for Mobile ManipulatorsDescribes an open-source teleoperation framework for mobile bimanual manipulators using commodity hardware: smartphone-based head tracking, leader arms, and foot pedals. User studies show improved task performance and reduced cognitive load versus keyboard-based control while enabling intuitive whole-body teleoperation.Link
RoTri-Diff: A Spatial Robot-Object Triadic Interaction-Guided Diffusion Model for Bimanual ManipulationHong Kong University of Science and Technology; National University of SingaporeIntroduces the RoTri representation capturing the triadic 6D pose relationships among two arms and an object, and RoTri-Diff, a diffusion-based IL framework that enforces these geometric constraints. The approach reduces inter-arm collisions and improves stability, outperforming baselines on RLBench2 and real bimanual tasks.Link
EquiBim: Learning Symmetry-Equivariant Policy for Bimanual ManipulationPurdue UniversityFormulates bilateral symmetry in dual-arm systems as group actions on observation and action spaces and enforces equivariance during imitation learning. The model-agnostic EquiBim framework improves performance and robustness across various sensing and action parameterizations in simulation and real bimanual manipulation.Link
Multifingered force-aware control for humanoid robotsPresents a model-based controller for humanoids with multifingered hands that uses learned tactile-force estimators to redistribute contact forces and keep the Center of Pressure close to the fingertip contact polygon centroid. Demonstrated on object balancing tasks, the approach attains over 80% success using estimated forces rather than raw tactile signals.Link
TacDexGrasp: Compliant and Robust Dexterous Grasping with Tactile FeedbackPeking UniversityDevelops a SOCP-based controller for multi-finger grasping that uses tactile feedback to constrain the tangential-to-normal force ratio at each contact below an estimated friction bound. Without explicit torque modeling, the method prevents both translational and rotational slip, showing robust real-world performance on diverse objects.Link
