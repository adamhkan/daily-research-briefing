Executive Summary

  • Only one selected paper matches the requested institutions: a Stanford work on world-model-based failure detection for bimanual manipulators.

Foundational World Models Accurately Detect Bimanual Manipulator FailuresStanford UniversityTrains a probabilistic world model in the latent space of a vision foundation model to monitor bimanual manipulation and flag anomalous failures via uncertainty-based conformal prediction. On both simulated and real bimanual datasets, it outperforms statistical and learning baselines while using far fewer parameters, enabling lightweight runtime failure detection for manipulators.Link
Unified Structural-Hydrodynamic Modeling of Underwater Underactuated Mechanisms and Soft RobotsMassachusetts Institute of TechnologyIntroduces a trajectory-driven global optimization framework, inspired by CMA-ES, to jointly identify structural and hydrodynamic parameters of underwater underactuated and soft robots. Achieves low end-effector error on an underactuated mechanism and transfers the learned parameters to a multi-arm octopus robot, showing scalable, high-fidelity underwater modeling.Link
Residual Control for Fast Recovery from Dynamics ShiftsMassachusetts Institute of TechnologyProposes a stability-aligned residual control architecture that overlays a bounded residual on top of a frozen nominal RL controller to handle unobserved mid-episode dynamics shifts. A Stability Alignment Gate enforces directional and magnitude constraints, yielding much faster recovery across several legged and wheeled platforms without retraining.Link
SSP: Safety-guaranteed Surgical Policy via Joint Optimization of Behavioral and Spatial ConstraintsMassachusetts Institute of TechnologyLearns an uncertainty-aware dynamics model with Neural ODEs and wraps arbitrary surgical policies in a Control Barrier Function-based safety filter enforcing both behavioral and spatial constraints. Demonstrations on SurRoL and dVRK show near-zero constraint violations while preserving high task success for RL, IL, and CLF-based policies.Link
Energy-Efficient Collaborative Transport of Tether-Suspended Payloads via Rotating EquilibriumUC BerkeleyAnalyzes collaborative quadrotor transport of a tethered payload in a rotating equilibrium, where centrifugal forces generate the required horizontal tension. This allows each vehicle to produce nearly vertical thrust, cutting power consumption by up to 20% compared to static lifting while accommodating a wider range of tether geometries.Link
Receding-Horizon Nullspace Optimization for Actuation-Aware Control Allocation in Omnidirectional UAVsNew York UniversityPresents a receding-horizon control allocation method for over-actuated omnidirectional UAVs that models asymmetric motor dynamics and exploits nullspace redundancy. Using constrained iterative LQR over a prediction horizon, it suppresses motor command oscillations and improves 6-DoF trajectory tracking versus single-step QP allocators.Link
Learning-Based Robust Control: Unifying Exploration and Distributional Robustness for Reliable Robotics via Free EnergyNew York UniversityBuilds a distributionally robust free-energy-based framework that jointly learns dynamics and rewards while accounting for epistemic uncertainty. On continuous-control benchmarks and Franka arm manipulation, the method narrows the sim-to-real gap and improves repeatability without task-specific fine-tuning.Link
Toward Global Intent Inference for Human Motion by Inverse Reinforcement LearningNew York UniversityUses Minimal Observation Inverse Reinforcement Learning with a compact set of candidate costs to infer time-varying cost weights that explain human planar reaching. Shows that a single subject- and posture-agnostic, time-varying cost function dominated by joint-acceleration regulation reconstructs trajectories well, supporting unified principles of human motion optimality.Link
Towards Scalable Probabilistic Human Motion Prediction with Gaussian Processes for Safe Human-Robot CollaborationMassachusetts Institute of TechnologyIntroduces a structured multitask variational Gaussian Process model for full-body human motion prediction with calibrated uncertainty and a compact parameterization. On Human3.6M, it delivers competitive accuracy and well-behaved confidence intervals at modest computational cost, making it attractive for real-time collaborative robotics.Link

MetaWorld-X: Hierarchical World Modeling via VLM-Orchestrated Experts for Humanoid Loco-ManipulationUniversity of Alberta; University of Science and Technology of ChinaProposes MetaWorld-X, a hierarchical world-model framework that decomposes humanoid loco-manipulation into specialized expert policies trained with imitation-constrained RL under human-motion priors. A VLM-supervised routing mechanism composes experts based on task semantics, yielding more natural, stable, and compositionally generalizable whole-body behaviors.Link
MoMaStage: Skill-State Graph Guided Planning and Closed-Loop Execution for Long-Horizon Indoor Mobile ManipulationIntroduces MoMaStage, which grounds a VLM in a hierarchical skill library and a topology-aware Skill-State Graph to plan long-horizon indoor mobile manipulation without explicit maps. Closed-loop execution monitors proprioception and triggers graph-constrained semantic replanning, improving logical consistency, robustness, and task success over prior MoMA frameworks.Link
Two-Stage Path Following for Mobile Manipulators via Dimensionality-Reduced Graph Search and Numerical OptimizationAuthors are with College of Mechanical and Vehicle Engineering, ChongQing UniversityPresents a two-stage planner that reduces an 8-DoF mobile manipulator path-following problem to a 2-DoF base optimization with a yaw-fixed assumption. It first finds a globally optimal discrete path with a Dijkstra-based DP on an IRM-derived graph, then refines it continuously via L-BFGS while enforcing reachability, achieving sub-millimeter accuracy in simulation and robust execution on hardware.Link
Multifingered force-aware control for humanoid robotsDevelops a force-aware controller for humanoid robots equipped with multifingered hands, using learned force estimators from tactile sensors to infer contact forces. A model-based control law redistributes forces by adjusting torso, arm, wrist, and finger motions to keep the center of pressure aligned with the fingertip contact polygon centroid, improving stability across varied objects.Link
DexKnot: Generalizable Visuomotor Policy Learning for Dexterous Bag-Knotting ManipulationIntroduces DexKnot, which learns a shape-agnostic keypoint representation of deformable bags from human deformations and uses it to condition a diffusion policy for knotting. By operating on sparse keypoints instead of full observations, the method generalizes to previously unseen bags and deformations while achieving reliable knotting performance.Link
TacDexGrasp: Compliant and Robust Dexterous Grasping with Tactile FeedbackPeking UniversityProposes TacDexGrasp, a multi-fingered grasp controller that uses tactile feedback and SOCP optimization to constrain tangential-to-normal force ratios below an estimated friction coefficient. Without explicit torque models or slip detection, it prevents both translational and rotational slip, producing robust and compliant grasps on diverse real-world objects.Link
EquiBim: Learning Symmetry-Equivariant Policy for Bimanual ManipulationPurdue UniversityEquiBim enforces bilateral symmetry equivariance in imitation-learned policies for dual-arm robots by modeling symmetry as group actions on observation and action spaces. The model-agnostic framework improves robustness and consistency across symmetric configurations in both simulation and real bimanual manipulation on RoboTwin.Link

The system both streamlines data collection for in-hand bimanual manipulation and yields contact-aware policies that substantially outperform baselines on complex dexterous tasks.Link
