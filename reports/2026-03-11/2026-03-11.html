Executive Summary

  • Stanford introduces a compact world-model-based runtime monitor that reliably detects failures in complex bimanual manipulation, outperforming larger anomaly detectors.
  • MIT contributes residual control for fast dynamics-shift recovery and a formally safe control barrier–based framework for surgical robot policies, both improving reliability under uncertainty.
  • MIT also presents a scalable probabilistic human motion predictor and an underwater robot modeling framework, targeting safer and more accurate interaction in challenging environments.
  • NYU demonstrates that a single, time-varying optimality principle can explain human reaching via efficient inverse RL, informing future human-robot interaction models.
  • UC Berkeley shows that rotating equilibria can cut power consumption by up to 20% for multi-UAV collaborative payload transport via centrifugal-force-based load sharing.

Foundational World Models Accurately Detect Bimanual Manipulator FailuresStanford UniversityTrains a probabilistic, history-aware world model in the latent space of a vision foundation tokenizer to monitor bimanual manipulation. Uncertainty estimates act as non-conformity scores in a conformal prediction framework, enabling runtime failure detection that outperforms standard anomaly and OOD baselines with ~1/20th of the trainable parameters.Link
Residual Control for Fast Recovery from Dynamics ShiftsMassachusetts Institute of TechnologyProposes a stability-aligned residual control architecture that layers a bounded adaptive residual on top of a frozen nominal RL controller to handle unobserved dynamics shifts. A Stability Alignment Gate constrains magnitude and direction of corrections, yielding large reductions in recovery time on multiple robot platforms without retraining.Link
SSP: Safety-guaranteed Surgical Policy via Joint Optimization of Behavioral and Spatial ConstraintsMassachusetts Institute of TechnologyIntroduces SSP, which learns a Neural ODE–based dynamics model from demonstrations and uses it to construct robust control barrier functions that wrap around RL/IL/CLF surgical policies. The controller minimally modifies actions to satisfy behavioral and spatial safety constraints, achieving near-zero violations while preserving task success on SurRoL and dVRK.Link
Unified Structural-Hydrodynamic Modeling of Underwater Underactuated Mechanisms and Soft RobotsMassachusetts Institute of TechnologyPresents a trajectory-driven global optimization framework, inspired by CMA-ES, to jointly identify structural and distributed hydrodynamic parameters of underwater underactuated and soft robots. The learned models reproduce real motion with under 5% normalized end-effector error and transfer from mechanisms to an octopus-inspired soft arm and full robot without retuning.Link
Towards Scalable Probabilistic Human Motion Prediction with Gaussian Processes for Safe Human-Robot CollaborationMassachusetts Institute of TechnologyDevelops a multitask variational Gaussian Process model with joint-dimension factorization and 6D rotation representation for full-body motion prediction. The approach yields competitive accuracy, well-calibrated uncertainty, and low parameter count, supporting real-time, uncertainty-aware planning and collision avoidance in human-robot collaboration.Link
Toward Global Intent Inference for Human Motion by Inverse Reinforcement LearningNew York UniversityUses the Minimal Observation IRL algorithm with a structured cost basis to infer time-varying cost weights explaining planar human reaching. Shows that a subject- and posture-agnostic, time-varying cost—dominated by joint-acceleration regulation—can reconstruct trajectories with substantially lower error than fixed-weight models.Link
Energy-Efficient Collaborative Transport of Tether-Suspended Payloads via Rotating EquilibriumUC BerkeleyProposes operating multi-UAV tethered payload systems in a steady rotating equilibrium so centrifugal forces provide horizontal tether tension, allowing each vehicle to produce primarily vertical thrust. This configuration broadens feasible tether layouts and reduces power consumption by up to 20% compared to static lifting.Link
