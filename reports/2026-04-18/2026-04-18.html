Executive Summary

  • Mobile manipulation sees a new data-efficient framework (DockAnywhere) for viewpoint-robust visuomotor policy learning from a single demonstration.
  • Dexterous manipulation is advanced via a low-cost, force-feedback teleoperation interface (DEX-Mouse) and a large-scale multimodal human–robot grasp dataset (HRDexDB).
  • Regrasp and multi-step manipulation planning are tackled with differentiable object pose connectivity metrics enabling gradient-based sequence optimization.
  • Whole-body and legged locomotion progress includes agile skill switching for humanoids, terrain-adaptive control for legged robots, and exploiting passive dynamics for robust bipedal walking.
  • Motion and trajectory planning methods span coverage on irregular hex grids, biologically inspired navigation cells, multi-UAV payload transport, assistive trajectory optimization, and POMDP-based object search.
  • A Stanford framework provides conformal-verified, vision-based human motion prediction for safer human–robot collaboration.

DockAnywhere: Data-Efficient Visuomotor Policy Learning for Mobile Manipulation via Novel Demonstration GenerationNanyang Technological University; National Taiwan UniversityProposes DockAnywhere, a low-cost demonstration generation framework that decouples base motion from contact-rich manipulation to synthetically lift a single demo to many feasible docking poses. By 3D point-cloud editing and structure-preserving trajectory augmentation, the approach markedly improves viewpoint generalization and success rates for mobile manipulation policies in simulation and on real robots.Link
DEX-Mouse: A Low-cost Portable and Universal Interface with Force Feedback for Data Collection of Dexterous Robotic HandsIntroduces DEX-Mouse, a sub-$150 handheld, calibration-free teleoperation device with kinesthetic force feedback for dexterous robot hands. The operator-agnostic design supports mounting the robot hand on the operator's forearm, yielding high-quality, robot-aligned demonstrations and improved task completion and workload compared to existing teleop setups.Link
Differentiable Object Pose Connectivity Metrics for Regrasp Sequence OptimizationUniversity of OsakaPresents a multi-step regrasp planner that models grasp feasibility with an energy-based model and defines a continuous energy landscape over object pose pairs. This enables gradient-based optimization of intermediate poses and an adaptive iterative deepening strategy, improving robustness and automatically finding minimum-step regrasp sequences, including cross-end-effector transfer.Link
HRDexDB: A Large-Scale Dataset of Dexterous Human and Robotic Hand GraspsReleases HRDexDB, a multimodal dataset of high-fidelity grasp trajectories for human and multiple robotic hands over 100 objects, with synchronized 3D motion, tactile sensing, and multi-view/egocentric video. The dataset includes both successful and failed grasps, designed to support multimodal policy learning and human-to-robot transfer in dexterous manipulation.Link
Switch: Learning Agile Skills Switching for Humanoid RobotsIntroduces Switch, a hierarchical multi-skill system for humanoids that builds a skill graph over motion data, trains a whole-body tracking policy with deep RL, and uses an online skill scheduler. The method supports smooth, on-demand transitions between diverse locomotion skills while maintaining robust motion imitation.Link
Model-Based Reinforcement Learning Exploits Passive Body Dynamics for High-Performance Biped Robot LocomotionUniversity of OsakaCompares model-based deep RL locomotion on biped models with and without passive mechanical elements such as springs. Exploiting passive dynamics yields attractor-driven learning that converges to stable limit cycles, resulting in more robust and energy-efficient walking and running, highlighting the value of passive properties in embodied AI.Link
CART: Context-Aware Terrain Adaptation using Temporal Sequence Selection for Legged RobotsProposes CART, a high-level controller that fuses proprioceptive and exteroceptive sequences to learn context-aware terrain representations for legged robots. Evaluated on ANYmal-C in simulation and Spot in the real world, CART improves success rates and significantly reduces base vibrations over multimodal baselines without increasing traversal time.Link
R3D: Revisiting 3D Policy LearningAnalyzes failure modes in 3D policy learning and attributes them to missing 3D augmentations and problematic batch normalization. Proposes a transformer-based 3D encoder paired with a diffusion decoder, designed for stable large-scale training, that substantially outperforms prior 3D approaches on challenging manipulation benchmarks.Link
Benchmarking Classical Coverage Path Planning Heuristics on Irregular Hexagonal Grids for Maritime Coverage ScenariosProvides a large benchmark of 10,000 Hamiltonian-feasible irregular hex-grid instances and 17 classical coverage path heuristics tailored to maritime-like areas. Results show that explicit shortest-path reconnection heuristics are reliable for relaxed coverage, while a tuned Warnsdorff variant is strongest for Hamiltonian tours, and that implementation details in residual-degree policies critically affect performance.Link
