## Executive Summary

- Mobile manipulation sees a new data-efficient framework (DockAnywhere) for viewpoint-robust visuomotor policy learning from a single demonstration.
- Dexterous manipulation is advanced via a low-cost, force-feedback teleoperation interface (DEX-Mouse) and a large-scale multimodal human–robot grasp dataset (HRDexDB).
- Regrasp and multi-step manipulation planning are tackled with differentiable object pose connectivity metrics enabling gradient-based sequence optimization.
- Whole-body and legged locomotion progress includes agile skill switching for humanoids, terrain-adaptive control for legged robots, and exploiting passive dynamics for robust bipedal walking.
- Motion and trajectory planning methods span coverage on irregular hex grids, biologically inspired navigation cells, multi-UAV payload transport, assistive trajectory optimization, and POMDP-based object search.
- A Stanford framework provides conformal-verified, vision-based human motion prediction for safer human–robot collaboration.
