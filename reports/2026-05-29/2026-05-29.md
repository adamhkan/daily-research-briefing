## Executive Summary

- Navigation and motion planning papers emphasize risk-aware control, connectivity-preserving search, and online uncertainty handling for ground, aerial, and multi-robot systems.
- Multiple works push dexterous and contact-rich manipulation via better tactile representations, contact planning, gentle force control, and diffusion-based action smoothing.
- Whole-body and locomotion control advances span humanoid loco-manipulation data generation, agile quadrupeds with active spines, and frequency/spectral priors for athletic gaits.
- Perception for navigation sees new terrain segmentation, self-supervised traversability, safer visual place recognition, and consistency-aware RGB-D odometry.
- Vision-Language(-Action) ecosystems mature with new navigation frameworks, manipulation-focused VLA architectures, and large-scale benchmarks for evaluating generalization.
