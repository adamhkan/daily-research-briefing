Executive Summary

  • Strong emphasis on dexterous and bimanual manipulation, including skill composition (DexCompose), stable in-grasp control, and human-to-robot transfer.
  • Several works push whole-body humanoid loco-manipulation and tracking, with new frameworks for coordinated upper/lower body control and contact-rich scene interaction.
  • World models and VLA architectures continue to mature for long-horizon, instruction-following manipulation, with advances in memory, grounding, redundancy analysis, and spiking implementations.
  • Navigation and SLAM see progress from dense multi-sensor fusion (LXD-SLAM) to low-latency on-device object-goal navigation (LocalNav) and energy-efficient spiking VLAs.
  • Multi-robot and multi-manipulator motion planning gains scalable, parallel, and asymptotically optimal variants of ARC for large robot teams.
  • Applied advances span aerial dynamic payload delivery, radiation-source localization, space fiducials, and socially compatible AV–human interaction models.

DexCompose: Reusing Dexterous Policies for Multi-Task Manipulation with a Single HandIntroduces DexCompose, a role-aware residual composition method that reuses pretrained dexterous hand policies to execute composite tasks without interference. By assigning finger-level action ownership and training dual residuals for stabilization and adaptation, it achieves high success on 16 multi-task manipulation benchmarks.Link

Demonstrates efficient, robust in-grasp repositioning and reorientation across objects and noise/friction conditions without sacrificing grasp stability.Link

With shared encoding and specialized decoders plus automatic role assignment, it significantly outperforms prior methods in RLBench2 and real-world bimanual tasks.Link
Translation as a Bridging Action: Transferring Manipulation Skills from Humans to RobotsArgues that full 6-DoF human action spaces transfer poorly to robots and instead uses relative wrist translation in the head-camera frame as a common action representation. A π₀-style VLA with masked action tokens leverages this for effective transfer of human bimanual skills to parallel-gripper robots on novel tasks.Link
S$^2$-VLA: State-Space Guided Vision-Language-Action Models for Long-Horizon ManipulationIntroduces S²-VLA with a State-Space Guided Adaptive Attention mechanism that maintains a belief state over task progress to dynamically fuse vision, language, and action cues. Despite a compact 2B size, it outperforms larger models on long-horizon manipulation benchmarks like LIBERO and SimplerEnv.Link
DIM-WAM: World-Action Modeling with Diverse Historical Event MemoryPresents DiM-WAM, a world-action model with multi-scale explicit memory banks that encode historical visual events, local future dynamics, and global task progress. On RMBench and real Franka tasks, it greatly improves long-horizon manipulation success compared to prior world-action baselines.Link
Direct Action-Head Injection of A Grounded 3D Point Unlocks Spatial and Task GeneralizationShows that representing grounding as a 3D point relative to the gripper and injecting it directly into the action head via adaptive layer norm dramatically boosts VLA spatial and task generalization. A small, backbone-agnostic MLP module yields large gains on LIBERO-PRO without altering pretraining.Link

SceneBot: Contact-Prompted General Humanoid Whole Body Tracking with Scene-InteractionIntroduces SceneBot, which conditions humanoid control on both reference motions and link-level contact labels to unify free-space locomotion and contact-rich manipulation.
