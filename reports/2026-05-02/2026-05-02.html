Executive Summary

  • Strong focus on contact-rich manipulation and task/motion planning, with new global trajectory optimization (Global-MPPI) and active-perception TAMP (VAP-TAMP).
  • Quadrupedal loco-manipulation advances toward whole-body mobile manipulation by integrating visuo-tactile hierarchy and large-scale RL with zero-shot sim-to-real transfer.
  • Foundation VLA and world models (LaST-R1, PRTS, MotuBrain, ExoActor) target long-horizon, instruction-following manipulation and humanoid control with improved dynamics and goal-reachability awareness.
  • Dexterous manipulation capabilities are pushed by function-based robotic hand co-design and improved modeling of redundant parallel manipulators’ internal forces.
  • New dynamics and simulation tools (RopeDreamer for deformable objects, DOT-Sim for optical tactile sensors) support more realistic planning and control in complex contact scenarios.
  • Navigation under dense dynamic obstacles benefits from hybrid latent-dynamics + MPPI methods that blend learned long-horizon intent with short-horizon physics-based safety.

Demonstrated on long-horizon tasks like PushT and dexterous in-hand manipulation, it finds higher-quality solutions with faster convergence and lower final cost than existing sampling-based baselines.Link
Can Tabular Foundation Models Guide Exploration in Robot Policy Learning?Carnegie Mellon UniversityIntroduces TFM-S3, a hybrid local-global policy optimization method that uses a pretrained tabular foundation model to cheaply screen candidate policies in a low-dimensional subspace constructed via SVD. By interleaving frequent local updates with foundation-model-guided global search, it accelerates early learning and improves final performance on continuous-control robotic benchmarks under fixed rollout budgets.Link
Robot Planning and Situation Handling with Active PerceptionCarnegie Mellon UniversityPresents VAP-TAMP, a framework that fuses task and motion planning with active perception driven by vision-language models to detect and resolve unforeseen execution-time situations. By using action knowledge to guide viewpoint selection and scene-graph reasoning, it enables mobile manipulators to handle disturbances such as blocked paths or failed actions in service tasks, both in simulation and on hardware.Link
DOT-Sim: Differentiable Optical Tactile Simulation with Precise Real-to-Sim Physical CalibrationStanford UniversityIntroduces DOT-Sim, a differentiable simulator for optical tactile sensors using Material Point Method elastodynamics and a learned residual image model to reproduce complex soft-deformation and optical responses. Rapidly calibrated from few demonstrations, it enables zero-shot transfer of classifiers and control policies, achieving high real-world accuracy on object classification, embedded tumor detection, and precise trajectory following.Link
Design and Characteristics of a Thin-Film ThermoMesh for the Efficient Embedded Sensing of a Spatio-Temporally Sparse Heat SourceNew York UniversityPresents ThermoMesh, a thin-film thermoelectric mesh that performs conduction-based thermal imaging with in-sensor compression to localize and characterize sparse heat sources. Through linear and nonlinear resistive interlayers (including NTC ceramics and VO₂), the design dramatically boosts minimum sensitivity and achieves high localization and temperature accuracy, targeting energy-efficient sensing in harsh environments.Link
