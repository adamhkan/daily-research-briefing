## Executive Summary

- New visuo-tactile gripper and sensing methods (VTAP, VTLoc, BayesContact, tensegrity and optical-fiber skins) significantly advance contact-rich and dexterous manipulation.
- Multiple works tackle navigation and exploration robustness, from humanoid path planning (RAVEN) and zero-shot object-goal navigation (SkillNav, T-DRN) to industrial UWB localization and SLAM/exploration pipelines.
- Motion planning and control see strong contributions in kinodynamic multi-objective planning (lexSST/coSST/poSST), fast task-space collision avoidance, and asymmetric Dubins airplane paths.
- Foundation VLA models and compositional training strategies (Xiaomi-Robotics-1, AC-VLA) improve mobile manipulation and out-of-distribution task execution, while new benchmarks (IMBench, reach-avoid, NeuroCommitSSM) expose remaining gaps.
- Whole-body and mobile manipulation interfaces (coupled egocentric teleoperation, NeuroCommitSSM) and morphology innovations (Handroid, CSSPD soft gripper, Opti-Hexa UAV) target practical deployment and resilience.
- Targeted data collection and active learning (contact-rich segments only, embodied active learning) improve data efficiency for manipulation and environment-specific perception under navigation and annotation budgets.
