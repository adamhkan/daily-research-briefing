## Executive Summary

- Surgeons successfully teleoperated general-purpose humanoids for laparoscopic tasks in vivo, offering a concrete benchmark of current humanoid manipulation limits in surgery.
- New dexterous-manipulation resources span large-scale benchmarks (DexVerse) and improved teleoperation/retargeting (AnyDexRT), supporting cross-task, cross-hand generalization.
- Vision-language(-action) models are being tightly integrated with memory, planning, and video-action backbones (Harness VLA, TFP, FabriVLA, LingBot-VA 2.0, APIVOT) to tackle long-horizon, contact-rich manipulation and planning.
- Navigation and motion-planning work emphasizes robustness under shift and constraints: safe tube-based control for unknown systems, radar/semantic localization and TTC-based avoidance, and stringent autonomous-driving benchmarks.
- Soft wearable and assistive devices (personalized exogloves, musculoskeletal exogloves, prosthesis privacy) target dexterous mobility and raise new safety and privacy questions for embodied AI.
- A growing focus on UAV and cooperative navigation (FSD-VLN, SASGeo, RadLoc, D-CLIPSE, bandwidth-constrained VLN) highlights semantic, communication-efficient strategies for long-horizon operation in GNSS-denied or cluttered environments.
