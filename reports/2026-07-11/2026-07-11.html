Executive Summary

  • Surgeons successfully teleoperated general-purpose humanoids for laparoscopic tasks in vivo, offering a concrete benchmark of current humanoid manipulation limits in surgery.
  • New dexterous-manipulation resources span large-scale benchmarks (DexVerse) and improved teleoperation/retargeting (AnyDexRT), supporting cross-task, cross-hand generalization.
  • Vision-language(-action) models are being tightly integrated with memory, planning, and video-action backbones (Harness VLA, TFP, FabriVLA, LingBot-VA 2.0, APIVOT) to tackle long-horizon, contact-rich manipulation and planning.
  • Navigation and motion-planning work emphasizes robustness under shift and constraints: safe tube-based control for unknown systems, radar/semantic localization and TTC-based avoidance, and stringent autonomous-driving benchmarks.
  • Soft wearable and assistive devices (personalized exogloves, musculoskeletal exogloves, prosthesis privacy) target dexterous mobility and raise new safety and privacy questions for embodied AI.
  • A growing focus on UAV and cooperative navigation (FSD-VLN, SASGeo, RadLoc, D-CLIPSE, bandwidth-constrained VLN) highlights semantic, communication-efficient strategies for long-horizon operation in GNSS-denied or cluttered environments.

DexVerse: A Modular Benchmark for Multi-Task, Multi-Embodiment Dexterous ManipulationIntroduces DexVerse, a large-scale dexterous-manipulation benchmark with 100 tasks, 3 arms, and 6 hands, covering grasping, articulated objects, tool use, bimanual and long-horizon skills. Provides controllable visual variation, rich multimodal demonstrations, and baseline results showing substantial challenges for current VLA and diffusion policies in task and embodiment generalization.Link
FabriVLA: A Lightweight Vision-Language-Action Model for Precise Multi-Task ManipulationPresents FabriVLA, a ~1B-parameter vision-language-action model using a flow-matching action head with gated self-attention and shallow VLM fusion. On Meta-World MT50 it reaches 90% tier-average success, showing that compact VLAs can deliver strong multi-task manipulation without massive backbones.Link
Harness VLA: Steering Frozen VLAs into Reliable Manipulation Primitives via Memory-Guided AgentsProposes Harness VLA, an agentic framework that wraps a frozen VLA as a retryable contact-rich primitive, composed with a small analytic primitive library for grounding, staging, navigation, and release. By learning operating ranges and failure models instead of new skills, it significantly boosts robustness to perturbations on LIBERO-Pro, RoboCasa365, and RoboTwin C2R without finetuning the VLA.Link
EgoWAM: World Action Models Beyond Pixels with In-the-Wild Egocentric Human DataStudies world representation choices for World Action Models trained on egocentric human videos for manipulation transfer. Through controlled co-training experiments, finds that predictions in DINO or 3D flow space scale better than pixel prediction, improving both out-of-distribution generalization and in-domain robot performance on bimanual tasks.Link
SkillPlug: Unsupervised Skill Mining for Few-Shot Adaptation in Robotic ManipulationNanyang Technological University; National Taiwan UniversityIntroduces SkillPlug, a plug-in skill-conditioning module that mines a compact library of reusable skills from multi-task demonstrations via self-supervised objectives. Keeping mined skills fixed, it adapts to new manipulation tasks by finetuning only lightweight routing and action heads, yielding improved multi-task performance and few-shot generalization in sim and on real robots.Link
TFP: Temporally Conditioned Memory-Fusion Policies for Visuomotor LearningTFP augments VLA backbones with an event-sensitive memory state governed by Liquid Time-Constant dynamics, directly modulating the flow-matching action decoder. This improves performance on stage-dependent, occluded manipulation tasks, raising success on LIBERO and LIBERO-plus and showing causal influence of the learned task-progress belief on generated action chunks.Link
Monocular Vision Based Control Framework for GraspingPresents a unified RGB-only grasping pipeline that handles both soft and rigid objects with a standard position-controlled gripper. It combines open-vocabulary detection, segmentation, tracking, monocular depth, and language-based stiffness estimation to choose strategies and uses deformation- or distance-based feedback for stable grasps on diverse food and household items.Link
STEMbot: A Compliant Robot for Under-Canopy Plant NavigationUniversity of MichiganIntroduces STEMbot, a miniature compliant climbing robot for navigating and inspecting plant stems under crop canopies. It integrates geometric PIN-SLAM with a semantic OcTree and a manifold-constrained A* planner with ray-traced goals, achieving reliable branch-aware traversal and accurate reconstructions needed for autonomous under-canopy navigation.Link
Shift & Drift: A Zero-Shot Benchmark for Generalizable and Robust Autonomous Driving Motion PlanningKarlsruhe Institute of TechnologyProposes Shift & Drift, a dual-track benchmark that tests closed-loop motion planners under semantic shift (German and SF city scenarios embedded in nuPlan) and injected state-distribution drift. Analysis shows imitation planners degrade sharply under shift and perturbations, while RL planners trade some ID fidelity for greater robustness, clarifying generalization challenges.Link
Latent Memory Palace: Reasoning for Control as Autoregressive Variational InferenceDevelops Latent Memory Palace, framing control reasoning as autoregressive variational inference over a latent memory space. The resulting policy exhibits adaptive test-time computation and strong performance on simulated and real control tasks, and also yields an action tokenizer that improves downstream autoregressive policy learning.Link
