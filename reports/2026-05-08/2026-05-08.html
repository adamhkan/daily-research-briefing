Executive Summary

  • Tactile sensing and control feature prominently, with new methods for high-detail tactile simulation, precision peg-in-hole assembly, and robust human-robot handovers.
  • Model-predictive and RL-based control methods advance whole-body and constrained robot control, including bipedal walking MPC and actuator-rate-safe reinforcement learning.
  • Generative and latent-model planners (HDFlow, ELVIS, Dream-MPC) push long-horizon visual and task planning toward more practical, efficient deployment.
  • New frameworks target spatiotemporal consistency and multimodal integration, from 4D VLA-based manipulation to unified mechanics-based laparoscope control.
  • Safety and specification-guided navigation are addressed via closed-form CBF safety filters on occupancy grids and language-to-logic STL architectures.
  • Fabrication research from the University of Washington demonstrates low-cost, self-folding 3D-printed robots with integrated sensing and actuation.

3D Printing of Passively Actuated Self-Folding Robots with Integrated Functional ModulesUniversity of WashingtonIntroduces an elastic-driven self-folding method that converts flat 3D-printed conductive PLA nets into functional 3D robots using routed elastic bands for passive actuation. The flat substrate integrates electrodes, sensors, and actuators, and a closed-form folding model provides a design map from geometric parameters to target fold angles, demonstrated on modular structures, a deployable gripper, and a tendon-driven finger.Link

Reduced-order Neural Modeling with Differentiable Simulation for High-Detail Tactile PerceptionChinese University of Hong Kong, Hong Kong SAR, China; The Chinese University of Hong Kong; University of Leeds; University of Manchester; University of MelbourneProposes a reduced-order neural simulation framework that couples coarse MPM dynamics with an implicit neural decoder to reconstruct fine-scale tactile deformation from compact latent states. The method yields faster, lower-memory differentiable tactile simulation with improved geometric fidelity, enabling high-detail, physically grounded contact modeling for dexterous manipulation and optimization.Link
From Reach to Insert: Tactile-Augmented Precision Assembly under Sub-Millimeter TolerancesChinese Academy of Sciences; University of Chinese Academy of SciencesIntroduces a two-stage IL+RL framework for tight-tolerance insertion, where imitation learning handles reaching and reinforcement learning performs the contact-rich insertion using tactile feedback. Novel tactile group sampling and a tactile critic improve exploration of critical contacts and value estimation, achieving high success rates and significantly reduced forces at 0.05 mm clearances.Link
Active Contact Sensing for Robust Robot-to-Human Object HandoverPresents an active sensing strategy where the robot executes information-gathering motions and observes human-applied forces to infer grasp state in handover. A Bayesian linear model over motion-force mappings distinguishes firm grasps from incidental contacts, yielding a substantial boost in handover reliability over passive baselines across varied users and objects.Link
Right Model, Right Time: Real-Time Cascaded-Fidelity MPC for Bipedal WalkingDevelops a real-time multi-phase whole-body MPC scheme that uses a detailed whole-body model in the near horizon and a simplified single-rigid-body model further ahead. Solved via SQP, it optimizes joint torques under a fixed contact schedule and target walking speed, reducing computation while preserving predictive accuracy for bipedal walking.Link
A Closed-Form Dual-Barrier CBF Safety Filter for Holonomic Robots on Incrementally Built Occupancy Grid MapsIntroduces a dual-barrier control barrier function filter that enforces safety by avoiding mapped obstacles and restricting entry into unexplored regions in occupancy grids. The closed-form velocity-space filter is lightweight enough for resource-constrained platforms and adapts frontier conservatism based on mapping progress, achieving collision-free quadrotor exploration.Link
ConsisVLA-4D: Advancing Spatiotemporal Consistency in Efficient 3D-Perception and 4D-Reasoning for Robotic ManipulationProposes ConsisVLA-4D, a VLA framework that enforces spatiotemporal consistency via modules for cross-view semantic alignment (CV-Aligner), cross-object geometric consistency (CO-Fuser), and temporally coherent reasoning (CS-Thinker). The resulting compact 3D/4D representations improve manipulation success and inference speed over OpenVLA on LIBERO and real robots.Link
HDFlow: Hierarchical Diffusion-Flow Planning for Long-horizon TasksIntroduces HDFlow, a hierarchical planner coupling a diffusion model that samples high-level latent subgoal sequences with a rectified flow model that generates low-level trajectories via ODE integration. This design leverages diffusion’s exploration and flow-based speed to handle long-horizon, sparse-reward tasks like furniture assembly and diverse locomotion/manipulation benchmarks.Link
Autonomous Laparoscope Control through Unified Mechanics-Based Representation of Multimodal Intraoperative InformationPresents a control framework that maps heterogeneous intraoperative signals—tool pose, force/torque, and images—into a unified wrench representation in task space. Through task-priority projection, the robot enforces RCM constraints, reduces trocar loads, allows compliant manual guidance, and autonomously tracks instruments in phantom and in vivo experiments.Link
From Language to Logic: A Theoretical Architecture for VLM-Grounded Safe NavigationUniversity of Maryland, College ParkOutlines an architecture that converts natural-language safety rules and operator preferences into Signal Temporal Logic constraints for navigation. Vision-language models are used for semantic scene understanding, grounding rules into cost maps and STL specifications that can be monitored and optimized during robot navigation in unstructured outdoor environments.Link
ELVIS: Ensemble-Calibrated Latent Imagination for Long-Horizon Visual MPCKU LeuvenELVIS augments latent-model MPC with Gaussian-mixture MPPI to maintain multiple hypotheses over long-horizon rollouts and an ensemble-based, uncertainty-aware lambda-return to stabilize imagination. The approach aligns actor-critic training with planning objectives and achieves state-of-the-art visual control on DeepMind Control tasks, transferring zero-shot to a challenging real sand-spraying scenario.Link
