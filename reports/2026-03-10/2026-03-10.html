Executive Summary

  • Multiple works advance dexterous and teleoperated manipulation, including new EMG- and force-feedback gloves and a visuotactile diffusion policy that explicitly grounds contact.
  • Mobile and legged manipulation see progress via scalable demonstration collection (SuperSuit) and efficient policies for opening heterogeneous articulated objects.
  • Control barrier functions and related safety concepts are extended to polytopic obstacle avoidance, distributed cooperative manipulation, and unified "control barrier corridors" for safe motion.
  • Vision-Language-Action (VLA) models are stress-tested and enhanced for robustness: to viewpoint shifts, linguistic contradictions, thermal perception, and zero-shot camera adaptation.
  • Motion planning and multi-robot coordination benefit from iterative convex MPC-DCBF formulations, STL-constrained multi-robot planning, and environment-aware toolpath generation for robotic additive manufacturing.
  • Institution-highlighted work from Stanford, ETH Zurich, NYU, MIT, and CMU emphasizes cross-embodiment transfer, zero-shot manipulation from generative video, soft-robot co-design, hierarchical latent actions, LiDAR calibration, and robustness verification for keypoint detectors.

Data Analogies Enable Efficient Cross-Embodiment TransferStanford UniversityAnalyzes how different compositions of heterogeneous demonstration data affect transfer to new robot embodiments. Finds that morphology shifts benefit most from paired "data analogies" across embodiments, improving real-world transfer by 22.5% over large, unpaired datasets without changing model architectures.Link
EmboAlign: Aligning Video Generation with Compositional Constraints for Zero-Shot ManipulationStanford UniversityUses vision-language models to extract compositional physical constraints that filter and refine rollouts from internet-pretrained video generative models. The selected rollout initializes a constraint-based trajectory optimizer, yielding a 43.3-point success improvement on six real-robot manipulation tasks without task-specific training data.Link
A Unified Low-Dimensional Design Embedding for Joint Optimization of Shape, Material, and Actuation in Soft RobotsETH ZurichProposes a structured, low-dimensional embedding that jointly parameterizes shape morphing, material distribution, and actuation fields via shared basis functions. This co-design space enables black-box optimization that consistently outperforms neural and voxel encodings across dynamic tasks using far fewer parameters.Link
Underactuated multimodal jumping robot for extraterrestrial explorationETH ZurichIntroduces a lightweight monoped that rolls, jumps, self-rights, and controls orientation using only two reaction wheels and one leg actuator. Simple mode switching between rolling and targeted jumping demonstrates versatile locomotion suitable for low-gravity exploration.Link
Hierarchical Latent Action ModelNew York UniversityBuilds a hierarchical latent action model (HiLAM) that discovers temporally extended skills from actionless videos by stacking a high-level skill layer on top of a pretrained low-level LAM. The hierarchy captures long-horizon dynamics, improving skill discovery and downstream control compared to flat LAM baselines.Link
Spatial Calibration of Diffuse LiDARsMassachusetts Institute of TechnologyPresents a simple calibration method that recovers each diffuse LiDAR pixel’s spatial footprint and sensitivity on a co-located RGB image plane using a scanned retroreflective patch. The resulting per-pixel response maps enable accurate LiDAR-RGB alignment and cross-modal fusion for wide-FOV diffuse ToF sensors.Link
From Decoupled to Coupled: Robustness Verification for Learning-based Keypoint Detection with Joint SpecificationsCarnegie Mellon UniversityIntroduces the first coupled robustness verification framework for heatmap-based keypoint detectors that jointly bounds deviation across all keypoints via a MILP formulation. By encoding inter-keypoint constraints, it certifies robustness under strict error thresholds where prior decoupled methods become overly conservative.Link

SuperSuit: An Isomorphic Bimodal Interface for Scalable Mobile ManipulationIntroduces SuperSuit, a wearable interface that unifies robot-in-the-loop teleoperation and active human demonstrations for wheeled mobile manipulators under a shared kinematic representation. By mapping human stepping to SE(2) base motion and using a delta-joint representation for the arm, it boosts demonstration throughput 2.6× while maintaining policy performance for long-horizon mobile manipulation.Link
Iterative Convex Optimization with Control Barrier Functions for Obstacle Avoidance among PolytopesProposes an MPC framework that embeds discrete-time control barrier function constraints derived from exact closest-point computations between convex polytopes. Local linearization of nonlinear dynamics and robot geometry yields iterative convex programs that achieve fast, safe navigation among polytopic obstacles with millisecond-level solve times.Link
DexEMG: Towards Dexterous Teleoperation System via EMG2Pose GeneralizationShanghai Jiao Tong UniversityPresents DexEMG, a portable teleoperation system that predicts hand kinematics directly from surface EMG using a learned EMG2Pose network trained with synchronized MoCap glove data. A retargeting algorithm maps poses to multi-finger robot hands in real time, enabling accurate, generalized dexterous teleoperation across objects and environments.Link
OpenHEART: Opening Heterogeneous Articulated Objects with a Legged ManipulatorDevelops a reinforcement learning framework for a legged manipulator to open diverse articulated objects such as doors and drawers. It introduces SAFE, a compact geometry encoder for handles and panels, and ArtIEst, which fuses proprioceptive and exteroceptive data to infer articulation parameters, enabling robust sim-to-real mobile manipulation.Link
CDF-Glove: A Cable-Driven Force Feedback Glove for Dexterous TeleoperationDescribes a low-cost (~US$230) cable-driven glove providing 20-DoF finger state sensing and force feedback for dexterous teleoperation. With 0.4° distal joint repeatability and ~200 ms haptic latency, it quadruples teleoperation task success versus no-feedback baselines and yields higher-quality demonstrations for diffusion-policy training.Link
Multi-Robot Trajectory Planning via Constrained Bayesian Optimization and Local Cost Map Learning with STL-Based Conflict ResolutionIntroduces cBOT, a constrained Bayesian Optimization-based tree planner that learns local cost maps and feasibility constraints, and STL-KCBS, a kinodynamic CBS variant that embeds Signal Temporal Logic monitoring. Together they generate efficient, STL-compliant multi-robot trajectories with improved sample efficiency and demonstrated robustness on autonomous surface vehicles.Link
Contact-Grounded Policy: Dexterous Visuotactile Policy with Generative Contact GroundingDuke University; Meta Reality Labs Research 3 University of Wisconsin–Madison /; Purdue University; University of Wisconsin-MadisonPresents Contact-Grounded Policy (CGP), a visuotactile controller that jointly forecasts future robot states and tactile signals via a conditional diffusion model and maps them to contact-consistent targets for a compliance controller. On Allegro and Tesollo hands with dense tactile sensing, CGP substantially outperforms visuomotor and prior visuotactile diffusion policies on in-hand and tool-use tasks.Link
Control Lyapunov Functions for Underactuated Soft RobotsFormulates task-space regulation and tracking for underactuated soft and soft-rigid robots by embedding a rapidly exponentially stabilizing control Lyapunov function as a convex inequality constraint. Simulations on increasingly underactuated tendon and continuum robots show improved accuracy and guaranteed Lyapunov convergence under actuator limits compared to nonlinear PD-style baselines.Link
TEGA: A Tactile-Enhanced Grasping Assistant for Assistive Robotics via Sensor Fusion and Closed-Loop Haptic FeedbackIntroduces TEGA, a teleoperation framework that fuses EMG-based intent-to-force estimation with visuotactile sensing and vibrotactile feedback via a wearable vest. The closed-loop system helps users, including those with upper-limb disabilities, modulate grasp force more accurately, improving grasp stability and task success in assistive manipulation.Link
Unified Learning of Temporal Task Structure and Action Timing for Bimanual Robot ManipulationLearns both symbolic Allen-style temporal relations and continuous timing parameters between bimanual actions from demonstrations. Using GMM-based subsymbolic timing models, DPLL-based relation assignment, and optimization-based planning, the approach generates executable, temporally parametrized plans that better match human timing than characteristic-demonstration baselines.Link
