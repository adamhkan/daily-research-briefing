Executive Summary

  • Mobile manipulation and contact-rich skills advance via symmetry-aware flow matching, follow-the-leader planning for continuum arms, and force-/tactile-driven diffusion policies for search and insertion.
  • Navigation sees new adaptive planners and learning frameworks, from UAV inspection in partially known indoor environments to LLM-designed rewards and online imitation for visual navigation.
  • VLA-based manipulation continues to mature, with predictive 3D forecasting, force-aware flow policies, geometry-aware compression, and hierarchical memory improving robustness, efficiency, and long-horizon competence.
  • Teleoperation and whole-body/whole-hand control progress through IMU-based humanoid teleop and MR-based dexterous twist retargeting, tightening the loop between human motion and robot embodiment.
  • Safety, evaluation, and infrastructure are increasingly emphasized via temporal-safety benchmarks, conservative offline value estimation, and cross-embodiment real-time I/O frameworks.
  • Perception for safe motion improves with LiDAR BEV pedestrian detection and weather-robust cross-view geo-localization, directly supporting navigation under challenging conditions.

ASIP-Planner: Adaptive Planning for UAV Surface Inspection in Partially Known Indoor EnvironmentsCarnegie Mellon UniversityProposes an adaptive UAV inspection framework that couples a segment-based global coverage planner with a local view-angle adaptation module for indoor, partially known environments. The method clusters planar surfaces into compact viewpoint sequences and adjusts view direction online to handle unforeseen occlusions, achieving near-complete coverage with shorter paths in simulation and validated real flights.Link
RIO: Flexible Real-Time Robot I/O for Cross-Embodiment Robot LearningCarnegie Mellon UniversityIntroduces RIO, a lightweight Python framework that standardizes robot I/O, teleoperation, sensor configuration, and policy deployment across diverse robot morphologies. By abstracting hardware-specific details, RIO simplifies collecting data and deploying VLAs on single-arm, bimanual, and humanoid platforms, demonstrated on multi-task household manipulation.Link

Morphologically Equivariant Flow Matching for Bimanual Mobile ManipulationIstituto Italiano di TecnologiaFormalizes morphological symmetry for bimanual mobile manipulation and enforces reflection equivariance in a C2-equivariant flow-matching policy. Symmetry-informed policies become ambidextrous, improving sample efficiency and enabling zero-shot generalization to mirrored configurations in both simulated and real TIAGo++ tasks.Link
Sampling-Based Follow-the-Leader Motion Planning for Manipulator-Mounted Continuum RobotsPresents a sampling-based planner for follow-the-leader motion of continuum robots mounted on manipulators, jointly considering continuum shape and 6-DoF base pose. By decoupling global shape search from closed-form base pose computation, the method offers resolution-complete planning with provable tip tracking and is validated in simulation and on a tendon-driven CR on a serial arm.Link
Closing the Motion Execution Gap: From Semantic Motion Task Constraints to Kinematic ControlIntroduces Motion Statecharts and the Giskard framework to bridge symbolic motion specifications and low-level kinematic control via a differentiable world model. A task-function-based lMPC controller executes semantic constraints with jerk-bounded transitions, transferring across eight robot platforms in varied environments.Link
EvoNav: Evolutionary Reward Function Design for Robot Navigation with Large Language ModelsAutomates RL reward design for robot navigation by having an LLM evolve candidate reward functions, evaluated through a staged proxy-to-full-training pipeline. The evolutionary search yields navigation policies that outperform manually crafted and prior automatic reward baselines while keeping training cost manageable.Link
NavOL: Navigation Policy with Online Imitation LearningProposes NavOL, which iteratively rolls out a pretrained navigation diffusion policy in simulation while querying a privileged global planner for optimal waypoints as online supervision. This online imitation loop avoids reward engineering and mitigates distribution shift, scaling to thousands of trajectories per hour and showing strong sim and real-world visual navigation performance.Link
DexTwist: Dexterous Hand Retargeting for Twist Motion via Mixed Reality-based TeleoperationTargets MR-based teleoperation of dexterous hands for twist-centric tasks like opening caps and turning keys by focusing on functional screw-axis alignment rather than pure pose matching. DexTwist detects tripod grasps, estimates intended twist, and refines joint trajectories with a virtual-object objective, improving turning accuracy and screw-axis stability over conventional retargeting.Link
ForceFlow: Learning to Feel and Act via Contact-Driven Flow MatchingDevelops ForceFlow, a hierarchical flow-matching framework that fuses force/torque signals and vision for contact-rich manipulation. By treating force as a global regulatory signal and separating approach and interaction phases with a Vision-to-Force handover, it significantly boosts success rates and zero-shot generalization compared to a force-aware VLA baseline.Link
SI-Diff: A Framework for Learning Search and High-Precision Insertion with a Force-Domain Diffusion PolicyQueen's UniversityUses a single force-domain diffusion policy with mode conditioning to handle both search and precise insertion in assembly tasks under pose uncertainty. A teacher-generated set of diverse demonstrations trains the policy to map tactile and velocity inputs to actions, extending tolerated misalignment and enabling zero-shot transfer to new part geometries.Link
Rainbow Deep Q-Learning with Kinematics-Aware Design for Cooperative Delta and 3-RRS Parallel Robot InsertionZhejiang UniversityCombines geometric design optimization of a 3-RRS parallel robot with Rainbow DQN to learn cooperative peg-in-hole insertion with a Delta-3-RRS pair. The tuned kinematics enlarge a safe workspace for RL exploration, and the enriched DQN with shaped rewards achieves more stable insertions and fewer constraint violations than vanilla DQN and sampling-based planning.Link
TriBand-BEV: Real-Time LiDAR-Only 3D Pedestrian Detection via Height-Aware BEV and High-Resolution Feature FusionIntroduces a three-band height-aware BEV encoding and lightweight network that reformulates 3D LiDAR detection as 2D BEV detection, then reconstructs 3D boxes. The system achieves competitive pedestrian AP on KITTI at 49 FPS, offering an efficient perception module for real-time autonomous driving and mobile robot navigation.Link
