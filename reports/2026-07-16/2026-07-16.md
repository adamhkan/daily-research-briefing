## Executive Summary

- Navigation and SLAM see multiple advances: dual-stream Mamba policies for LiDAR obstacle avoidance, pixel-level loop closures for topological shortcuts, physics-aware radar SLAM, and LLM-driven elastic multi-robot formations.
- Motion planning contributions focus on constrained, diverse trajectory generation and model-based diffusion optimal control for multi-robot systems, plus spaceborne manipulator-based attitude maneuvers.
- Mobile and aerial platforms gain stronger capabilities via robust off-road multimodal navigation, nighttime agricultural visual navigation, tight quadrotor formation flight with learned residual dynamics, and unified UAV tracking-to-terminal guidance.
- Manipulation and dexterous control work span 3D Gaussian-grounded VLA policies, dense reward learning from synthesized failures, in-hand rolling with physics priors, and improved progress/value estimation for contact-rich tasks.
- Whole-body and loco-manipulation are extended by humanoid dribbling with privileged visual representations and GaitSpan’s single policy spanning walking to running regimes.
- VLA and robot-learning infrastructure advances address dense rewards, structured exploration, temporal redundancy in inference, and instance-enriched semantic maps for visual-language navigation.
