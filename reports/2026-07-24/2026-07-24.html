Executive Summary

  • Strong focus on safe and robust navigation and motion planning, from social navigation and V2X driving to UAVs under spoofing and vehicle drifting on ice.
  • Multiple works advance embodied navigation benchmarks and cross-embodiment policies, including indoor–outdoor NavVerse, embodiment-aware EA-Nav, and referring-based visual tracking in ReferTrack.
  • Mobile and whole-body control is extended to new platforms and use cases, such as a low-cost robotic guide dog (Milo), miniature humanoid tele-loco-manipulation, and versatile limbless locomotion (Morphing MILR).
  • Manipulation research emphasizes data efficiency and abstraction, with one-shot skill acquisition from a single human video (HOST), language-guided multi-embodiment grasping (SeededGrasp), and LLM-guided environment simplification (LENS).
  • New control and learning frameworks tackle safety and recovery: distributed MPC+CBF for multi-robot boats, spoofing-aware UAV trajectory planning, human-derived recovery behaviors (EgoRecovery), and contact-persistent full actuation for aerial interaction.
  • MIT contributes both to distributed motion planning for aquatic robots and to soft-robot-enabled distributed acoustic localization, underscoring institutional strength in field robotics and sensing.

Distributed Motion Planning with Safety Guarantees for Self-Reconfiguring Robotic BoatsMassachusetts Institute of TechnologyIntroduces a hybrid distributed framework for multi-robot aquatic shape formation that combines ADMM-based distributed MPC with local CBF filters to guarantee inter-robot collision avoidance. The approach mitigates nonconvex local minima while maintaining real-time safety, and is validated in simulation with up to 25 agents and on hardware with four robotic boats.Link
Distributed Acoustic Localization Array Deployed Using a Soft Everting Vine RobotMassachusetts Institute of TechnologyPresents a distributed microphone array integrated along a soft everting vine robot for victim localization in confined spaces, using a dynamic SRP-PHAT framework for both far-field DOA and near-field 3D localization. Experiments across array placements and robot shapes show that accurate localization is possible even with partially deployed arrays, highlighting soft robots as carriers for adaptable sensing architectures.Link

Towards Miniature Humanoid Tele-Loco-Manipulation Using Virtual Reality and Reinforcement LearningUniversity of LouisvilleDevelops a full-body telepresence control stack for miniature humanoids that couples VR-based upper-body teleoperation with RL-based lower-body locomotion. Demonstrations on ROBOTIS OP3 show compliant walking up to 0.45 m/s and tele-loco-manipulation in a cube relocation task, making advanced whole-body humanoid control more accessible.Link
SeededGrasp: Language-Guided Grasping in Complex Scenes with Multiple EmbodimentsProposes a two-stage grasping framework where a VLM predicts a semantic seed point conditioned on language, which then guides a lightweight grasp generator. By decoupling high-level reasoning from low-level geometry and training on a new 2.5M-grasp multi-embodiment dataset, SeededGrasp improves success in cluttered tabletop scenes in both simulation and real robots.Link
Unified Prediction and Planning via Conflict-Aware Disjoint Parameter TrainingKorea Advanced Institute of Science and TechnologyIdentifies "Skill Conflict" in compact unified models that jointly perform motion prediction and navigation planning, where overlapping parameters harm specialization. Introduces Disjoint Parameter Training, a model-merging framework with sparse parameter fusion that alleviates conflicts and improves crowd-navigation performance on JRDB and JTA benchmarks.Link
EA-Nav: Learning Safe Visual Navigation Policies with Embodiment AwarenessZhejiang UniversityPresents an imitation-learning-based navigation framework that conditions policies on explicit embodiment geometry tokens to resolve action ambiguity across different robots. Using Internet-scale cross-embodiment video data and risk-aware correction modules, EA-Nav improves cross-embodiment navigation safety and performance without extensive RL interaction.Link
SOPD-SocialNav: Selective On-Policy Distillation for Vision-Language Social NavigationNVIDIAIntroduces selective on-policy distillation from a large VLM teacher to a lightweight student for socially aware navigation, using entropy-based token selection and JS-divergence on informative decisions. The distilled model outperforms standard distillation and supervised baselines on social navigation benchmarks and runs in real time on a small robot.Link
NavVerse: Benchmarking Indoor-to-Outdoor Embodied Navigation in Continuous Robot SimulationIntroduces NavVerse, a physics-based benchmark with 10,000 episodes spanning indoor, outdoor, and indoor-to-outdoor tasks for object, language, and place navigation. Evaluation of RL, VLA, and modular baselines reveals large performance gaps in cross-context navigation, especially for indoor-to-outdoor adaptation and safety under kinodynamic constraints.Link
Remote ID Spoofing-Aware Trajectory Planning for Small Unmanned Aerial SystemsProposes a decentralized trajectory planner for UAVs that explicitly models potential spoofing in Remote ID broadcasts by fusing signal-strength measurements to detect and localize spoofers. A chance-constrained formulation turns uncertainty into risk-bounded unsafe regions in an MDP planner, reducing near mid-air collisions in multi-UAV simulations.Link
LENS: LLM-guided Environment Simplification for Planning and Control in ClutterIntroduces LENS, a plug-in module that uses LLMs to generate task-specific scene abstractions by pruning or merging objects in cluttered manipulation environments. By dynamically simplifying the world model in closed loop, LENS boosts success of classical planners, model-based controllers, and VLAs across complex multi-object tasks.Link
Milo, a Fully Autonomous Indoor/Outdoor Robotic Guide DogPresents Milo, a low-cost Unitree Go2-based robotic guide dog with fully onboard perception and navigation for assisting blind and low-vision users indoors and outdoors. A voxel-based mapping and obstacle/pedestrian detection stack feeds a learned obstacle-avoidance policy, achieving smoother and safer navigation than a costmap baseline; hardware and software are open-sourced.Link
ReferTrack: Referring Then Tracking for Embodied Visual TrackingProposes a two-stage embodied tracking policy that first selects the language-described target among detected boxes and then outputs waypoints conditioned on this grounded decision. Temporal-viewpoint-bbox indicator tokens maintain target context over time, yielding state-of-the-art single-camera performance on EVT-Bench and successful deployment on legged and humanoid robots.Link
