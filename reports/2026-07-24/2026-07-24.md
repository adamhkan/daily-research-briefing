## Executive Summary

- Strong focus on safe and robust navigation and motion planning, from social navigation and V2X driving to UAVs under spoofing and vehicle drifting on ice.
- Multiple works advance embodied navigation benchmarks and cross-embodiment policies, including indoor–outdoor NavVerse, embodiment-aware EA-Nav, and referring-based visual tracking in ReferTrack.
- Mobile and whole-body control is extended to new platforms and use cases, such as a low-cost robotic guide dog (Milo), miniature humanoid tele-loco-manipulation, and versatile limbless locomotion (Morphing MILR).
- Manipulation research emphasizes data efficiency and abstraction, with one-shot skill acquisition from a single human video (HOST), language-guided multi-embodiment grasping (SeededGrasp), and LLM-guided environment simplification (LENS).
- New control and learning frameworks tackle safety and recovery: distributed MPC+CBF for multi-robot boats, spoofing-aware UAV trajectory planning, human-derived recovery behaviors (EgoRecovery), and contact-persistent full actuation for aerial interaction.
- MIT contributes both to distributed motion planning for aquatic robots and to soft-robot-enabled distributed acoustic localization, underscoring institutional strength in field robotics and sensing.
