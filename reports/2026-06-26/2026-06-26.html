Executive Summary

  • Strong emphasis on navigation and spatial reasoning: multiple works advance semantic/LLM-guided navigation, active SLAM, and crowd- or surveillance-aided planning.
  • Mobile and dexterous manipulation remains highly active, with new methods for deformable objects, forceful control via sEMG, dynamic mobile grasping, and spatially grounded language-conditioned policies.
  • Whole-body and locomotion control progresses through morphology-aware perception integration, RL+MPPI hybrid control, fault-tolerant and energy- or preference-aware locomotion, and extreme-terrain quadruped policies.
  • Vision-Language-Action models are being rethought with action priors, reflective histories, in-context world modeling, delay-aware adapters, and more efficient or robust RL fine-tuning and online adaptation.
  • Safety, estimation, and infrastructure for long-term autonomy see advances in causality-aware control barrier functions, multi-modal localization, ultra-fast LiDAR-inertial odometry, and communication-efficient multi-robot mapping.
  • Multi-robot and swarm settings extend into air-ground tunnel inspection, drone and flying-speck choreographies, and automated VLA testing/oracle generation.

DynaMOMA: Instantaneous Prediction of Grasp Poses for Mobile Manipulation of Dynamic ObjectsCouples an anchor-based diffusion model for short-horizon dynamic grasp trajectory prediction with a whole-body RL controller for a mobile manipulator. The system anticipates target motion and adaptively shifts grasping targets, achieving robust dynamic object capture in simulation and real-world tests.Link
RoboAtlas: Contextual Active SLAMIntroduces an Active SLAM framework that balances geometric frontier exploration with large-scale 3D semantic mapping using OpenRoboVox. A contextual multi-armed bandit selects between exploration and VLM-guided semantic navigation, yielding state-of-the-art performance on GOAT-Bench and robust real-world operation on a Unitree Go2.Link
Learning Robot Visual Navigation in Crowds via Intention-Aware Scene RepresentationsProposes iCrowdNav, a DRL-based crowd navigation policy that uses a spatio-temporal encoder and an attention-based Intent-Interact Former to infer human motion intentions from egocentric vision. The resulting compact state embedding improves safety and efficiency in dense pedestrian environments, validated in simulation and real-world deployment.Link
SAGE-Nav: Leveraging LLM Planning and Alignment Fusion for Hierarchical Scene Graph-Guided NavigationSAGE-Nav combines an LLM global planner with a hierarchical scene graph encoder and a goal-aware fusion network to bridge semantic planning and low-level control for object-goal navigation. It achieves state-of-the-art efficiency and zero-shot generalization in i-THOR and RoboTHOR while maintaining low control latency.Link
RAVEN: Long-Horizon Reasoning & Navigation with a Visuo-Spatio-Temporal MemoryRAVEN stores visual embeddings indexed by pose and time in a vector database, enabling accurate semantic, spatial, and temporal retrieval without lossy captioning. It supports long-horizon QA and language-goal navigation on a Unitree Go1 across large environments at substantially reduced retrieval cost.Link
SurveilNav: Collaborative Object Goal Navigation with Robot and Surveillance SystemBeihang University; Chinese Academy of Sciences; University of Chinese Academy of SciencesIntroduces a large-scale indoor dataset with dense surveillance cameras and a collaborative navigation framework that jointly schedules cameras, builds 2D/3D maps, and uses VLM-based value estimation. By fusing robot and surveillance views, SurveilNav significantly improves exploration efficiency and object-goal success rates in multi-floor environments.Link
Event-Adaptive Motion Planning with Distilled Vision-Language Model in Safety-Critical SituationsChinese Academy of Sciences; Gong are with the Southern University; S-TUBITAK Joint Call under the International Partnership Program of the Chinese AcademyEAMP introduces a prompt-configurable event trigger that detects behavioral anomalies and activates a distilled SemNav-VLM only when needed, reducing latency. A semantic MPC layer maps high-level strategies into replanned trajectories, boosting safety margins in dynamic logistics scenarios without sacrificing real-time performance.Link
Large-Scale Tunnel Air-Ground Collaboration With FLISP: Fast LiDAR-IMU Synchronized Path PlannerKey Laboratory of Autonomous Intelligent Unmanned Systems, Tongji University, Shanghai 201210, ChinaFLISP is a mapless LiDAR-IMU-based planner that jointly generates paths for a UGV-UAV pair using a single synchronized sensor suite. It replaces slow grid or sampling-based methods with tailored solvers and hierarchical refinement, achieving 7 ms latency and 100% success in 1.2 km hydropower tunnel inspections.Link
MIL-LC: A Robust Magnetometer-Inertial-LiDAR Fusion Multimodal Localization FrameworkNanyang Technological University; National Taiwan UniversityMIL-LC fuses ambient magnetic field measurements with LiDAR and IMU in a custom sensor suite to maintain localization in GNSS-denied, repetitive, or textureless environments. It remains robust when LiDAR degenerates or magnetic maps drift, supporting reliable AMR navigation over long deployments.Link
FAR-LIO: Enabling High-Speed Autonomy through Fast, Accurate, and Robust LiDAR-Inertial OdometryFAR-LIO is a CUDA-accelerated LiDAR-inertial odometry system featuring a voxel hashmap, sparsity-aware GICP, and EKF fusion with delay compensation. Evaluated up to 250 km/h on racecars, it reduces positional error and runtime versus state-of-the-art, enabling low-latency odometry for aggressive navigation.Link
