Executive Summary

  • Strong focus on dexterous and whole-body manipulation: new frameworks span bimanual piano playing, humanoid loco-manipulation, and concurrent prehensile/nonprehensile tasks.
  • Motion planning advances include ADMM-based continuous trajectory optimization, tensor-compressed configuration-space exploration, and a robust reduced-space trajectory optimizer.
  • Humanoid and hand hardware/teleop pipelines (SPARK, HumDex, CRAFT, vision-based hand shadowing) lower the barrier for collecting high-quality whole-body and hand manipulation data.
  • Foundation and agentic VLA models (Ψ₀, HumDex’s IL, SaPaVe, RoboClaw, SELF-VLA, SVLL) target long-horizon, active, and contact-rich manipulation with improved data efficiency and robustness.
  • Reliability and safety around learned policies are addressed via deployment-time mechanisms, anomaly detection, load distribution theory, and data-grounded generalization analysis.
  • Autonomous data engines (RADAR, RoboClaw) and evaluation tools (RADAR analysis, ActiveManip-Bench, LIBERO-Anomaly-10) emphasize scalable, closed-loop data generation and benchmarking.

Structured spatial refinement and residual RL enable successful execution of five songs with only ~30 minutes of real data, achieving 1.8× better hardware performance than direct sim-to-real transfer.Link
SPARK: Skeleton-Parameter Aligned Retargeting on Humanoid Robots with Kinodynamic Trajectory OptimizationUC BerkeleyProposes a URDF-based skeleton alignment scheme plus staged kinodynamic trajectory optimization to retarget human motion onto diverse humanoids. The method reduces IK error and tuning effort while producing dynamically feasible motion and torque profiles suitable as references for learning-based humanoid controllers.Link
ADMM-based Continuous Trajectory Optimization in Graphs of Convex SetsMassachusetts Institute of TechnologyDevelops an ADMM-based solver that represents trajectories as polynomials and couples them with a spatio-temporal allocation graph derived from a mixed-integer formulation. By jointly optimizing discrete spatial choices and continuous time, it finds higher-quality trajectories in non-convex environments and converges reliably from naive initializations.Link
Grounding Robot Generalization in Training Data via Retrieval-Augmented VLMsStanford UniversityIntroduces RADAR, a retrieval-augmented VLM pipeline for characterizing how evaluation tasks relate to a policy’s training distribution. By retrieving similar training episodes and using VLM analysis, it classifies the type of generalization required and provides interpretable diagnostics for robot manipulation benchmarks.Link
Vision-Based Hand Shadowing for Robotic Manipulation via Inverse KinematicsUC BerkeleyPresents a low-cost egocentric RGB-D teleoperation pipeline that tracks human hands with MediaPipe, lifts to 3D, and solves IK in PyBullet to control a 6-DOF manipulator and gripper. Achieves 90% success on structured pick-and-place and compares against several VLA policies, while revealing limitations under occlusion in clutter.Link
Real-time Rendering-based Surgical Instrument Tracking via Evolutionary OptimizationUC San DiegoCombines CMA-ES with batch rendering to jointly estimate pose and joint angles of articulated surgical tools from images. The evolutionary rendering-in-the-loop approach improves robustness and runtime over prior vision-based trackers, supporting both mono- and bi-manual tracking in surgical settings.Link

This residual formulation stabilizes optimization, improves sample efficiency and returns on DeepMind Control, and yields smoother, more energy-efficient control trajectories for robotics.Link
