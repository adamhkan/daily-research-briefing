Executive Summary

  • Humanoid and whole-body control advances emphasize trajectory optimization, egocentric video learning, and force-adaptive RL to support more natural and robust interaction during locomotion and bimanual tasks.
  • New frameworks in VLA-based manipulation (neuro-symbolic reasoning, memory, LoRA experts, progress-aware planning) substantially improve long-horizon, multi-task, and data-efficient manipulation performance.
  • Risk-bounded and ergodic motion planning methods provide provably safe manipulator trajectories under uncertainty and SE(3)-aware surface coverage with strong real-world validation.
  • Sensor and hardware innovations—including dual-modal visuo-tactile sensing, efficient visuotactile calibration, and characterization/control of commercial dexterous hands—enhance contact-rich manipulation capabilities.
  • Data-centric robot learning progresses via influence-function-based demo selection, portable teleoperation for in-the-wild data, and autonomous play-driven world models for contact-rich interactions.
  • Low-cost mobile manipulation and modular task-and-motion planning systems lower deployment barriers, while scene cloning and visual prompt editing improve zero-shot adaptation to new environments.

Stein Variational Ergodic Surface Coverage with SE(3) ConstraintsUC BerkeleyIntroduces an SE(3)-aware Stein Variational Gradient Descent method for ergodic trajectory generation over 3D point-cloud surfaces. The approach reformulates coverage as a manifold sampling problem with SE(3)-specific updates and preconditioning, yielding better local optima and coverage quality for surface manipulation and drawing tasks in both simulation and real-robot experiments.Link
Quality over Quantity: Demonstration Curation via Influence Functions for Data-Centric Robot LearningNew York University; UC BerkeleyProposes QoQ, an influence-function-based method to score and select high-quality demonstration samples for robot learning. By aggregating influence across state-action pairs and trajectories, the framework systematically curates data that most improves validation performance, consistently outperforming prior heuristic selection strategies in both simulated and real manipulation settings.Link
Improving through Interaction: Searching Behavioral Representation Spaces with CMA-ES-IGMassachusetts Institute of TechnologyIntroduces CMA-ES-IG, an evolutionary strategy that selects robot behaviors for user ranking by explicitly trading off informativeness and user experience. The method scales to high-dimensional preference spaces, remains robust to noisy feedback, and is preferred by non-expert users compared to existing preference-learning query strategies.Link
Robust Spatiotemporal Motion Planning for Multi-Agent Autonomous Racing via Topological Gap Identification and Accelerated MPCETH ZurichPresents a racing framework that identifies topological gaps in predicted opponent occupancies and plans overtakes via a fast Linear Time-Varying MPC with a Pseudo-Transient Continuation solver. The system achieves markedly faster maneuvers and higher overtaking success rates on F1TENTH compared to prior planners while respecting kinematic constraints.Link
Fly, Track, Land: Infrastructure-less Magnetic Localization for Heterogeneous UAV-UGV TeamingETH ZurichDemonstrates a compact magneto-inductive localization system that enables a UAV to hover, track, and land on a moving quadruped UGV with centimeter-level accuracy. A single receive coil and embedded estimation pipeline provide short-range 6-DoF relative pose at 20 Hz, validated in dynamic docking experiments without external infrastructure.Link

On the NIST M1 peg-in-hole benchmark it reaches 90% success, maintaining 80% at 0.1 mm clearance, substantially outperforming vision-only policies.Link
