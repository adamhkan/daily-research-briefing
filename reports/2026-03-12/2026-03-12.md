## Executive Summary

- Humanoid and whole-body control advances emphasize trajectory optimization, egocentric video learning, and force-adaptive RL to support more natural and robust interaction during locomotion and bimanual tasks.
- New frameworks in VLA-based manipulation (neuro-symbolic reasoning, memory, LoRA experts, progress-aware planning) substantially improve long-horizon, multi-task, and data-efficient manipulation performance.
- Risk-bounded and ergodic motion planning methods provide provably safe manipulator trajectories under uncertainty and SE(3)-aware surface coverage with strong real-world validation.
- Sensor and hardware innovations—including dual-modal visuo-tactile sensing, efficient visuotactile calibration, and characterization/control of commercial dexterous hands—enhance contact-rich manipulation capabilities.
- Data-centric robot learning progresses via influence-function-based demo selection, portable teleoperation for in-the-wild data, and autonomous play-driven world models for contact-rich interactions.
- Low-cost mobile manipulation and modular task-and-motion planning systems lower deployment barriers, while scene cloning and visual prompt editing improve zero-shot adaptation to new environments.
