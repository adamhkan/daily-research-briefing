Executive Summary

  • Motion planning and navigation dominate today’s robotics papers, spanning stochastic kinodynamic planning, STL-based planning/control, POMDP interception in crowds, and long-horizon UAV navigation with learned world models.
  • Formal methods and safety appear repeatedly, from STL-constrained planning and behavior tree synthesis to theoretical limits of sampling-based reachability and integrity-gated eco-driving.
  • Learning-centric approaches for locomotion and control include torque-driven quadruped RL, Koopman-based balancing, and contact-implicit MPC for hybrid aerial–terrestrial robots.
  • Embodied world modeling and vision-language systems advance via unified world action models, masked visual actions, STL-structured VLM control, and test-time scaled VLMs for UAV navigation.
  • Mobile and multi-agent systems see progress in GPS-denied UAV geo-localization, hierarchical LLM+DRL multi-UAV navigation, confidence-gated vision for UAV–UGV cooperation, and eco-CACC under model integrity checks.
  • Manipulation and dexterity are addressed through underactuated hand design, twist-space task decomposition, and rich intermediate representations/datasets for manipulation reasoning and control.

By planning over closed-loop policies and using Monte Carlo particle rollouts for on-tree risk evaluation, the methods yield Pareto fronts of probabilistically safe trajectories with finite-sample chance-constraint guarantees.Link

A task-specific planner generates detours offline while an online CBF-QP regulates joint velocities, achieving millimeter-level accuracy and zero sampled self-collisions across large simulated task sets.Link
