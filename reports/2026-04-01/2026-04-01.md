## Executive Summary

- NYU and CMU feature prominently today with advances in model-based manipulation planning, humanoid locomotion control, and hybrid air–ground actuation.
- Multiple works push long-horizon and safety-aware motion planning, including spectral inverse-dynamics exploration, dynamic roadmap validation, topological diffusion planning for tethers, and HJ-based contingency planning.
- Dexterous and contact-rich manipulation see notable hardware and sim contributions: tactile teleop gloves, visuotactile RL simulation, anisotropic friction fingertips, and stiffness-aware soft grippers.
- Teleoperation, vision-language-action, and RL methods increasingly target dynamic, long-horizon manipulation and shared autonomy, including dynamic catching and progress-guided VLA policies.
- Several papers advance learning stability and efficiency in control and RL, from dual-stream replay and analytical exploration to differentiable meta-tuning, tokenization-free flow policies, and experience-efficient demo-augmented RL.
- Benchmarks and infrastructure (ManipArena, ROSClaw, multi-view data synthesis, embodied localization) continue to mature evaluation and deployment pipelines for manipulation and mobile manipulation.
