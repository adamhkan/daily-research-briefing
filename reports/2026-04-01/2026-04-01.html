Executive Summary

  • NYU and CMU feature prominently today with advances in model-based manipulation planning, humanoid locomotion control, and hybrid air–ground actuation.
  • Multiple works push long-horizon and safety-aware motion planning, including spectral inverse-dynamics exploration, dynamic roadmap validation, topological diffusion planning for tethers, and HJ-based contingency planning.
  • Dexterous and contact-rich manipulation see notable hardware and sim contributions: tactile teleop gloves, visuotactile RL simulation, anisotropic friction fingertips, and stiffness-aware soft grippers.
  • Teleoperation, vision-language-action, and RL methods increasingly target dynamic, long-horizon manipulation and shared autonomy, including dynamic catching and progress-guided VLA policies.
  • Several papers advance learning stability and efficiency in control and RL, from dual-stream replay and analytical exploration to differentiable meta-tuning, tokenization-free flow policies, and experience-efficient demo-augmented RL.
  • Benchmarks and infrastructure (ManipArena, ROSClaw, multi-view data synthesis, embodied localization) continue to mature evaluation and deployment pipelines for manipulation and mobile manipulation.

Spectral Decomposition of Inverse Dynamics for Fast Exploration in Model-Based ManipulationNew York UniversityIntroduces a search-tree planner that uses the spectral decomposition of the inverse dynamics map (actuator to object displacement) as an orthogonal exploration basis. This enables dynamically feasible, long-horizon contact-rich manipulation plans (45 s, 10+ contact modes) to be generated in roughly 15 s, integrating seamlessly with methods like RRT.Link
Cost-Matching Model Predictive Control for Efficient Reinforcement Learning in Humanoid LocomotionNew York UniversityProposes a cost-matching framework that trains an MPC with centroidal dynamics to approximate a high-fidelity RL action-value function. By learning MPC cost parameters from data without repeatedly solving MPC during training, the method improves humanoid locomotion robustness to model mismatch and disturbances.Link
Learning Energy-Efficient Air--Ground Actuation for Hybrid Robots on Stair-Like TerrainCarnegie Mellon UniversityTrains a single continuous RL policy that coordinates propellers, wheels, and tilt servos on a hybrid air–ground robot, using energy-aware rewards derived from calibrated thrust/power models. The learned controller discovers thrust-assisted driving strategies that are up to 4× more energy-efficient in simulation and 38% lower power than a rule-based baseline on hardware.Link
Rainbow-DemoRL: Combining Improvements in Demonstration-Augmented Reinforcement LearningUC San DiegoSystematically compares strategies for leveraging offline demonstrations to improve online RL sample efficiency. Finds that directly reusing offline data and initializing with behavior cloning consistently outperform more complex offline RL pretraining recipes, and identifies effective hybrids for demonstration-augmented control.Link
Control Without Control: Defining Implicit Interaction Paradigms for Autonomous Assistive RobotsCarnegie Mellon UniversityAnalyzes two assistive robot systems to articulate an "implicit control" interaction paradigm, where robot behavior adapts to natural user cues rather than explicit commands. The study highlights how such designs can reduce perceived workload while preserving users’ sense of control via responsiveness, contextual awareness, and preference adaptation.Link
TerraSkipper: A Centimeter-Scale Robot for Multi-Terrain Skipping and CrawlingCarnegie Mellon UniversityPresents a 3D-printed centimeter-scale robot that combines fin-based crawling with a high-power rotary springtail to execute impulsive skipping across diverse terrains. Experimental characterization shows skipping outperforms crawling on viscous and granular media, extending mobility on substrates where standard crawling fails.Link

With the TacAlign procedure to reduce sim-to-real domain gaps, policies trained on contact-rich tasks like peg insertion transfer zero-shot with high real-world success.Link
