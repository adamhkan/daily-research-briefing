## Executive Summary

- ETH Zurich presents UMBRELLA, an uncertainty-aware multi-robot coordination framework using LTL tasks and conformal prediction to cut makespan and variance in dynamic environments.
- Multiple works advance motion planning and control with learning-based representations, including connectivity-aware constrained planning and fast one-step diffusion over movement primitives.
- Vision-Language-Action (VLA) models continue to mature, with several papers improving long-horizon manipulation, robustness via 3D scene flow, and sensing via thermal cues.
- New methods target mobile and aerial manipulation: minimum-energy whole-body control for redundant mobile manipulators and physics-guided transfer of VLA policies to aerial pick-and-place.
- Embodied and musculoskeletal control progresses with MuscleMimic, enabling scalable, physiologically realistic full-body motor learning on GPUs.
- Human-in-the-loop manipulation and teleoperation benefit from AR visualizations of impedance control and language-conditioned optical-flow trajectory generation.
