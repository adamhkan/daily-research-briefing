## Executive Summary

- Soft, sensorless musculoskeletal hands and text-driven whole-body humanoid control highlight new hardware and control paradigms for safe, compliant interaction.
- Generalist neural motion planning and knowledge- or language-guided planners advance long-horizon, clutter-robust manipulation in both tabletop and 3D warehouse-like settings.
- New control-theoretic tools (CBFs for singularity avoidance, parallel differential IK) and reachability learning target safer, more efficient manipulation and harvesting.
- VLA-focused work (3D-Mix, SOMA, TAG, event-driven assistance, episodic memory) improves robustness, spatial grounding, and long-horizon reliability in foundation-model policies.
- Large-scale trajectory generation and time-optimal spline planning demonstrate scalable motion planning for swarms and mobile robots with continuous safety guarantees.
- Learning-guided multi-agent path finding from MIT offers higher-throughput warehouse navigation through RL-informed priority assignment.
