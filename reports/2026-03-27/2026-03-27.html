Executive Summary

  • Soft, sensorless musculoskeletal hands and text-driven whole-body humanoid control highlight new hardware and control paradigms for safe, compliant interaction.
  • Generalist neural motion planning and knowledge- or language-guided planners advance long-horizon, clutter-robust manipulation in both tabletop and 3D warehouse-like settings.
  • New control-theoretic tools (CBFs for singularity avoidance, parallel differential IK) and reachability learning target safer, more efficient manipulation and harvesting.
  • VLA-focused work (3D-Mix, SOMA, TAG, event-driven assistance, episodic memory) improves robustness, spatial grounding, and long-horizon reliability in foundation-model policies.
  • Large-scale trajectory generation and time-optimal spline planning demonstrate scalable motion planning for swarms and mobile robots with continuous safety guarantees.
  • Learning-guided multi-agent path finding from MIT offers higher-throughput warehouse navigation through RL-informed priority assignment.

A Sensorless, Inherently Compliant Anthropomorphic Musculoskeletal Hand Driven by Electrohydraulic ActuatorsETH ZurichPresents an anthropomorphic, tendon-driven musculoskeletal hand actuated by remote Peano-HASEL electrohydraulic modules, prioritizing intrinsic compliance and safety over payload. A pulley-based tendon routing amplifies limited actuator stroke, and self-sensing via current monitoring enables contact-aware, closed-loop grasping without external force or position sensors, successfully manipulating fragile objects.Link
Toward Generalist Neural Motion Planners for Robotic Manipulators: Challenges and OpportunitiesNew York UniversityReviews the state of neural motion planners for robotic manipulators, analyzing how learned planners address high-dimensional, multi-modal planning and where they fail to generalize to out-of-distribution scenarios. The paper outlines open challenges and a roadmap toward truly generalist neural motion planners that can serve as low-level modules in broader manipulation frameworks.Link
Learning-guided Prioritized Planning for Lifelong Multi-Agent Path Finding in Warehouse AutomationMassachusetts Institute of TechnologyIntroduces RL-RH-PP, which augments classical prioritized planning for warehouse multi-robot navigation with a learned, attention-based priority assignment policy. Framed as a POMDP, the learned policy improves long-horizon throughput by proactively decongesting traffic and generalizes across robot densities, horizons, and layouts.Link
Evidence of an Emergent "Self" in Continual Robot LearningNew York UniversityProposes an operational definition of a robot "self" as the most invariant subnetwork within a continually adapting policy. Empirical comparisons show that robots undergoing continual learning develop a significantly more stable subnetwork than those trained on a fixed task, suggesting an emergent internal structure analogous to selfhood.Link
CoordLight: Learning Decentralized Coordination for Network-Wide Traffic Signal ControlMassachusetts Institute of TechnologyPresents CoordLight, a MARL framework for large-scale adaptive traffic signal control using queue-based state encoding and an attention-driven Neighbor-aware Policy Optimization algorithm. The method yields coordinated, decentralized control across up to 196 intersections, outperforming prior approaches on real-world traffic datasets.Link

SafeFlow: Real-Time Text-Driven Humanoid Whole-Body Control via Physics-Guided Rectified Flow and Selective Safety GatingSafeFlow combines a physics-guided rectified flow motion generator with a three-stage safety gate to turn free-form text prompts into physically feasible humanoid whole-body trajectories. On the Unitree G1, it improves success rate, physical compliance, and inference speed over prior diffusion-based controllers while filtering unsafe or out-of-distribution commands.Link
Grounding Vision and Language to 3D Masks for Long-Horizon Box RearrangementProposes RAMP-3D, which uses a 3D vision-language model to ground language into paired 3D masks specifying what to pick and where to place, effectively turning long-horizon box rearrangement into reactive mask prediction. The system achieves high success on warehouse-style rearrangement with up to 30 objects, outperforming 2D VLM baselines without explicit symbolic planning.Link
Knowledge-Guided Manipulation Using Multi-Task Reinforcement LearningMohamed bin Zayed University of Artificial IntelligenceIntroduces KG-M3PO, which augments egocentric vision with an online 3D scene graph and dynamic relational edges to provide structured world knowledge for multi-task manipulation RL. A graph neural encoder and unified latent space over visual, proprioceptive, linguistic, and graph inputs yield better sample efficiency, success rates, and generalization under occlusion and partial observability.Link
Chameleon: Episodic Memory for Long-Horizon Robotic ManipulationNanyang Technological University; National Taiwan UniversityChameleon implements geometry-grounded multimodal episodic memory with a differentiable stack to retain disambiguating context for manipulation tasks suffering from perceptual aliasing. On the Camo-Dataset of real UR5e tasks, it improves reliability and long-horizon performance over standard memory and retrieval baselines.Link
Event-Driven Proactive Assistive Manipulation with Grounded Vision-Language PlanningUniversity of OsakaShifts collaborative manipulation assistance from instruction-driven to event-driven by triggering robot planning on detected human-induced state transitions. Using pre/post visual snapshots and a grounded VLM planner over discrete action primitives, the system proactively completes solvable tabletop tasks and appropriately refrains on unsolvable scenes.Link
Learning What Can Be Picked: Active Reachability Estimation for Efficient Robotic Fruit HarvestingFormulates reachability as a binary prediction problem learned from RGB-D observations, replacing repeated IK or motion planning checks in fruit harvesting. Active learning strategies select informative samples, achieving higher reachability accuracy with fewer labels and enabling fast, task-level decisions about which fruits to target.Link
Task-Space Singularity Avoidance for Control Affine Systems Using Control Barrier FunctionsJohns Hopkins University; University of Maryland, College ParkDevelops a control barrier function framework that treats task-space singularities as unsafe regions derived from the eigenvalues of the input-output mapping. The method enforces a safety margin from rank-deficient configurations, enabling smooth trajectory tracking for manipulators and other control-affine systems while greatly reducing control spikes.Link
MIRROR: Visual Motion Imitation via Real-time Retargeting and Teleoperation with Parallel Differential Inverse KinematicsCalifornia Institute of Technology; Technology Innovation InstituteMIRROR introduces a GPU-parallel continuation-based differential IK scheme that evaluates multiple constrained QPs in parallel with self-collision-avoiding CBFs and Lyapunov-based selection to escape local minima. Coupled with visual skeletal pose estimation, it enables safe, responsive upper-body humanoid teleoperation on THEMIS in real-world tasks.Link
Accelerated Spline-Based Time-Optimal Motion Planning with Continuous Safety Guarantees for Non-Differentially Flat SystemsKU LeuvenProposes decoupling separating hyperplane computation from spline-based optimal control to enforce continuous-time collision avoidance without including hyperplane parameters as decision variables. The resulting planner retains strong safety guarantees while reducing computation time by up to ~60% in cluttered environments for non-differentially flat mobile robots.Link

It computes transitions for over 1000 drones in about one second on a laptop, enabling rapid, visually smooth formation changes with guaranteed collision avoidance.Link
