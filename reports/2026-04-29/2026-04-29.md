## Executive Summary

- Safety-aware control and planning are a strong focus, with new MPC, CBF/HQP, and sliding-mode+C3BF frameworks for manipulators and mobile robots under constraints and obstacles.
- Vision-Language-Action (VLA) manipulation continues to mature, with methods for phase-structured control, reactive tactile feedback, multi-modal sensing, and mitigating fine-tuning lock-in while enabling efficient RL adaptation.
- New planning and navigation techniques span vector-accelerated multi-robot motion planning, direction-aware convex free-region generation, active perception beam search, and robust learning-from-demonstration with failure awareness.
- Whole-body and legged/humanoid control advances include quiet, GRF-aware locomotion, agile wheeled-legged evasion via HRL, and contact-rich humanoid recovery that leverages environment contacts.
- Perception and data-centric methods target dynamic 6-DoF pose via event cameras, semi-supervised segmentation for household objects, and smoothness-based metrics for curating high-quality imitation data.
- Learning-based MPC and generative/vector-field approaches demonstrate increasing cross-over between modern ML (diffusion, score models, flatness-based learning) and classical motion planning/control.
