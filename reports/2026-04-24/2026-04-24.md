## Executive Summary

- Foundation VLA models and calibration techniques (PokeVLA, JoyAI-RA, TD calibration) target more robust, generalizable long-horizon manipulation policies.
- Multiple works advance dexterous and bimanual manipulation via new tactile hardware, simulation, datasets, and LLM-based multi-arm coordination (FingerEye, ETac, VTouch++, BiCICLe).
- Several papers focus on kinematic design and evaluation tools for multi-fingered hands, improving early-stage dexterous hand design and analysis.
- World-model and optimal-control style methods (Cortex 2.0, TD-MPC2, Koopman RL, ORS) push planning, control, and credit assignment for complex motion and manipulation tasks.
- New safety and specification tools (stochastic barrier certificates, STL-based lexicographic planning, passive variable impedance) address safe operation around dynamic obstacles and in shared-control settings.
- Interactive and multimodal skill learning frameworks (MOMO) and domain-specific VLAs (ultrasound-guided needle insertion) demonstrate practical adaptation in industrial and medical contexts.

Using multiple FingerEye sensors, the authors train a vision-tactile imitation learning policy, supported by a digital twin and visual augmentation, enabling robust manipulation of diverse small objects and contact regimes. | https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.20689 |
