Executive Summary

  • Zero-shot and sim-to-real manipulation saw major advances, with large-scale simulated data and new frameworks (MolmoB0T, ExpertGen, SimDist) enabling strong real-robot performance without real-world fine-tuning or heavy reward engineering.
  • Dexterous and 6-DoF grasping progressed via morphology-aligned cross-hand policies, contact-aware data augmentation, and depth-free metric 3D reconstruction from sparse RGB (DexGrasp-Zero, FSG/AutoWS, MG-Grasp).
  • Core motion and coverage planning advanced with ultrafast kinodynamic sampling through differential flatness, flow-based MILP inspection planning, and LLM-aligned geometric waypoint generation for narrow passages.
  • Whole-body and compliant control expanded from soft continuum manipulators and amphibious salamander robots to humanoid compliance-constrained RL, pushing dynamic precision and stability in complex bodies.
  • Foundation-style manipulation models matured: VLM/VLA-based planners gained better physical grounding, velocity-aware tracking, sound-centric sensing, shortcut video-action foresight, and VLM-driven reward shaping.
  • ETH Zurich, CMU, and MIT contributed notable work on quadrupedal assistance for mobility-impaired users, resource-aware LLM reasoning in embodied agents, and robust LiDAR-inertial odometry and BCI-driven exoskeleton control.

Resource-Aware Reasoning via Reinforcement Learning for Embodied Robotic Decision-MakingCarnegie Mellon University; Massachusetts Institute of TechnologyProposes RARRL, a hierarchical RL framework that learns when and how to invoke large-model reasoning in embodied agents given latency and compute constraints. By orchestrating reasoning calls instead of low-level control, it improves task success, reduces execution delays, and increases robustness compared to fixed or heuristic reasoning schedules.Link

Trained jointly on four robotic hands, the policy zero-shot transfers to unseen hands in simulation and real-world platforms, achieving high success on novel objects.Link

Scalable Inspection Planning via Flow-based Mixed Integer Linear ProgrammingReformulates graph inspection planning as a network-flow-based MILP, enabling a specialized Branch-and-Cut solver that scales to tens of thousands of vertices and thousands of inspection points. Across medical, infrastructure, and synthetic benchmarks, it yields tighter lower bounds and smaller optimality gaps than prior formulations.Link
Coverage First Next Best View for Inspection of Cluttered Pipe Networks Using Mobile ManipulatorsUniversity of Manchester; University of TokyoDevelops a next-best-view coverage planner that trades off exploration and exploitation for inspecting cluttered, confined pipe networks with a mobile manipulator. Extends vector-field inequalities with chance constraints to handle geometric uncertainty, demonstrating full coverage and collision avoidance in real-world experiments.Link
MG-Grasp: Metric-Scale Geometric 6-DoF Grasping Framework with Sparse RGB ObservationsHong Kong University of Science and Technology; Tsinghua UniversityIntroduces a depth-free 6-DoF grasping pipeline that reconstructs metric-scale, multi-view-consistent dense point clouds from sparse RGB using a 3D foundation model. Ultrafast Sampling-based Kinodynamic Planning via Differential FlatnessRice UniversityProposes AkinoPDF, which uses differential flatness to move kinodynamic planning into a flat-output space where two-point BVPs and dynamics integration admit analytical solutions.
