## Executive Summary

- Rich cluster of new vision-language-action (VLA) work targets contact-rich manipulation, dynamic camera/view control, and safer long-horizon execution via flow matching and corrective inference.
- Multiple papers push real-robot RL efficiency and sim-to-real reliability using world models, actuator shaping, and single-demonstration intervention schemes.
- Navigation-focused contributions span language-conditioned local flow fields, crowd-aware social preferences, SE(2) navmeshes, and safe/lightweight RL for UAV and USV platforms.
- Dynamic and challenging environments receive attention via physics-principled 3D world models, dynamic SLAM with Gaussian splatting, stereo SLAM with motion filtering, and human-in-the-loop driving simulation.
- ETH Zurich contributes two water-domain control frameworks: cross-platform adaptive RL for autonomous surface vehicles and a choreographed aquatic robot swarm architecture.
