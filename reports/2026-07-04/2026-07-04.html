Executive Summary

  • Rich cluster of new vision-language-action (VLA) work targets contact-rich manipulation, dynamic camera/view control, and safer long-horizon execution via flow matching and corrective inference.
  • Multiple papers push real-robot RL efficiency and sim-to-real reliability using world models, actuator shaping, and single-demonstration intervention schemes.
  • Navigation-focused contributions span language-conditioned local flow fields, crowd-aware social preferences, SE(2) navmeshes, and safe/lightweight RL for UAV and USV platforms.
  • Dynamic and challenging environments receive attention via physics-principled 3D world models, dynamic SLAM with Gaussian splatting, stereo SLAM with motion filtering, and human-in-the-loop driving simulation.
  • ETH Zurich contributes two water-domain control frameworks: cross-platform adaptive RL for autonomous surface vehicles and a choreographed aquatic robot swarm architecture.

Cross-Platform Control for Autonomous Surface Vehicles via Adaptive Reinforcement LearningETH ZurichProposes an adaptive RL policy for trajectory tracking that generalizes across surface vessels with differing dynamics by conditioning on interaction history and a learned latent dynamics representation. Trained in simulation with randomized vessel properties, the single policy transfers zero-shot to two real boats, significantly outperforming non-adaptive baselines and approaching platform-specific tuned controllers.Link
Choreographing the Way of Water: A Computational Framework for Aquatic Robotic ArtETH ZurichIntroduces Way of Water, a cyber-physical stack for choreographing fleets of autonomous surface vessels equipped with laminar water nozzles and lighting for large-scale aquatic performances. A browser-based authoring tool integrates trajectory optimization and MPC through a timeline interface, enabling non-programmers to design music-responsive water and light choreographies validated in two public deployments.Link

CoFL-S: Spatially Queryable Sector Flow Fields for Local Language-Conditioned NavigationPresents CoFL-S, a low-level VLA framework that predicts a continuous, language-conditioned flow field over the robot’s local sector and rolls out trajectories directly from it. By converting VLN-CE episodes into frame-level supervision and evaluating in a continuous-time Habitat benchmark with a shared velocity controller, CoFL-S outperforms token-based baselines and transfers zero-shot to real robots for closed-loop navigation.Link
Multi-Rate Nonlinear Model Predictive Control for Wall-Supported Bipedal Locomotion of Quadrupedal RobotsUniversity of Texas at El Paso, TX 79968, USA; Virginia TechDevelops a multi-rate NMPC framework that jointly plans contact points and CoM/orientation trajectories for quadrupeds performing wall-assisted bipedal locomotion. A high-level SRB-based MR-NMPC optimizes contact and motion under nonlinear dynamics, while a whole-body controller enforces full-order dynamics, yielding significantly higher success rates on rough terrain than heuristic foot-placement MPC.Link
SE(2) Navigation MeshIntroduces SE(2) NavMesh, a polygonal representation that encodes yaw-dependent traversability using footprint masks and layered yaw graphs, overcoming limitations of yaw-invariant navmeshes for non-circular robots. Combined with a hierarchical ASA pathfinding strategy and online incremental updates from point clouds, it expands traversable area and outperforms sampling-based planners in constrained real and simulated environments.Link
SPLC: Social Preference Learning for Crowd Robot Navigation. Zheng are with the SchoolProposes Social Preference Learning for Crowd Robot Navigation, which replaces hand-crafted social reward design in offline RL with automatically generated preference data based on principled social criteria. By encoding pedestrian dynamics and social norms into a preference signal, SPLC consistently improves socially compliant navigation metrics in simulation and on a TurtleBot4 platform.Link
Lightweight Safe Reinforcement Learning for End-to-End UAV NavigationPresents a perception-control integrated framework for high-speed UAV navigation using a lightweight CNN to encode sparse observations into risk-aware features and a safe PPO variant within a constrained MDP formulation. Coupled with curriculum learning and hierarchical control, the approach improves success, safety, and efficiency over RL baselines in dense obstacle fields while remaining suitable for onboard deployment.Link
WaveLander: A Generalizable Hierarchical Control Framework for UAV Landing on Wave-Disturbed Platforms via Reinforcement LearningIntroduces WaveLander, where an RL policy outputs vertical velocity references for landing on wave-disturbed platforms, while a conventional low-level controller handles attitude and lateral stabilization. This hierarchical design reduces landing to a timing-aware scalar control problem and yields robust, smooth landings that generalize to unseen wave conditions in simulation.Link
Path planning for unmanned naval surface vehiclesCombines a global planner—merging Grassfire, a modified Grassfire, and a novel Probabilistic Roadmap variant—with a local planner that reasons about relative obstacle motion to route unmanned surface vehicles behind moving obstacles. Simulations against D* and other dynamic planners show improved handling of both known static and moving obstacles.Link
PhysMani: Physics-principled 3D World Model for Dynamic Object ManipulationProposes PhysMani, which couples a divergence-free 3D Gaussian velocity field world model with an action policy that attends to predicted future dynamics for manipulating fast-moving objects. The physics-grounded online-optimized velocity field enables accurate forecasting in unstructured 3D scenes, leading to superior performance on a new 16-task dynamic manipulation benchmark in sim and on robots.Link
A Stereo Visual SLAM System Using Object-Level Motion Estimation and Geometric Filtering Based on Cross DisparityIndian Institute of Technology Kanpur; Indian Institute of Technology KharagpurIntroduces OCD SLAM, extending ORB-SLAM2 with a geometric "cross disparity" measure that combines temporal and stereo inconsistency to detect dynamic features, plus object-level motion classification via 3D detection and Kalman tracking. The system robustly separates static and dynamic elements, improving trajectory accuracy over ORB-SLAM2 and several dynamic SLAM baselines on KITTI.Link
DL-SLAM: Enabling High-Fidelity Gaussian Splatting SLAM in Dynamic Environments based on Dual-Level ProbabilityPresents DL-SLAM, a monocular Gaussian Splatting SLAM that fuses semantic and geometric cues into pixel-level dynamic probabilities, then aggregates them into object-level dynamic scores. This dual-level scheme prunes dynamic Gaussians to maintain an artifact-free static map while exploiting transiently static objects for pose estimation, yielding up to 13% better tracking and high-fidelity semantic maps.Link
