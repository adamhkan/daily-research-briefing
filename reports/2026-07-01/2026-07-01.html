Executive Summary

  • New planning and control frameworks push long-horizon, cluttered, and contact-rich manipulation to larger scales (LAMP, Chronos, WARP, ConCent, HJ-SafeDMP).
  • Whole-body humanoid and hybrid mobile-manipulation capabilities advance rapidly via synthetic data, closed-loop behavior models, and domain adaptation (VLK, ReactiveBFM, AnyBody, KYON, FADA).
  • Vision-language(-action) models mature into practical manipulation and navigation systems, with improved tokenization, robustness, memory, and test-time self-improvement (ZR-0, SA-VLA, Event-VLA, T^2VLA, ReGuide).
  • Navigation research spans learning-based planners, world-action models, and swarm/UAV methods that emphasize memory, risk-awareness, and timing robustness (CORE Planner, SWAM, SPACE, RoamFlow, LNN-Fly, multi-UAV/quadrotor methods).
  • Touch, visuo-tactile fusion, and structured perception/memory emerge as key enablers for contact-rich and long-horizon tasks (HTT, TAP-VLA, TacGen, YOTO, analytic concept-centric memory, HUMEMBR).
  • Institution-highlighted work includes CMU’s scalable multi-robot manipulation planner (LAMP) and UC Berkeley’s thermal LiDAR-inertial SLAM (PL-LIT) extending operation to adverse visual conditions.

LAMP: Long-Horizon Adaptive Manipulation Planning for Multi-Robot Collaboration in Cluttered SpaceCarnegie Mellon UniversityProposes LAMP, a long-horizon multi-robot manipulation framework that couples a learned generative manipulation model with two planners: LAMPA* for systematic search in joint object–robot space and LAMP-Lazy for real-time replanning via deferred evaluation. It scales to dense, cluttered scenes where prior RL or surrogate-planning approaches fail, enabling coordinated contact-rich manipulation over extended horizons.Link
PL-LIT: A LiDAR-Inertial-Thermal SLAM Using Point-Line Features and Thermographic MappingUC BerkeleyIntroduces PL-LIT, a tightly coupled LiDAR–inertial–(thermal or RGB) SLAM system with online photometric calibration and deep point–line feature extraction suited to low-contrast thermal imagery. Using an ESIKF formulation and a probabilistic thermal-intensity voxel map, it delivers robust long-range odometry and supports real-time thermal anomaly detection in adverse visual conditions.Link
Understanding LLM Intervention Explanations in Multi-Party Human-Robot InteractionETH ZurichAnalyzes 610 explanations generated by an LLM-based orchestrator controlling two social robots in three-human group discussions to understand when and why robots intervene. Thematic analysis shows explanations emphasize facilitation (agreement, participation, flow), with role-specific patterns between "mover" and "opposer" robots, informing explainable, multi-party HRI design.Link

LAMP: Long-Horizon Adaptive Manipulation Planning for Multi-Robot Collaboration in Cluttered SpaceCarnegie Mellon UniversityLAMP tackles long-horizon multi-robot manipulation in highly cluttered environments by combining a learned generative manipulation model with two planners: LAMPA* for exhaustive search in joint object–robot space and LAMP-Lazy for efficient real-time replanning. It jointly reasons about contact formations, coupled dynamics, and collision avoidance, solving dense scenarios that defeat prior RL and surrogate-planning baselines.Link
VLK: Learning Humanoid Loco-Manipulation from Synthetic Interactions in Reconstructed ScenesVLK creates large-scale vision–language–kinematics supervision by reconstructing indoor environments with 3D Gaussian splats, synthesizing navigation and interaction trajectories, and rendering egocentric views post hoc. A learned policy outputs short-horizon whole-body trajectories for a Unitree G1 humanoid, enabling sim-to-real loco-manipulation from purely synthetic interactions.Link
ReactiveBFM: Reactive Closed-Loop Motion Planning Towards Universal Humanoid Whole-Body ControlReactiveBFM integrates a generative behavior foundation model with closed-loop planning, using scheduled prefix sampling to train error-recovery behaviors and asynchronous replanning to match high-frequency tracking. On the Unitree G1, it achieves robust, text-conditioned whole-body motions including zero-shot moving-target reaching, significantly outperforming open-loop cascaded baselines under perturbations.Link
KYON: Semi-Modular Wheel-Legged Quadruped With Agile Bimanual CapabilityIstituto Italiano di TecnologiaPresents KYON, a wheel–leg hybrid quadruped with a bimanual upper body designed for loco-manipulation in complex terrain. A whole-body control framework plus RL-based policies drive dynamic locomotion and independent bimanual manipulation, validating agile, reconfigurable mobile manipulation in unstructured environments.Link
WARP: Whole-Body Retargeting for Learning from Offline Human DemonstrationsWARP introduces a whole-body-aware retargeting pipeline that maps human pose data to consistent robot actions for mobile manipulators by explicitly modeling embodiment differences. Using a closed-form SEW (Shoulder–Elbow–Wrist) solver and lazy base control, it yields precise trajectories that support zero-shot whole-body mobile manipulation from offline human videos without teleoperation.Link
OpenSPM: An Environment-Transferable Robotic Key Spatial Pose Memory and Closed-Loop High-Frequency Flow-Matching Action Generation ModelOpenSPM stores object-centric key spatial poses as a persistent memory, retrieved via language and transferred to new scenes with SE(3) transforms to guide manipulation. A lightweight conditional flow-matching model generates high-frequency closed-loop action chunks with residual corrections, achieving >1 kHz effective control and strong performance on LIBERO-GOAL tabletop tasks.Link
CORE Planner: Contextual-memory Oriented Reinforcement-learning in Unknown Environments for Robot NavigationXi'an Jiaotong UniversityCORE Planner combines a sparse visibility-graph representation with a Transformer-based contextual memory to support RL-based navigation in large, unknown environments. It reduces path length and improves sim-to-real transfer over traditional and learning baselines, achieving zero-shot real-world navigation without fine-tuning.Link
AnyBody: Free-Form Whole-Body Humanoid Control from Arbitrary Keypoint GuidanceAnyBody learns a latent motion space for humanoids that can be driven by any subset of body keypoints specified at deployment, enabling flexible tracking and free-form control. A privileged teacher tracker is distilled into a latent motor prior, and a keypoint transformer aligns arbitrary keypoint sets to this latent, supporting teleoperation and downstream loco-manipulation behaviors via a lightweight residual corrector.Link
HJ-SafeDMP: Hamilton-Jacobi Reachability-Guided Dynamic Movement Primitives for Provably Safe Robot MotionIndian Institute of ScienceHJ-SafeDMP augments Dynamic Movement Primitives with a learned Hamilton–Jacobi Control Barrier Value Function that acts as a closed-form safety filter on top of nominal motions. Trained offline via finite-difference HJ recursion with conformal prediction calibration, it yields provably safe, robust arm motions with orders-of-magnitude faster execution than optimization-based CBF-QPs while preserving DMP adaptability.Link
HUMEMBR: Learning Human Routines for Predictive Embodied NavigationGeorge Mason UniversityHUMEMBR builds structured, continuous memory of human activities from long-term robot observations to support routine-aware navigation and question answering. By separating ongoing memory construction from query-time retrieval, it improves long-horizon reasoning about where and when people are likely to be present and is validated both in simulation and on physical robots in real environments.Link
