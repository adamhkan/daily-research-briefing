Executive Summary

  • New foundation models and benchmarks (Qwen-RobotManip, EBench, GeneralVLA-2, EgoInfinity, AnnotateAnything) push large-scale, generalist robot learning, especially for manipulation.
  • Multiple works target more reliable, safer control: warm-started trajectory optimization for space manipulators, latency-aware diffusion policies, learnable priors, and uncertainty-aware VLA policies.
  • Navigation and mapping advance via scalable learned navigation backbones, visual scale-aware policies, radar-based SLAM, and active-perception planning for UAVs.
  • Whole-body and dexterous interaction see progress with a compact low-cost anthropomorphic hand, humanoid whole-body control benchmarking, multisensory manipulation, and rapid damage adaptation in soft systems.
  • Skill acquisition and continual learning are enabled by sleep-phase consolidation, inference-time visual verification, reinforcement learning over world–action models, and unified human–robot pretraining.
  • Assistive and social settings benefit from personalized exoskeleton adaptation, abstention-aware rearrangement, and socially informed pedestrian trajectory prediction and cooperative path planning.

Transformer-Based Warm-Starting for Feasible and Optimal Terminal Approach to Tumbling Objects with Space ManipulatorsStanford UniversityIntroduces a causal transformer warm-start for the computational bottleneck in sequential convex programming for space manipulator terminal guidance to tumbling targets. Decomposing translation and coupled attitude–manipulator planning, the method cuts second-stage SCP iterations by up to 28% and runtime by 23%, while preserving control cost and improving nonconvex feasibility projection robustness.Link
Continual Online Personalization of Exoskeleton Control via Manifold-Aware Experience ReplayCarnegie Mellon UniversityProposes a manifold-aware experience replay framework to preserve user-specific exoskeleton control across varying gait tasks during online adaptation. By replaying experiences organized on a learned gait manifold without explicit task labels, it mitigates catastrophic forgetting and improves torque and gait phase tracking by 40–60% over non-replay baselines.Link

EBench: Elemental Diagnosis of Generalist Mobile Manipulation PoliciesXi'an Jiaotong UniversityPresents EBench, a simulation benchmark of 26 mobile manipulation tasks annotated along capability and generalization axes to go beyond single success-rate metrics. Evaluating leading generalist policies (π0, π0.5, XVLA, InternVLA-A1), it reveals divergent skill profiles and failure modes across dexterous and mobile subtasks despite similar overall success, enabling fine-grained diagnosis for model design.Link
Qwen-RobotNav Technical Report: A Scalable Navigation Model Designed for an Agentic Navigation SystemIntroduces Qwen-RobotNav, a scalable base navigation model with a parameterized interface over task modes and observation configurations, enabling reconfigurable perception–planning at inference. Trained on 15.6M samples with vision–language co-training, it achieves state-of-the-art on multiple navigation benchmarks and supports hierarchical planners that dynamically switch modes for long-horizon tasks.Link
WireCraft: A Simulation Benchmark for Industrial DLO ManipulationIntroduces WireCraft, a benchmark for industrial deformable linear object (wire/cable) manipulation across connector insertion, clip routing, and channel seating tasks with configurable difficulty and physics models. Comparative evaluations of RL, IL, and VLA policies show privileged-state RL can solve representative tasks, while vision-based policies still struggle with contact-rich alignment, highlighting open challenges in dexterous DLO manipulation.Link
EAGG: Embodiment-Aligned Grasp Generation via Geometry-Aware Graph ConditioningTsinghua UniversityProposes EAGG, a cross-end-effector grasp generator that encodes each hand or gripper as a topology-aware graph and low-dimensional control space, with a frozen backbone producing geometry-aware tokens. Iterative geometry injection keeps conditioning synchronized with evolving hand configurations, achieving near-specialized performance across six end-effectors and strong zero-shot transfer on the MultiGripperGrasp benchmark.Link
A Hybrid Optimization Framework for Grasp Synthesis under Partial ObservationsNVIDIA; University of SydneyCombines an energy-based model with ICP in an SVGD optimization loop to synthesize robust grasps from partial point clouds. Across 5,360 attempts on 67 objects, the hybrid approach attains 60.9% success, outperforming AnyGrasp, GPD, and AS-ICP, demonstrating the value of coupling learned priors with geometric refinement for grasp planning.Link
LAGO Policy: Latency-Aware Asynchronous Diffusion Policies with Goal-Directed Collision-Free Planning for Smooth ManipulationHong Kong University of Science and Technology; POSTECH; The University of Hong KongIntroduces LAGO, an asynchronous diffusion-based control framework that accounts for inference latency and integrates trajectory optimization for smooth, collision-free manipulation. It conditions diffusion on future actions for temporal consistency, predicts task-relevant interaction goals, and refines trajectories with spatio-temporal optimization, yielding smoother, safer executions in real-world experiments.Link
HumanoidArena: Benchmarking Egocentric Hierarchical Whole-body LearningPresents HumanoidArena, a simulation benchmark for hierarchical whole-body humanoid control where high-level policies map egocentric inputs to intermediate whole-body actions executed by motion trackers. Seven leg-critical HOI/HSI tasks expose tight coupling between decision-making and locomotion, showing that performance is highly tracker-dependent and cross-tracker transfer of intermediate actions remains fragile.Link
FLAP: FOV-Constrained Active Perception Planning for Prior-Map-Free 3D NavigationZhejiang UniversityProposes FLAP, a trajectory optimization framework that embeds field-of-view and sensing constraints directly in the planning problem for UAV navigation in unknown 3D clutter. Using sensor-frame perception constraints, a velocity-triggered activation mechanism, and an active perception sub-trajectory, it supports aggressive 3D maneuvers without prior maps, improving safety and efficiency in simulations and real flights.Link
RICH-SLAM: Radar SLAM with Incremental and Continuous Hilbert MappingChinese Academy of Sciences; Hong Kong University of Science and Technology; University of Science and Technology of China; Zhejiang UniversityIntroduces RICH-SLAM, a radar-based SLAM system combining a Rao–Blackwellized particle filter with an incremental Hilbert-space reduced-rank GP map. The method converts sparse, noisy radar returns into continuous, uncertainty-aware occupancy maps and uses posterior-aware particle weighting, enabling robust localization and planning in adverse conditions.Link
DexLink Hand: A Compact, Affordable, 16-DOF Linkage-Driven Hand with Human-Like DexterityNational University of Singapore; Zhejiang UniversityPresents DexLink, a fully self-contained anthropomorphic hand with 20 joints driven by 16 actuators, fitting within human-hand size at under 400 USD. A hybrid planar–spatial linkage architecture delivers biomimetic thumb motion, joint synergies, and high load capacity, achieving maximum Kapandji score and all 33 Feix grasp types in experiments.Link
