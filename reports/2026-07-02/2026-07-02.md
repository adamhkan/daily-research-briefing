## Executive Summary

- MIT and Stanford lead a comprehensive synthesis on robustness in robotic manipulation, formalizing definitions, mechanisms, and evaluation gaps across perception, planning, control, and hardware.
- Task-conditioned Gaussian-splatting from MIT shows how language-specified regions of interest can drive efficient, real-time 3D mapping for downstream robot tasks.
- Navigation and motion-planning work span hierarchical semantic maps, temporal-logic-guided language navigation, UAV trajectory generation, and compressed latent-space planning, emphasizing long-horizon efficiency and safety.
- Dexterous and whole-body manipulation advances include zero-demo compositional dexterity, grasp-pretrained functional dexterity, humanoid manipulation benchmarks, and multi-modal tactile/VLA frameworks.
- New datasets and benchmarks (tactile, human-present manipulation, safety-aware damage simulation, humanoid and lab dexterity) target generalization, safety, and human-awareness in real-world deployments.
