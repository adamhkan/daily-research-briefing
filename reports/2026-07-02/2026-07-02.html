Executive Summary

  • MIT and Stanford lead a comprehensive synthesis on robustness in robotic manipulation, formalizing definitions, mechanisms, and evaluation gaps across perception, planning, control, and hardware.
  • Task-conditioned Gaussian-splatting from MIT shows how language-specified regions of interest can drive efficient, real-time 3D mapping for downstream robot tasks.
  • Navigation and motion-planning work span hierarchical semantic maps, temporal-logic-guided language navigation, UAV trajectory generation, and compressed latent-space planning, emphasizing long-horizon efficiency and safety.
  • Dexterous and whole-body manipulation advances include zero-demo compositional dexterity, grasp-pretrained functional dexterity, humanoid manipulation benchmarks, and multi-modal tactile/VLA frameworks.
  • New datasets and benchmarks (tactile, human-present manipulation, safety-aware damage simulation, humanoid and lab dexterity) target generalization, safety, and human-awareness in real-world deployments.

GaussLite: Online Task-Conditioned 3D Gaussian Splatting for Real-Time Robotic MappingMassachusetts Institute of TechnologyIntroduces a task-driven 3D Gaussian Splatting mapper that allocates representational capacity according to a natural-language task description, focusing density and optimization effort on task-relevant regions. Using online relevance masks from open-vocabulary detection, it achieves significantly higher ROI reconstruction quality at fixed Gaussian budgets and real-time rates on constrained hardware.Link

RRT-Rope: A deterministic shortening approach for fast near-optimal path planning in large-scale uncluttered 3D environmentsProposes RRT-Rope, which first finds a feasible path with an altered RRT-Connect and then applies a deterministic, node-aware shortcutting scheme to rapidly approach near-optimal solutions. Motion Planning in Compressed Representation SpacesLearns a highly compressed, tokenized latent space with a hierarchical autoencoder and performs motion planning directly via search over latent tokens instead of raw trajectories. This unifies learning-based priors with model-based optimization, enabling efficient, flexible planning for tasks like autonomous driving while optimizing arbitrary test-time objectives.Link
LeCropFollow: Latent Space Planning for Navigation in Unstructured Crop FieldsUniversity of Illinois Urbana-ChampaignIntroduces a visual navigation framework that plans directly in a learned latent manifold using semantic heatmaps and a model-based RL planner (TD-MPC2), avoiding brittle geometric reconstruction. Demonstrates robust under-canopy navigation in irregular crop rows with strong zero-shot sim-to-real transfer and substantial reductions in semantic failures versus geometric baselines.Link
MVP-Nav: Multi-layer Value Map Planner NavigatorPresents an RGB-only object-goal navigation framework that reconstructs a 3D semantic occupancy map using foundation models and encodes both semantics and geometry into a Multi-layer Value Map. This unified cost space supports physically grounded planning and achieves state-of-the-art zero-shot object-goal navigation without active depth sensing.Link
DynFly: Dynamic-Aware Continuous Trajectory Generation for UAV Vision-Language Navigation in Urban EnvironmentsBridges high-level vision-language navigation and low-level UAV control by learning B-spline control-point trajectories via a Spline-DiT generator with flow matching. Dynamic-aware supervision over position, velocity, acceleration, and heading yields continuous, executable UAV paths that substantially improve navigation metrics and trajectory quality on OpenUAV benchmarks.Link
Diffusion-based 4D Trajectory Prediction and Distributed Control for UAV SwarmsHong Kong University of Science and Technology; The University of Hong KongCouples a dimension-decoupled trajectory forecaster with a diffusion-based residual dynamics model to predict 4D swarm motion under uncertainty, feeding predictions into a distributed NMPC controller. On a new multi-scenario UAV swarm dataset, the framework reduces tracking error by 10–15% and achieves sub-0.07 m errors in complex environments at real-time rates.Link
Hierarchical 3D Scene Graph Construction and Belief-based Planning for Semantic NavigationMaintains an online Hierarchical 3D Scene Graph over objects, zones, and regions to provide multi-scale state abstraction for semantic navigation. A belief-based planner performs rollouts on this graph, fusing priors with exploration evidence to choose macro-actions, yielding improved success and path efficiency, especially for long-distance navigation.Link
ViTL: Temporal Logic-Guided Zero-Shot Natural Language Navigation via Vision-Language ModelsLehigh UniversityTransforms natural-language commands with temporal structure into Linear Temporal Logic and DFA, orchestrating multi-goal, temporally constrained navigation with dynamic replanning.
