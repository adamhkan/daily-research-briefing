Executive Summary

Goal-Conditioned Neural ODEs with Guaranteed Safety and Stability for Learning-Based All-Pairs Motion PlanningUniversity of SydneyProposes goal-conditioned neural ODEs constructed via bi-Lipschitz diffeomorphisms for all-pairs motion planning with formal guarantees of global exponential stability and set invariance safety. Provides explicit bounds on convergence and tracking error, and demonstrates the method on a constrained 2D corridor navigation problem, showing how learning-based planners can retain rigorous control-theoretic guarantees.Link
Differentiable SpaTiaL: Symbolic Learning and Reasoning with Geometric Temporal Logic for Manipulation TasksLehigh University; University of California, RiversideIntroduces a fully tensorized, end-to-end differentiable spatio-temporal logic toolbox operating directly on polygonal sets, with smooth relaxations of key geometric predicates such as distance, intersection, and containment. This enables gradient-based trajectory optimization under rich spatio-temporal constraints and learning of spatial logic parameters from demonstrations, targeting complex manipulation in clutter.Link
Multi-View Video Diffusion Policy: A 3D Spatio-Temporal-Aware Video Action ModelPresents MV-VDP, a multi-view video diffusion policy that jointly predicts future RGB and heatmap videos to model 3D spatio-temporal evolution of manipulation scenes. With as few as ten demonstrations and no extra pretraining, it achieves state-of-the-art data-efficient multi-task manipulation performance and robust generalization on Meta-World and real-robot benchmarks.Link
ARM: Advantage Reward Modeling for Long-Horizon ManipulationProposes Advantage Reward Modeling (ARM), which replaces dense progress scores with tri-state human labels (Progressive/Regressive/Stagnant) to learn relative advantages for long-horizon manipulation. Integrated into offline RL with DAgger-style data, ARM automates reward shaping and filtering of poor samples, reaching 99.4% success on a challenging towel-folding task with minimal human intervention.Link
Tune to Learn: How Controller Gains Shape Robot Policy LearningSystematically studies how position controller gains affect behavior cloning, RL from scratch, and sim-to-real transfer for manipulation policies. Finds that compliant/overdamped gains aid imitation learning, RL can adapt across regimes with proper tuning, and stiff/overdamped gains hurt transfer, arguing that gain choice should be driven by learnability, not just desired task stiffness.Link
F2F-AP: Flow-to-Future Asynchronous Policy for Real-time Dynamic ManipulationIntroduces an asynchronous manipulation policy that predicts object flow to synthesize future observations, combined with a flow-based contrastive objective aligning predicted and true future features. By explicitly compensating for perception–action latency, the method improves responsiveness and success rates when interacting with rapidly moving objects in dynamic tasks.Link
The Compression Gap: Why Discrete Tokenization Limits Vision-Language-Action Model ScalingAnalyzes VLA pipelines through an information-theoretic lens and shows that discrete action tokenization creates a dominant bottleneck that prevents scaled vision encoders from improving manipulation performance. Empirical studies on LIBERO demonstrate that continuous-action diffusion policies benefit strongly from better encoders, while discretized models plateau unless codebook capacity is increased.Link
Open-Loop Planning, Closed-Loop Verification: Speculative Verification for VLAProposes SV-VLA, where a heavyweight VLA serves as a low-frequency macro-planner that outputs action chunks plus context, and a lightweight verifier checks each step against a closed-loop reference conditioned on current observations. The verifier triggers replanning only when needed, combining the efficiency of action chunking with robustness to environment changes.Link
Learning Structured Robot Policies from Vision-Language Models via Synthetic Neuro-Symbolic SupervisionItalian Ministry of UniversityPresents a neuro-symbolic framework where a VLM maps images, language instructions, and system specs to executable Behavior Tree policies instead of opaque end-to-end controllers. A synthetic data pipeline auto-generates domain-randomized scenes and instruction–policy pairs, and the learned models transfer to real manipulators, yielding interpretable structured policies for multimodal decision-making.Link
OMNI-PoseX: A Fast Vision Model for 6D Object Pose Estimation in Embodied TasksDevelops OMNI-PoseX, a foundation model for open-vocabulary 6D pose estimation that separates semantic object understanding from SO(3)-aware reflected flow matching for rotations. Trained on large-scale pose datasets, it achieves state-of-the-art accuracy and real-time performance, providing geometrically consistent poses that support reliable grasping of novel objects.Link
