## Executive Summary

- Neuro-symbolic and world-model-style planners are advancing long-horizon task and motion reasoning, including image-grounded STRIPS operators and bilevel task planners that run on real quadruped mobile manipulators.
- Vision-Language-Action (VLA) manipulation policies are being strengthened via structured action representations (splines, voxel heatmaps, action-token plans) and better alignment of latent actions, with new tools for rapid task adaptation and failure recovery.
- Robust visuomotor manipulation and grasping see progress through explicit action-coordinate visualization, cross-view 6-DoF grasp fusion, and affordance-driven hierarchical RL for quadruped “pedipulation.”
- Navigation and trajectory planning work spans fine-grained UAV and language-guided indoor navigation, offline diffusion-based long-horizon route composition, active semantic exploration, and test-time optimization in driving and lane changes.
- Foundational perspectives highlight that scaling VLAs is not enough, emphasizing interfaces for learning from unstructured human videos and procedurally generated manipulation puzzles, while new co-design methods speed up locomotion robot morphology optimization.
