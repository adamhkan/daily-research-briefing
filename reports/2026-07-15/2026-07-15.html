Executive Summary

  • MIT and CMU contribute a strong cluster of infrastructure papers, from GPU-accelerated large-batch MPC (WarpMPC) and Koopman-augmented legged RL (SKooP) to mmWave radar mapping and robust LiDAR-inertial odometry for navigation in adverse conditions.
  • Navigation and motion-planning advances span stochastic path priors (AutoPath), physics-informed rectified flows for fast collision-free control (PIER-Flow), and multiple social or human-aware navigation formulations (HUMA, SFM-NMPC, MRRD).
  • Manipulation-focused works push diffusion/flow policies, image-space and classification-based action parameterizations (MoF, SegDiff, AMP, Pix2Act), and whole-body loco-manipulation frameworks (PAKE, TAC-LOCO), including bimanual and quadruped-arm systems.
  • Dexterous and teleoperated manipulation see new pipelines that leverage single human demos, teleoperation with co-tracking controllers, and large egocentric video corpora (REGRIND, TeleDexter, EgoSteer), plus cross-hand grasp generators (GraspGraphNet).
  • Vision-language(-action) integration continues to mature across city-scale VLN, VLA-based general manipulation, UAV active perception, and navigation foundation models (DA-Nav, VIA, ABot-N1, Traj-VLN, ActiveFly-Bench, TS-Mask VLA, Lumo-2).
  • Surveys and toolkits (coverage path planning survey, VLN survey, CR-Solver for continuum robots) highlight consolidation of methods alongside emerging safety frameworks for exploration, multi-robot systems, and driving (SEAMLiS, D-SafeMPC, BucketKD).

SKooP: Symmetric Koopman Predictions for Faster and More Generalizable Legged Robot Locomotion with Reinforcement LearningMassachusetts Institute of TechnologySKooP jointly learns a Koopman dynamics model and symmetry-aware policy for legged locomotion, using Koopman predictions as privileged critic inputs. This yields smoother, more informative features for RL, accelerating convergence and improving reward across challenging bipedal-style tasks on a quadruped.Link
WarpMPC: Large-Batch MPC on GPU via ADMM with Unrolled $LDL^\top$ FactorizationMassachusetts Institute of TechnologyWarpMPC exploits shared MPC problem structure to unroll sparse LDLᵀ factorizations on GPU, dramatically increasing throughput for large batches of SQP iterations. The approach attains 3–25× speedups on cartpole, quadrotor, and humanoid benchmarks and can quickly generate data to train neural approximations of MPC policies.Link
3D Scene Graph Prediction: Generating Hierarchical Models from Partially Observed EnvironmentsMassachusetts Institute of TechnologyThis work predicts hierarchical 3D scene graphs—floor-plan and traversability at the room level plus per-room object layouts—from partial observations. A mixed-domain graph diffusion model handles rooms, while an existing diffusion component generates object arrangements, enabling robots to anticipate unseen structure for exploration and search.Link
Interleaved POMDP Planning for Multi-Object Search in Unknown Multi-Room Household EnvironmentsMassachusetts Institute of TechnologyInter-POMDP decomposes multi-object search into a high-level POMDP over object-location beliefs and a low-level navigation POMDP over obstacle-aware particles. By interleaving the two planners and seeding beliefs from LLM-informed histograms, it reduces collisions, steps, and detections versus baselines in simulated and real homes.Link

The resulting ROS 2 Jazzy implementation improves robustness, supports multiple LiDAR families, and is validated on several sensor setups.Link

NeuralActuator: Neural Actuation Modeling for Robot Dynamics and External Force PerceptionMassachusetts Institute of TechnologyNeuralActuator learns a neural model of actuator behavior that predicts effective joint efforts, external forces with a contact gate, and motor-condition scores from telemetry. Mixture of Frames Policy: Multi-Frame Action Denoising for Bimanual Mobile ManipulationMoF is a diffusion-based policy that denoises a shared SE(3) action state across multiple task-aligned frames with frame-specialized denoisers, then fuses their predictions. It leverages a column-based 6D rotation parameterization to keep frame transforms differentiable even for noisy states, outperforming single-frame and MoE baselines in simulated and real bimanual mobile manipulation.Link
PAKE: Learning Whole-Body Loco-Manipulation with Partial Kinematic EmbeddingsPAKE decomposes quadruped-with-arm loco-manipulation into partial reference motion generation via a Kinematic Normalizing Flow and low-level imitation control. A high-level controller navigates the KNF latent space to exploit redundancy, enabling accurate end-effector and base tracking in simulation and real-world whole-body tasks.Link
TAC-LOCO: Unified Whole-Body Control for Quadrupedal TACtile-Informed LOCO-ManipulationTAC-LOCO fuses tactile array inputs from a compliant gripper with proprioception in a unified RL policy for leg–arm–gripper control on a quadruped platform. The learned controller tracks base and end-effector commands while modulating grasp force, achieving substantial force reduction and very low object drop rates under varying external loads.Link
A Minimalist Retargeting-Guided Reinforcement Learning Recipe for Dexterous ManipulationREGRIND retargets a single human hand–object demo into a robot reference that preserves contact geometry, then trains a residual RL policy to track object-centric keypoints in simulation. With careful system identification, the policy transfers zero-shot to real multi-fingered hands for tool-use tasks such as scissors operation and screwdriver turning.Link
Towards Human-level Dexterous TeleoperationTeleDexter learns a hand–object co-tracking controller that maps operator intent to low-level contact-rich execution using RL on subgoal sequences extracted from human motion. The single-stage RL pipeline, with domain randomization and action masking, transfers zero-shot to hardware across multiple dexterous hands, enabling challenging in-hand reorientation and tool use.Link
GraspGraphNet: Graph-Structured Multi-Embodiment Dexterous Grasp GenerationKorea Advanced Institute of Science and TechnologyGraspGraphNet represents each robotic hand as a URDF-derived kinematic graph and performs conditional flow matching directly in joint and palm-pose space. Trained jointly on several hand types, it generates executable dexterous grasps with high success and low inference latency, generalizing without retraining to topology variations such as finger removal.Link
AutoPath: Learning Transferable Goal-Conditioned Stochastic Path Prior for Safe Navigation Without Human DemonstrationsHarbin Institute of Technology; Shanghai Jiao Tong University; Zhejiang UniversityAutoPath learns a goal-conditioned stochastic path prior on a canonical, rotation-normalized state space, modeling multimodal local paths consistent with geometry. A polar action manifold and risk-sensitive utility shaping enable sampling diverse yet safe trajectories that transfer across robot platforms, from differential-drive bases to quadrupeds, without retraining.Link
DA-Nav: Direction-Aware City-Scale Vision-Language NavigationChinese Academy of Sciences; University of Chinese Academy of SciencesDA-Nav turns commercial turn-by-turn directions into executable actions by formulating navigation as discrete spatial grounding on the image plane and layering Chain-of-Thought reasoning for deviation detection, action choice, and target selection. Trained with the new ReDA dataset in CARLA, it outperforms prior VLN methods and runs zero-shot on quadruped and humanoid robots for kilometer-scale real-world navigation.Link
Stop to Decide: Latency-Aware Proprioceptive Navigation Primitives for Mapping-Free Quadruped InspectionFocusing on compute-limited quadrupeds, this work shows that in-motion stair-summit detection fails systematically at low navigation loop rates. A modified "climb–settle" cadence combined with simple proprioceptive and range sensing yields near-zero overshoot and enables a fully onboard, mapping-free inspection stack that reliably follows corridors and stairs at varying control frequencies.Link
Learning to Navigate Efficiently with Only 0.58M Trainable ParametersNanyang Technological UniversityThis navigation system analytically computes projective geometry, occupancy, and frame transforms, using them as interfaces between three tiny learned modules for goal grounding, trajectory distribution estimation, and residual-diffusion trajectory sampling. Training just 0.58M parameters on modest data, it approaches state-of-the-art point-goal performance with far fewer trainable weights, lowest collision rates, and 50 Hz inference.Link
