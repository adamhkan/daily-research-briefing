## Executive Summary

- MIT and CMU contribute a strong cluster of infrastructure papers, from GPU-accelerated large-batch MPC (WarpMPC) and Koopman-augmented legged RL (SKooP) to mmWave radar mapping and robust LiDAR-inertial odometry for navigation in adverse conditions.
- Navigation and motion-planning advances span stochastic path priors (AutoPath), physics-informed rectified flows for fast collision-free control (PIER-Flow), and multiple social or human-aware navigation formulations (HUMA, SFM-NMPC, MRRD).
- Manipulation-focused works push diffusion/flow policies, image-space and classification-based action parameterizations (MoF, SegDiff, AMP, Pix2Act), and whole-body loco-manipulation frameworks (PAKE, TAC-LOCO), including bimanual and quadruped-arm systems.
- Dexterous and teleoperated manipulation see new pipelines that leverage single human demos, teleoperation with co-tracking controllers, and large egocentric video corpora (REGRIND, TeleDexter, EgoSteer), plus cross-hand grasp generators (GraspGraphNet).
- Vision-language(-action) integration continues to mature across city-scale VLN, VLA-based general manipulation, UAV active perception, and navigation foundation models (DA-Nav, VIA, ABot-N1, Traj-VLN, ActiveFly-Bench, TS-Mask VLA, Lumo-2).
- Surveys and toolkits (coverage path planning survey, VLN survey, CR-Solver for continuum robots) highlight consolidation of methods alongside emerging safety frameworks for exploration, multi-robot systems, and driving (SEAMLiS, D-SafeMPC, BucketKD).
