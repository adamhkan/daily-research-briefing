Executive Summary

  • New standardized benchmarks and datasets are emerging for dexterous hands (POMDAR) and assembly-focused spatial reasoning (AssemBench), aiming to make manipulation research more comparable and data-rich.
  • Foundational advances in motion and impedance control target portability and safety, from unified low-level planners for collaborative arms to robust adaptive impedance for contact-rich mobile manipulation.
  • Multi-modal perception methods are pushing beyond vision-only pipelines, using touch, proprioception, and diffusion priors for 3D reconstruction under severe occlusion and sparse contact.
  • Risk-aware whole-body control and design frameworks are maturing, with differentiable safety-constrained locomotion control for quadrupeds and latent-space optimization for humanoid kinematics.
  • Agentic data generation pipelines and assembly-specialized multimodal LLMs are being proposed to bridge high-level reasoning with physically feasible low-level manipulation and assembly execution.
  • Personalized control for wearable robotics is advanced via musculoskeletal simulation and RL, pointing toward scalable exoskeleton assistance without extensive in-person tuning.

Demonstrations in drawing, dynamic object grasping, and teleoperated dexterous manipulation show smooth, interruptible trajectories that transfer across heterogeneous manipulators.Link
Robust Adaptive Backstepping Impedance Control of Robots in Unknown EnvironmentsDevelops a robust adaptive backstepping impedance controller that handles coupled robot dynamics, external disturbances, and unmodeled effects without requiring known dynamic parameters. Simulations on a mobile manipulator and experiments on a Franka Panda show safer contact-rich interaction and accurate trajectory/force tracking versus PD control.Link
Task-Aware Bimanual Affordance Prediction via VLM-Guided Semantic-Geometric ReasoningGerman Research Center for Artificial IntelligenceFrames bimanual manipulation as a joint affordance localization and arm allocation problem, fusing multi-view RGB-D with a VLM to select task-relevant contact regions and assign arms accordingly. Real dual-arm experiments across nine tasks show higher task success than geometric or purely semantic baselines for coordinated bimanual actions.Link

AssemLM: Spatial Reasoning Multimodal Large Language Models for Robotic AssemblyIntroduces AssemLM, a multimodal LLM that ingests assembly manuals, point clouds, and text to predict task-critical 6D assembly poses, supported by a specialized point-cloud encoder for fine-grained 3D geometry. The accompanying AssemBench dataset (900K+ samples) enables assessment of 3D spatial reasoning, and real-robot tests show multi-step assembly execution.Link
TouchAnything: Diffusion-Guided 3D Reconstruction from Sparse Robot TouchesUses a pretrained 2D visual diffusion model as a semantic and geometric prior to reconstruct object shape from sparse tactile contacts and a coarse class label. By optimizing shapes under tactile consistency and diffusion guidance, the method achieves accurate, category-agnostic 3D reconstructions from few touches, outperforming prior tactile reconstruction approaches.Link
Physically Grounded 3D Generative Reconstruction under Hand Occlusion using Proprioception and Multi-Contact TouchProposes a multimodal structure-VAE and flow-matching diffusion pipeline to reconstruct metric-scale SDFs of objects heavily occluded by a manipulating hand. Conditioning on RGB, visibility masks, hand pose latents, and tactile contacts, plus physics-based interpenetration penalties, yields more accurate and physically plausible reconstructions that transfer to real humanoid manipulation.Link

Deployed on Unitree A1 and ANYmal C, it cuts safety violations by up to 10× and energy use by up to 15% versus CBF, MPC, and RL baselines while maintaining 200 Hz real-time operation.Link
