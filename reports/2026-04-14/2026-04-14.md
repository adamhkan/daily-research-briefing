## Executive Summary

- New standardized benchmarks and datasets are emerging for dexterous hands (POMDAR) and assembly-focused spatial reasoning (AssemBench), aiming to make manipulation research more comparable and data-rich.
- Foundational advances in motion and impedance control target portability and safety, from unified low-level planners for collaborative arms to robust adaptive impedance for contact-rich mobile manipulation.
- Multi-modal perception methods are pushing beyond vision-only pipelines, using touch, proprioception, and diffusion priors for 3D reconstruction under severe occlusion and sparse contact.
- Risk-aware whole-body control and design frameworks are maturing, with differentiable safety-constrained locomotion control for quadrupeds and latent-space optimization for humanoid kinematics.
- Agentic data generation pipelines and assembly-specialized multimodal LLMs are being proposed to bridge high-level reasoning with physically feasible low-level manipulation and assembly execution.
- Personalized control for wearable robotics is advanced via musculoskeletal simulation and RL, pointing toward scalable exoskeleton assistance without extensive in-person tuning.
